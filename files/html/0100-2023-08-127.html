3. Excepted Service Nonpreference Eligible Temporary Appointees

The statutory definition of employee includes, 5 USC 7511(a)(1):

(C) an individual in the excepted service (other than a preference eligible)--

(i) who is not serving a probationary or trial period under an initial appointment pending conversion to the competitive service; or

(ii) who has completed 2 years of current continuous service in the same or similar positions in an Executive agency under other than a temporary appointment limited to 2 years or less;...

Before Mitchell v. MSPB, 741 F.3d 81 (Fed. Cir. 2014) (discussed earlier in this chapter, "Time in Nonpermanent Appointment as a Component of Current Continuous Service"), Coleman v. FDIC, 62 MSPR 187, 189-90 (1994), concluded that service in a temporary appointment was not included in continuous service time following conversion of the appellant's excepted service appointment from temporary to career-conditional:

In his petition for review, the appellant argues that he meets the statutory definition of an employee under 5 USC 7511(a)(1)(C)(ii). In this connection, he argues that Congress intended the CSDPA to bestow Board appeal rights on excepted service employees and "eliminate procedural differences in their right to appeal to the [Board] as compared to competitive service and preference eligible employees." Thus, he contends that, while "strictly temporary employment may not be cumulated to satisfy initial probationary periods, where an employee is converted from a temporary status to a career-conditional or career status, prior service in temporary positions may be counted toward completion of any new probationary periods." In support of his contention, he relies on the legislative history of the CSDPA, and several Board decisions defining the term "employee" for purposes of jurisdiction under 5 USC 7511.[2]

[2] See Pennington v. Department of Veterans Affairs, 57 MSPR 8 (1993); Raman v. Department of Housing and Urban Development, 34 MSPR 428 (1987), aff'd, 845 F.2d 1034 (Fed. Cir. 1988); Sullivan v. Department of Agriculture, 32 MSPR 194 (1987); Roden v. Tennessee Valley Authority, 25 MSPR 363 (1984).

Nevertheless, the appellant has not established that he is an employee under the relevant provision of the statute. 5 USC 7511(a)(1)(C)(ii) grants appeal rights to a non-preference eligible in the excepted service "who has completed two years of current, continuous service in the same or similar positions under other than a temporary appointment...." See 5 USC 7511(a)(1)(C)(ii); Pennington v. Dept. of Veterans Affairs, 57 MSPR 8, 10-11 (1993). Consequently, since the appellant does not dispute that he served under a temporary appointment from January 16, 1990, until June 17, 1990, he does not meet the definition of an employee who has appeal rights to the Board under the CSDPA because he had only 22 and 1/2 months of "current, continuous service...under other than a temporary appointment." Further, with the exception of Pennington, all of the Board decisions cited by the appellant were issued prior to the enactment of the CSDPA, and deal with either competitive service or preference eligible employees. Moreover, while the appellant in Pennington was a non-preference eligible in the excepted service, there is nothing in Pennington to support the proposition that a non-preference eligible in the excepted service may count prior periods of temporary employment to meet the two years of current, continuous service requirement. See Pennington, 57 MSPR at 11. Therefore, the appellant has not established that he was an employee under 5 USC 7511(a)(1)(C)(ii). Id.

See Weidel v. DOJ (Fed. Cir. 2000 NP No. 98-3285) (although "tacking" of periods of temporary service is permitted for other categories of employees, the general statement in the FPM regarding the tacking of probationary periods does not justify an inference that tacking is permitted for nonpreference eligible individuals in the excepted service who are given provisional temporary appointments); O'Brien v. Office of Indep. Counsel, 74 MSPR 192, 196 (1997) (a nonpreference eligible excepted service employee does not acquire appeal rights through two temporary appointments, each less than two years in duration).

If the excepted service nonpreference eligible temporary employee has what amounts to an indefinite appointment, as for a student trainee whose appointment was intended to continue through completion of her education and study-related work requirements, service beyond two years establishes Board appeal rights. The revision in the law followed Van Wersch v. DHHS, 197 F.3d 1144 (Fed. Cir. 1999) [discussed later in this chapter, "Disabled Employee"], summarized by Beck v. GSA, 86 MSPR 489, 494 ¶ 11 (2000):

[T]he court found that an employee in the excepted service who was serving a probationary period, but had more than two years of qualifying current continuous service, was entitled to appeal her removal to the Board under 5 USC § 7511(a)(1)(C)(ii), not withstanding her ineligibility to appeal under section 7511(a)(1)(C)(i). Van Wersch, 197 F.3d at 1151.
