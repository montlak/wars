6. Past Practice

Whether a past practice exists is a factual question under the nonfact analysis. AFGE Council 238 and EPA, 62 FLRA 466, 468 (2008) ("At arbitration, the parties disputed the factual question of whether a past practice existed. As the Authority will not grant a nonfact exception where the alleged fact was disputed before the arbitrator."); see Dept. of Commerce, NOAA and IBEW Local 80, 67 FLRA 244, 246 (2014) ("in the arbitration context, the Authority applies a nonfact analysis to assess arguments concerning whether a past practice exists"), overruled, Dept. of Navy, Puget Sound Naval Ship Yard and Bremerton MTC, 70 FLRA 754, 756 (2018) (Member DuBester dissenting) ("[W]e acknowledge that the Authority has previously stated that an agreement's silence on a matter addressed by an arbitrator does not, by itself, demonstrate that the arbitrator's award fails to draw its essence from the agreement. However, to the extent that such precedent is inconsistent with this decision, we reverse that precedent."); AFGE Local 801 and DOJ, FCI Waseca, 64 FLRA 791, 792 (2010) (FLRA will not find award deficient on basis of arbitrator's determination of any factual matter the parties disputed at arbitration; existence of binding past practice is subject to nonfact analysis); cf. Broadcasting Bd. of Gov. and AFGE Local 1812, 66 FLRA 1012, 1017 (2012) ("Within the context of arbitration, even in cases where an arbitrator has resolved an unfair labor practice allegation, the Authority addresses issues as to whether a past practice exists under the nonfact framework. U.S. Dep't of the Treasury, IRS, 64 FLRA 972, 976 (2010) (Member DuBester dissenting in part as to other matters) (citation omitted). Where the issue concerns whether the arbitrator improperly interpreted a past practice, the Authority considers the issue under the essence framework. Id. (citation omitted).").
