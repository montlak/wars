broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 22: Grievance Procedures, Section D: Election of Statutory, Negotiated Procedures, Subsection 01: Agency Grievance Systems

1. Agency Grievance Systems

An agency does not sustain a compelling need determination as to the scope of a grievance procedure by arguing that the agency grievance procedure should control the scope of the negotiated grievance procedure. Under § 7121 a negotiated grievance procedure extends to all matters that could be covered under the provisions of law, unless the parties agree to a procedure with a narrower coverage. NFFE Local 15 and Army Armament Materiel Readiness Command, Rock Island, 9 FLRA 478, 479 (1982). No compelling need was established by agency protestations that considerations of patient care barred a broad-scope grievance procedure. Illinois Nurses Ass'n and VAMC, N. Chicago, 27 FLRA 714, 718 (1987) (Proposal 2). FLRA dismissed an agency argument that OPM-defined agency grievance procedures, excluding the type of grievance that would arise under a union proposal, could narrow the broad scope of 5 USC 7121, covering the matters at issue unless the parties excluded them through bargaining. AFGE Local 2782 and Dept. of Commerce, Bureau of Census, 6 FLRA 314, 322 (1981).

A proposal was nonnegotiable that gave employees of NAF facilities a choice of protesting certain nondisciplinary adverse actions either through the agency grievance procedure or through the negotiated procedure. Although § 7121 gives employees the right to make a choice in certain actions between statutory appeals procedures and contractual grievance procedures, the agency grievance procedure is not a statutory appeals procedure. The legislative history of the Statute suggested that statutory appeals procedures be established by or pursuant to law, not the case as to the agency internal administrative appeals system. The proposal was inconsistent with the exclusivity mandate of § 7121(a)(1). AFGE Local 1799 and Dept. of Army, Aberdeen Proving Ground, 22 FLRA 574, 577 (1986).

Because the agency and negotiated grievance procedures exist independently, the latter for the benefit of unit employees, an ALJ suggested that changes in agency grievance procedures may be made without bargaining. NASA and Goddard Eng'rs, Scientists and Technicians Ass'n, 18 FLRA 282, 297 (1985) (ALJ Decision). But Portsmouth Naval Shipyard and IFPTE Local 4, 23 FLRA 475, 480 (1986), determined that it can be a violation of §§ 7116(a)(1) and (2) to deny employees access to an administrative grievance procedure because they are represented by a union in an appropriate unit and covered by a collective bargaining agreement. Although § 7121(a)(1) provides, with exceptions, that the grievance procedures of a collective bargaining agreement are to be the exclusive procedures for resolving grievances within their coverage, it does not prohibit employees from using the agency grievance procedure to raise issues outside the scope of the negotiated grievance procedure. The issue of co-extensive coverage was revisited in the negotiability context, IFPTE Local 4 and Dept. of Navy, Portsmouth Naval Shipyard, 25 FLRA 895, 898-99 (1987):

It is undisputed that the proposal seeks to establish a contractual requirement that the Shipyard extend coverage of the agency administrative grievance procedure to unit employees. The proposal seeks coverage only for those matters not covered by the negotiated grievance procedure of the parties' collective bargaining agreement. Section 7121(a)(1) of the Statute states that collective bargaining agreements shall provide procedures for the settlement of grievances, except those excluded by section 7121(c) or by the parties' agreement (see section 7121(a)(2)), and that as relevant here, the negotiated procedure "shall be the exclusive procedure() for resolving grievances which fall within its coverage."... Therefore, Congress intended to require unit employees covered by a collective bargaining agreement to use the negotiated procedure to grieve matters covered by that procedure. This proposition is not in dispute.

However, the Agency construes section 7121(a)(1) as reflecting a further Congressional intent; namely, that Congress specifically intended to preclude bargaining unit employees covered by a contract from using an agency administrative grievance procedure for those matters not covered by the parties' negotiated grievance procedure. We find no such Congressional intent.... [S]ection 7121(a)(1) "does not prohibit employees from using the agency grievance procedure to raise issues outside the scope of the negotiated grievance procedure." In our view, section 7121(a)(1) does not address...the only matter here at issue: whether coverage of the agency administrative grievance procedure can extend to unit employees for matters outside the scope of the parties' negotiated grievance procedure. Therefore, we reject the Agency's argument that the proposal does not pertain to conditions of employment because it concerns a matter "specifically provided for by Federal statute" within the meaning of section 7103(a)(14)(C)....

We also reject the Agency's "technical" argument...that the proposal is inconsistent with section 7121 because it concerns an agency grievance procedure which does not include binding arbitration,...[S]ection 7121's requirements apply to the contents of negotiated grievance procedures, not those of agency administrative grievance procedures....

Finally, we reject the Agency's argument that the proposal is inconsistent with 5 C.F.R. § 771.204 and is therefore outside the duty to bargain.... The proposal in this case is not inconsistent with 5 C.F.R. § 771.204. That regulation does not prohibit extension of coverage of the agency administrative grievance procedure to unit employees for matters outside the scope of the negotiated grievance procedure....

Refer to Panama Canal Comm'n and Panama Area Metal Trades Council, 43 FLRA 1483, 1501-05 (1992), noting the availability of agency grievance procedures for certain excepted service personnel beyond the protections of the Civil Service Due Process Amendments and as to whom a grievance procedure could not be negotiated.
