broida flra Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards, Subchapter 07: Procedures for Review of Arbitration Awards, Section B: Requirements of Exceptions; Regulations, Subsection 01: Expedited Decision

1. Expedited Decision

The FLRA may issue what it styles an expedited decision on exceptions to an arbitration award, 5 CFR 2425.7 (2022):

Requests for expedited, abbreviated decisions in certain arbitration matters that do not involve unfair labor practices.

Where an arbitration matter before the Authority does not involve allegations of unfair labor practices under 5 U.S.C. 7116, and the excepting party wishes to receive an expedited Authority decision, the excepting party may request that the Authority issue a decision that resolves the parties' arguments without a full explanation of the background, arbitration award, parties' arguments, and analysis of those arguments. In determining whether such an abbreviated decision is appropriate, the Authority will consider all of the circumstances of the case, including, but not limited to: whether any opposition filed under § 2425.3 of this part objects to issuance of such a decision and, if so, the reasons for such an objection; and the case's complexity, potential for precedential value, and similarity to other, fully detailed decisions involving the same or similar issues. Even absent a request, the Authority may issue expedited, abbreviated decisions in appropriate cases.

VA Gainesville and AFGE Local 2779, 71 FLRA 225, 225 (2019), stated the basis for granting an unopposed request for an expedited decision:

...Upon full consideration of the circumstances of this case--including the case's complexity, potential for precedential value, and similarity to other, fully detailed decisions involving the same or similar issues, as well as the absence of any allegation of an unfair labor practice brought before the Arbitrator--we grant the Agency's request.

Expedited decisions under 5 CFR 2425.7 are ordinarily issued at the request of the party filing the exceptions. The resulting decision is brief--a page or two. The decision gives a general description of the nature of the case and the bases for the exceptions, and announces a result--usually denying the exceptions (a complex case would not likely result in an expedited decision). FLRA may decide on its own to issue an expedited decision--a type of summary affirmation of the award--a result that may occur when the exceptions appear of no redeeming value or substance. See AFGE Local 1969 and VA St. Paul Reg'l Office, 67 FLRA 279 (2014); AFGE Local 1367 and Dept. of Air Force, Lackland AFB, 67 FLRA 206 (2014); cf. Tidewater Virginia FEMTC and Dept. of Navy, NAVFAC, 70 FLRA 288 (2017) (correction of math error in backpay award, addition of interest to backpay remedy: "Upon full consideration of the circumstances of this case--including the case's complexity, potential for precedential value, and similarity to other, fully detailed decisions involving the same or similar issues, as well as the absence of any allegation of an unfair labor practice--we grant the Union's request."). The expedited process may be used to address jurisdictional issues. See NLRB and NLRB Prof. Ass'n., 71 FLRA 196 (2019) (Member DuBester dissenting).

AFGE Local 1945 and Dept. of Army, Anniston Army Depot, 67 FLRA 436, 436-37 (2014), made two points concerning expedited review of exceptions to awards: first, FLRA fully considers the parties' submissions and, second, having considered them and issued an expedited decision, the reconsideration process is not to be used to obtain a more complete decision:

In cases in which the Authority grants a request for an expedited, abbreviated decision, or otherwise considers an expedited, abbreviated decision appropriate, the Authority gives parties' submissions the same full consideration that the Authority gives parties' submissions in cases decided under the Authority's regular procedures. The only distinction between these two categories of cases is that, as § 2425.7 makes clear, an expedited, abbreviated decision "resolves the parties' arguments without a full explanation...and analysis."...

...

...Also, because an expedited, abbreviated decision does not include "a full explanation...and analysis" of the arguments supporting an exception, we do not provide such explanation or analysis here either. To do otherwise would allow a party to benefit from the expedited resolution of exceptions under § 2425.7 while circumventing the concomitant agreement to have those exceptions resolved without full explanation and analysis.

If a party requests an expedited decision, although the other party offers no objection, FLRA may deny the request if the regulatory criteria are not met and a reasoned decision is required. See AFGE Local 1034 and DOJ, Bureau of Prisons, FCC Pollock, 68 FLRA 718, 721 (2015) (Member DuBester dissenting in part: "Where the filing party has requested such a decision and the opposing party agrees, or does not object, as here, I think that, consistent with the Regulation's intent, the parties' interests in an expedited decision should take priority over our discretion not to grant the request."); cf. AFGE Local 2338 and Pershing VAMC, 71 FLRA 1039 (2020) (expedited decision inappropriate to review arbitrator's disposition of ULP issues). If a party opposes an expedited decision, it should explain why. See NAGE Local R3-10 and Dept. of Transp., FAA, 69 FLRA 510, 510 (2016) ("In its opposition, the Agency opposes an expedited, abbreviated decision under § 2425.7 of the Authority's Regulations, but does not support or explain that position. We have determined that this case is appropriate for issuance as an expedited, abbreviated decision under § 2425.7 of the Authority's Regulations.").
