2. Criteria

"The issue in severance cases is whether there are unusual circumstances that would warrant allowing a group of employees to be severed from an established unit of employees with whom the employees...still have a community of interest, and to form a separate appropriate unit." DHHS, Region II and NTEU, 43 FLRA 1245, 1255 (1992); see Dept. of Army, Hq., Ft. Dix and IBPO, NAGE, SEIU, 53 FLRA 287, 297 (1997) (remanding to regional director to determine whether unusual circumstances required severance and self-determination election). Rejected as a controlling factor in a severance determination was the means leading to certification of the incumbent exclusive representative. It was not determinative that the incumbent was selected without an election through procedures predating the CSRA. See Dept. of Air Force, Carswell AFB and Ft. Worth Fed. Firefighters Ass'n, 40 FLRA 221, 229-30 (1991).

If an established bargaining unit continues to be appropriate and no unusual circumstances are presented, a petition seeking to remove certain employees from the overall unit and to separately represent them must be dismissed. Dismissal of the severance petition is required to reduce the potential for unit fragmentation and promote effective dealings and efficiency of agency operations. That is so even if the smaller unit operates under a supplemental negotiated agreement tacked onto a master agreement for the broader unit. FLRA set aside a regional director's Decision and Direction of Election to avoid creation of a fragmented unit in Library of Congress and FOP, Library of Congress Police Force Labor Comm., 16 FLRA 429, 432 (1984); see Dept. of Navy, Marine Corps Air Station and IAM Lodge 2296, 71 FLRA 630, 631 (2020) (Member Abbott dissenting) ("The Authority will dismiss a severance petition where an existing unit continues to be appropriate and there are no unusual circumstances to justify severance of the petitioned-for employees."), recon. den., 71 FLRA 940 (2020); Dept. of Navy, NAS Jacksonville and FOP Lodge 125, 61 FLRA 139, 142 (2005) ("The Authority has long held that absent unusual circumstances, where an established bargaining unit continues to be appropriate, a petition seeking to sever employees from an overall unit represented by a labor organization will be dismissed in the interest of reducing the potential for unit fragmentation and, thereby, promoting effective dealings and efficiency of agency operations."); Dept. of Navy, Naval Air Station, Pt. Mugu and Fed. Firefighters Ass'n, Local 55, 26 FLRA 620, 622 (1987) ("In Library of Congress, the Authority held that where an established bargaining unit continues to be appropriate and no unusual circumstances are presented, a petition seeking to remove certain employees from the overall unit and to separately represent them must be dismissed, in the interest of reducing the potential for unit fragmentation and thereby promotion effective dealings and efficiency of agency operations.").
