broida flra Chapter 08: Unfair Labor Practices Chapter 08: Unfair Labor Practices, Subchapter 02: Management Violations, Section D: Unilateral Changes in Employment Conditions and Past Practice, Subsection 08: Specific Changes Requiring Impact or Decision Bargaining, Part b: Office Relocation, Reorganization, Abolition
b. Office Relocation, Reorganization, Abolition

FLRA considered effects of a reorganization or realignment greater than de minimis when a change affected the amount of employee travel, an employee's ability to earn overtime, structure of employees' offices, and equipment used by employees. See PBGC and NAGE Local R3-77, 59 FLRA 48 (2003). An office relocation over a distance of four or five miles had more than a de minimis impact on the unit employees involved; the impact was reasonably foreseeable. SSA, Office of Hearings and Appeals, Region II, New York and AFGE Local 1760, 19 FLRA 328, 344-45 (1985) (ALJ Decision). Relocation of offices, and, particularly, a change in the allocation of space and facilities to conduct union activities at a new office location, required bargaining as to impact and implementation. DHHS, SSA, Baltimore and AFGE, 41 FLRA 1268, 1279 (1991) (ALJ Decision); see Dept. of Transp., FAA and PASS, 19 FLRA 472, 476 (1985) (reorganization resulting in likely geographical relocation or retirement of employees was more than de minimis in impact). An office relocation had more than a de minimis impact where it involved the movement of two entire agency offices over a distance of several city blocks and brought about differences in building structures and office space. The duration of the moves was permanent. Proposals concerned noise levels, wall coverings, availability of water coolers, lunchrooms, and day care, among others. SSA and AFGE Local 1760, 21 FLRA 546, 548-49 (1986); see Dept. of Air Force, Air Force Logistics Command, Sacramento Air Logistics Ctr., McClellan AFB and AFGE Local 1857, 35 FLRA 217, 219 (1990) (bargaining should have occurred as to relocation of employees from one building to offset undesirable environmental conditions of second building).

A relocation of 23 unit employees from one section of a building to another, a move involving less space per person, fewer lighting fixtures, no windows and increased noise, was deemed to have no more than a de minimis impact on the employees; their duties did not change and the employees accounted for only small portion of a unit of about 1,500. There was no past practice as to prior relocations or analogous changes in working conditions. Army Reserve Components Personnel and Admin. Ctr. and AFGE Local 900, 20 FLRA 117, 119 (1985). The effects of a relocation of 12 employees in a national unit to an office 50 feet away, although smaller and noisier, amounted to no more than a de minimis in impact. EPA Region II and AFGE, 20 FLRA 644, 646 (1985), rev'd on remand, EPA Region II and AFGE, 25 FLRA 787, 789-90 (1987) (ordering prospective bargaining order) (following DHHS, SSA and AFGE Local 1760, 24 FLRA 403 (1986)); see Dept. of Treasury, IRS, Chicago and NTEU Chap. 10, 33 FLRA 147 (1988) (30-mile relocation of about 20 inspectors).

A reorganization affecting more than 50 employees had more than a de minimis effect. FAA and PASS, 19 FLRA 436, 437 (1985). Management's failure to bargain as to the impact or proposals concerning an office reorganization and to delay implementation of other proposals constituted a ULP. DHHS, SSA, Baltimore and AFGE, 22 FLRA 91, 115-16 (1986) (ALJ Decision). Elimination of an organizational unit demanded impact bargaining even when only one employee was displaced. The reorganization affected the opportunity of that employee (or others who might have moved into the unit) for long-term work assignments, beneficial working conditions, and the acquisition of experience in particular types of duties. See INS, Border Patrol, Del Rio and AFGE, Nat'l Border Patrol Council, Local 2366, 47 FLRA 225, 231-32 (1993). Although contracting out may not itself be a negotiable condition of employment, contracting may require impact and implementation bargaining. Dept. of Army, Hq., Ft. Sill and NFFE, 29 FLRA 1110, 1133 (1987).
