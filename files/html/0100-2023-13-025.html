C. OUTSIDE AUTHORITY

5 CFR 2424.50(c) (2022) establishes as one nonexclusive condition of compelling need that "[t]he rule or regulation implements a mandate to the agency or primary national subdivision under law or other outside authority, which implementation is essentially nondiscretionary in nature." There has been little discussion of what constitutes "outside authority." An agency does not meet the test of a compelling need determination by reciting the existence of one of its own regulations and the existence of a government-wide regulation by another organization, e.g., OPM. Under 5 CFR 2424.50 (2022), the agency must demonstrate one of the conditions required by the regulation: (a) the essential nature of the regulation relative to the execution of the agency mission, or that of a primary component of the agency; (b) that the regulation is necessary to insure merit principles; or (c) that the regulation implements a mandate to the agency under law or from outside authority making implementation nondiscretionary. The existence of an agency regulation and an OPM regulation or guideline on flextime did not, by themselves, preclude negotiability of flexitime proposals. NFFE Local 1789 and DHUD, St. Louis Area Office, 4 FLRA 708, 709-11 (1980) (relying on AFGE Local 1928 and Dept. of Navy, Naval Air Dev. Ctr., Warminster, 2 FLRA 451 (1980)).

Although the boundaries of "outside authority" are not fully defined, the phrase has been construed to include statements of congressional intent in conference reports. See NFFE Local 1669 v. FLRA, 745 F.2d 705, 708-09 (D.C. Cir. 1984), aff'g (with some concern about paucity of FLRA's reasoning) NFFE Local 561 and Army Corps of Eng'rs, Mobile, 17 FLRA 759 (1985); NFFE Local 1669 and Arkansas Air Nat'l Guard, 13 FLRA 176, 177-78 (1983) (compelling need was established by nondiscretionary mandate from Congress in form of House Committee on Appropriations directive contained in report accompanying Defense Department appropriations bill); cf. AFGE Local 1786 and Dept. of Navy, Marine Corps Combat Dev. Command, Marine Corps Base, Quantico, 49 FLRA 534, 544 (1994) (personal letter from congressman was not controlling; "there is no indication Congress as a whole was aware of those views"). Continuing congressional oversight of and involvement in agency programs does not, by itself, establish a nondiscretionary mandate to prohibit particular negotiated benefits. SEIU Local 556 and Dept. of Navy, Navy Exch., Pearl Harbor, 37 FLRA 320, 341 (1990).
