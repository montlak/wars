3. Isolated Noncompliance

Ordinarily a single failure or refusal by a party to comply with a contract provision does not constitute a rejection of the negotiated agreement so as to demonstrate a ULP. IRS, Detroit Dist. and NTEU Chap. 24, 12 FLRA 445, 446 (1983). A single instance of a failure by management to hold a grievance meeting when management issued a reply to a grievance did not constitute a rejection of the negotiated agreement. Customs Serv., Region VII and NTEU, 10 FLRA 251, 254 (1982). There was no clear and patent violation of a contract when an agency missed a step-three grievance meeting but attempted to rectify the problem and acceded to a request for arbitration. SSA and AFGE Local 1923, 16 FLRA 434, 434-35 (1984). If a party has not disavowed its contract obligation, but failed to timely comply because of factors beyond its control, FLRA may conclude there is not a clear and patent contract breach. US Penitentiary, Florence and AFGE Local 1301, 54 FLRA 30, 31 (1998).

A single violation may constitute repudiation of an agreement if the violation is sufficiently egregious. See DOD, Warner Robins Air Logistics Ctr., Robins AFB and AFGE Local 987, 40 FLRA 1211, 1281-19 (1991). The parties negotiated a ground rules agreement that several union officials be assigned to the day shift so that they could participate in negotiations. When negotiations were to begin, the employer informed the union that one of the employees could not be released from the evening shift. Management committed a ULP by its single, but significant, violation of the ground rules agreement:

We agree that not every breach of contract is necessarily a violation of the Statute, but that the repudiation of an agreement does violate the Statute. We also note that these general principles are similar to the principles followed by the National Labor Relations Board (NLRB) in the private sector. See Indiana & Michigan Electric Co., 284 NLRB 53, 59 (1987), in which NLRB stated that "it is well settled that a breach of contract is not per se an unfair labor practice.... On the other hand, where a breach of contract, under all the circumstances, amounts to a wholesale repudiation of the collective-bargaining agreement, [the NLRB] will find [a statutory] violation."

We find that the nature and scope of the failure or refusal to honor an agreement must be considered, in the circumstances of each case, in order to determine whether the Statute has been violated. Because the breach of an agreement may only be a single instance, it does not necessarily follow that the breach does not violate the Statute. That suggests that a single breach of an agreement, no matter how significant, would not violate the Statute. Rather, it is the nature and scope of the breach that are relevant. Where the nature and scope of the breach amount to a repudiation of an obligation imposed by the agreement's terms, we will find that an unfair labor practice has occurred in violation of the Statute.

Another example of repudiation as a result of a single breach is presented by Panama Canal Comm'n and Panama Area Metal Trades Council, 43 FLRA 1483, 1508 (1992) (ALJ Decision). The parties' agreement allowed employees to appeal adverse actions through administrative appeals procedures. The agency repudiated the provision based on its incorrect interpretation of the law that employees were barred from using an administrative grievance process for that purpose. The "nature and scope of the Respondent's actions constituted more than a mere breach of the terms of the parties' agreements, but...'went to the heart of the agreement[s].'" For there to be a repudiation of an agreement, there must be an agreement that the parties agree is in effect. The record must identify the agreement at issue, its nature, and the actions of the respondent that purportedly repudiated the agreement. Dept. of Air Force, 56th Combat Support Group, MacDill AFB and NFFE Local 153, 41 FLRA 850, 854-55 (1991).
