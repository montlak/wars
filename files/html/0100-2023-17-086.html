7. Amendment of RIF Notice; Change in Position Offer

The agency may change its offer of a position to an employee through amendment of the RIF notice as long as it does not diminish the grade or pay of the offered position, Klamm v. DOD, 97 MSPR 276, 278-81 ¶¶ 6-11 (2004):

As part of its burden of proving that it properly applied the RIF regulations, the agency must show that it afforded the appellant his proper assignment rights. McMillan v. Department of the Army, 84 M.S.P.R. 476 ¶ 18 (1999). The appellant contends that the AJ did not specifically address his claim that, under 5 C.F.R. § 351.603, the agency did not have the discretion to withdraw the offer of assignment to the GS-11 Traffic Management Specialist position once he accepted that offer. Because the AJ did not fully address this argument, we reopen this appeal and address it further.

The Office of Personnel Management (OPM) has prescribed regulations for the release of competing employees in a RIF. 5 U.S.C. § 3502(a). As set forth above, an employee reached for release from a competitive level shall be offered assignment to another position in accordance with subpart G of part 351, and, "[i]f the employee accepts, the employee shall be assigned to the position offered." 5 C.F.R. § 351.603. As the AJ found, however, an employee's assignment rights in a RIF are determined based on a specific representative rate, not a particular position. Thus, "an employee is entitled to only one proper offer and has no right to a choice of positions." Endsley v. Department of the Army, 55 M.S.P.R. 46, 49 (1992); see Jorgenson v. Department of Agriculture, 22 M.S.P.R. 207, 209 (1984) (the appellant had no right to select among available positions).

We find that the regulatory history of section 351.603 supports the AJ's decision in this case. See Guillebeau v. Department of the Navy, 362 F.3d 1329, 1332-33 (Fed. Cir. 2004) (relying on the history of a regulation as an aid to its interpretation); Connolly v. Department of Justice, 766 F.2d 507, 511 (Fed. Cir. 1985) (same). The language currently set forth in section 351.603 was promulgated in a final rule issued by OPM on October 25, 1983. In the Federal Register notice, OPM stated that "§ 351.603 has been revised for clarification but reflects no policy change." 48 Fed. Reg. 49,462. The prior version of section 351.603 that OPM "clarifi[ed]" with no policy change had provided that, "[s]ubject to Subpart G of this part, when an agency selects an employee for release from a competitive level, it shall: (a) Assign the employee with his or her consent to a position for which qualified which will last at least 3 months; (b) Furlough the employee; or (c) Separate the employee." 48 Fed. Reg. 32,304, 32,309-310 (July 14, 1983). Thus, we interpret section 351.603's statement that "[i]f the employee accepts, the employee shall be assigned to the position offered," as merely indicating that RIF assignments may only be made with the employee's consent. See Chavez v. Department of Veterans Affairs, 65 M.S.P.R. 590, 594 (1994) (an amendatory act is not to be construed to change the original act or section further than expressly declared or necessarily implied). We do not interpret section 351.603 as imposing a new restriction on the agency that prohibits amended RIF notices once the employee consents to an assignment.

This interpretation of section 351.603 is supported by the Board's decision in Petranek v. Department of the Army, 4 M.S.P.R. 419 (1980). Mr. Petranek, a WG-8 Repair Parts Specialist, was offered assignment to the GS-6 position of Supply Technician, which he accepted. Id. at 420. The agency, upon learning of the availability of a WG-8 Automotive Mechanic position, which it determined to be a better position, amended its offer of assignment to that position. Id. Mr. Petranek accepted the offer of the automotive mechanic position, but appealed to the Board alleging, among other things, that the supply technician position was the better position because he was more qualified for that position and that, upon his acceptance of the first offer, a binding agreement was created between himself and the agency. Id. The Board found that the appellant was entitled to the best available position under 5 C.F.R. § 351.703, which involved no reduction, or the least possible reduction in the representative rate, regardless of the "quality" of the offered position. Id. at 421. The Board also concurred with the finding that the agency's offer to Mr. Petranek was not intended to be binding upon acceptance, noting that, "[s]ince a RIF action may often involve continuing changes in the agency's organization and, thus, in the kind and number of available positions, it would be unreasonable to conclude that an offer made under these circumstances is binding on the agency in view of the agency's obligation, under 5 C.F.R. Part 351, to assign the employee to the best available position." Id. Here, as in Petranek, the agency's initial offer of the GS-11 Traffic Management Specialist position was not intended to be binding, given our finding that the appellant's "acceptance" merely indicated his consent to be assigned to that position. As in Petranek, we also find that a RIF action often involves continuing changes in an agency's organization, and in the kind and number of available positions, such that it would be unreasonable to conclude that an offer initially made and consented to is binding on the agency. Cf. Bartel v. Federal Aviation Administration, 14 M.S.P.R. 24, 35-36 (1982) (in general, Federal employment is not governed by contract principles).

We further find that the above interpretation of section 351.603 and the agency's actions in this case are consistent with the agency's internal regulation regarding RIFs, which requires the filling of vacancies during a RIF before the exercise of assignment rights to occupied positions. Section VI.C.10.a of "DLA Regulation No. 1410.1," entitled "Reduction in Force, Furlough, and Transfer of Function," provides that "[a]ll available vacancies will be used to place surplus employees before and during a RIF" and that "[e]mployees have no right of choice in determining any placement offer." See Warren v. Department of Defense, 87 M.S.P.R. 426 ¶ 10 (2001) (the Board will enforce an agency's own policy or regulation regarding the implementation of a RIF). Similarly, section VI.C.7.b(1) states that an employee released from his competitive level "will be reassigned to a vacancy if one exists within the 3-grade level limits of RIF assignment rights. If no vacancy exists, the employee's assignment rights (bump or retreat rights) will be exercised to an occupied position." Section II.B provides that the agency's policy is to mitigate the effect of any necessary RIF by such means as "reassignment of surplus employees to available vacancies," and section VI.A provides that "[p]lans should seek to achieve the minimum disruption and dislocation of employees," that "[e]very reasonable effort should be made to avoid or mitigate a RIF or furlough," and that, "[i]n this regard, management must use vacant continuing positions to place surplus employees." Id.

The appellant's argument, raised below and on review, that 5 C.F.R. § 351.805(c) prohibited the agency from issuing the amended RIF notice is without merit. The appellant claims that section 351.805(c) permits an amended notice only when a better offer of assignment becomes available and that there was no better offer of assignment in this case because both offered positions were at the GS-11 level. Section 351.805(c), however, provides in relevant part that "[a]n agency must give an employee an amended written notice and allow the employee to decide whether to accept a better offer of assignment under subpart G of this part that becomes available before or on the effective date of the reduction in force." Although section 351.805(c) requires an amended notice under the circumstances set forth in the regulation, it does not prohibit an agency from issuing an amended written notice under other circumstances, such as those at issue in this case. We therefore do not read section 351.805, which describes situations in which an agency must issue an amended RIF notice, as setting forth an exhaustive list of situations in which an agency may issue an amended RIF notice.
