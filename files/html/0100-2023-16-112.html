a. Role of Performance Standards

Performance standards govern WIGI actions. "In the Civil Service Reform Act, Congress 're-established the connection between performance appraisals and within-grade pay raises based on ALOC [acceptable level of competence] determinations that had been broken by the Federal Salary Reform Act of 1962.' Parker v. Defense Logistics Agency, 1 MSPR 505, 1 MSPB 489, 523 (1980)." Daigle v. VA, 84 MSPR 625, 630 ¶ 11 (1999). The agency must demonstrate that the ALOC determination is based on performance standards developed under an agency performance appraisal system approved by OPM. Callan v. Dept. of Navy, 26 MSPR 6, 10 (1984); see Smith v. USDA, 64 MSPR 46, 56 (1994) (considering the validity of the appellant's performance standards in a WIGI appeal). Denial of a step increase must be based on Chapter 43 performance standards. Nadolney v. EPA, 25 MSPR 544, 545 (1985); 5 CFR 531.404(a), .409(b) (2022). There must be standards under Nadolney, and there must be reasonable notice of those standards. It was sufficient that the employee obtained his standards at least 30 days or more before the end of the waiting period; it was immaterial that the employee had not received a position description. Guy v. Farm Credit Admin., 25 MSPR 500, 505 (1984). The Board will not, however, sustain the denial of a WIGI based upon standards retroactively applied. Cho v. Dwight David Eisenhower Army Med. Ctr., 8 MSPR 52, 55, 7 MSPB 548 (1981) (decided based on law predating the Reform Act; there is, however, no basis to believe the result would differ after the Act).

During the early years following enactment of the CSRA, an agency was not required to use specific performance standards to determine level of competence. Prior law is summarized in a series of cases: Rea v. DMA, 20 MSPR 262, 265 (1984); Van Winkle v. DHUD, 19 MSPR 393, 395 (1984); Griffin v. SSA, 8 MSPR 36, 38 (1981); Griffin v. Dept. of Navy, 7 MSPR 678, 7 MSPB 533 (1981) (Table); Overton v. IRS, 6 MSPR 439, 444, 6 MSPB 373 (1981); Pennington v. DHHS, 4 MSPR 557, 559, 5 MSPB 81 (1981); Fleming v. DHHS, 4 MSPR 197, 200 n.4, 4 MSPB 277 (1980); Hurt v. DHHS, 3 MSPR 434, 436, 3 MSPB 507 (1980); Herrera v. DHEW, 3 MSPR 400, 401 (1980); Degani v. Dept. of Air Force, 1 MSPR 671, 676, 2 MSPB 81 (1980).
