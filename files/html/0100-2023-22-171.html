1. Transcript

Bennett, supra, prohibits recovery through MSPB of the costs of transcripts in a CSRA fee application. The prohibition against recovery of transcription costs (and photocopy costs, discussed below), extends as well to fee petitions processed under EAJA. SSA v. Goodman, 33 MSPR 325, 338 n.12 (1987) (dictum). The preclusion of recovery for transcript costs extends to payment for court reporting services. McInville v. USPS, 31 MSPR 297, 302 (1986). If a party uses a deposition in a court appeal, it may be recoverable as part of the "costs" on appeal under 28 USC 1920. In Russell v. Dept. of Navy, 43 MSPR 157, 166 (1989), modified in part on other grounds, Garstkiewicz v. USPS, 50 MSPR 476, 478-79 (1991), the Board added, for good measure, as to transcripts:

[T]he administrative judge properly denied the appellant's request for payment of the costs and copying and enhancing the tape-recording of the hearing proceedings, and the cost of the transcript of the rehearing. It is well settled that the cost of transcripts is borne by the appellant. See generally Helt v. Veterans Administration, 34 MSPR 165 (1987). If the appellant believes that the reporting company's failure to supply a usable transcript, necessitating the rehearing and the appellant's purchase of an additional rehearing transcript, is a breach of the Board's contract for services with the reporting company and that he has rights under that contract as a third-party beneficiary, his remedy lies in another forum.
