9. Effect of Failure to File Exception

If no timely exceptions to an arbitrator's award are filed under § 7122(a), an agency's subsequent failure or refusal to implement the award is a ULP in violation of § 7116(a)(1) and (8). Once an agency's timely exceptions are denied, the agency must implement the award. When exceptions are timely filed, the award is not final and binding. Because an agency is only required by § 7122(b) to take actions required by an arbitrator's final award, failure to comply with the award until the pending exceptions are resolved by FLRA is not noncompliance or a violation of § 7116. Soldiers' and Airmen's Home and AFGE Local 3090, 15 FLRA 139, 142 (1984).

Rejecting a defense that a party may not be required to perform an unlawful act to comply with an unexcepted arbitrator's award, FLRA declared in the ULP decision DOD, Dept. of Navy, USMC, Marine Corps Air Station, Cherry Point and IAM Dist. 110, 15 FLRA 686, 687-88 (1984):

[T]he Authority specifically rejects the Judge's determination that, even though no timely exceptions to the arbitrator's award had been filed with the Authority, he was nevertheless required to consider the merits of the Respondent's assertion as an affirmative defense to the unfair labor practice allegation that compliance with the arbitrator's award would compel performance of an unlawful act.... Given the Congressionally mandated exclusivity of section 7122(a) as a mechanism for questioning arbitrators' awards, it would be inappropriate for the Authority to entertain in subsequent unfair labor practice proceedings matters which more appropriately should be pursued in accordance with section 7122(a) of the Statute. In the Authority's view, any contention that an arbitrator's award is deficient because it is contrary to any law, rule, or regulation must be made by invoking the procedures established by Congress in section 7122(a) of the Statute. If a party fails to file exceptions to an arbitrator's award pursuant to section 7122(a) within the 30 day period established therein, the award, pursuant to section 7122(b) of the Statute, becomes "final and binding."... [A]ny failure to comply with a validly obtained arbitrator's award to which no exceptions have been timely filed constitutes a failure to comply with the requirements of section 7122 of the Statute in violation of section 7116(a)(1) and (8) of the Statute. Any other conclusion would render section 7122(b) meaningless, as a party could fail to file exceptions to an arbitrator's award pursuant to section 7122(a), take no action, and then present its argument in the course of defending against the unfair labor practice allegation. To permit such a protracted procedure would be inconsistent with the policy that the arbitration process provides both an expeditious and a final resolution of grievances....

The analysis in Marine Corps Air Station was applied in another case, the result being that the agency was required to pay interest on a back pay award pursuant to an arbitrator's order when the agency did not file timely exceptions to the award. VAMC, Phoenix and AFGE Local 2382, 15 FLRA 706, 707 (1984) (case arising before amendments to Back Pay Act that waived sovereign immunity to interest on federal employees' awards of back pay). Objections to the legality of an arbitration award concerning overtime payment must be addressed through exceptions under § 7122(a). If timely exceptions are not filed, the objections will not be entertained as a defense to a ULP proceeding brought to enforce the award. Dept. of Navy, Naval Submarine Base, New London and NAGE Local R1-100, 19 FLRA 250, 251 (1985).

FLRA's rule concerning the effect of failure to file exceptions appears to have been adopted in court, although the court may review the subsequent ULP ruling by FLRA issued to remedy the failure by the agency to abide by an unexcepted award. Dept. of Navy v. FLRA, 815 F.2d 797, 798-99 (1st Cir. 1987). But cf. SBA and AFGE Council 228, Local 2532, 38 FLRA 386, 406-09 (1990) (declining to enforce through ULP proceedings settlement that was beyond the authority of the agency representative to negotiate).
