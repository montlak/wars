broida flra Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards, Subchapter 08: Grounds for Review, Section D: Award is Based on "Nonfact", Subsection 02: Contract Interpretation

2. Contract Interpretation

Although the nonfact ground permits an appealing party a limited challenge to the factual basis of an arbitrator's award, the appealing party may not challenge the arbitrator's interpretation and application of a collective bargaining agreement as a nonfact, AFGE Local 3302 and DHHS, SSA, 52 FLRA 677, 679 (1996). DHS, ICE and AFGE Local 1917, 65 FLRA 792, 795 (2011):

[T]he Authority will not find an award deficient on the basis of an arbitrator's determination on any factual matter that the parties disputed at arbitration. Id. at 594 (citing Nat'l Post Office Mailhandlers v. U.S. Postal Serv., 751 F.2d 834, 843 (6th Cir. 1985)). Additionally, an arbitrator's interpretation of a collective bargaining agreement does not constitute a matter that can be challenged as a nonfact. See AFGE, Nat'l Council of EPA Locals, Council 238, 59 FLRA 902, 904 (2004) (citing U.S. Dep't of Veterans Affairs, Ralph H. Johnson Med. Ctr., Charleston, S.C., 57 FLRA 489, 493 (2001)).

The Agency's nonfact exception challenges the Arbitrator's interpretation of Article 9(A) of the parties' agreement, specifically his determination that the Agency violated the procedure defined in Article 9(A) when it did not advise or permit the Union to engage in pre-decisional involvement regarding the change to its FUGOPS teams. Because the Agency's exception challenges the Arbitrator's interpretation of the parties' agreement, it does not provide a basis for finding that the award is based on a nonfact. See id.

An arbitrator's conclusion that is based on an interpretation of the parties' collective bargaining agreement does not constitute a fact that can be challenged as a nonfact." Dept. of Treasury, IRS Kansas City and NTEU Chap. 66, 60 FLRA 401, 402 (2004); see AFGE Council 222 and DHUD, 73 FLRA 54, 55 (2022) ("a challenge to an arbitrator's legal conclusion or interpretation of the parties' agreement cannot be challenged as a nonfact"); FEA Stateside Region and DODDEA, 72 FLRA 724, 725 (2022) ("the Union's argument challenges the Arbitrator's legal conclusions and interpretation of the parties' agreement, and does not demonstrate that the award is based on a nonfact"); VA Regional Office St. Petersburg and AFGE Local 1594, 70 FLRA 799, 800-01 (2018) (Member DuBester concurring in part and dissenting in part) ("However, the Authority has held that 'conclusions based on the [arbitrator's] interpretation of a collective-bargaining agreement' cannot be challenged as nonfacts"); AFGE Local 1101 and Dept. of Air Force, Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson, 70 FLRA 644, 646 (2018) (Member DuBester concurring) ("However, the Authority has held that parties may not challenge an arbitrator's interpretation of a collective-bargaining agreement on nonfact grounds."); SSA and AFGE Local 3627, 63 FLRA 274, 277 (2009) ("The appealing party may not challenge an arbitrator's interpretation and application of a collective bargaining agreement as a nonfact. E.g., United States Dep't of Homeland Security, United States Customs and Border Prot., JFK Airport, Queens, N.Y., 62 FLRA 129, 131 (2007)."); AFGE Council 238 and EPA, 59 FLRA 902, 904 (2004) (stating the rule); AFGE Local 3615 and SSA, OHA, 55 FLRA 1160, 1161-62 (1999) (concerning nonfact rule applied to case involving allegations that nonpromotions resulted from racial or disability discrimination); USIA Voice of Am. and AFSCME Local 1418, 55 FLRA 197, 200 (1999) ("[A]n appealing party may not challenge the arbitrator's interpretation and application of a collective bargaining agreement as a non-fact."); SBA and AFGE Local 2951, 55 FLRA 179, 185 (1999) (reciting the rule); AFGE Local 1151 and VA, 54 FLRA 20, 23 (1998) ("A party may not raise nonfact allegations concerning a matter that was disputed below."); Indian Educators Fed'n and Bureau of Indian Affairs, 53 FLRA 352, 358 (1997) (conclusion resulting from application by arbitrator of law and parties' agreement to evidence presented did not constitute "fact" underlying award); Dept. of Navy, Naval Training Ctr. and IUE Local 673, 53 FLRA 103, 106 (1997) (stating rule); cf. UPTO and Dept. of Army, Corps of Eng'rs, 67 FLRA 311, 315 (2014) ("To the extent that the Union is challenging the Arbitrator's interpretation of the settlement agreement, as stated above, challenges to arbitrator's contract interpretations do not provide a basis for finding nonfact."); cf. VA and AFGE, 72 FLRA 518, 520 (2021) (Chairman DuBester concurring) ("the Authority will deny a nonfact exception when it is premised on a denied essence exception").
