1. Effect of Reemployment

5 USC 8344 provides, in part, as to CSRS coverage, concerning annuitants who again become federal employees:

(a) If an annuitant receiving annuity from the Fund, except--

(1) a disability annuitant whose annuity is terminated because of his recovery or restoration of earning capacity;

(2) an annuitant whose annuity, based on an involuntary separation (other than an automatic separation or an involuntary separation for cause on charges of misconduct or delinquency), is terminated under subsection (b) of this section;

(3) an annuitant whose annuity is terminated under subsection (c) of this section; or

(4) a Member receiving annuity from the Fund;

becomes employed in an appointive or elective position, his service on and after the date he is so employed is covered by this subchapter. Deductions for the Fund may not be withheld from his pay unless the individual elects to have such deductions withheld under subparagraph (A). An amount equal to the annuity allocable to the period of actual employment shall be deducted from his pay, except for lump-sum leave payment purposes under section 5551 of this title. The amounts so deducted shall be deposited in the Treasury of the United States to the credit of the Fund. If the annuitant serves on a full-time basis, except as President, for at least 1 year, or on a part-time basis for periods equivalent to at least 1 year of full-time service, in employment not excluding him from coverage under section 8331(1)(i) or (ii) of this title--

(A) deductions for the Fund may be withheld from his pay (if the employee so elects), and his annuity on termination of employment is increased by an annuity computed under section 8339(a), (b), (d), (e), (h), (i), (n), (q), (r), and (s) as may apply based on the period of employment and the basic pay, before deduction, averaged during that employment; and

(B) his lump-sum credit may not be reduced by annuity paid during that employment.

The Board may be called upon to determine the nature of the employment, e.g., temporary, permanent, intermittent, of an annuitant who again becomes a federal employee so that it can be determined whether that person's annuity will need to be readjusted based on that person's length of service in the position held following the onset of the annuity. See, e.g., Edwards v. OPM, 11 MSPR 562 (1982) (annuitant reentered employment as an arbitrator for the National Mediation Board; discussion of Dove v. United States, 161 Ct. Cl. 768 (1963)). Brown v. OPM, 18 MSPR 670, 672-73 (1984), added:

...The statute provides that a re-employed annuitant serving on a full-time basis for at least a year is entitled to an increased annuity. See 5 U.S.C. § 8344. The regulation implementing this provision states that "full-time service" means any actual service in which the re-employed annuitant is scheduled to work the number of hours and days required by the administrative workweek for his or her grade or class (normally forty hours). 5 C.F.R. § 831.802(e). By contrast, "intermittent employees" means non-full-time employees without a prearranged regular tour of duty. 5 C.F.R. § 831.201(2). In Edwards, supra, we found that an appellant who was re-employed on a WAE basis and who worked at least ninety-five percent of the potential workdays each month for approximately nine years was a full-time employee entitled to the annuity benefits provided by 5 U.S.C. § 8344(a).

Similarly, in the present case, the presiding official found that appellant proved by a preponderance of the evidence that he actually worked a regular schedule of 7:30 A.M. to 4:30 P.M., Monday through Friday, during the period from January 5, 1980, through July 31, 1981. The presiding official also noted that the employing agency's Civilian Personnel Officer stated that appellant was a full-time employee assigned to an intermittent position for reasons of expediency and flexibility.

We agree with the presiding official that under the statute, regulations, and our decision in Edwards, appellant's eligibility for a supplemental annuity is determined by the actual service he performed rather than by how OPM characterized this service. See Weaver v. Department of the Navy, 2 MSPB 297 (1980). Since appellant was re-employed for more than one year in a full-time capacity, he is entitled to a supplemental annuity under 5 U.S.C. § 8344.
