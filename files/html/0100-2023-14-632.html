3. Delays in Actions

Negotiations could not be required on a proposal that a performance-related disciplinary action be delayed 120 days during a reorganization and then be reevaluated. AFSCME Local 3097 and DOJ, 24 FLRA 453, 455-56 (1986) (Proposal 3). FLRA determined in Local 3097 that the 120-day delay could prevent the agency from disciplining the employee under 5 USC 4303. Under § 4303(b) an employee is entitled to 30 days' advance notice for a performance action. That notice period may be extended no more than 30 days, pursuant to internal agency regulations, and for an additional period only in accordance with OPM regulations. The purpose of the notice period is to permit the employee to demonstrate improved performance to avoid the performance action. Section 4303(c) requires that the decision on the action be made within 30 days after the expiration of the notice period. The agency must complete a performance action within 90 days of the date notice is given to the employee. The 120-day delay was inconsistent with § 4303 and outside the duty to bargain under § 7117(a)(1). Stay procedures have been approved, however, for performance-based actions not subject to Chapter 43. AFGE, Nat'l Council of VA Locals and VA, 29 FLRA 515, 540-42 (1987). FLRA did not find objectionable a proposal that new standards would not become effective until "the first day of the month following the date this agreement is signed." The proposal was designed to avoid implementation of new standards retroactively, a requirement consistent with the statutory mandate that standards be communicated to employees at or before the beginning of an applicable appraisal period. NFFE Local 1974 and VA Reg'l Office, Portland, 46 FLRA 1170, 1181 (1993) (Proposal 6).
