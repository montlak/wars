4. Statutory Grievance System; Foreign Service Grievance Board

Employees of the foreign service are appointed under authorities separate from those involving competitive and excepted service personnel. The Foreign Service Grievance Board (FSGB) and negotiated agreements provide appellate rights for many foreign service personnel. The Board has authority, however, to adjudicate foreign service RIFs. Its jurisdiction intersects with that of the FSGB and, as a result, an employee may be required to elect between the Board and the FSGB when that employee challenges a foreign service RIF. Delaney v. AID, 80 MSPR 146, 150-51 ¶ 8-12 (1998), considered jurisdiction of the FSGB over a RIF appeal:

The Secretary of State is authorized to conduct a RIF in the Foreign Service. 22 USCA § 4010a(a). A Foreign Service RIF triggers the following statute:

An employee against whom an action is taken under this section may elect to file either a grievance under subchapter XI of this chapter or to appeal to the Merit Systems Protection Board under procedures prescribed by the Board. Grievances under subchapter XI of this chapter shall be limited to cases of reprisal, interference in the conduct of an employee's official duties, or similarly inappropriate use of the authority of this section.

22 USCA § 4010a(c)....

...

...In a seminal decision issued over 8 months after the RIF, and over 11 months after the appellant filed his second grievance, the FSGB held (in a decision involving another employee) that 22 USCA § 4010a(c) limits the scope of Foreign Service RIF grievances to those in which the grievant claims that the agency invoked its RIF authority in bad faith. According to the FSGB, section 4010a(c) precludes FSGB review of the "ordinary planning for, and ordinary operation of, a RIF."

Based on the limitations on FSGB jurisdiction, and the fact that the FSGB first explained those limitations long after the appellant grieved his RIF, the Board accepted jurisdiction over Mr. Delaney's appeal. See Egmond v. AID, 83 MSPR 198, 201 ¶ 9 (1999) (reaching the same result as Delaney; once the FSGB declined jurisdiction, a Board appeal must be initiated within 30 days).
