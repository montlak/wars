11. Time Off for Physical Fitness

A proposal was nonnegotiable that, if no other obligation exists, teachers may use the last half-hour of the duty day to participate in "adult school activity that promotes the physical fitness and good mental health of the teacher." Ft. Bragg Ass'n of Educators, NEA and Dept. of Army, Ft. Bragg Sch., 30 FLRA 508, 547 (1987) (Proposal 42). The proposal required the agency to assign specific duties to be accomplished during the last 30 minutes of the day to prevent employees from leaving early. FLRA noted that in NTEU v. FLRA, 793 F.2d 371, 373-74 (D.C. Cir. 1986), the court determined that even if an employee completes assigned tasks before the end of a specified period of time, the employee is not entitled to take off the remainder of the period, but must continue to perform work. That the agency was not precluded from calling the employee back was immaterial. It would be impractical or impossible to locate the employee in the brief remaining period of the workday.

NAGE Local R12-105 and DOD, Nat'l Guard Bureau, Adjutant Gen., California NG, 37 FLRA 462, 462, 465-67 (1990), a nonnegotiable proposal required that "[t]ect (3) hours per week of extended lunch hours or breaktime [be] authorized for physical fitness on a sign out program for aerobic fitness only (Jogging, Biking, Exercise room, walking)." The union recognized that Ft. Bragg Ass'n of Educators, NEA and Dept. of Army, Ft. Bragg Sch., 30 FLRA 508, 547-48 (1987), ruled a similar provision, Proposal 42, nonnegotiable and inconsistent with the right to assign work, but NAGE urged FLRA to allow the proposal negotiable as an appropriate arrangement for employees who, as members of the National Guard, were expected to maintain their physical fitness. FLRA decided the benefits of the proposal were outweighed by its detriments:

The Union seeks to mitigate the adverse effect on employees of management's exercise of its right to establish physical fitness requirements as a condition of retaining the civilian technician position.... The Union asserts that a technician "may be removed from his civilian position if he is physically unable to perform his duties or fails to meet the medical requirements of his technician position."...

...

The Union states that the benefit of the proposal is that "employees are provided duty time to engage in activities designed to ensure that they meet the physical and medical requirements of their position." The Union also states that the Agency benefits by "low turnover." That is, the proposal would minimize the instances where technicians are removed from their positions because they fail to meet the physical fitness requirements. The burden, as identified by the Agency, is on the Agency's ability to assign work. Under the proposal, the Agency would be precluded from assigning work to technicians during the time they are engaged in the activity described in the proposal. This would result in a loss to the Agency of 3 hours of work each week as to each technician in the collective bargaining unit. In our opinion, the burden on the Agency's right to assign work is substantial.

The benefit to employees of having duty time to engage in physical fitness activities is of relatively limited significance when compared to the significant burden on the Agency. Because the potentially adverse effects may arise only in limited circumstances as to particular employees and are not inevitable, it is excessively burdensome to require the Agency to provide 3 hours of duty time each week for every unit employee to engage in physical fitness activities. Further, the proposal does not allow exceptions. Thus, the Agency would be prevented from denying technicians duty time for physical fitness activities, for example, to respond to staffing shortages.

California NG was followed, with the same result, in NAGE Locals R12-1222, R12-222 and DOD, Washington NG, Tacoma, 38 FLRA 295, 303 (1990) (Proposal 2). [Refer later in this chapter to "Weight Control, Fitness" concerning the use of administrative leave for weight control and fitness programs.]
