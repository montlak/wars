g. Contract Provisions That Restate Statutory Requirements

Under Dept. of Treasury, IRS, Wage & Investment Div. and NTEU, 66 FLRA 235, 240 (2011):

...When a grievance involves a dispute regarding a bargaining obligation as defined by the parties' agreement, "the issue of whether the parties have complied with the agreement becomes a matter of contract interpretation for the arbitrator." Broad. Bd. of Governors, Office of Cuba Broad., 64 FLRA 888, 891 (2010) (quoting Soc. Sec. Admin., Md., Balt., 55 FLRA 1063, 1068 (1999)).

Contract provisions that restate statutory provisions are treated as statutes for purposes of the Authority's analysis. See Antilles Consol. Educ. Assn. and DODEA, 64 FLRA 675, 676 n.2 (2010) ("The Authority applies statutory standards in assessing the application of contract provisions that mirror, or are intended to be interpreted in the same manner as, a statutory counterpart. See, e.g., U.S. Dep't of the Treasury, U.S. Customs Serv., Port of New York & Newark, 57 FLRA 718, 721 (2002) (citing U.S. Dep't of Justice, Fed. Corr. Facility, El Reno, Okla., 51 FLRA 584, 589 n.5 (1995) (Port of N.Y. & Newark))."); Dept. of Treasury, Customs Serv. and NTEU Chap. 161, 57 FLRA 718, 721 (2002) ("The Authority previously has applied statutory standards in assessing the application of provisions that mirror, or are intended to be interpreted in the same manner as, the Statute."), and AFGE and SSA Valrico, 59 FLRA 767, 769 (2004) (applying same review standard as NTEU Chap. 161). "In applying a standard of de novo review, the Authority assesses whether the Arbitrator's legal conclusions are consistent with the underlying factual findings." AFGE Local 3631 and EPA, 57 FLRA 42, 44 (2001); see DOD Educ. Activity and FEA, 56 FLRA 711, 717 (2000) (in determining legal issues, FLRA defers to arbitrator's factual findings).

If the disputed contract provision restates a provision of the Statute, FLRA "must exercise care" to ensure that the arbitrator's interpretation is consistent with the precedent of FLRA interpreting the statutory provision. GSA Region 9 and AFGE Council 236, 56 FLRA 683, 685 (2000) (citing DOD, DMA, Aerospace Ctr., St. Louis and NFFE Local 1827, 43 FLRA 147, 153 (1991)); see Dept. of Treasury, IRS, Wage & Investment Div. and NTEU, 66 FLRA 235, 240 n.5 (2011) ("The Authority has held that, where a contract provision restates a provision of the Statute, the Authority "must exercise care" to ensure that an arbitral interpretation of the contract provision is consistent with the Authority precedent interpreting the statutory provision. Gen. Servs. Admin., Region 9, L.A., Cal., 56 FLRA 683, 685 (2000) (quoting U.S. Dep't of Def., Def. Mapping Agency, Aerospace Ctr., St. Louis, Mo., 43 FLRA 147, 153 (1991)).").

If the issue is one of contract, not statutory, interpretation, the contract, not a statute, controls, AFGE Local 1633 and VA DeBakey VAMC, 70 FLRA 519, 520 (2018):

The Union argues that the award is contrary to § 7116(a)(1) and (5) of the Statute because the Agency refused to respond to the Union's request to bargain. We review this exception de novo.

Here, the parties did not stipulate to an issue, and the issue framed by the Arbitrator did not include an unfair labor practice or any other statutory claim. Rather, according to the Arbitrator, the Union argued that the Agency violated Articles 32.5 and 49.4 of the parties' agreement. The Arbitrator found that the Agency did not violate those provisions.

The Authority has long held that where an arbitrator is not required to apply a statutory standard, any alleged misapplication of that standard does not provide a basis for finding the award deficient.[8]
