4. Set-Aside of Jobs

The union may not require management to negotiate a proposal that results in setting aside a certain number of vacancies to be filled through an upward mobility program. AFGE Local 12 and Dept. of Labor, 17 FLRA 674, 674-75 (1985) (Proposal 1). A proposal was nonnegotiable that the parties agree in negotiations over affirmative action on the number and types of vacancies to be filled through the upward mobility program each year. The proposal was inconsistent with the right of the agency to hire and assign employees. The result followed NTEU and IRS, 2 FLRA 281 (1979). AFGE Local 32 and OPM, 8 FLRA 460, 466 (1982) (Proposal IV); see AFGE, Council of Prison Locals and DOJ, Bureau of Prisons, 11 FLRA 286 (1983) (nonnegotiable as infringement on right of management to select and assign employees Provision 1, calling for agreement that institution will have certain percentage of eligible employees involved in Upward Mobility Program at any one time). A provision was nonnegotiable that required the employer to set aside positions for upward mobility and training programs. It interfered with management's right to assign employees under § 7106(a)(2)(A). It was immaterial that there might have been legal requirements for the employer to take these steps. The proposal independently violated management's rights under the Statute. AFGE Local 2761 and Army Adjutant Gen. Publication Ctr., 17 FLRA 899, 904 (1985) (Proposal 5). A proposal was not negotiable that required details of at least eight participants in an agency administrative detail program designed to provide underrepresented groups an opportunity to demonstrate potential for management. It required assignment to positions irrespective of the need for the work. AFSCME Locals 2477 and 2910 and Library of Congress, 12 FLRA 316 (1983). Also nonnegotiable because they excessively interfered with management rights to hire and assign employees and to make selections from any appropriate source were proposals designed to combat underrepresentation of minorities at higher-grade levels by requiring internal recruitment for 13 positions annually and requiring that 60% of the selectees for 65 specified positions be taken from underrepresented groups. AFGE Local 1923 and DHHS, HCFA, Baltimore, 44 FLRA 1405, 1462 (1992) (Proposals 17, 18). [Refer earlier in this chapter to "Quotas; Upward Mobility" and "Bridge Positions."]
