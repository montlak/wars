6. Partial Disapproval of Agreement by Agency Head

When an agency head exercises review authority under § 7114(c) (review of a completely negotiated contract), rejection of a portion of the contract equates to rejection of the contract as a whole, unless some other agreement has been reached by the parties concerning the effect of those provisions not specifically rejected. See Dept. of Army, Watervliet Arsenal and NFFE Local 2109, 34 FLRA 98, 104 (1989). The parties may agree that, rather than disapproving an entire contract under § 7114(c), the parties will implement all provisions of an agreement not disapproved by the agency head or alleged to be outside the duty to bargain. SSA and Nat'l Council of SSA Field Operations Locals, AFGE, 25 FLRA 238, 242 (1987) (citing Dept. of Interior, Nat'l Park Serv., Colonial Nat'l Historical Park, Yorktown and NAGE Local R4-68, 20 FLRA 537 (1985)).

A proposal was negotiable that challenge by the agency to some provisions of a negotiated agreement would not prevent the other provisions from being made effective. USDA, Food and Nutrition Serv. and NTEU, 28 FLRA 580, 585-86 (1987). A proposal was approved that "[t]he provisions of this agreement which conflict with a national agreement shall expire on the date the national agreement goes into effect and all other items of the agreement remain in effect." FLRA explained in AFGE Local 1458 and DOJ, U.S. Attorney, S. Dist. of Florida, 29 FLRA 3, 25, 26 (1987) (Provision 17):

...The provision is an effort to provide for the disposition of the negotiated agreement in the event that a unit consolidation occurs and an agreement is negotiated at the consolidated level of recognition. Rather than dictating any terms to be included in the higher level agreement, the provision looks to that agreement to determine the fate of the terms of the agreement in which it appears. That is, the higher level agreement would determine what terms, if any, of the agreement here would remain in effect. Consequently, we cannot agree with the Agency that the provision is an impermissible impediment to bargaining at a higher level of recognition....
