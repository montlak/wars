2. Excepted Service Preference Eligible Temporary Appointees

Under 5 USC 7511(a)(1)(B), the excepted service preference eligible obtains appeal rights after completion of one year of current continuous service in the same or similar positions. Service under temporary appointments is not excluded. The contrasting treatment of excepted and competitive service temporary appointees was made plain in Compton v. Dept. of Navy, 31 MSPR 402, 404 (1986), involving a competitive service temporary appointee. A laborer who held a series of temporary, one-year appointments argued, when terminated, that he was a tenured employee entitled to adverse action procedures. The Board disagreed:

The statutory provision applicable to preference eligible employees in the excepted service, 5 USC 7511(a)(1)(B), defines an "employee" as one who has "completed 1 year of current continuous service in the same or similar positions." However, the provision applicable to employees in the competitive service, 5 USC 7511(a)(1)(A), defines an "employee" (insofar as it is relevant here) as one who has "completed 1 year of current continuous employment under other than a temporary appointment limited to one year or less." Thus while service in a temporary excepted service position may be counted toward the completion of one year under 7511(a)(1)(B), Van Skiver v. USPS, 9 MSPB 28, 9 MSPR 380 (1982), service under a temporary limited appointment may not be counted in determining whether an employee in the competitive service has completed one year of current continuous service....

The Board finds, therefore, that it is immaterial that combined service under a series of temporary appointments may be greater than a year in duration. See Fish v. Department of the Navy, supra, at 598 (competitive service employee was not an employee" under 5 USC § 7511(a)(1)(A), where "each of...eight appointments was a temporary appointment limited to one year or less"). Compare Roden v. Tennessee Valley Authority, 25 MSPR 363, 367-68 (1984) (excepted service employee was an "employee" under 5 USC § 7511(a)(1)(B) since he has satisfied the "continuous service" requirement by serving under a series of consecutive appointments in "the same or similar positions").

In the instant case, since appellant was in the competitive service serving under a series of temporary appointments limited to one year or less, we find no error in the presiding official's determination that the appeal was outside the Board's jurisdiction. Fish, supra.

Moreover, appellant was separated at the expiration of his last temporary appointment. Civil Service regulations at 5 CFR § 752.401(c)(6) specifically exclude from the coverage of 5 CFR Part 752 "[t]ermination of appointment on the expiration date specified as a basic condition of employment at the time the appointment was made." For this reason also, therefore, appellant may not appeal his separation to the Board.

See Hales v. USPS, 53 MSPR 503, 507 (1992) ("preference eligible employees in the excepted service, such as the appellant, may count their service in a temporary position toward completion of the 1 year of current continuous service requirement of 5 U.S.C. § 7511(a)(1)(B). See, e.g., Compton v. Department of the Navy, 31 M.S.P.R. 402, 404 (1986); Geter v. Department of the Army, 19 M.S.P.R. 143, 145 (1984); Van Skiver v. U.S. Postal Service, 3 M.S.P.R. 380, 381-82 (1982).").
