8. Equal Access to Justice Act

FLRA regulations implementing the EAJA are at 5 CFR Part 2430. EAJA does not apply to grievance procedures under the FSLMRS. Grievance procedures are established under negotiated agreements and not under the Administrative Procedure Act (APA). They do not constitute adversary adjudications, defined by 5 USC 504(b)(1)(C) as an adjudication under 5 USC 504, the APA. Army Corps of Eng'rs and NFFE Local 639, 17 FLRA 424, 425 (1985); see VA Central Texas Veterans Health Care Sys. and AFGE Local 2109, 65 FLRA 895, 896 (2011) (exception asserting that hourly rate exceeded EAJA cap: "5 C.F.R. § 2430 governs awards of attorney fees under the EAJA. See U.S. Dep't of Def., Def. Distrib. Region E., New Cumberland, Pa., 51 FLRA 155, 163 n.7 (1995). However, FLRA has held that proceedings before arbitrators under the Statute are not adversary adjudications for purposes of the EAJA because they are not subject to the procedural requirements of the Administrative Procedure Act. Id. The attorney fees in this case, thus, were not awarded under the EAJA. Accordingly, 5 C.F.R. § 2430 is inapposite and does not demonstrate that the award is deficient."); cf. AFGE Local 3599 v. EEOC, 920 F.3d 794, 797 n.1 (Fed. Cir. 2019) (suggesting that EAJA does not support a fee award by an arbitrator in an adverse action). FLRA lacks the authority under EAJA to award counsel fees incurred in arbitration. The only proceedings that do qualify for fees under EAJA are ULP proceedings conducted by FLRA's General Counsel against a labor organization. AFGE Local 1960 and Naval Educ. and Training Program Dev. Ctr., Pensacola, 34 FLRA 799, 803 (1990); see USDA, APHIS and Nat'l Ass'n of Agric. Employees, 35 FLRA 998, 1004 (1990) (FLRA could not award fees under Back Pay Act or 5 USC 7105, 7118 for time spent on grievant's case); 5 CFR 2430.2(a) (2022) (regulation implementing EAJA for ULPs against unions). [Refer to Chapter 8, "Equal Access to Justice Act," concerning EAJA awards in ULP cases.]
