25. Record Keeping and Disclosure Requirements

A proposal that dictated agency retention requirements concerning retention of information about drug testing and rehabilitation was nonnegotiable under NTEU and Customs Serv., 31 FLRA 118, 125-30 (1988). It was inconsistent with requirements of the FPM and the Executive Order. AFGE Dept. of Educ. Council of AFGE Locals and Dept. of Educ., 38 FLRA 1068, 1103-11 (1990), enforcement denied as to other proposals, Dept. of Interior, Minerals Mgmt. Serv. v. FLRA, 969 F.2d 1158, 1163 (D.C. Cir. 1992), determined nonnegotiable Section D of Proposal 6, that the employer destroy records concerning unconfirmed test results. The proposal was inconsistent with government-wide regulations concerning the testing program and maintenance of related records. The same result was reached in AFGE Local 1808 and Dept. of Army, Sierra Army Depot, 42 FLRA 542, 555-56 (1991) (Proposal 8); see AFGE Local 446 and Dept. of Interior, Nat'l Park Serv., Blue Ridge Parkway, 43 FLRA 836, 884, 886 (1991) (Proposal 6C; same result); AFGE Dept. of Educ. Council of AFGE Locals and Dept. of Educ., 38 FLRA 1068, 1103, 1106-07 (1990) (Proposal 6, Section B--"[e]mployees will be guaranteed confidentiality in all matters relating to drug testing. Information will be released only to those officials of the employer that have an absolute need to know"--was found too vague for a negotiability determination), reviewed, Dept. of Interior, Minerals Mgmt. Serv. v. FLRA, 969 F.2d 1158, 1163 (D.C. Cir. 1992) (denying enforcement of other proposals).

Negotiable in AFGE Local 446 and Dept. of Interior, Nat'l Park Serv., Blue Ridge Parkway, 43 FLRA 836, 884, 887 (1991), was Proposal 6B, requiring that "[i]nformation will be released only to those that have an absolute need to know." The union explained that the intent of the section was to permit the release of information to those persons "who are authorized by applicable law, rule or regulation to receive the information." NTEU and NRC, 43 FLRA 1279, 1328 (1992), resolved as negotiable a component of Proposal 4, requiring management to remove any reference to the reasonable suspicion test from the employee's personnel files if management determined, following the employee's explanation, not to administer the test.

Approved as negotiable in IFPTE Local 89 and Dept. of Interior, Bureau of Reclamation, Grand Coulee Project Office, 48 FLRA 516, 528, 529 (1993), was Proposal IV.C.6: "Anonymous, statistical information maintained regarding drug testing shall include the race, sex, and age of those being tested." The proposal did not call for disclosure of the information, leading the Authority to find no violation of the right of the agency to determine its own internal security practices.
