d. Deposition of Agency Head

Reaching a peculiar result, the Board's Vice Chairman opined in Barber v. FDIC, 37 MSPR 620, 626-27 & n.3 (1988), that members of an agency tripartite Board of Directors were required to testify concerning an adverse action they collectively decided, but they were not required to submit to deposition. Instead, less intrusive methods, e.g., deposition by "written interrogatories," should have been followed. The MSPB Vice Chairman commented:

It is well established that heads of agencies are not normally subject to depositions. Kyle Engineering Co. v. Kleppe, 600 F.2d 226 (9th Cir. 1979). There are, however, exceptions to that rule. See, e.g., American Broadcasting Companies v. U.S. Information Agency, 599 F. Supp. 765, 769 (D.D.C. 1984), citing Sykes v. Brown, 90 F.R.D. 77, 78 (E.D. Pa. 1981) (where an agency head possesses particular information necessary to the development or maintenance of the party's case, which cannot be reasonably obtained by another discovery mechanism, the deposition should be allowed to proceed); Community Federal Savings & Loan, 96 F.R.D. at 621 (the deposition of an agency head may be permitted when the official has relevant, first-hand knowledge of matters material to the decision that are not a part of the administrative record and not available from some other source). Because of the availability of considerable discovery and other documentary evidence, including verbatim transcripts of the Board meetings, I believe that the CALJ acted within his discretion in denying the appellant's motion to compel the Board members' depositions....

Cf. Bacon v. DHUD, 757 F.2d 265, 269-70 (Fed. Cir. 1985) ("[T]he Supreme Court has unequivocably drawn the line against inquiry into the mental process of an agency head. (Citing United States v. Morgan, 313 U.S. 409, 421-22 (1941) and Montgomery Ward & Co. v. Zenith Radio Corp., 673 F.2d 1254, 1263-64 (CCPA 1982))."). [The Barber decision is also discussed in Chapter 3, "Obligation to Permit Relevant Testimony" and "Agency Head."]
