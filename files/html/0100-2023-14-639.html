broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 20: Performance Appraisal, Section A: Frequency and Timing of Appraisal

A. FREQUENCY AND TIMING OF APPRAISAL

A negotiable proposal required annual performance appraisals. No provision of law established greater or lesser frequency. The timing of performance appraisal was within the discretion of the agency. AFGE Local 1968 and Dept. of Transp., St. Lawrence Seaway Dev. Corp., Massena, 5 FLRA 70, 72-76 (1981); see West Point Elem. Sch. Teachers Ass'n and U.S. Military Academy West Point Elem. Sch., 34 FLRA 1008, 1014, 1016-17 (1990) (approving proposal for two annual appraisals); SSA and AFGE Local 1760, 21 FLRA 401, 403 (1986). There was no impediment to a proposal that required an agency to rate employees annually and to complete ratings within five working days of the anniversary date. AFGE Local 3028 and DHHS, Pub. Health Serv., Alaska Area Native Health Serv., 13 FLRA 697, 701 (1984); see AFGE, Nat'l Council of VA Locals and VA, 29 FLRA 515, 526-29, 561 (1987) (negotiable proposals: for annual appraisal; appraisal within five days of employee's stated intent to leave facility; one formal rating conference 180 days before annual rating; procedure for self-evaluation; procedure for response by employee to appraisal and consideration by employer of response; conferences with employee every 30 days during a 90-day improvement period to address employee performance; new appraisal of employee who demonstrates satisfactory level during improvement period).

There was no bar to the negotiation of a proposal that appraisals be issued not later than 45 days after the end of the rating period. The proposal constituted a negotiable procedure that did not interfere with the right of management to assign work to its employees. Distinguishing proposals that specified the particular employees who would perform tasks, e.g., prepare ratings, FLRA observed that most procedures require an assignment to someone of the work involved in carrying out a procedure. To bar negotiation of procedures due to incidental assignments of work to someone in an agency would nullify § 7106(b)(2) and overlook the purpose and intent of that statutory subsection. AFGE Local 2671 and Army Publications Distrib. Ctr., St. Louis, 32 FLRA 1006, 1013-16 (1988) (Provision 5).

More structured proposals concerning the manner and frequency of appraisals were approved as negotiable in POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 47 FLRA 10, 53 (1993), Provision 21:

5(A)--Reviews of work for purposes of performance evaluation of non-primary examiners will take place prior to the signing by a Primary Examiner of the examiner's action or issue for functions which receive a close review. For functions which receive a cursory review, reviews for purposes of performance evaluation will normally take place prior to signing by a Primary Examiner of the examiner's action or issue. Under exceptional circumstances, the review of the examiner's work product may be conducted subsequent to signing the examiner's action or issue.

POPA explained, 47 FLRA at 54:

Provision 21 requires the Agency to evaluate employees' work at the end of each assignment, as well as at the end of the annual appraisal period. In our view, Provision 21 constitutes a negotiable procedure under section 7106(b)(2) of the Statute.

We reject the Agency's claim that the provision directly interferes with management's right to assign work under section 7106(a)(2)(A) and (B) of the Statute because it determines when evaluations will occur. Provision 21 merely requires the Agency to provide employees throughout the evaluation period with work appraisals, rather than providing such appraisals only at annual performance evaluations. The provision neither describes the nature or extent of the interim work evaluations nor affects the Agency's discretion to evaluate employees during their annual formal appraisal. Further, Provision 21 does not obligate the Agency to conduct any additional performance evaluations and does not require it to change the nature of its present performance standards or elements or the manner in which the Agency evaluates employees.

Proposals that determine when appraisals are to be given to employees do not directly interfere with management's right to assign work and constitute negotiable procedures under section 7106(b)(2) of the Statute. See, for example, National Labor Relations Board Union and National Labor Relations Board, 42 FLRA 1305, 1319 (1991).... Consistent with these decisions, we conclude that Provision 21 is a negotiable procedure under section 7106(b)(2) of the Statute.

The 1993 POPA decision also determined Provision 32 to be negotiable, 47 FLRA at 69:

Each rating official shall conduct one formal performance appraisal of each employee annually and one informal progress review at the approximate midpoint of the rating year. Additional performance appraisals on an individual basis may be made only in connection with a request by the employee or when the supervisor has identified specific deficiencies which, if continued, would justify an overall rating that is less than satisfactory. Any problem or exceptionally good work should be brought to the employee's attention whenever it is discovered.

A proposal was negotiable that required close-out ratings when employees or their supervisors changed positions. Under the proposal, ratings above the fully successful level received on a critical element as a close-out rating would serve as the rating of record for purposes of the annual performance appraisal when that critical element did not continue in the employee's new position or under a new supervisor. FLRA construed the proposal as allowing the employer to treat as close-out ratings assessments of performance at levels lower than fully successful. The proposal was not inconsistent with the then-controlling regulation, 5 CFR 430.206(f), requiring agencies to transfer performance ratings and to prepare summary ratings for employees moving to new organizations in the employing agency during the appraisal period. NAGE Local R1-144, FUSE and Dept. of Navy, Naval Underwater Sys. Ctr., Newport, 38 FLRA 456, 458-64 (1990) (Proposal 2). Nonnegotiable was a proposal that demanded that the employer weigh or balance two ratings during a period of change in performance standards. The proposal interfered with the right of the agency to direct employees. It was not evaluated as an arrangement. NFFE Local 1974 and VA Reg'l Office, Portland, 46 FLRA 1170, 1182-83 (1993) (Proposal 7).

Given the negotiability of more complex proposals concerning the timing of appraisals, in a not particularly dramatic holding FLRA approved the negotiability of Provision 34 in POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 47 FLRA 10, 72-73 (1993), requiring that "[t]he rating official shall initiate the formal annual appraisal by providing notice to the employee of the date and time for the formal appraisal meeting." Also approved for negotiation was Provision 36, that the employee sign and date a statement to indicate that the proposed rating was discussed with the rating official.
