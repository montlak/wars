13. Low Turnout

Dept. of Interior, Bureau of Indian Affairs and NFFE Council of Consol. Bureau of Indian Affairs, 34 FLRA 67, 69-70 (1989), rejected an agency objection that an election result was invalid for a poor showing at the polls. Only three of 17 eligible voters cast ballots:

In view of the Activity's agreement that the election would be conducted by mail ballot, we agree with the Regional Director's rejection of the...objection to the use of mail ballot procedures.... [I]n the absence of a showing that the election was not properly publicized or that unusual circumstances improperly limited voter participation, the election was valid.... [T]here is no requirement under our Rules and Regulations that a specific percentage or number of eligible voters cast ballots in order for a representation election to be valid.

We also find that the NLRB decisions in Kit Mfg. Co. [198 NLRB 1 (1971)] and Gold & Baker [55 NLRB 591 (1944)] offer no support for the Activity's application for review. In Lemco Construction, 283 NLRB 459 (1987), the NLRB overruled Kit Mfg. Co. and Gold & Baker. The NLRB stated: "[W]e have decided to abandon any analysis dependent on a numerical test to determine the validity of a representation election. Rather, we will issue certifications where there is adequate notice and opportunity to vote and employees are not prevented from voting by the conduct of a party or by unfairness in the scheduling or mechanics of the election."
