broida flra Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards, Subchapter 08: Grounds for Review, Section G: Contrary to Law; Award is Inconsistent with Law, Rule, or Regulation, Subsection 06: Promotion, Part e: Basic and Relative Qualifications
e. Basic and Relative Qualifications

Arbitrators may apply contract provisions that expand on rather than limit management's selection options, as long as management can set minimal qualifications for a position. A contract provision that interferes with the right of management to set qualifications for positions infringes the right of management to make selections under 5 USC 7106(a)(2)(C). NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, IRS, 61 FLRA 618, 622 (2006), reconsideration denied, 61 FLRA 846 (2006). But an award granting a promotion was set aside when the employee did not meet OPM specialized experience qualification standards for the job at the higher grade. Dept. of Navy, NAWS China Lake and IAF Local F-32, 70 FLRA 980 (2018) (Member DuBester dissenting).

Promotion grievances may challenge the qualifications of the individual who was selected. In VA Hosp. and AFGE Lodge 2201, 4 FLRA 419, 421-23 (1980), the agency argued that under the late FPM it had the right to select any of the three candidates referred for consideration and that the award favoring the grievant and ordering his promotion ignored management's right under the FPM to decide not to fill the position at all. To this FLRA responded:

The Agency's exception that the Arbitrator's award is contrary to the Federal Personnel Manual is a ground on which the Authority will find an arbitrator's award deficient under section 7122(a)(1) of the Statute. In this case, however, the Agency has failed to demonstrate in what manner the Arbitrator's award, ordering the grievant promoted, is inconsistent with management's right to select or not select under the Federal Personnel Manual [allowing an agency to select or not select from a group of best qualified candidates from among appropriate sources]. The Office of Personnel Management has previously advised that consistent with the Federal Personnel Manual, an agency may be required to promote a particular employee when an arbitrator has found that such employee would have been promoted at a particular time but for the agency's violation of its negotiated agreement.

In his award, the Arbitrator has clearly made the findings requisite to a proper order of promotion. As was noted, the Arbitrator specifically found that the Activity violated the negotiated agreement when "Management did not properly follow the substantive requirements of the promotion procedures." He further determined that because "Management's actions improperly and substantially supported [the selected employee's] candidacy his selection and his candidacy must be set aside." Additionally, the Arbitrator also specifically found that the "Grievant, who was the only other candidate rated as 'highly qualified,' would have been selected but for the improper selection of [the employee selected]." Thus, the Agency has failed to demonstrate that the Arbitrator's award, ordering the grievant promoted in the foreman's position, is contrary to the Federal Personnel Manual....
