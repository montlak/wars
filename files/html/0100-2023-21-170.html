6. VA Medical Professional; VA Benefit Reduction

For part-time VA medical professionals, annuity benefit calculations were described, with reference to 38 USC 7426, in Falso v. OPM, 70 MSPR 470, 473 (1996). See Sanderson v. OPM, 72 MSPR 311, 317 (1996) (also considering service credit for part-time VA physicians). Anderson v. OPM, DE-0831-13-0032-I-1 (NP 5/19/2014), described the scope of appointments within 38 USC 7426:

...38 U.S.C. § 7426 applies only to individuals appointed to the Veterans Health Administration (VHA), an administration within the DVA, under subchapter I of chapter 38. 38 U.S.C. § 7426(b)(1); see James v. Von Zemensky, 284 F.3d 1310, 1315 (Fed. Cir. 2002) ("Concerned that the Veterans Administration had been unable to attract qualified medical professionals under the civil service system's regulations and rates of pay, Congress enacted provisions specifically relating to the hiring, promotion, pay, hours and conditions of employment, retirement, and discipline of health-care professionals employed by the predecessor to the VHA."); see also Heaney v. U.S. Veterans Administration, 756 F.2d 1215, 1218 (5th Cir. 1985) ("In passing the legislation which established the VA's hospitals and health-care facilities, Congress was well aware of the special nature of professionals--physicians, dentists, and surgeons. Title 38 of the United States Code, which deals with the VA, is replete with examples of special treatment for professionals in the [Veterans Health Administration]."[4] ); see also Falso v. Office of Personnel Management, 70 M.S.P.R. 470, 473 (1996) (stating that "Title 38 of the U.S. Code contains a number of provisions addressing the rights and benefits of physicians, registered nurses, and other health-care personnel serving with DVA" and that 38 U.S.C. § 7426(b) addresses "the computation of an annuity for a DVA health-care employee"), aff'd in part, vacated in part on other grounds, and remanded, 116 F.3d 459 (Fed. Cir. 1997). Such appointments are limited to positions "necessary for the health care of veterans," and Police Officer is not included in the list of occupations that satisfy this requirement. 38 U.S.C. § 7401. The Secretary may appoint individuals in occupations not specifically enumerated in 38 U.S.C. § 7401; however, this authority is limited to individuals in "such other classes of health care occupations" that are "necessary for the recruitment and retention needs of the Department," which expressly excludes "occupations relating to...protective services." 38 U.S.C. § 7401(3)(A)(i)....

[4] Heaney refers to the Department of Medicine and Surgery (DMS). The DMS is now referred to as the Veterans Health Administration. See Von Zemensky, 284 F.3d at 1315.

The amount of a disability annuity is subject to reduction when the annuitant is also receiving military retirement pay or equivalent compensation from the VA, except when those receipts are the result of statutorily-specified types of service, Swan v. OPM, 93 MSPR 622, 625 ¶ 8 (2003):

A disability annuitant is guaranteed a minimum annuity, computed under a method not relevant here, unless he "is receiving retired pay or retainer pay for military service.... or pension or compensation from the Department of Veterans Affairs in lieu of such retired or retainer pay"; in that case his annuity is calculated under a different method, and "excluding credit for military service." 5 U.S.C. § 8339(g). This restriction on eligibility for a minimum disability annuity does not apply, however, to an individual "specified in" 5 U.S.C. § 8332(c)(2). That section provides as follows:

If an employee or Member is awarded retired pay based on any period of military service, the service of the employee or Member may not include credit for such period of military service unless the retired pay is awarded (A) based on a service-connected disability--(i) incurred in combat with an enemy of the United States; or (ii) caused by an instrumentality of war and incurred in line of duty during a period of war as defined by section 1101 of title 38.

5 U.S.C. § 8332(c)(2).
