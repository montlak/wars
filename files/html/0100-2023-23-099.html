2. Debt to Retirement Fund

Noting that a debt to the retirement fund should be repaid through a back pay offset only if the debt derives from erroneous payments arising from the disallowed personnel action, Harris v. USDA, 50 MSPR 686, 697-98 (1991), added that before implementing the offset, an agency is required to contact OPM to determine the amount of the refund (debt) to be offset against the back pay entitlement. Under Harris, in accordance with the then applicable FPM provisions concerning back pay deductions, the order of precedence for deducting erroneous payments from back pay awards when the net amount of back pay is insufficient to cover all the deductions is as follows: (1) refund of retirement contributions; (2) severance pay; (3) lump sum payment for accrued annual leave.

The right of the agency to offset the appellant's back pay for debts due to the retirement fund is limited to debts incurred as a result of the action being corrected through the appeal leading to the back pay obligation. See Metcalf v. OPM, 73 MSPR 215, 220 (1997) (considering waiver of overpayment but noting that "[i]t is well-settled that OPM may attempt to collect its remaining debt because its lump sum and monthly annuity payments to the appellant were paid in error once the legal basis for such payments was removed when the appellant was reinstated to his former position"). The back pay obligation resulting from a current appeal cannot be used as a source of funds to repay the retirement fund for a preexisting debt arising from some earlier action involving a refund of retirement contributions. Hymon v. Dept. of Navy, 39 MSPR 106, 108-09 (1988). Niday v. Dept. of Army, 42 MSPR 673, 678 (1990), added:

...Thus, we find here that back pay is governed by OPM's regulations and that the computation of back pay is not a matter for negotiation between the employee and the agency....

...

The appellant further contends that the agency is authorized under its own regulations...to waive his indebtedness to the Civil Service Retirement and Disability Fund.... [A]lthough the FPM provides for waiver of "recovery of erroneous payments of any amount from the Civil Service Retirement and Disability Fund," such a waiver may be granted by OPM, not by the employing agency, and requests for a waiver should be submitted to OPM. See FPM Supp. 990-2, subch. S8-7d(5).

The Board lacks authority to grant an appellant the replacement of retirement funds that accrued while she was working and were withdrawn because she needed the money. Although the Back Pay Act is intended to put the employee in the position he would have been in had there been no unjustified personnel action, neither the Back Pay Act nor its implementing regulations provide for replacement of money earned prior to the unjustified action and spent because the employee was not receiving paychecks. Fryer v. Dept. of Air Force (Fed. Cir. 1985 NP No. 85-2049); see Rittgers v. Dept. of Army, 123 MSPR 31, 36-37 ¶¶ 9-10, 2015 MSPB 59 (2015) (TSP hardship withdrawals are not remediable through the Back Pay Act or associated enforcement proceedings); cf.Rhodes v. VA, AT-0752-12-0316-X-1 (NP 4/26/2022) (confusion and delay by agency over deductions from back pay; direction that agency waive debt generated by retirement contributions that agency failed to deduct from back pay payment after the period passed for a request to OPM for a waiver); Harris v. USDA, 53 MSPR 78, 82 n.2 (1992) (determining "not pertinent" to a compliance proceeding the complaint of the appellant that the OPM had not complied with his request to release his retirement funds).
