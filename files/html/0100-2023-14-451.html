broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 15: Pay, Premiums, and Allowances, Section F: Pay and Employment for Employees of Particular Agencies, Subsection 09: Department of Veterans Affairs

9. Department of Veterans Affairs

A proposal was negotiable that a medical center participate in a local survey of nurses' salaries, that the information be made available to the union, and that the information be considered by the medical center in reaching a decision or recommendation for special pay rates or pay adjustments, Illinois Nurses Ass'n and VAMC, N. Chicago, 27 FLRA 714, 726-27 (1987) (Proposal 4):

...Under 38 U.S.C. § 4107(g) the VA Administrator may increase the rates of basic pay of nurses and other professional medical employees on a nationwide, local, or other geographic basis. The increase in the rate of basic pay may be made to provide pay competitive with that paid to the same category of personnel at non-Federal facilities in the same labor market.

Under VA regulations, the Facility Director is responsible for submitting a request for a change in the rate of basic pay.... A request which is approved by officials within DM&S above the level of the facility is forwarded to the VA Administrator for concurrence. Therefore, the VA Administrator has discretion under 38 U.S.C. § 4107(g) not only to adjust rates of basic pay but to do so based on information as to salary rates at non-Federal hospitals in a given locality.

The proposal simply outlines a series of steps which the Agency is to follow in gathering information on salary rates for nurses in the local area...a procedure whereby the Agency exercises its discretion under law regarding possible increases....

We also find that Proposal 4 does not interfere with the Agency's right to assign work.... The proposal would allow the Union to submit data regarding wage rates in the area. The proposal is silent concerning who is to accomplish the review.... [T]he Agency retains total discretion to assign the task of reviewing the data.

A similar proposal concerning surveys of nurses' wages and recommendations by a medical center for pay adjustments was approved for negotiation. VA Staff Nurses Council, Local 5032 and VAMC, Wood, 29 FLRA 849, 868-70 (1987) (Proposal 12). New York State Nurses Ass'n and VA Bronx Med. Ctr., 30 FLRA 706, 739-42 (1987) (Proposal 14), approved a proposal concerning salary surveys and a provision that required management to appoint one representative from a group of union nominees to be a member, on official time, of the medical center's data collection team. The proposal was nonnegotiable to the extent that it allowed the union to designate a particular employee as a team member, because management was precluded from considering its patient care needs in making the appointment. AFGE, Nat'l Council of VA Locals and VA, 29 FLRA 515, 524-31, 561-62 (1987) (Proposal 4), required negotiation of proposals setting the waiting periods for periodic step increases. Title 38, the governing pay statute, left the matter to agency discretion. [These holdings must be viewed with discretion, for they may be tempered by other, more recent, pronouncements and statutory developments concerning the coverage of Title 38 employees under the FLMRS. Those decisions are discussed in this chapter, "Department of Veterans Affairs Positions."]

Negotiable in NAGE Local R5-136 and VA, Ralph H. Johnson Med. Ctr., 56 FLRA 346, 348-49 (2000), was a provision that wage surveys involving hybrid Title 38 pharmacists include the local retail sector in addition to local hospitals. Under 38 USC 7455, the Secretary has the authority to increase pay in order to provide wages competitive in the local labor market, assure adequate staffing, and recruit personnel with certain specialized skills. The proposal, requiring the agency to survey the named employers only when the agency decided to conduct a survey, did not prescribe how the survey was to be conducted or what was to be done with the results of the survey. The agency provided no evidence that the Secretary's pay-setting discretion was sole and exclusive. Situations involving non-hybrid employees were distinguished because pay-setting authority for those employees was subject to the Secretary's exclusive discretion under 38 USC 7422(b)(3).

Proposals were nonnegotiable in NAGE Local R1-109 and VAMC, Newington, 36 FLRA 175, 176, 178-82 (1990), that, first, employees be paid at least the minimum rate paid to any employee at a particular location that the employer pays for a given grade under 38 USC 4111(b), and, second, the level of pay be made retroactive. They were nonnegotiable because they effectively required adjustments in the pay rates of Title 5 employees--whose wages were not negotiable--and because the proposals required salary adjustments without regard to the statutory constraints of 38 USC 4107(g), requiring that pay increases can only be made to provide pay that is competitive with pay at comparable non-federal facilities, achieve adequate staffing, or recruit personnel with specialized skills, especially those with skills that are difficult or demanding. The statute limited the amounts of any increases and required that the Administrator notify the President not less than 90 days before various increases; statutory authority remains in the President to disapprove the increase within that period of time.
