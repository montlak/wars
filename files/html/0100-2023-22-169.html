b. Amounts

$2,520: McBride v. USDA, 3 MSPR 495, 498, 4 MSPB 17 (1980) (performance case: 36 hours of work).

$3,360: Seth v. OPM, 33 MSPR 415, 419 (1987) (retirement appeal).

$7,122: McKinney v. Dept. of Air Force, 26 MSPR 267, 270 (1985) (RIF appeal).

$7,509: West v. Dept. of Treasury, 32 MSPR 338, 341 (1987) (107 hours of work for an unacceptable performance action appeal).

$10,404: Kilpatrick v. VA, CB-7121-13-0181-A-1 (NP 1/16/2015) (review of arbitration award; fee reduced for lack of success on a reprisal claim; $450 per hour).

$11,450: Carter v. SBA, 28 MSPR 296, 300 (1985) (RIF appeal at a rate of $100 per hour).

$13,830: Melanson v. VA, 19 MSPR 228, 233 (1984) (removal was found to be whistleblowing reprisal).

$13,894: Alexander v. Dept. of Navy, 28 MSPR 569, 572 (1985) (unidentified adverse action plus additional work on a PFR involving a fee award; an hourly rate of $125 for the lead attorney; $100 for the second chair).

$14,418: Gavette v. OPM, 31 MSPR 115, 121 (1986) (involuntary retirement appeal: 192 hours, including 67 hours of research and brief preparation, in a complex case extending over a period of several years and including 34 hours for preparation of a PFR, 50 hours of telephone conversations and conferences during a period of two years, 12 hours for the preparation of a fee petition including a nine-page itemization of fees and expenses, and disallowing 16 hours for preparing a reply to the agency opposition to the PFR (the reply is not permitted under Board rules)).

$14,882: Bartel v. FAA, 22 MSPR 398, 399 (1984) (consolidated appeals of air traffic controllers fired for striking).

$16,516: Coltrane v. Dept. of Army, 32 MSPR 6, 10-11 (1986) (Chapter 43 demotion at a rate of $90 per hour).

$17,247: Losure v. ICC, 18 MSPR 388, 394 (1983) (reduced from $20,296 awarded by the AJ).

$18,425: Dishman v. USPS, 37 MSPR 295, 298 (1988) (misconduct removal at $125 per hour).

$19,328: Ceja v. DLA, 34 MSPR 399, 406 (1987) (misconduct removal at $100 per hour).

$19,964: Weaver v. Dept. of Army, 29 MSPR 565, 570 (1985) (Chapter 43 demotion at $80 per hour).

$21,300: Hanson v. Dept. of Transp., 28 MSPR 176, 182 (1985) (ATC removal at $50 per hour).

$21,707: Harris v. USDA, 33 MSPR 237, 242 (1987) (removal mitigated to suspension and demotion: 189 hours for the initial litigation; 72 hours for the PFR; 29 hours for the fee petition at an hourly rate of $75).

$24,968: Crumbaker v. Dept. of Labor, 24 MSPR 627, 635 (1984) (misconduct removal at $80-130 per hour for lead counsel).

$23,000: Gibson v. DOJ, 12 MSPR 436, 445, 11 MSPB 78 (1982) (removal appeal: approximately $23,000, plus $3,171 for the preparation of a fee application, and costs, for a total of $28,000, a reduction from the AJ's award of about $60,000).

$25,182: Gibson v. VA, 30 MSPR 97, 100 (1986) (appeal from demotion: 223 hours for a senior counsel; 123 hours for an associate; 15 hours were stricken for duplication).

$27,650: Kelly v. Dept. of Navy, 43 MSPR 430, 438 (1990) (removal appeal: approximately 220 hours, including 34.3 hours of phone conversations in an appeal involving three days of hearings and two years of representation).

$28,800: Logan v. DHUD, 23 MSPR 345, 352 (1984) (removal reversed as prohibited personnel practice: 288 compensable hours; 184 hours were disallowed).

$29,965: Broadnax v. USPS, 28 MSPR 528, 530 (1985) (unidentified adverse action at the apparent rate of $90 per hour).

$31,403: Freeman v. VA, 66 MSPR 125, 131 (1995) (removal appeal: finding "the case was not simple...it involved two charges, three affirmative defenses, and two days of hearing").

$34,766: D'Iorio v. DHUD, 43 MSPR 531, 537 (1990) (settlement of a removal; 254 hours on the appeal; 22.5 hours responding to a PFR on the fee issue).

$35,488: Robinson v. DHHS, 32 MSPR 683, 689 (1987) (appeal to the MSPB from an arbitration case involving a performance-based demotion).

$35,638: Crumbaker v. Dept. of Labor, 40 MSPR 71, 81 (1989) (removal mitigated to a suspension: including a contingency adjustment; 84 hours to work on the removal prior to appeal, 91 hours on the appeal at the regional office, 109 hours for the PFR, 67 hours spent on complex issues involved in the attorney fee litigation).

$39,597: Key v. USPS, 33 MSPR 142, 155 (1987) (misconduct removal at a rate of $150 per hour for lead counsel).

$43,524: Wiatr v. Dept. of Air Force, 50 MSPR 441, 455 (1991) (suspension appeal involving claims of whistleblowing reprisal: the joint petitions of three groups of lawyers involved in various stages of a case were resolved through a settlement calling for reasonable fees).

$46,463: Andress v. Dept. of Transp., 23 MSPR 260, 265 (1984) (appeal from removals for alleged strike participation: consolidated appeals).

$51,178: SSA v. Balaban, 33 MSPR 309, 323 (1987) (defense of a case against an ALJ; a total of 469 hours was allowed for the Board aspect of the litigation, divided among three lawyers).

$66,509: Rose v. Dept. of Navy, 47 MSPR 5, 18 (1991) (misconduct removal; multiple counsel).

$71,800: Buckler v. Fed. Retirement Thrift Inv. Bd., 78 MSPR 198, 199 (1998) (RIF appeal; extensive litigation).

$72,985: Burch v. DHS, 109 MSPR 426, 2008 MSPB 185 (2008) (removal reversed as reprisal for filing age discrimination complaint).

$85,228: Wightman v. VA, 111 MSPR 109, 114 2009 MSPB 55 (2009) (constructive suspension and constructive removal through involuntary retirement).

$88,408: Boltz v. SSA, DE-0752-08-0436-A-1 (NP 8/20/2010) (reversal of misconduct charge leading to removal).

$93,699: Krape v. DOD, 97 MSPR 430, 439 ¶ 19 (2004) (consolidated cases involving misconduct-based removals mitigated to suspensions).

$96,278: Smith v. Dept. of Navy, 113 MSPR 430, 2010 MSPB 55 (2010) (unacceptable performance appeal).

$98,055: Schultz v. USPS, PH-0752-94-0233-M-1 (NP 12/16/2016) (prolonged compliance proceedings).

$104,264: Rumsey v. DOJ, 123 MSPR 502, 2016 MSPB 28 (2016) (IRA case: multiple counsel; reduction for unsuccessful claims).

$109,000: McCann v. Dept. of Treasury, 852 F.2d 1294 (Fed. Cir. 1988) (regional office decision approving the settlement agreement).

$143,000: Sherrell v. Dept. of Navy, 92 MSPR 15, 17 ¶ 4 (2002) (award resulting from a settlement agreement enforced by the Board).

$160,622: McKenna v. Dept. of Navy, 108 MSPR 404, 2008 MSPB 69 (2008) (protracted RIF appeal).

$189,062: Rothschild v. DHUD, 54 MSPR 238, 243 (1992) (prolonged adverse action litigation including a petition for mandamus required to compel DHUD to issue a decision on the proposed action so that an appealable result could be obtained).

$196,000: Guzman v. DHS, SF-0752-15-0170-A-2 (NP 1/6/2023) (IRA appeal).

$214,800: Hart v. Dept. of Transp., 115 MSPR 10, 2010 MSPB 183 (2010) (complex constructive suspension appeals involving consolidation of numerous appeals).

$254,320: Waters v. Dept. of Transp., AT-0752-10-0197-R-1 (NP 11/7/2012) (initial decision shows reliance on Laffey Matrix rates; fee further augmented in the 2012 decision); Waters v. Dept. of Transp., AT-0752-10-0197-A-2 (NP 8/29/2013) (supplemental fees).

$517,576: Coffman v. OSC & DHS, 2022 MSPB 18 (2022) (defense of OSC disciplinary complaint).

$642,000: Goeke and Bottini v. DOJ, CB-0752-15-0228-A-1 (NP 8/12/2016) (suspensions reversed based on procedural errors).
