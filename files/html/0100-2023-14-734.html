N. TRAVEL EXPENSES AND FACILITIES FOR UNION OFFICIALS

Whether the parties must negotiate payment by an agency of travel expenses and per diem allowances incurred by employees using official time in the conduct of labor-management relations was considered in NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, Customs Serv., 21 FLRA 6 (1986), after the Supreme Court rejected the notion of statutory entitlement as a matter of law in BATF v. FLRA, 464 U.S. 89 (1983). FLRA concluded that payments were within the discretion of management, subject to provisions of the Federal Travel Regulations. The agency presented no information to show that the union's proposal resulted in significant and unavoidable increases in costs. There was no showing that the proposal conflicted with the right to determine the agency budget. FLRA readily decided that the proposal affected conditions of employment. Representation of employees in matters concerning their employment affected their working conditions. A negotiable proposal called for the agency pay travel and per diem to employee negotiators on official time. Payment was within management's discretion, and negotiable, under the FTR. NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, IRS, 21 FLRA 97 (1986). Another negotiable proposal called on the agency to provide government transportation for employees at labor relations workshops. Overseas Educ. Ass'n and DODDS, 27 FLRA 492, 511 (1987) (Proposal 13). Negotiations were barred, however, for travel expenses and arrangements of nonunit employees serving as union representatives. Bargaining could occur only as to unit employees' working conditions. Overseas Educ. Ass'n v. FLRA, 827 F.2d 814, 818-20 (D.C. Cir. 1987), aff'g Overseas Educ. Ass'n and DODDS, 22 FLRA 351, 368 (1986) (Proposal 11).

Comptroller General decisions concerning allowance of travel and per diem expenses for labor-management activities are founded on a determination that the payment serves the convenience of the agency or is otherwise in the primary interest of the government. A proposal would not be negotiable that presupposed payment of expenses without regard to the primary interest test, but the agency may be compelled to negotiate the circumstances defining when travel attendant to labor-management activities is in the primary interest of the government. The primary interest determination can be made on a case-by-case basis. NLRB Union and NLRB, 22 FLRA 486 (1986) (Proposal 1). [Refer to Chapter 3, "Official Time" and "Travel and Per Diem."]
