j. Calculation of Overpayment; Interest

As to disputes over the exact amounts of overpayments, the Board is inclined to accept documents from OPM that can lead to calculations rather than to insist on mathematical computations. See DeVille v. OPM, 30 MSPR 461, 463 (1986) ("The Board held recently that OPM was not required, in cases such as this, to include actual mathematical computations on which an annuity determination is based when the statutory provision on which it relies includes a formula by which the annuity is to be calculated, and when OPM presents sufficient other evidence. Kerr v. Office of Personnel Management, 29 M.S.P.R. 284, 286 (1985).").

For guidance on interest on overpayments, refer to Mauro v. OPM, 50 MSPR 28, 34 (1991). Disputes over collection or amount of interest assessed by OPM on overpayments are subject to Board review, Dubin v. OPM, 38 MSPR 437, 438-40 (1988) (Chairman Levinson dissenting):

In his petition for review, appellant again argues that the Board has jurisdiction to adjudicate OPM's denial of his request for waiver of interest charges on his annuity overpayment. We agree.

The Board's jurisdiction in retirement annuity cases derives from 5 U.S.C. Chapter 83. See Scarborough v. Office of Personnel Management, 723 F.2d 801, 811 (11th Cir. 1984). Specifically, under 5 U.S.C. § 8347(d)(1), an individual whose "rights or interests" are affected by an administrative action or order under 5 U.S.C. Chapter 83 may file an appeal with the Board.

Appellant's "interest" under 5 U.S.C. § 8347(d)(1), for purposes of determining the Board's jurisdiction in this case, consists of the total amount of his obligation to repay the overpayment made to him from the Civil Service Retirement and Disability Fund (CSRDF or Fund). That total consists of both the principal amount of the overpayment and the interest assessed thereon by OPM.[3] The interest payments necessarily attach to the principal portion of the overpayment and cannot exist apart from it. Since the Board has jurisdiction over appeals concerning OPM's refusal to waive the principal portion of debts owed the CSRDF, 5 C.F.R. § 831.1304, it is logical to conclude, in the absence of evidence to the contrary, that we also have jurisdiction over appeals concerning OPM's refusal to waive the interest charges attaching to those debts.

[3] Agencies may charge interest on debts owed them. 31 U.S.C. § 3717. Waiver of such interest is authorized by 31 U.S.C. § 3717(h), which indicates that such waivers are to be governed by regulations prescribed by each agency to best fit its own requirements. OPM has established such regulations at 5 C.F.R. § 831.1305(b).

OPM's regulations relative to the processing of debts owed the CSRDF provide that, before any collection may begin, the debtor will be notified in writing of, inter alia: (1) OPM's policy on interest, penalties, and administrative charges; (2) the standards for determining entitlement to waiver of those charges; and (3) the debtor's right to appeal to the Board (if OPM's waiver decision finds the individual liable). 5 C.F.R. § 831.1304. These regulations in no way limit the Board's jurisdiction to questions relating exclusively to the principal obligation of debts owed the Fund. Id. See also 5 C.F.R. § 831.110. Our examination of the legislative histories of the pertinent statutory provisions and the explanatory materials published by OPM (to supplement its regulations concerning the recovery of annuity overpayments and the standards for waiver of overpayments) also fails to reveal any intention by either Congress or OPM to impose such a limitation on the Board's jurisdiction. See 1978 U.S. Code Cong. & Ad. News 1379 and 2723; 1975 U.S. Code Cong. & Ad. News 2064; 50 Fed. Reg. 34,663 (1985); 45 Fed. Reg. 23,631 (1980).

Further, the inclusion of interest and waiver issues within OPM's retirement regulations demonstrates that both interest and waiver thereof are integral parts of the overall debt collection process. OPM's regulation on waiver of interest, 5 C.F.R. § 831.1305(b), appears under the caption "[c]ollection of debts" and provides, inter alia, for waiver in accordance with the standards applicable for waiver of overpayments. It fails to state that its provisions are unreviewable by the Board. Moreover, inasmuch as the same factors are considered by OPM in deciding to waive repayment of interest as are used in deciding on a request for waiver of an overpayment, a determination on waiver of interest would generally follow from a waiver of overpayment decision. Accordingly, we find that the Board has jurisdiction to consider an appeal from a final OPM decision concerning a request for waiver of interest on an annuity overpayment claim.

Snee v. OPM, 39 MSPR 227 (1988).
