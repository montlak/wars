broida flra Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards, Subchapter 05: Awards Involving Adverse Actions and Performance Actions Unreviewable by FLRA: 5 USC 7122, 5 USC 7121(f), Section D: Pre-Decisional Advisory Arbitrations

D. PRE-DECISIONAL ADVISORY ARBITRATIONS

Under 5 USC 7513(c) an agency may provide, by regulation, for a hearing in lieu of or in addition to the oral and written reply opportunity allowed in an adverse action case. That statute permits contract provisions allowing for predecisional advisory arbitrations. The arbitrator advises management whether she thinks the proposed disciplinary action should be taken, mitigated or canceled. After management considers the advisory award and takes its action, the grievant has the opportunity to invoke arbitration again to challenge the final agency decision. The arbitration decision then issued is a final decision, subject only to statutory appeal rights. The two arbitrations may be before different arbitrators. An advisory arbitration procedure that constitutes part of a statutory reply process in an adverse action case is not reviewable by FLRA. The advisory arbitration is separate and distinct from the grievance or rights arbitration process provided by § 7121 or continued by the Reform Act savings clause, 5 USC 7135, statutory provisions that do not invoke FLRA jurisdiction without an otherwise reviewable award involving a final agency decision. AFGE Council, San Francisco Region and DHHS, SSA, 9 FLRA 161, 162-63 (1982); cf. Mercer v. DHHS, 772 F.2d 856, 858-59 (Fed. Cir. 1985) (describing the advisory arbitration process).
