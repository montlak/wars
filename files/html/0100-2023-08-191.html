16. Agriculture: Stabilization and Conservation Service; Cooperative Extension Services

County executive directors of the Agriculture Stabilization and Conservation Service are not federal employees for purposes of Board adverse action jurisdiction. They do not meet the definition of "employee" at 5 USC 2105(a)(1). Hedman v. USDA, 915 F.2d 1552 (Fed. Cir. 1990); see Hamlet v. United States, 63 F.3d 1097, 1106 (Fed. Cir. 1995) (considering claim of former county program assistant for the ASCS: "because ASCS county employees are appointed by the county executive director and because the director is appointed by the county committee, which is comprised of elected members of the county's farming community, such ASCS county employees are not included within the definition of 'employee' in 5 U.S.C. § 2105(a) (1994)."). Bivings v. USDA, 225 F.3d 1331, 1334-36 (Fed. Cir. 2000), affirmed that the Board had no jurisdiction to review the termination of a wildlife specialist with the University of Arkansas Cooperative Extension Service who held a parallel excepted service appointment with the USDA extension service. The federal appointment, served without salary but with retirement and insurance benefits, was dependent upon the state appointment. When the state position was terminated, so too the federal job. Since the Board lacked authority over the state action, no relief was available as to the federal removal. Moore v. Glickman, 113 F.3d 988 (9th Cir. 1997), determined that an ASCS chief program assistant, not an "employee" under the Reform Act, could bring an action under the Administrative Procedure Act in federal district court to review her dismissal following her pursuit of agency administrative appeals, but she could not sue individual officials of the agency for damages resulting from her dismissal. A unit leader and a regional director of the Cooperative Extension Services were employees under 5 USC 2105 for purposes of IRA jurisdiction. The appellants were under the joint control of the agency and the University of Illinois. Their appointments were in the excepted service. Simmons v. USDA, 80 MSPR 380, 386-87 ¶ 12 (1998) (raising, but not deciding, whether the Board could provide a meaningful remedy if federal positions depended on university appointments beyond the control of MSPB).
