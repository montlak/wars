J. BARGAINING PROCEDURES

Not negotiable was a proposal that negotiations over impact and implementation of management-proposed changes in working conditions be combined with negotiations over the basic agreement and that also provided that, without an overriding exigency, any proposed change be deferred until the new basic agreement had been signed. The proposal did not distinguish between changes that were and were not de minimis. Requiring the agency to bargain over changes of no more than de minimis impact created a bargaining duty beyond the statutory requirement. Although an agency could elect to bargain over those types of changes, it could not be required to do so. POPA and PTO, 21 FLRA 580, 584-86 (1986) (Proposal 3).

Nothing barred a proposal for inclusion in a contract of an obligation that management establish or modify personnel practices and policies after consultation with the union. The proposal enabled the union to offer its nonbinding views before the agency made its decision. New York State Nurses Ass'n and VA Bronx Med. Ctr., 30 FLRA 706, 752-53 (1987) (Proposal 19) (citing AFGE Local 1738 and VAMC, Salisbury, 27 FLRA 52 (1987) (Proposal 19)).

Rejected as nonnegotiable was a proposal that negotiation impasses, following unsuccessful mediation attempts, be resolved through mediation/arbitration, with the results subject to agency head review. FLRA determined that § 7119(b)(2) provides that parties may adopt a procedure for binding arbitration of negotiation impasses only if the procedure is approved by FSIP. NTEU v. Dept. of Treasury, IRS, 35 FLRA 7, 8 (1990); see NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, BATF, 35 FLRA 26, 29 (1990) (proposal nonnegotiable that requested mediation/arbitration of impasses by FMCS because proposal did not require prior FSIP approval of procedure). Determined to be a permissive subject of bargaining in FDIC and NTEU, 18 FLRA 768, 769 (1985), was a proposal soliciting the union's agreement that the employer could implement changes, despite referral of disputed issues to a third party for resolution, if agreement had not been reached within 45 days from the onset of requested negotiations. The FDIC proposal was not a mandatory subject of bargaining because it compelled waiver of benefits derived from statutory impasse procedures, including a stay of changes in working conditions pending resolution of impasses.
