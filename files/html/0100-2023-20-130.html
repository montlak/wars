e. Serial Complaints to OSC

Under Ward v. Merit Systems Protection Board, 981 F.2d 521 (Fed. Cir. 1992), a complainant may not raise through an IRA appeal a matter different than what was presented to OSC. The appellant must go back to OSC with another complaint on that matter with the option to later raise the matter with the Board after exhausting the OSC process. Ward did not consider the appellant who raises the same matter in a series of complaints to OSC and then, following the most recent complaint, takes the matter to the Board. Although considerations of res judicata or collateral estoppel would apply to a Board decision on a particular complaint when substantially the same matters were raised in another Board IRA appeal, nothing precludes repeated or duplicative filings with OSC as a preclude to one or more IRA appeals, as was implicit from Dunlap v. Dept. of Navy, AT-1221-10-0927-W-1 (NP 5/20/2011):

We disagree with the administrative judge's conclusion that the appellant untimely filed her IRA appeal. The administrative judge correctly stated that the filing deadline for an IRA appeal is statutory and, therefore, may not be waived. IAF, Tab 13 at 3 (citing MacDonald v. Department of Justice, 105 M.S.P.R. 83, ¶ 11 (2007)). The administrative judge then stated, however, that an employee should not be allowed to "frustrate" congressional mandate by the "artifice" of filing successive OSC complaints over the same matter and that the Board has not specifically addressed this issue. The Board, however, addressed this matter in Becker v. Department of Veterans Affairs, 76 M.S.P.R. 292, 298 n.3 (1997), where it noted that the appellant had filed ten previous complaints with OSC on the same or related subjects. The Board, found, however, that the appellant could raise the matters that were the subject of the last OSC complaint that preceded the appellant's IRA appeal. Applying Becker to the present appeal yields the conclusion that the appellant timely filed her IRA appeal based upon OSC's July 30, 2010 closure letter.

Referenced in Dunlap was a footnote to Becker v. VA, 76 MSPR 292, 298 (1997):

[3] In this regard, we note that the April 1, 1997 letter to the appellant from OSC states that he has filed ten previous complaints with the Special Counsel on this or related subjects. The record also contains an August 23, 1996 initial decision dismissing a prior IRA appeal for lack of jurisdiction. The administrative judge should caution the appellant that, in this appeal, he may only meet his burden of proof by relying on matters that he raised in the OSC complaint that led to the filing of this IRA appeal. Thus, we will not consider any matters raised in the previous OSC complaints, unless they were raised again in the OSC complaint that led to this IRA appeal.
