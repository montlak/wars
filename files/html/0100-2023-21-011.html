4. Temporary Employment

Vanaman v. OPM, 55 MSPR 358, 360-61 (1992) (corrected, Vanaman v. OPM, 59 MSPR 598 (1993), post), related temporary employment and civil service annuity entitlements:

Entitlement to a deferred retirement annuity under the Civil Service Retirement Act (CSRA) is dependent upon two types of Federal service, "creditable service" and "covered service." An individual must have completed five years of creditable civilian service and must have served at least one of his last two years of Federal service in a position qualifying as covered service. 5 U.S.C. § 8333(a)-(b); 5 U.S.C. § 8338(a). Creditable service includes, inter alia, Federal civilian service performed by an individual meeting all of the provisions of 5 U.S.C. § 2105(a), the statute defining an "employee" of the Federal government. 5 U.S.C. § 8331(a); Noveloso v. Office of Personnel Management, 45 M.S.P.R. 321, 323-24 (1990), aff'd, 925 F.2d 1478 (Fed. Cir. 1991) (Table). Thus, creditable service includes almost all Federal service. Covered service, however, refers only to those Federal employees who are subject to the CSRA and who must deposit a portion of their pay into the Civil Service Retirement Fund. Noveloso v. Office of Personnel Management, 45 M.S.P.R. at 323.

Employees appointed to a non-permanent, indefinite ("temporary") position and individuals employed on an intermittent basis have been excluded from coverage by the retirement system. 5 U.S.C. § 8347(g); 5 C.F.R. § 831.201(a)(1)-(2), (12)-(13). Therefore, these employees do not contribute to the retirement fund, and their employment does not qualify as covered service. This exclusion, however, has no bearing on an employee's creditable service. Noveloso v. Office of Personnel Management, 45 M.S.P.R. at 324.

The appellant held three positions with the Federal government between 1943 and 1951. The evidence of record shows that the first position, between 1943 and 1945, was subject to the CSRA, constituting both creditable and covered service. The other positions, between 1945 and 1951, were temporary appointments, qualifying only as creditable service. The length of the appellant's service, 6 1/2 years, is not in dispute. Therefore, he met the requirement of five years of creditable service, and the decisions by OPM and the administrative judge to the contrary were in error.

It is the second requirement that the appellant failed to meet. One of the last two years of Federal service must be in a covered position. 5 U.S.C. § 8333(a). The appellant served in temporary positions for the last 4 1/2 years of his Federal service, positions excluded from retirement coverage. Since the appellant did not serve one of his last two years in a covered position, he is not eligible for a civil service retirement annuity. 5 U.S.C. § 8333(a).

Temporary or intermittent appointments may not qualify for service credit under CSRA, Castro v. OPM, 5 MSPR 326, 327 (1981):

An applicant for retirement benefits must have completed, under the Civil Service Retirement System, a minimum of five years of civilian service. See 5 U.S.C. 8333(a); 8337(a).

5 U.S.C. 8347(g) provides that OPM may exclude an employee or group of employees in or under an Executive agency whose employment is temporary or intermittent. Thus, the regulations at 5 C.F.R. 831.201 set forth a list of categories of employees excluded from retirement coverage. The categories applicable to the instant case are: 1) employees serving under appointments limited to 1 year or less, 2) employees serving under non-permanent appointments made pursuant to section 1 of Executive Order 10180 of November 13, 1950, and 3) employees serving under non-permanent appointments, designated as indefinite, made after January 23, 1955.

Duncan v. OPM, SF-0842-15-0458-B-1 (NP 4/28/2016), said of FERS coverage:

An individual is not eligible to make a deposit for FERS retirement unless she is an "employee" or "former employee"; that the term "employee" is defined, for this purpose, as an individual whose employment is covered by FERS, i.e., as one who meets the definition of "employee" in 5 U.S.C. § 8401(11); and that, under 5 U.S.C. § 8401(11)(C)(ii), 5 U.S.C. § 8402(c)(1), and 5 C.F.R. § 842.105(a), individuals holding temporary or intermittent appointments such as those held by the appellant are excluded from that definition.
