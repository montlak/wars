1. Objections to Elections and Preliminary Proceedings

Section 2422.26(a) of the FLRA's regulations allows for objections to the conduct of an election to be filed by a party. "Party" is defined by § 2421.11(a) and (b) of those rules as a person filing or named in a charge, petition, or request; or whose intervention in a proceeding has been permitted or directed by FLRA. An individual employee is not a "party" and may not file objections to the conduct of an election. VA, Pershing Med. Ctr. and AFGE Local 2338, 45 FLRA 326, 327 (1992). FLRA recapitulated and added to its description of standing to file objections in DHUD, Region IX, San Francisco and NFFE Local 1450, 50 FLRA 334 (1995), reconsideration denied, 50 FLRA 575 (1995). The underlying decision determined, 50 FLRA at 336:

Section 2422.21(b) of the Authority's Regulations provides that a "party" to an election may file objections to a representation election. "Party" is defined to include, as relevant here, any person: (1) filing a charge, petition, or request; (2) named in a charge, complaint, petition, or request; or (3) whose intervention in a proceeding has been permitted or directed by the Authority. 5 C.F.R. § 2421.11.

Local 2403 has not established that it is a party in this proceeding, as defined in section 2421.11. There is no contention that Local 2403 filed the instant petition or was named in it. In addition, the record is clear that AFGE National, not Local 2403, was the exclusive representative of the petitioned-for employees. As such, Local 2403 does not qualify as an incumbent intervenor under section 2422.5 [now 2422.8] of the Authority's Regulations. See Defense Commissary Agency, Defense Commissary Store, Fort Drum, New York, 50 FLRA 249, 253 (1995) ("a union qualifies as an 'incumbent' only when it is the exclusive representative of all the employees in the unit sought in a petition"). Local 2403 did not, apart from claiming intervenor status as an incumbent, seek to intervene by filing a motion accompanied by an adequate showing of interest, as required by section 2422.5 [now 2422.8] of the Authority's Regulations.

A union may not raise objections, e.g., failure to provide an eligibility list or failure of an intervenor to secure a proper showing of interest, for the first time before the Authority. Objections must be initially presented to the regional director. Dept. of Army, Hq., Ft. Sam Houston and AFGE Local 2154, 12 FLRA 766, 767-68 (1983); 5 CFR 2429.5 (2022). [Refer later in this chapter to "Review of Regional Director's Decision"; refer to earlier discussion of "Showing of Interest" and comments concerning the use ULP charges challenging conduct affecting showing of interest.]

Objections to conduct affecting an election must be filed within five days after the tally of ballots was furnished. Army Health Care Sys. Support Activity and NFFE Local 28, 16 FLRA 1178, 1180 (1984); see Dept. of Army, Ft. McClellan and AFGE, 57 FLRA 108, 109 & n.2 (2001) (time period runs five days after service of tally; intervening weekends and holidays are excluded from computations); 5 CFR 2422.26 (2022). Objections cannot be amended after the five-day period for filing to add new objections in the absence of evidence that is newly discovered and previously unavailable. PBGC and Ind. Union of Pension Employees for Democracy & Justice, 66 FLRA 349, 352 (2011). For purposes of calculating the date of the objection, there is but one tally, and that tally may be signed by an FLRA employee on behalf of the regional director. There is no distinction between an "official" and an "unofficial" tally; confusion on that score did not serve to extend the time for filing objections or provide an extraordinary reason for accepting a late objection. VA, Chattanooga Nat'l Cemetery and AFGE, 45 FLRA 263, 267-68 (1992). The date of the tally is not counted toward the five days. If the filing period for objections expires on a weekend, the filing deadline moves to Monday. See USDA, Forest Serv., Springerville, Ariz., and NFFE, 47 FLRA 945, 951 (1993).
