d. Full-Time/Part-Time

The Board lacks jurisdiction over the claim of an employee who is reassigned from a full-time to a part-time clerk position if there is no reduction in grade and no indication on the record that the employee suffered a reduction in the number of hours worked. Cortez v. MSPB (Fed. Cir. 1984 NP No. 84-924). The Board disclaimed jurisdiction over the appeal of an hourly USPS part-time postmaster whose small post office was reclassified in a way that reduced the employee's hours from six to four a day. The reduction was not in basic pay. The action was considered an unappealable reduction in rank. Wood v. MSPB, 938 F.2d 1280 (Fed. Cir. 1991); see Gage v. MSPB, 482 Fed. Appx. 546, 549 (Fed. Cir. 2011 NP) ("As to Mr. Gage's second claim, an involuntary reassignment without a reduction in pay or grade is not appealable to the MSPB, even if the reassignment results in a reduction of work hours. See Wood v. Merit Sys. Prot. Bd., 938 F.2d 1280, 1282 (Fed. Cir. 1991)."); Howard v. USPS (Fed. Cir. 2002 NP No. 02-3092) ("neither a change to 'part-time status' nor the denial of leave constitutes an adverse action appealable to the Board"); Long v. USPS, SF-3443-10-0745-I-1 (NP 3/2/2011) ("For the first time on review he asserts that he lost overtime pay and 'out of schedule pay due to the Victorville Post Office changing [his] starting and ending times.' The Board need not consider an argument raised for the first time in a petition for review absent a showing that it is based on new and material evidence not previously available despite the party's due diligence, and the appellant made no such showing here. Banks v. Department of the Air Force, 4 M.S.P.R. 268, 271 (1980). Even considering his new argument, however, we conclude there is no basis to overturn the initial decision. His alleged loss in overtime pay and any other reduction in pay is solely the result of a reduction in his assigned work hours and is not appealable. See Wood v. Merit Systems Protection Board, 938 F.2d 1280, 1282 (Fed. Cir. 1991)."); cf. Martin v. DOD, SF-0351-16-0620-I-1 (NP 7/19/2022) (no RIF jurisdiction over reduction to a half-time position of a school nurse).
