3. Motion Must Be Complete When Filed

A fee petition should be complete. The Board does not provide the right of reply to an agency submission. Sims v. Dept. of Navy, 13 MSPR 393, 395 (1982) ("In fact the regulations merely require that the agency be given an opportunity to respond to a motion for attorney fees."). Nor does the Board consider arguments supporting a fee petition raised for the first time in a PFR challenging the regional office initial decision. Santella v. Special Counsel, 86 MSPR 48, 67 ¶ 38 (2000), reconsideration denied, 90 MSPR 172 (2001)); Shahid v. Dept. of Navy, 33 MSPR 682, 683-84 (1987) (applying the rule to an agency; evidence should first be presented to the AJ, not for the first time on the PFR); Rudolph v. OPM, 28 MSPR 261, 262 n.* (1985) ("Appellant fails to establish that the Board should consider the other arguments in support of an award of attorney fees which are raised for the first time in his petition for review of the addendum decision."); Robinson v. VA, 25 MSPR 673, 674 (1985) (declining to consider on a PFR argument and documentation that should have been submitted to the AJ in support of a fee petition); Adkins v. DHUD, 13 MSPR 570, 572 (1982) (declining to consider a justification for a fee award that was first raised in the PFR); cf. Mynard v. OPM, 108 MSPR 58, 63 n.4, 2008 MSPB 23 (2008) (reference to counsel fees in an enforcement petition does not constitute a motion for attorney fees). There is nothing that precludes a reply; one should be considered if there are factual issues raised by the response and a need arises for either discovery or a hearing to resolve the factual disputes. It is suggested that when a fee petition is submitted, the record remain open for a reasonable time to reply to the agency opposition. Many AJs will provide that opportunity in their acknowledgment order that will be issued after the fee petition is docketed. If there are errors in the fee petition, they should be corrected by the time the record closes. [Refer later to "Obligation of Judge to Ensure Due Process."]
