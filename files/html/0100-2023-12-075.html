j. Discovery of Additional Legal Argument

The Board expressed its unwillingness to use the reopening and reconsideration process to excuse a belated PFR raising new legal arguments, Nelson v. FDIC, 68 MSPR 1, 3 (1995), aff'd, 83 F.3d 1375 (Fed. Cir. 1996):

As good cause to waive the filing deadline, the appellant asserted that, after the time for filing a petition for review had expired, he learned of the Board's earlier decision in Fox v. Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, 62 MSPR 447 (1994), which the appellant asserted supports a finding of Board jurisdiction. The Board has held, however, that the discovery of additional legal arguments after the time period for filing a petition for review does not constitute good cause for waiver of the filing deadline for a petition for review. See Acree v. USPS, 64 MSPR 566, 569 (1994); Puckett v. Dept. of the Air Force, 56 MSPR 25, 27 (1992).

We also decline to reopen this appeal on our own motion. Generally, the Board will not reopen an appeal to cure an untimely filing. See Devine v. USPS, 64 MSPR 289, 292-93 (1994). The Board may reopen an appeal on its own motion to correct its own errors or to modify its judgment, decree, or order. See Golden v. USPS, 60 MSPR 268, 272 (1994). However, in this appeal, the appellant has not shown that an error was made. The Board case that he cites to support his allegation that his appeal is within the Board's jurisdiction as a reduction in pay, Fox, 62 MSPR 447, is distinguishable from this appeal.

See Geehan v. USDA, PH-0752-14-0397-I-1 (NP 9/8/2015) ("As for the appellant's allegation that her current representative discovered the administrative judge's purported abuse of discretion in March 2015, 'the discovery of additional legal arguments after the time period for filing a petition for review does not constitute good cause for waiver of the filing deadline.' Nelson v. Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, 83 F.3d 1375, 1376 (Fed. Cir. 1996) (quoting Nelson v. Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, 68 M.S.P.R. 1, 3 (1995)).").
