1. Administratively Uncontrollable Overtime

Administratively uncontrollable overtime allows the head of an agency under OPM regulations at 5 CFR 550.151-164 (2022) and consistent with 5 USC 5545 to authorize premium pay on an annual basis for an employee whose hours of duty cannot be controlled administratively and who must work substantial irregular, unscheduled overtime. Administratively uncontrollable overtime may be paid only if the amount involved would be less than the employee would be entitled to under regular overtime. Nat'l Border Patrol Council, AFGE and INS, 23 FLRA 106, 109-10 (1986). The AUO scheme was explained by DHS, C&BP and AFGE NBPC Local 1929, 61 FLRA 741, 742 (2006):

AUO, which is authorized by 5 U.S.C. § 5545(c)(2), is a system for compensating employees with premium pay for irregular unscheduled overtime. AUO is authorized for employees who hold "a position in which the hours of duty cannot be controlled administratively and which requires substantial amounts of irregular or occasional overtime work, with the employee generally being responsible for recognizing, without supervision, circumstances which require the employee to remain on duty." Id. (quoting 5 C.F.R. § 550.151). Border Patrol Agents are one category of Federal officers eligible to earn AUO.

AUO pay is computed by application of a formula based on the number of AUO hours worked over a period of time to compute a weekly average of AUO hours. The computation is applied on a sliding scale so that if an employee averages: (1) at least three, but not more than five hours of AUO per week, the employee receives 10% of his/her base pay; (2) between five and seven hours per week, 15%; (3) between seven and nine hours per week, 20%; and (4) nine or more hours, 25%. Where an employee is off work for holidays, sick or annual leave, training, etc., with no opportunity to perform AUO, it would penalize the employee to include the "off" hours in computing the weekly average. Thus, the concept of "excludable days" is applied, whereby time spent on leave or engaged in other activities, such as training, are excluded from the number of workdays used to compute the daily average of AUO hours.

A union may negotiate with an agency to determine the specific procedures governing computations as to appropriate rates of premium pay for administratively uncontrollable overtime. Nat'l Border Patrol Council, AFGE and INS, 23 FLRA 106, 110-12 (1986). [Refer to Chapter 9, "Administratively Uncontrollable Overtime and Other Overtime Entitlements."]
