e. Bureau of Indian Affairs; Indian Health Service

The Board considered a few appeals involving the relationship between local school boards and BIA education reorganizations. See Amiri v. Dept. of Interior, 21 MSPR 22, 28-29 (1984) (BIA RIF was properly conducted with local school board involvement); Allen v. Dept. of Interior, 20 MSPR 713, 717-20 (1984) (more analysis of the necessity, prior to a RIF, of consultation with and approval by local school boards); Chino v. Bureau of Indian Affairs, 15 MSPR 252, 255-57 (1983) (concerning the statutory requirements relating to RIFs affecting employees of BIA schools and the requirements of local school board approval necessary for those RIFs); Bennally v. Dept. of Interior, 14 MSPR 132, 136-40 (1982) (discussing the statutory structure for a BIA RIF of full-time to part-time positions). Blackhat v. DHHS, 117 MSPR 552, 556 ¶ 8, 2012 MSPB 41 (2012), determined there was no jurisdiction over appeals by individuals on Intergovernmental Personnel Act assignments to the Tuba City Regional Health Care Corporation (TCTHCC) following abolition of their IHS positions. The Board had no control over the TCTHCC, a nonfederal entity. As to the separations from DHHS, jurisdiction was lacking because the appellants were covered by a collective bargaining contract that did not exclude RIFs from the grievance procedure. See Honga v. DHHS, DE-0351-16-0072-I-1 (NP 5/6/2016) ("The appellant's IPA assignment was terminated by the San Carlos Apache Healthcare Corporation pursuant to the terms of the Special Purpose Assignment Agreement. The San Carlos Apache Healthcare Corporation is not a Federal agency and, therefore, is not part of the Federal Government subject by statute to competitive or excepted service requirements. See 5 U.S.C. § 3371(2)‑(3); Blackhat v. Department of Health & Human Services, 117 M.S.P.R. 552, ¶ 5 (2012). Accordingly, we find that the administrative judge correctly determined that the Board lacks jurisdiction to review the San Carlos Apache Healthcare Corporation's decision to terminate the appellant's IPA assignment.").
