12. Advance Notice and Timing of Tests

AFGE Local 1808 and Dept. of Army, Sierra Army Depot, 37 FLRA 1439, 1440, 1444-47 (1990), declared nonnegotiable four testing proposals: Provision 1 was for the employer to agree that when an employee is required to be tested, the employee will be notified, two hours prior to each required test, of certain specified matters concerning the test; Provision 2 concerned the frequency or intervals for testing employees; Provision 3 was that two hours prior to each required test, the employee will be informed of the location of drug abuse counseling and referral services for voluntary self-referral "prior to testing without reprisal"; Provision 4 required that if an employee being tested cannot provide a sufficient volume of urine, the employee may return the next day to complete the specimen. The first three provisions interfered with management's right to determine its internal security practices, as FLRA determined in the series of decisions involving NFFE Local 15 and the Army Command at Rock Island, reported at NFFE Local 15 and Dept. of Army, Army Armament, Munitions and Chem. Command, Rock Island, 30 FLRA 1046, 1057 (1988), 33 FLRA 436 (1988), and 35 FLRA 936 (1990); the last provision was contrary to government-wide regulations of DHHS, "Mandatory Guidelines for Federal Workplace Drug Testing." See IFPTE Local 1 and Dept. of Navy, Norfolk Naval Shipyard, 49 FLRA 225, 241, 243-45 (1994) (Proposal 4 nonnegotiable, requiring one hour of advance notice); IFPTE Local 89 and Dept. of Interior, Bureau of Reclamation, Grand Coulee Project Office, 48 FLRA 516, 530 (1993) (Proposal IV.E.4) (nonnegotiable: proposal that if employee cannot provide sufficient sample within reasonable time, he or she will be allowed to return next day, until necessary sample could be provided); AFGE Local 446 and Dept. of Interior, Nat'l Park Serv., Blue Ridge Parkway, 43 FLRA 836, 854, 857-58 (1991) (Proposal 2D was nonnegotiable to extent that it provided for two hours of notice before reporting to site of transportation to testing site); NAGE, FUSE, Local R1-144 and Dept. of Navy, Naval Underwater Sys. Ctr., Newport, 42 FLRA 730, 754 (1991) (nonnegotiable: portion of Provision 5 for agreement that samples not be supplied outside of work hours; finding negotiable portion of same provision that employees undergoing testing will not be denied their normal lunch hours, providing that those employees may be kept under surveillance); AFGE Local 3457 and Dept. of Interior, Minerals Mgmt. Serv., 42 FLRA 567, 582-84 (1991) (Proposal 5 nonnegotiable: permitting employees being tested for illegal drug usage to return next day for testing when they cannot provide urine specimen of sufficient volume); NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, BATF, 41 FLRA 1106, 1135, 1136-37 (1991) (nonnegotiable, Provision 9: "If an employee is unable to provide a sufficient volume of urine within a reasonable time on the appointed day, he/she may return on the next day until the necessary amount is voided").

Nonnegotiable in AFGE Local 3457 and Dept. of Interior, Minerals Mgmt. Serv., 37 FLRA 1456, 1457-58, 1463-64 (1990), was a proposal that a day before each drug test the agency give employees a written statement of whether the test is mandatory or voluntary, the reasons for ordering drug testing, how the employees were selected for the test, the frequency or intervals of testing, the consequences of a positive result or refusal to cooperate, the opportunity available for retesting, the location and availability of drug counseling services, and the right to union representation. Advance notice of drug testing could interfere with the conduct and result of the test; the proposals were nonnegotiable because they interfered with the right of the agency to determine its internal security practices. See NTEU and Dept. of Energy, 41 FLRA 1241, 1256-58 (1991) (Provisions 3, 7, 12, 13, and 14). [Refer in this chapter to "Union Representation; Observer; Lab Inspections."]

A longer period of notice of the initiation of a drug testing program, e.g., 60 days, would be negotiable if it did not allow employees to evade the purpose of the tests. See AFGE Local 446 and Dept. of Interior, Nat'l Park Serv., Blue Ridge Parkway, 43 FLRA 836, 864 (1991) (Proposal 4, nonnegotiable: requiring specific notice of drug testing program coupled with preclusion of testing until 30 days after that notice "clearly is incompatible with the Agency's right to conduct its drug testing program and could result in the frustration of the purposes of drug testing"; also nonnegotiable, section of Proposal 4 requiring 24 hours' advance notice of test); AFGE, Dept. of Educ. Council of AFGE Locals and Dept. of Educ., 38 FLRA 1068, 1084-91 (1990) ("Proposal 4 would have the effect of putting employees on notice that they were to be tested. However, because management would have the discretion under the proposal to provide that information to employees at the time that it directed them to report for the test, the point at which employees are on notice that they are subject to a drug test would be the same as the point at which they are directed to report for the test."), enforcement denied as to other proposals, Dept. of Interior, Minerals Mgmt. Serv. v. FLRA, 969 F.2d 1158, 1161-63 (D.C. Cir. 1992). As to a notice to the union of the requirement that an employee be tested, FLRA discerned no impediment to a proposal for a copy of a "Notice of Test" to be given to the union. NTEU and Dept. of Energy, 41 FLRA 1241, 1248-55 (1991). [Refer in this chapter to "Explanation of Reasons for Testing."]
