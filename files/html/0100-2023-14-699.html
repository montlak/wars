1. Local Agreements

DOJ, Bureau of Prisons, FCC Lompac and AFGE Local 3048, 66 FLRA 978, 980-81(2012), noted that:

Under Authority precedent, it is well established that there is no statutory obligation to bargain below the level of recognition. See, e.g., U.S. FDA Admin., Ne. & Mid-Atl. Regions, 53 FLRA 1269, 1274 (1998) (FDA); U.S. Dep't of the Air Force, Ogden Air Logistics Ctr., Hill Air Force Base, Utah, 39 FLRA 1409, 1417-18 (1991) (Hill AFB). But the Authority also has held that parties at the national level may "authorize local components to bargain supplemental and other agreements over particular subjects or in particular circumstances." FDA, 53 FLRA at 1274; see also Hill AFB, 39 FLRA at 1417.

See DOJ, Bureau of Prisons, MDC Brooklyn and AFGE Local 3148, 69 FLRA 44, 46 (2015) ("the Authority has also acknowledged that '[p]arties to a national, consolidated bargaining unit may, and often do, authorize local components to bargain supplemental and other agreements over particular subjects or in particular circumstances.'" (citing FDA and AFGE Council 242, 53 FLRA 1269, 1273-80 (1998), reconsideration denied, 54 FLRA 630 (1998)).).

The union may propose that the agency negotiate supplemental local agreements. The duty to bargain at the level of exclusive recognition extends to proposals authorizing negotiation of supplemental agreements at a sublevel. The issue of negotiability of those proposals is deferred until the time they are made. AFGE Int'l Council of Marshals Serv. Locals and DOJ, Marshals Serv., 11 FLRA 672, 678-79 (1983) (Proposal 5). The basic bargaining obligation is at the level of exclusive recognition. Local negotiation below the level of recognition is a permissive subject of bargaining. NATCA and Dept. of Transp., FAA, 62 FLRA 174, 182 (2007).
