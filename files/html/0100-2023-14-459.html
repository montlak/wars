broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 15: Pay, Premiums, and Allowances, Section I: Overtime Rates; Callback Pay

I. OVERTIME RATES; CALLBACK PAY

Overtime rates for wage grade employees were not negotiable. Minimum callback times were nonnegotiable. Those matters were covered by statute and not within the statutory definition of conditions of employment. IBEW Local 2080 and Army Corps of Eng'rs, Nashville, 10 FLRA 222, 223, 226-27 (1982); see Int'l Bhd. of Police Officers and GSA, Region 2, 47 FLRA 397, 399-400 (1993) (Proposal 2, that details not be compensated at overtime rates but instead be paid at rate of $20 per hour, was nonnegotiable because overtime rates are set by statute, 5 USC 5541-5550a, governing payment of premium pay for overtime work). Because premium pay rates for prevailing rate employees are set by 5 USC 5544, proposals for special rates for overtime work were nonnegotiable, outside the statutory definition of conditions of employment. IBEW Local 570 and Dept. of Army, Yuma Proving Ground, 14 FLRA 432, 434-36 (1984). There was no objection to a proposal that did no more than incorporate into the contract agency regulations establishing overtime rates. No specific cost increase was established by the agency to support an argument that the proposal interfered with the agency budget. AFGE Local 1770 and Dept. of Army, Ft. Bragg Dependent Sch., 28 FLRA 493, 497-505 (1987) (Provision 2).

5 USC 5542(b)(1) establishes an exception to the general rule that only overtime worked can be paid at overtime rates. The statute allows for a minimum of two hours callback overtime work. Proposals expanding on that entitlement are not negotiable if they provide for general overtime to be paid for minimum hours or for callback overtime exceeding two hours. AFGE Local 2612 and Dept. of Air Force, Hq., 416th Combat Support Group, Griffiss AFB, 19 FLRA 1012, 1012-14 (1985); see AFGE Local 3157 and USDA, Fed. Grain Inspection Serv., 44 FLRA 1570, 1592-94 (1992) (Proposal 4 was nonnegotiable under 5 USC 5542(b)(1), requiring minimum of four hours of pay for General Schedule employees called back to perform overtime work); FOP Lodge 1F and VAMC, Providence, R.I., 32 FLRA 944, 948-49 (1988) (Proposal 2). A proposal for four hours of minimum pay for a callback was contrary to the two-hour limit on minimum payment under 5 USC 5542(b)(1) for General Schedule employees and 5 CFR 532.503(c) for wage grade employees. NFFE Local 1380 and Dept. of Navy, Naval Coastal Sys. Ctr., Panama City, 11 FLRA 129, 129-30 (1983). Overtime can only be paid for the actual performance of work, so that employees must place themselves at their regular places of work and return to their homes at their own expense. A proposal was not negotiable that would have started overtime at the time an employee was called back to his place of employment and reimbursed the employee for travel between residence and agency incident to the callback overtime. IBEW Local 570 and Dept. of Army, Yuma Proving Ground, 14 FLRA 432, 436-37 (1984).
