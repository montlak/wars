3. Job Restructuring; Qualification Waiver; Training

In AFGE and Air Force Logistics Command, Wright-Patterson AFB, 2 FLRA 604, 620-22 (1980), one proposal required that procedures for skills utilization include the redesign of jobs if feasible. Although Proposal X, to redesign a job in a particular manner, conflicted with management's right to assign work by prescribing the work to be assigned, a general operating principle embodied in the proposal--identifying job redesign as one alternative procedure that may be followed when feasible--did not infringe work assignment. A specific proposal to require "overhires" to permit disabled or ill employees to use leave without undue impact on the agency was not negotiable. 5 USC 7106(b)(1) provides that the number of employees may be negotiated only at the election of the agency. But under AFGE Local 1923 and DHHS, HCFA, Baltimore, 44 FLRA 1405, 1522 (1992), Proposal 32 was negotiable, that "[r]easonable accommodations are to be considered as exceptions to [its] general fiscal restrictions." The provision did not interfere with the right of the agency to determine its budget. The proposal did not inject the union into the budget process. The agency did not show whether the costs resulting from implementation of the proposal would be offset by compensating benefits.

FLRA commented on proposals, part of an EEO article, concerning redesign of jobs, AFGE Local 2317 and Marine Corps Logistics Base, Nonappropriated Fund Instrumentality, Albany, 29 FLRA 1587, 1607-08 (1987):

As to job redesign, the Authority has held that a proposal which requires management to redesign jobs to create promotion opportunities interferes with the rights to determine organization and to assign work. American Federation of Government Employees, AFL-CIO Local 32 and Office of Personnel Management, 17 FLRA 790 (1985) (Proposal 4). On the other hand, the Authority found that a proposal which merely identified job redesign as one alternative which could be followed for utilizing the present skills of employees was negotiable. In so finding, the Authority noted that a proposal to redesign a position or job in a particular manner would conflict with the right to assign work. American Federation of Government Employees and Air Force Logistics Command, Wright Patterson Air Force Base, 2 FLRA 604, 620-21 (1980), aff'd, Department of Defense v. FLRA, 659 F.2d 1140 (D.C. Cir. 1981), cert. denied, 455 U.S. 945 (1982).

A proposal was outside the duty to bargain that required management to provide work opportunities commensurate with the skills and abilities of employees, the purpose being to correct underutilization of minorities and women. The proposal could result in an arbitrator substituting her judgment for that of management as to the assignment of duties. AFGE Local 2263 and Dept. of Air Force, Hq., 1606th Air Base Wing (MAC), Kirtland AFB, 15 FLRA 580, 582 (1984) (Proposal 3). [Refer later in this chapter to "Set-Aside of Jobs."]
