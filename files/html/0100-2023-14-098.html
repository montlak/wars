broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 04: Job Content; Work Assignment, Section D: Work Preservation, Subsection 01: Unit Work

1. Unit Work

Nonnegotiable was a prohibition against work being assigned outside the unit. IBEW Local 570 and Dept. of Army, Yuma Proving Ground, 14 FLRA 432, 434 (1984); cf. AFGE Local 1985 and VAMC Dublin, 55 FLRA 1145, 1150-52 (1999) (Proposal 3) (nonnegotiable: proposal to increase staffing of unit employees and decrease staffing of positions in another unit). A provision was not bargainable that the employer recognized that performance of duties normally assigned to unit members should be performed by properly qualified bargaining unit members. It directly interfered with work assignment. A proposal was nonnegotiable that precluded work from being assigned to supervisors that was normally assigned to unit employees. The proposal interfered with work assignment. It did not relate to working conditions, since it concerned supervisors. NAATS and Dept. of Transp., FAA, 6 FLRA 588, 594-95 (1981) (Proposal V). A prohibition against military personnel or supervisors doing bargaining unit work was not bargainable. IAM Local 2424 and Dept. of Army, Aberdeen Proving Ground, 8 FLRA 679, 679-80 (1982). A limitation on the right of management to assign work to supervisors or foremen was not to be an appropriate arrangement when considered under the excessive interference test. Int'l Plate Printers Local 2 and Dept. of Treasury, Bureau of Engraving and Printing, 25 FLRA 113, 123 (1987).

A proposal that prevented management from assigning duties to an employee, e.g., a proposal that precluded management from requiring employees to perform unit work when they substitute for supervisory personnel, was a violation of the right to assign work. NLRB Union, Local 21 and NLRB, 15 FLRA 798, 798-99 (1984). A union could not compel negotiations of a provision that required management to agree that volunteers and "stay-in-school" workers not be assigned with the purpose of replacing regular unit employees. AFGE Nat'l Council of SSA Field Operations Locals and SSA, 25 FLRA 622, 623-24 (1987). Nonnegotiable was a prohibition against assignment of duties performed by employees in one classification series being performed by employees in any other classification series. Nat'l Ass'n of Gov't Inspectors and Quality Assurance Personnel, Unit 2 and Naval Air Eng'r Ctr., Lakehurst, N.J., 8 FLRA 144, 145-46 (1982). Precluding assignment of collateral duty positions, e.g., property officer or motor pool officer, to nonunit employees was inconsistent with the right to assign work, AFGE Int'l Council of Marshals Serv. Locals and Marshals Serv., 15 FLRA 333, 336 (1984), as was a requirement that unit employees continue to perform the scope of work they have traditionally performed in the past. IBEW Local 121 and GPO, 8 FLRA 188 (1982).

A proposal was negotiable that the employer make reasonable efforts to ensure that work normally assigned to unit members was performed by properly qualified unit members. It established a general, nonquantitative contractual standard allowing the agency's exercise of reserved authority to assign work that could be evaluated in a subsequent grievance. NAATS and Dept. of Transp., FAA, 6 FLRA 588, 597-98 (1981) (Proposal VII).
