broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 19: Critical Elements and Performance Standards, Section G: Coverage of Grievance Procedure; Grievance Review Standard, Subsection 01: Cases Before 1987

1. Cases Before 1987

For years FLRA ruled nonnegotiable proposals that a grievance procedure include challenges to critical elements and performance standards. FLRA determined that because § 7106 provides that "nothing" in the Statute shall "affect the authority" of an agency to exercise its rights, no grievance procedure could be negotiated under § 7121 denying the authority of an agency to exercise its statutory rights under § 7106. Allowing the grievance procedure to be used to challenge critical elements and performance standards permitted the procedure to extend to the exercise by the agency of its rights to direct employees and assign work under § 7106(a)(2)(A) and (B), subjecting the agency's exercise of these rights to arbitral review and to the possibility of an arbitrator substituting his judgment for that of the agency as to those statutory rights. See, e.g., AFGE, Nat'l Council of CSA Locals and Community Servs. Admin., 5 FLRA 748, 748 (1981) (Proposal 4) (proposal nonnegotiable: "In any case in which there is disagreement over a performance standard, the standard proposed by management will be used, subject to challenge through the grievance procedure.").

Although a grievance procedure could not permit a direct challenge to a critical element or performance standard, the employee could challenge application of the element and standard through the grievance procedure. It was permissible for a union to request negotiations as to a proposed grievance article that permitted the employee to challenge a performance appraisal. AFGE Local 3804 and FDIC, Chicago Region, 7 FLRA 217, 230-32 (1981); see UPTO and Dept. of Army, Corps of Eng'rs, Walla Walla, 44 FLRA 1145, 1152 (1992) (section of Proposal 2 was negotiable that sought an agreement that agency apply standards "fairly, equitably, objectively, and uniformly for like duties in like circumstances"). A proposal was negotiable that required any and all standards used to evaluate performance to be "fair and equitable" and be "implemented and administered in a way that every employee can reasonably be expected to obtain the standard." AFSCME Local 2477 and Library of Congress, 17 FLRA 786, 786-88 (1985). A grievance raised under a provision involving application of a standard did not allow the arbitrator to preclude management from determining the content of elements and standards and did not result in the setting of new elements and standards. Bureau of Engraving & Printing, Dept. of Treasury and Washington Plate Printers Union, Local No. 2, 20 FLRA 380, 382 (1985). A negotiable procedure called on management to agree that disagreements concerning elements and standards be brought to the attention of the reviewing official who could exercise authority to make changes after discussion with the rating official. NTEU and USDA, Food and Nutrition Serv., 25 FLRA 1067, 1071, 1073 (1987) (Proposal 3). FLRA reasoned that the proposal was intended to operate as a mechanism for review within the agency, and the Authority specifically noted that the union stated the proposal was not intended to subject the substance of elements or standards to arbitral review.

The universe of pre-AFGE Local 2221 decisions relating to negotiability of proposals and application of contract provisions concerning critical elements and performance standards was summarized in Bureau of Prisons, DOJ and AFGE Local 148, 21 FLRA 74, 76-79 (1986), involving review by an arbitrator of a performance appraisal: an arbitrator could resolve a grievance by an employee who believed that he or she had been adversely affected by management's application of performance standards in a performance appraisal to that employee. See AFGE Local 1799 and Dept. of Army, Aberdeen Proving Ground, 26 FLRA 926, 932 (1987) (Proposal 3) ("[G]eneral, nonquantitative requirements by which the application of performance standards may subsequently be evaluated in a grievance, by an employee who is adversely affected, do not directly interfere with management's right under section 7106(a) to establish performance standards."). A proposal to permit arbitration of unacceptable performance actions was unobjectionable. AFGE Local 1968 and Dept. of Transp., St. Lawrence Seaway Dev. Corp., Massena, 5 FLRA 70, 77-82 (1981), aff'd, AFGE Local 1968 v. FLRA, 691 F.2d 565, 569-72 (D.C. Cir. 1982) (court decisions noted earlier in this chapter, "Relating Standards to Position Description and Assigned Duties.").
