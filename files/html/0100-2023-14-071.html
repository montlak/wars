4. Personnel Who May Be Assigned Work

The union could not insist on negotiation of a provision that management not assign work to employees other than those familiar with the requirements and qualified for the class of work to be performed. IBEW Local 570 and Dept. of Army, Yuma Proving Ground, 14 FLRA 432, 432 (1984); see NAGE, SEIU and Conn., Adjutant Gen. Office, 27 FLRA 801, 803 (1987) (nonnegotiable proposal precluded assignment of menial or lower-grade duties, or duties employees were not technically qualified to perform). Negotiations could not be compelled on a provision that had the effect of dictating to the agency the specific employees to be assigned to process various types of cases. AFGE Local 1501 and DHHS, SSA, Baltimore, Md., 24 FLRA 470, 474 (1986). Proposals involving the assignment of a specific type of work to a specific agency office, and there to specific classifications of employees, infringed the right of to assign work; implicit in the FLRA decision was a determination that the proposals were negotiable at the election of the agency. AFGE Local 1748 and Dept. of Labor, Kansas City, 73 FLRA 233 (2022). A proposal was negotiable that every effort be made to insure uniform assignment of personnel to inspectional activities and tours of duty. It did not limit management's discretion to set required qualifications and skills or limit the judgment of management in determining if a particular employee met those qualifications. NTEU Chap. 153 and Dept. of Treasury, Customs Serv., 21 FLRA 1116, 1120 (1986) (Proposal 3).

Most proposals have the potential to cause the agency to incur greater expense through the assignment of personnel to implement the proposal. Carried to extremes, the prohibition against proposals directing the assignment of work would lead to no negotiations at all. FLRA bans proposals that call for certain personnel to perform tasks, but as long as a task is generally described and management is left with the discretion of who is going to perform that task and how precisely it is to be done, the proposal is not objectionable. See AFGE Local 1759 and DOD, Dept. of Army, Hq., Ft. McPherson, 29 FLRA 261, 261-62 (1987) (proposal to create system for safekeeping of personal belongings of commissary employees).
