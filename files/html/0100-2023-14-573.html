broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 18: Reductions in Force, Section B: Placement; Filling of Vacancies During RIF; Seniority

B. PLACEMENT; FILLING OF VACANCIES DURING RIF; SENIORITY

As proposals involving placement requirements during RIFs move from the general to the specific, the chances of their negotiability diminishes. AFGE Local 2024 and Dept. of Navy, Portsmouth Naval Shipyard, 37 FLRA 249, 253-54 (1990) (Proposal 3), provides an idea of the breadth of proposals, somewhat limited by qualifying language in the union's submission, that are considered negotiable as appropriate arrangements for RIFs. FLRA approved for negotiation the proposal that:

The Employer shall minimize displacement actions incurred by a reduction in force to the extent possible through reassignment, restricting the filling of vacancies for which employees affected by the reduction in force may qualify by outside hiring or promotion, waiving qualifications and standards for employees affected by the reduction in force and other actions that may be taken to retain career employees.

FLRA explained in AFGE Local 2024, 37 FLRA at 256-58:

...The Authority has found that proposals providing for management to fill vacancies with employees who have lost, or are threatened with the loss of, their jobs due to a RIF or technological changes constituted appropriate arrangements where the proposals applied only after management decided to fill a position and did not require the agency to assign an unqualified employee....

...

In assessing the impact of Proposal 3 on management's rights we must construe the phrase "to the extent possible." As we cannot ascertain the meaning of that phrase from the plain wording of the proposal, we refer for explanation to the Union's detailed statement as to what the words are intended to mean. The Union states that it does not intend the proposal to be applied rigidly and, therefore, the phrase...is used in recognition of the fact that the actions to be taken by management described in the proposal would not always be reasonable or feasible. As to the precise meaning of the phrase, the Union states that it is intended to mean "possible under the circumstances of accomplishing the overall purpose of the reduction in force." The Union concludes...that the phrase is intended to connote that the proposal would apply only if management decided to fill a position.

The Union states, further, that the phrase is intended to reflect an understanding that the proposal would only apply subject to "normal considerations" such as determination regarding the capabilities of employees and the qualifications required to perform particular jobs or the need of management to fill a job that could not be filled by one of the affected employees. Insofar as the proposal would require "waiving qualifications and standards," the Union states that the proposal contemplates "case-by-case examination to determine...if an employee's general abilities allow the waiving of qualifications so that the employee could be retrained in a short period of time." We find that this comprehensive statement of the Union's intent regarding the meaning of the phrase "to the extent possible" is consistent with the more generally worded proposal and we adopt if for purposes of this decision....

Based on the foregoing, we construe Proposal 3 to apply only after management decides to fill a vacant position. Insofar as it would require a waiver of qualifications such a waiver would be of only temporary duration and only for employees whose abilities allowed them to become at least minimally qualified through training in a short period.... Proposal 3 encompasses an overriding intent to ensure that the purpose of a RIF can be accomplished and, consequently, for management to retain the discretion to exercise its rights as necessary to attain that end. Only if management were to decide to fill a particular vacant position would it be necessary to address the actions specified in the proposal to mitigate the adverse impact on employees by filling the position from among those affected by the RIF. The proposal then would apply to filling the position, subject to normal management considerations such as making qualifications determinations or having a need to fill a job from outside the group of employees affected by the RIF.

...Proposal 3, by requiring management to attempt to minimize displacement actions in a RIF to the extent possible by filling vacant positions, which it decides to fill, from among qualified employees, or those who through training could quickly become qualified...does not excessively interfere with management's rights and is an appropriate arrangement....

Although FLRA consistently determines that proposals requiring an agency to fill vacancies interfere with management rights under § 7106(a), AFGE Local 3354 and USDA, Farm Servs. Agency, 54 FLRA 807, 812 (1998), a proposal calling on management to fill vacancies to the maximum extent possible during a RIF, intended as hortatory, not mandatory, was negotiable. It did not limit management from assigning work or making selections from appropriate sources of candidates. AFGE Int'l Council of Marshals Serv. Locals and Marshals Serv., 15 FLRA 333, 334-35 (1984) (Proposal 2); see AFGE Council 257 and Dept. of Navy, Naval Aviation Depot, Pensacola, 30 FLRA 1144 (1988) (no violation of late FPM Chapter 335, distinguishing NFFE Local 29 and Army Corps of Eng'rs, Kansas City Dist., 21 FLRA 630 (1986)). AFGE Local 1770 and Dept. of Army, Ft. Bragg Dependent Sch., 28 FLRA 493, 519-20 (1987) (Provision 7, Section 4), declared negotiable a proposal that, in the event of a RIF, existing vacancies be used to the maximum extent feasible to place in continuing positions employees who otherwise would be separated from employment.

A proposal was negotiable, as an arrangement for the exercise of a management right that in the event of a RIF existing vacancies be used to the maximum extent possible to place employees and that RIFs be carried out in strict compliance with applicable laws and regulations. Management fully retained its statutory discretion whether to use existing vacancies beyond the assignment rights. AFGE Local 1692 and Dept. of Air Force, Mather AFB, 8 FLRA 194, 195 (1982) (Provision 2); see Bremerton Metal Trades Council and Naval Supply Ctr. Puget Sound, 32 FLRA 643, 657 (1988) (same); Overseas Educ. Ass'n and DODDS, 29 FLRA 734 (1987); NTEU and DHHS, Region X, 25 FLRA 1041, 1047 (1987) (negotiable: proposal that agency make diligent efforts to provide qualified employees with lateral reassignment to vacancies consistent with agency needs; although it interfered with management's discretion to assign employees, it was an appropriate arrangement for adversely affected employees and did not totally abrogate management's discretion). But see Bremerton Metal Trades Council and Naval Supply Ctr. Puget Sound, 32 FLRA 643, 654 (1988) (proposal was nonnegotiable that was construed to require management to fill all vacancies to alleviate disruption caused by RIF). Another proposal was negotiable to the extent it called on the agency to search for appropriate vacancies during a RIF and consider qualified employees. NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, Fin. Mgmt. Serv., 29 FLRA 422, 430 (1987). A proposal was negotiable providing that vacancies not be filled from outside the activity, without just cause, if employees facing separation were qualified and available for the vacancies. The proposal was interpreted by the union to require the agency to consider but not necessarily select employees. The phrase "good cause" included management's decision not to fill or to abolish the vacant position or to select a better-qualified candidate from an outside source. NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, Bureau of Gov't Fin. Operations, 18 FLRA 787, 788 (1985) (relying on AFGE Local 2782 and Dept. of Commerce, Bureau of Census, 6 FLRA 314 (1981)).

The matter of vacancies was explored in NFFE Local 797 and Dept. of Navy, 29 FLRA 333, 338-41 (1987) (Provision 4), involving a proposal reducing the impact of RIFs by requiring the employer to: adjust the workforce through reassignment or transfer of unit employees to vacancies within their qualifications; avoid filling vacant positions under recruitment until a decision is made whether an adversely affected employee can be placed in the position under RIF procedures; and fill trainee and development positions under recruitment at the target level through RIF regulations. The proposal was negotiable:

Provision 4 requires the Agency to reassign or transfer employees targeted for a RIF to available vacancies prior to separating them. The Agency has not substantiated its claim that Provision 4 interferes with its right under section 7106 to assign employees. However, the Authority has found that proposals regarding placement of employees in vacant positions in RIF situations relate to an agency's right...to make selection for appointments.... By requiring placement of employees in vacant positions prior to separating them in a RIF, Provision 4 directly interferes with management's right to make selections for appointments.

It is clear that a RIF has a significant adverse impact on affected employees.... The Union intends this provision to be an appropriate arrangement within the meaning of Section 7106(b)(3) of the Statute for employees adversely affected by the Agency's decision to conduct a RIF.

We find that Provision 4 is intended as an arrangement for employees who would otherwise be separated from their positions because of a RIF. Although Provision 4 does not expressly reserve to management the right to fill a position, it is clear from the record that the Union intended this provision to take effect after management determined to fill a vacancy.... We interpret Provision 4, consistent with the Union's stated intent, as leaving the Agency free to decide to fill or not to fill a vacant position and to determine the qualifications necessary to perform the work of the position.

Provision 4 is distinguishable from two recent Authority decisions. In National Federation of Federal Employees Local 1450 and U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development, 23 FLRA No. 1 (1986), the provision found nonnegotiable required the Agency to fill vacancies in a RIF situation only with affected employees who met minimum X-118 standards. The Authority held that the provision violated management's right to choose from among candidates from "any appropriate source" because it precluded the Agency from determining the requisite qualifications for vacant positions beyond the minimum requirements of the X-118 standards.... In National Association of Government Employees Local R14-87 and Department of the Army, Kansas Army National Guard, 21 FLRA No. 105 (1986), the proposal found nonnegotiable required the Agency to offer positions which might become available in their original location to employees who were transferred, without personal cause, as a result of a RIF to a different duty station. The Authority held the proposal violated management's rights to assign employees...and to make selections for vacancies...because it precluded the Agency from deciding to fill or not to fill a position and from determining the qualifications necessary to perform the work of the position. Slip op. at 4.

As to whether the arrangement proposed in Provision 4 is appropriate...we find that the portion of the provision which would prohibit the Agency from filling vacancies by recruitment until a decision is made whether an employee who would otherwise be separated can be placed in the position under RIF procedures is similar to Provision 32 determined to be an appropriate arrangement in International Plate Printers Die Stampers and Engravers Union of North America, Local 2 and Dept. of Treasury, Bureau of Engraving and Printing, Washington, D.C., 25 FLRA No. 9 (1987). We found Provision 32 in that case, which similarly required the Agency to offer employees affected by a RIF vacant positions which the agency decided to fill, did not excessively interfere with the Agency's rights...to determine whether to fill a position, to determine whether an employee is qualified for that position or to fill positions from any appropriate source.

Provision 4 in this case provides that vacant positions are to be filled by qualified employees. The Agency is left with discretion to determine whether employees who would otherwise be separated are qualified to fill vacant positions and to hire from outside the Agency if none of the affected employees are qualified.... Moreover, based on the record, we interpret Provision 4 as being applicable only prior to the actual effectuation of the RIF.... Thus, Requirement 4 of subchapter 1-4, chapter 335 of the Federal Personnel Manual does not apply.... Accordingly, we find that Provision 4 does not excessively interfere with the agency's rights and is an appropriate arrangement....

A proposal that during a RIF the employer assign employees to vacant positions on the basis of their preference rather than regulatory assignment rights was nonnegotiable. It violated the agency's right to assign employees under § 7106(a)(2)(A). The agency was required to forego its discretion as to the selection of the employees to be assigned to those positions. AFGE and Air Force Logistics Command, Wright-Patterson AFB, 2 FLRA 604, 626-27 (1980) (Proposal XIII) (refer to earlier discussion about this decision concerning appointment of EEO counselors). Proposals requiring an agency to honor seniority or preference in assignments to vacancies during displacement following abolition of positions were nonnegotiable, for they interfered with management's right to assign employees under § 7106(a)(2)(A). AFGE Council 214 and Dept. of Air Force, Hq., Air Force Logistics Command, Wright-Patterson AFB, 8 FLRA 425, 426 (1982) (Proposal 1). Proposals were nonnegotiable that required vacancies on various shifts to be filled on the basis of seniority during a RIF. The proposals were contrary to OPM regulations defining what factors would be considered as to RIF assignment rights. Laborers' Int'l Union Local 1267 and DLA, Defense Depot Tracy, 14 FLRA 686, 692 (1984) (Proposal 6). Negotiations could not be required on a proposal requiring management to fill vacancies during a RIF with unit members who were qualified or could be quickly trained for those vacancies. The proposal precluded management from selecting from other appropriate sources of candidates. AFGE Local 987 and Hq., Warner Robins Air Force Logistics Command, Robins AFB, 8 FLRA 667, 673 (1982) (Proposal IV). Management need not consider a limitation on its discretion to fill bargaining unit positions during a RIF with only those employees who occupied unit positions prior to placement or individuals selected through the contract's merit promotion provisions. AFSCME Local 2910 and Library of Congress, 9 FLRA 816 (1982). A union could not compel negotiations on who would make decisions concerning qualifications and eligibility of employees for various positions during a RIF. CREA and Library of Congress, Congressional Research Serv., 25 FLRA 306, 323-24 (1987) (Proposal 9).
