C. FLRA PROCEEDINGS

Under 5 USC 7131(c), "[e]xcept as provided in subsection (a) of this section, the Authority shall determine whether any employee participating for, or on behalf of, a labor organization in any phase of proceedings before the Authority shall be authorized official time for such purposes during the time the employee otherwise would be in duty status." 5 CFR 2429.13 and 2429.14 (2022) require that employees be provided official time and per diem for time deemed necessary by the Authority General Counsel or other Authority agents in preparation for Authority proceedings. The regulation states, 5 CFR 2429.13 (2022):

Official time for witnesses.

If the participation of any employee in any phase of any proceeding before the Authority, including the investigation of unfair labor practice charges and representation petitions and the participation in hearings and representation elections, is deemed necessary by the Authority, the General Counsel, any Administrative Law Judge, Regional Director, Hearing Officer, or other agent of the Authority designated by the Authority, the employee shall be granted official time for such participation, including necessary travel time, as occurs during the employee's regular work hours and when the employee would otherwise be in a work or paid leave status.

5 CFR 2429.14 (2022) provides:

Witness fees.

(a) Witnesses, whether appearing voluntarily or pursuant to a subpoena, shall be paid the fee and mileage allowances which are paid subpoenaed witnesses in the courts of the United States. However, any witness who is employed by the Federal Government shall not be entitled to receive witness fees.

(b) Witness fees, as appropriate, as well as transportation and per diem expenses for a witness shall be paid by the party that calls the witness to testify.

Field attorneys at FLRA regional offices determine when it is necessary for employees to participate in prehearing preparation on official time. Dept. of Treasury, BATF and NTEU Chap. 88, 10 FLRA 10, 12 (1982). Once it is determined that an employee's presence is required in FLRA proceedings, the employee is to be placed on official time. There is no test of reasonableness regarding the determination that the employee's presence is needed. DHHS, SSA, Great Lakes Program Serv. Ctr. and AFGE Local 1395, 10 FLRA 510, 510-11 (1982). As to official time for hearing preparations with FLRA agents, the entitlement is absolute. Dept. of Treasury, BATF and NTEU Chap. 88, 13 FLRA 558, 559 (1983). Once the participation of an employee is deemed necessary by FLRA in any phase of any proceeding before it, there is no discretion for the employing agency to determine whether an employee should be on official time. The agency may not interfere by requiring the employee to wear a military work uniform or attend the hearing in an annual leave status. Arizona Air Nat'l Guard and AFGE Local 2924, 21 FLRA 715, 716 (1986).

An employee must be granted official time to appear as a witness in a ULP case even though he is employed in a unit different than the respondent in the case. It is up to the respondent to work out, through internal agreement with the employer of a witness, any arrangements for reimbursement of the time and costs attendant to his appearance. Dept. of Air Force, Sacramento Air Logistics Ctr., McClellan AFB and Callaway, 26 FLRA 674, 676-77 (1987) (relying on Air Force Logistics Command, McClellan AFB and Solorio, 24 FLRA 274, 275-76 (1986)); see Dept. of Air Force, Hq. Air Force Logistics Command, Wright-Patterson AFB and AFGE Council 214, 24 FLRA 187, 189 (1986) ("once Palacio's participation was deemed necessary by an agent of the Authority, Respondent was obligated to comply or to seek review in that proceeding as provided in the Authority's Regulations; no collateral attack is permitted"). The coverage of § 7131(c) and the regulation, 5 CFR 2429.13 (2022), are not the same. 7th Infantry Div., Ft. Ord and Johnson, 47 FLRA 864, 871-72 (1993), concluded that § 7131 did not require an agency to grant official time to a charging party to appear at a ULP hearing against a union when that individual could not be considered to have "participated for, or on behalf of, a labor organization." Footnote 9 of the Ft. Ord opinion stated that FLRA was not deciding whether § 2429.13 of its regulation might be a basis for directing official time to the charging party.

Many decisions in this section were issued before E.O. 13,837 in May 2018, curtailing official time and allowable expenses for union representatives.
