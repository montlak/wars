a. Shoplifting

Removal was mitigated to a 30-day suspension for a mailhandler technician who, while off duty, shoplifted an item worth $3.59, involving criminal proceedings leading to a fine and six months of unsupervised probation. Mallery v. USPS, 41 MSPR 288, 293 (1989) (de minimis value was a mitigating factor, along with a long and otherwise good service record, and the fact that the item taken did not come into the appellant's possession through his employment); see Stuhlmacher v. USPS, 89 MSPR 272, 282-83 ¶¶ 28-29 (2001) (shoplifting-related offense was mitigated when the offense occurred off duty and did not involve government property); Austin v. DOJ, 11 MSPR 255, 261, 10 MSPB 221 (1982) (removal of a DEA special agent for shoplifting). In Mallery, 41 MSPR at 293, the Board declined to infer lack of trustworthiness of the employee: "While the deciding official's testimony indicates that he believed that someone who took an item of minimal value from a store could not be trusted because many Postal Service losses resulted from small thefts in the mail, no evidence was presented to link the appellant to such work-related thefts." Another incident of off-duty shoplifting that led to removal was mitigated to a 90-day suspension. The appellant had a long career, satisfactory performance ratings, and potential for rehabilitation. The shoplifting was off duty and the items she took did not come into possession as a result of her agency position. Kelly v. DHHS, 46 MSPR 358, 363 (1990).

An offense akin to shoplifting resulted in mitigation of removal of a postmaster to demotion to a nonmanagerial position when there was a long service record, evidence of depression, and factors indicating rehabilitative potential. See Stuhlmacher v. USPS, 89 MSPR 272, 282-83 ¶¶ 27-31 (2001) (summarizing similar cases leading to the mitigation of penalties imposed because of shoplifting). But see Fields v. USDA, 28 MSPR 172, 174 (1985) (repeated incidents of shoplifting, some conducted while in travel status on agency business, warranted removal).
