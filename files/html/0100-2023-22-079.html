5. Performance-Based Appeal

In an unacceptable performance action, an employee who demonstrated that errors charged to her were not made, and that her performance was satisfactory, was substantially innocent of the charges. She received a fee award. Young v. Dept. of Air Force, 29 MSPR 589, 590-91 (1986) (appellant was substantially innocent of the charge that she failed to meet the performance requirements for her position); McBride v. USDA, 3 MSPR 495, 497, 4 MSPB 17 (1980) (fees awarded when appellant was substantially innocent of the performance-based charges brought by the agency in a Chapter 43 case). In another performance case, the agency lost the case by not presenting first-hand knowledge of the first-level supervisor. Counsel fees were warranted under Yorkshire. The agency did not establish even substantial evidence to bolster its case. Akin v. Dept. of Army, 27 MSPR 61, 63-64 (1985). Fees were warranted for an appellant whose agency failed to prove that deficiencies were related to performance standards and failed to demonstrate that one proven deficiency warranted an unacceptable rating under a critical element considered as a whole. Jones v. VA, 28 MSPR 578, 580 (1985). Fees were also granted in an (unsuccessful) action against an ALJ accused of poor performance after the agency case failed for lack of valid statistics to support its productivity charges. SSA v. Goodman, 33 MSPR 325, 331 (1987) (granting fees when the agency took an unsupported performance action against an ALJ: "'Substantial innocence' does not depend on the reasonableness of the agency action, anyhow; the proper focus is the result before the Board[.] "); SSA v. Balaban, 33 MSPR 309, 316-17 (1987) (citing SSA v. Goodman, 33 MSPR 325, 331 (1987), and 28 MSPR 120, 126 (1985), granting fees for the same reason).
