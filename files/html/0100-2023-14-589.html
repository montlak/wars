2. Repromotion, Recall by Seniority

AFGE Local 32 and OPM, 29 FLRA 380 (1987), stated FLRA would no longer follow some earlier decisions involving reemployment of individuals separated by RIF. With that caveat, a proposal was nonnegotiable that called upon the agency to give mandatory placement rights, in order of retention standing and without regard to outstanding performance ratings, to employees downgraded as a result of reclassification. NAGE Local R7-23 and Dept. of Air Force, Scott AFB, 3 FLRA 185, 186 (1980). The proposal required placement to be made on the basis of seniority in violation of the management right to assign employees under § 7106(a)(2)(A). FLRA relied on AFGE and Air Force Logistics Command, Wright-Patterson AFB, 2 FLRA 604 (1980). A proposal seeking repromotion of employees to positions at their former grades and competitive levels in accordance with seniority qualified as an appropriate arrangement but it was not negotiable because it violated the then-controlling Requirement 4 of Subch. 1-4 of Chapter 335 of the late FPM, permitting agencies to fill positions by making selections from any appropriate source. NFFE Local 29 and Army Corps of Eng'rs, Kansas City Dist., 21 FLRA 630, 633 (1986) (analysis no longer followed under AFGE Local 32). A proposal was nonnegotiable that employer recall separated temporary employees on the basis of their length of service with the agency. The proposal was nonnegotiable because it eliminated the agency's discretion to fill positions. By mandating recall of temporary employees, the proposal excessively interfered with the rights to hire and assign employees and to make selections in filling positions. The proposal interfered with the requirement of the FPM that agencies be permitted to fill positions from any appropriate source. The proposal required the agency to fill vacancies only by selecting those temporary employees who were separated from employment. AFGE Local 3186 and DHHS, Office of Social Security Field Operations, Philadelphia Region, 23 FLRA 230, 234 (1986) (Proposal 2) (analysis no longer followed under AFGE Local 32).
