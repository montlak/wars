broida flra Chapter 04: Subjects of Bargaining: Procedures for Negotiability Determinations Chapter 04: Subjects of Bargaining: Procedures for Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 04: Framework for Negotiability Appeals, Section G: Filing, Timing, and Service of Petition for Review, Subsection 02: Timeliness

2. Timeliness

The time limit is strict. The filing deadline for a negotiability appeal is statutory, 5 USC 7117:

(c)(1) Except in any case to which subsection (b) of this section applies, if an agency involved in collective bargaining with an exclusive representative alleges that the duty to bargain in good faith does not extend to any matter, the exclusive representative may appeal the allegation to the Authority in accordance with the provisions of this subsection.

(2) The exclusive representative may, on or before the 15th day after the date on which the agency first makes the allegation referred to in paragraph (1) of this subsection, institute an appeal under this subsection by--

(A) filing a petition with the Authority; and

(B) furnishing a copy of the petition to the head of the agency.

(3) On or before the 30th day after the date of the receipt by the head of the agency of the copy of the petition under paragraph (2)(B) of this subsection, the agency shall--

(A) file with the Authority a statement--

(i) withdrawing the allegation; or

(ii) setting forth in full its reasons supporting the allegation; and

(B) furnish a copy of such statement to the exclusive representative.

(4) On or before the 15th day after the date of the receipt by the exclusive representative of a copy of a statement under paragraph of this subsection, the exclusive representative shall file with the Authority its response to the statement.

The regulatory time limit for filing a petition for review of an agency allegation of nonnegotiability is set by 5 CFR 2424.21 (2022):

(a) A petition for review must be filed within fifteen (15) days after the date of service of either:

(1) An agency's written allegation that the exclusive representative's proposal is not within the duty to bargain, or

(2) An agency head's disapproval of a provision.

(b) If the agency has not served a written allegation on the exclusive representative within ten (10) days after the agency's principal bargaining representative has received a written request for such allegation, as provided in § 2424.11(a), then the petition may be filed at any time.

During the Pandemic starting in early 2020, FLRA is not accepting in-person filings. The FLRA website should be checked to determine when in-person filings will again be accepted.

The filing period under 5 USC 7117(c)(2) may not be extended or waived by FLRA under its regulation providing for extensions or waivers of time limits. 5 CFR 2429.23(d) (2022); see IAM Local 2333 and Dept. of Air Force, Hq. 2750th ABW, 46 FLRA 1208, 1208 (1993) (time limit for filing petition for review of negotiability issues may not be extended or waived by FLRA); IAM Lodge 67 and Naval Ordnance Station, Indian Head, Md., 38 FLRA 1180, 1181 (1990) (as to petitions for review of nonnegotiability allegations, parties filing actions with FLRA are responsible for being knowledgeable of statutory and regulatory filing requirements); Int'l Ass'n of Firefighters and Naval Air Station, San Diego, Cal., 37 FLRA 460, 461 (1990) (timeliness bar operates even if agency does not object to untimely negotiability appeal); AFGE Nat'l Council of Field Labor Locals and Dept. of Labor, MSHA Acad., 33 FLRA 845, 845 (1988) ("The time limit for filing a petition for review of negotiability issues is 15 days after service on the Union of the Agency's allegation that the duty to bargain in good faith does not extend to the matter proposed to be bargained. 5 C.F.R. § 2424.3. Further, the time limit may not be extended or waived by the Authority. 5 C.F.R. § 2429.23(d).").

If the agency's allegation of nonnegotiability is served by mail, the 15-day period for filing a petition for review with FLRA runs from the date that the agency allegation is deposited in the mail, not from the date the allegation is received. AFGE Local 1735 and Dept. of Navy, Pub. Works Dept., Naval Air Station Kingsville, Tex., 28 FLRA 771, 773 (1987); see AFGE Local 644 and Dept. of Labor, MSHA, 33 FLRA 825, 825 (1988) (date of service of allegation of nonnegotiability is day the matter served is deposited in U.S. mail or is delivered in person); 5 CFR 2429.27(d) (2022). The union may be required to show some evidence of the date the agency sent the allegation of nonnegotiability if the union claims the allegation was sent after it was dated. AFGE Local 2608 and DHHS, SSA, Santruch Branch Office, 33 FLRA 873, 874 (1989) (suggesting union could have demonstrated precise date of mailing by enclosing with the negotiability appeal envelope used by agency to mail allegation to union).

Under earlier regulations, Federal Express delivery was considered personal delivery, not mail service. AFGE Local 2047 and DLA, Defense Gen. Supply Ctr., Richmond, 35 FLRA 1089, 1089-90 (1990) (two methods for filing documents with FLRA are U.S. mail or, if not by mail, then in person by a party, by an agent or by delivery service (e.g., Federal Express)); see AFGE Local 3434 and NASA Marshall Space Flight Ctr., 49 FLRA 382, 384 (1994) ("Federal Express is not a component of the U.S. Postal Service. Therefore, the Agency's statement of position is deemed to have been filed on the date it was received by the Authority."); NAGE Local R7-72 and Dept. of Army, Rock Island Arsenal, 36 FLRA 271, 272 (1990) (Federal Express is delivery service not associated with U.S. Postal Service).

As to proof of mailing, FLRA states that "affidavits, standing alone, are insufficient to establish service of documents"; if certified mail is used to prove the mailing, there must be "an official U.S. Postal Service postmark on the certified receipts." NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, IRS, 42 FLRA 160, 161 (1992); cf. DOD, Stateside Dependents Sch. and Overseas Educ. Ass'n, 49 FLRA 393, 395 (1994) (regarding untimely application for review of unit determination: "The Authority consistently has held that personal affidavits, standing alone, are insufficient to establish the date of service or mailing of documents."); Dept. of Treasury, IRS and NTEU, 44 FLRA 538, 540 (1992) (denying motion for reconsideration of regional director's decision in representation case: only postmark on envelope or on certified mail receipt will rebut presumption that filing was mailed five days prior to its receipt by FLRA; although filings are often mailed to FLRA in envelopes that are not postmarked, an agency may request that a specific mailing be postmarked or obtain a postmarked certified mail receipt). DOD Stateside noted for comparison NFFE Local 405 and Army Aviation Sys. Command, 33 FLRA 604, 605-06 (1988) (as postmark on agency head disapproval was illegible, agency date stamp on certified mail receipt coupled with affidavit constituted sufficient proof of service).

Rejecting an agency's offer of proof on the timeliness of mailing, FLRA declined to consider the agency's statement of position in AFGE Local 997 and Dept. of Air Force, Maxwell AFB, 66 FLRA 499, 499-500 (2012):

The Authority granted the Agency an extension of time until December 8, 2009, to file its SOP. The Agency's SOP filed with the Authority had a regular-mail postmark of December 10, 2009, and the Authority issued an order directing the Agency to show cause why its SOP should not be dismissed as untimely. The Agency claims that it filed its SOP by certified mail on December 4, 2009, and received a dated certified-mail receipt. But the Agency is unable to locate the certified-mail receipt. Instead, it submits two statements from Agency employees declaring that the SOP was mailed by certified mail on December 4, 2009.

Under the Authority's Regulations, if a document is filed by mail, then the document's filing date is the date it is "deposited in the U.S. mail." 5 C.F.R. § 2429.27(d). When the document has a postmark, the postmark determines the filing date. 5 C.F.R. § 2429.21(b). Here, the envelope in which the Agency mailed its SOP has a regular-mail postmark of December 10, 2009. The Agency's unsubstantiated statements that it filed the SOP by certified mail on December 4, 2009, are not sufficient to establish that the Agency timely filed its SOP. Compare NTEU, 42 FLRA 160, 161 (1991) (affidavits, alone, are insufficient to establish a document's mailing date), with Haw. Fed. Emps. Metal Trades Council, 57 FLRA 450, 452 (2001) (postmarked, certified-mail receipt, together with an affidavit attesting to mailing the document on the date on certified-mail receipt, was sufficient to establish the filing date). Accordingly, we find the SOP untimely, and we do not consider it.[1] But, as it is part of the record, we consider the Agency's allegation of nonnegotiability attached to the Union's petition. See AFT, Indian Educators Fed'n, Local 4524, 63 FLRA 585, 585 (2009) (Local 4524); Marine Eng'rs' Beneficial Ass'n., Dist. No. 1-PCD, 60 FLRA 828, 829 n.2 (2005) (Dist. No. 1) (Chairman Cabaniss dissenting).[2]

[1] In accordance with Authority precedent, we consider the Union's response to the SOP because it complied with all filing requirements. See AFT, Indian Educators Fed'n, Local 4524, 63 FLRA 585, 585 (2009).

[2] In following Local 4524 and Dist No. 1, we note that the Union does not challenge these decisions and does not argue that, because the SOP was untimely, the Agency's allegation of nonnegotiability is precluded from consideration by part 2424 of the Authority's Regulations.

On the matter of postmarks, a postage meter postmark is acceptable as the date of filing if the Postal Service does not indicate that the mark is incorrect, NTEU and NRC, 47 FLRA 370, 372-73 (1993):

In this case, the Union's corrected petition was sent through the U.S. mail. A postmark of May 20, 1992, is stamped on the envelope containing the corrected petition. The Agency argues that because that date was affixed on the envelope by a Union-controlled postal meter rather than by the U.S. Postal Service, such date is not determinative of the date that the corrected petition was filed. We disagree. The fact that the postmark date was placed on the envelope by an approved postal meter establishes the date of filing in this case. We note that under the U.S. Postal Service Regulations, the date shown in a meter postmark is presumed to be the actual date of deposit, unless the U.S. Postal Service indicates that the date is incorrect. See U.S. Postal Service Domestic Mail Manual, §§ 144.532, 144.54, (Sept. 1992). See also Hunt v. U.S. Postal Service, 41 MSPR 381, 383 (1989). In this case, there is no evidence to show that the U.S. Postal Service determined that the date on the envelope containing the Union's corrected petition was incorrect. We conclude, therefore, based on the date on the envelope containing the Union's corrected petition, that the corrected petition was...timely filed....

See AFGE Local 3529 and DCAA Central Region, 58 FLRA 151, 152 (2002) (negotiability appeal: "[T]he date shown in a meter postmark is presumed to be the actual date of deposit in the U.S. mail, unless the U.S. Postal Service indicates that the date is incorrect.").
