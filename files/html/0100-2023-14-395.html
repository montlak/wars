2. Environmental Conditions; Indoor Temperature

It was within the bargaining obligation for a union to propose that the indoor temperature of the office not fall below 65 degrees and that in hot weather adequate ventilation and air conditioning be provided. IRS and NTEU Chap. 95, 9 FLRA 648 (1982), was cited. Although proposals concerning ventilation and lighting are negotiable, proposals are not negotiable that prevent management from assigning work if the conditions of the proposal are not met, and nonnegotiable are proposals requiring management to take specific actions based upon recommendations of a safety and health committee. NFFE Local 2052 and Dept. of Interior, BLM, Boise Dist. Office, 30 FLRA 797, 808-09 (1987); cf. POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 56 FLRA 69, 78-79 (2000) (proposals were nonnegotiable as arrangements that created "nonexamining time" if the workplace temperature was too high or too low). But in BLM FLRA declared nonnegotiable another proposal that building temperature be maintained between 65 to 70 degrees, 30 FLRA at 810:

[T]he Agency cites a GSA regulation, 41 C.F.R. § 101-20.116-3, requiring the maintenance of temperatures between 78 to 80 degrees F. during working hours during the seasonably hot months and the maintenance of temperatures between 65 to 68 degrees F. during the seasonably cold months. The Union's proposal clearly exceeds these guidelines. In the absence of any further Union explanation of the proposal, we conclude that the first sentence of part (d) of this proposal is nonnegotiable as it conflicts with a Government-wide regulation....

Negotiable in AFGE, SSA Gen. Comm. and SSA Baltimore, 68 FLRA 407, 409-10 (2015) (Member Pizzella dissenting in part), was Proposal 3, allowing the agency to decide when space heaters brought into the office by employees were unsafe but requiring the agency either to correct the condition necessitating the heater or provide replacement equipment. A part of a proposal establishing a procedure for review and approval or disapproval of space heaters brought into the offices was nonnegotiable because it was contrary to the level of approval required under GSA regulations. Also negotiable were Proposals 5 and 8, considered arrangements for employees adversely affected by a management crackdown on space heaters, calling for lists of approved space heaters and criteria for space heater approval. Cf. Ft. Knox Teachers Ass'n and Ft. Knox Dependents Sch., 27 FLRA 750, 753 (1987) (although hours of instruction during extremely hot days affected working conditions because of the resultant unsatisfactory behavior of students, a proposal that limited the length of the school day was nonnegotiable. It interfered with the right of the agency to assign work).
