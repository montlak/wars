a. Excepted Service

Excepted service employees are covered by RIF regulations governing competitive levels, 5 CFR 351.403(b)(1), (2) (2022), and retention order, 5 CFR 351.502 (2022). See NTEU and NRC, 31 FLRA 566 (2000) (discussion of proposal 38.2). But the excepted service employee does not have rights to assignment involving other positions outside his abolished competitive level. Whitehurst v. TVA, 43 MSPR 486, 492 (1990); see Dodd v. TVA, 770 F.2d 1038 (Fed. Cir. 1985) (immaterial that appellant held veterans preference status); Killingsworth v. DHHS, 11 MSPR 273, 276 (1982) ("Since appellant was an excepted service employee, he had no right of assignment to any position under 5 C.F.R. § 351.703. See 5 C.F.R. § 351.603."); Horne v. ICC, 5 MSPR 208, 211, 5 MSPB 259 (1981) ("excepted service employees, have no assignment rights pursuant to the provisions of 5 C.F.R. 351.703(a)").

An excepted service employee has no assignment rights other than what an agency allows under its regulations. 5 CFR 351.705(a)(3) (2022); Slatem v. DOD, 7 MSPR 9, 11, 6 MSPB 623 (1981); see Targan v. Dept. of Army (Fed. Cir. 1994 NP No. 94-3498) (agency could extend assignment rights by collective bargaining to excepted service personnel without explicit authorization in an agency regulation when that discretionary authority was extended by the FPM and by the regulation then controlling, 5 CFR 351.705(a)(3)).

The FLRA clarified the nature of assignment rights for those in the excepted service in MSPB Professional Ass'n and MSPB, 31 FLRA 258, 259-61 (1988) (even merit protectors need protection):

OPM RIF regulations provide that excepted service employees...are classified on retention registers "in a way that corresponds" to the classification of competitive service employees. See 5 CFR 351.502. In the event of a RIF resulting from the abolishment of positions held by excepted service employees, those employees in a particular competitive area compete with each other for the remaining positions exclusively on the basis of their relative retention standing.

...

Unlike the regulatory provisions applicable to competitive service employees, the regulations do not require excepted service employees to be provided with the right to "bump" other employees or the right to "retreat" to positions essentially identical to ones previously held by them. The regulations do, however, authorize agencies, at their discretion, to afford excepted service employees such "bumping and retreat" rights. 5 CFR 351.705(a)(3). The proposal here seeks to require the Agency to exercise its regulatory discretion to confer such rights on bargaining unit employees. If "bumping and retreat" rights are provided for excepted service employees, the regulations require that the rights "[s]hall be uniformly and consistently applied in any one reduction-in-force." 5 CFR 351.705(b)(2).

Although, unlike competitive service employees, excepted service employees exercise "bump" and "retreat" rights only if the agency extends those rights through administrative discretion under 5 CFR 351.705 (2022), assignment rights granted must be uniformly and consistently applied during a RIF. Cox v. TVA, 41 MSPR 686, 692 (1989). Competitive service employees have no assignment rights to excepted service positions. May v. ICC, 20 MSPR 557, 562 (1984). Employees of the Tennessee Valley Authority are in the excepted service and have no assignment rights, since those rights have not been extended to them by the employing agency. Dodd v. TVA, 770 F.2d 1038 (Fed. Cir. 1985).
