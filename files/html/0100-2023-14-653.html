3. Production of Information

It was permissible for the union negotiate that all information or objections to data derived from work studies be provided to the union within five days from issuance. AFGE Local 3804 and FDIC, Chicago Region, 7 FLRA 217, 229 (1981) (Proposal 5). AFGE Local 3272 and DHHS, SSA, Chicago, 34 FLRA 675, 675-76, 676-79 (1990), considered the negotiability of a proposal that: statistics used in employee evaluation be provided to employees within five days from receipt of those statistics by their supervisors; if the statistics are furnished after five days, the employees will be given written reasons for the delay; and statistics collected or released too late for employees to review or rebut them will not be used for employee evaluation. The first two provisions were negotiable; the third was not:

...The [first] sentence does not dictate what statistics management will use, nor how the statistics will be used. We note that applicable regulations encourage exchanges of information concerning employee performance. 5 C.F.R. § 430.206(c)...permits "communications about appraisal of performance between a supervisor and an employee prior to the determination of the rating of record."... In our view, providing employees with statistics regarding their performance in relation to the Agency's performance standards is consistent with that regulatory provision....

The second sentence...does not preclude the Agency from evaluating employee performance on the basis of statistics which are not provided within 5 days. The only obligation imposed on management by the sentence is to provide a written explanation for the delay beyond the 5-day period. Therefore, the sentence has no impact on management's authority to use whatever statistics it chooses in evaluating performance.... [T]he second sentence...is also negotiable....

The third...sentence...interferes with management's rights...to direct employees and to assign work because it limits the information that may be used in evaluating employee performance....

Proposals which prevent management from using particular information in evaluating employee performance also preclude management from accurately evaluating an employee's work....

The last sentence...prevents management from using information it has decided is necessary in assessing employee performance. The sentence precludes the use of statistics which otherwise would have been factors in evaluating employee performance. Consequently, management would not be able to evaluate the full range of employees' work products to determine whether the employees' performance is acceptable. Therefore, the last sentence revises the content of the performance standards themselves and is not negotiable.

POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 39 FLRA 783, 811, 813 (1991), determined negotiable Proposal 12, that the agency provide to employees copies of review sheets prepared by supervisors examining their work. The disclosure could not be defeated by management's claim that it violated management's deliberative process right. The deliberative process "does not prohibit the disclosure of information relied on in exercising those rights." POPA also approved as negotiable Proposal 13, seeking release of the same information to the union. See Ft. Bragg Sch., 30 FLRA 508 (1987), supra. NTEU and DHHS, SSA, Office of Hearings and Appeals, 47 FLRA 705, 714, 723-25 (1993), resolved as negotiable Provisions 2 and 3, broadly stating management's recognition of the duty to maintain and retain adequate documentation supporting ratings and specifically requiring that employees be provided documentation, on request, when a decrease in performance is alleged, and prior to the issuance of an appraisal to an employee based on that documentation. The requirement that management maintain documentation was a procedural aspect of the performance appraisal process. The requirement of disclosure of the documentation did not expand into an agreement concerning the failure to maintain or disclose the documentation. The disclosure requirement was also a negotiable procedure. [Refer to Chapter 3, "Performance Appraisals; Related Data," concerning disclosure to unions of information pertaining to employees' evaluations and related data.]
