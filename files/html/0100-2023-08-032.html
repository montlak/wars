b. Attempt to Preclude Publication of Decision

Redschlag v. Dept. of Army, 89 MSPR 589, 596 n.1 (2001), advised that publication of MSPB opinions is required by FOIA and may not be wholly precluded through an appellant's request for a protective order:

On July 16, 2001, the appellant filed a "Motion for Protective Order." The appellant has requested that this Opinion and Order be issued as an unpublished decision and withdrawn or withheld from dissemination on any electronic website or on any other internet site accessible for public viewing. The appellant has also requested that the Board restrict access to the documents, decisions, and records associated with her appeals to the parties, the Board, the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission Office of Federal Operations, and the Office of Personnel Management. Finally, the appellant requests that the Board restrict from distribution and withhold from publication any and all documents, records and decisions associated with her appeals which are in the official custody and control of the Department of the Army and the Board's Denver Field Office. The Board's consideration of this motion is circumscribed by the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA), 5 U.S.C. § 552, and the appellant has not demonstrated that the records she wishes the Board to withhold from dissemination are exempt from mandatory disclosure under FOIA. See Coons v. Department of the Treasury, 85 M.S.P.R. 631 ¶¶ 23-29 (2000). Furthermore, FOIA requires the Board to make its final decisions available to the public and to make its final opinions electronically available. See 5 U.S.C. § 552(a)(2). Accordingly, the Board hereby denies the appellant's motion. However, if a third party requests access to Board records regarding the appellant's appeals, such request will be considered and processed pursuant to the Board's regulations regarding the availability of official information. See 5 C.F.R. Part 1204.
