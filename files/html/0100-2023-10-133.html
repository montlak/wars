c. Consent Election, Pre-Election Agreements

The parties may establish preelection ground rules orally or in writing, and those election agreements will be enforced to the extent that violations of the agreements may cause election results to be invalidated. See Ft. Campbell Dependents Sch. and Ft. Campbell Educ. Ass'n, Overseas Educ. Ass'n, 46 FLRA 219, 224 (1992). If a union participates in a pre-election agreement defining the unit to exclude certain positions, FLRA will not entertain a unit clarification petition to include the previously excluded positions in the unit after the election without a showing of a subsequent, meaningful change in the duties or classifications of the excluded positions. FTC and AFGE Local 2211, 15 FLRA 247, 248 (1984). But units must be properly defined, and FLRA properly modifies the unit defined in a consent election agreement if it learns that some individuals within that unit have supervisory responsibilities. Dept. of Army, Law Enforcement Command Pac., Ft. Shafter and Haw. Teamsters and Allied Workers Local 996, 53 FLRA 1602, 1604 (1998) (Member Wasserman filed a concurring opinion noting that it can be a ULP for an agency to foster the inclusion of ineligible employees for the purpose of affecting an election, interfering with union rights, or controlling the union); see Dept. of Navy, NAS New Orleans and NAIL, 67 FLRA 422 (2014) (preelection agreement did not override regional director's responsibility to exclude professional employee from unit of nonprofessionals; statutory requirements governing professional employees' unit status trumped the agreement).

An election agreement is not a condition to an election. Under DOD, Pentagon Force Protection Agency and AFGE, 68 FLRA 761, 765-66 (2015):

Under § 2422.16(b) of the Authority's Regulations, "[i]f the parties are unable to agree on...[the] method of election[,]...[then] the [RD] will decide" the appropriate election procedures.[51] The Authority has explained that § 2422.16(b) gives an RD the discretion to establish the procedures for an election where parties are not able to agree to those procedures on their own.[52]

[52] E.g., Army & Air Force Exch. Serv., Dallas, Tex., 55 FLRA 1239, 1241 (2000).

Here, the RD found that the Agency was "not willing to agree to certain procedural matters," specifically "the language of the election notice and wording on the ballot," and, therefore, she decided the procedural details. The Union argues that the Agency's arguments regarding impounding the ballots are "disingenuous" because the Agency "consented to the impoundment of the ballots" and failed to contest the impoundment before it filed its objections. However, even assuming that the Agency's challenge to impoundment is properly before us, that challenge is without merit. Under § 2422.16(b), the RD had the discretion to determine the conduct of the election if the parties could not agree to all the procedural terms--including whether to impound the ballots--and the Agency provides no basis for finding that the RD committed prejudicial procedural error by doing so.

As to the RD's decision to proceed with the election while the Agency's first application was pending before the Authority, that decision is consistent with the Authority's Regulations[56] as well as established precedent in both the federal[57] and private sectors.[58] As discussed above, the Statute and the Authority's Regulations,[59] as well as Authority precedent,[60] provide that an RD's direction of an election is not automatically stayed pending the Authority's decision on an application for review. Further, the Authority has explained that promptly conducting an election is necessary to "ensure that the vote reflects the wishes of the bargaining unit at a time sufficiently proximate to the filing of the petition to preserve the rights of the employees under [§] 7102 [of the Statute]."[61] In this connection, the Authority has held that there is a "public interest[ ]" in "allowing employees to vote for the representative of their choice, without undue delay or the possible influence of extraneous factors caused by the passage of time."[62]

Additionally, the Authority's practice is consistent with that found in the private sector. The Authority has noted that "the structure, role, and functions of the Authority were closely patterned after those of the [National Labor Relations Board (the Board)]," and, therefore, relevant Board precedent is due "serious consideration."[63] In this regard, it is the Board's practice in representation cases that regional directors process and issue appropriate certifications "notwithstanding the pendency of a request for review, subject to revision or revocation by the Board pursuant to a request for review."[64] The Board's regulations expressly state that once a regional director directs an election, the regional director should schedule the election "for the earliest date practicable," and that the election notice should advise the parties that any eligibility questions will be resolved after the election.[65]
