d. Other Discrimination

Under OPM's regulation, 5 CFR 315.806 (2022):

(d) An employee may appeal to the Board under this section a termination that the employee alleges was based on discrimination because of race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy and gender identity), national origin, age (as defined by the Age Discrimination in Employment Act of 1967, as amended), or disability. An appeal alleging a discriminatory termination may be filed under this subsection only if such discrimination is raised in addition to one of the issues stated in paragraph (b) [partisan political reasons or marital status] or (c) [procedures pertaining to termination for conditions arising before appointment] of this section.

The decisions follow the regulation. If a probationer invokes jurisdiction based on marital status or partisan political discrimination, the Board also considers allegations of other types of discrimination connected with the appealed action. Hadley v. Dept. of Navy, 2 MSPR 530, 532, 3 MSPB 51 (1980) (including an allegation of discrimination based on physical disability). If there is no alleged marital status or partisan political discrimination, the Board lacks jurisdiction to consider whether an employee's termination constituted disability discrimination. Lizewski v. Dept. of Army, 15 MSPR 417, 419 (1983); see Danner v. MSPB (Fed. Cir. 1999 NP No. 99-3088) (racial discrimination allegation alone does not establish Board jurisdiction over probationer's MSPB appeal); Cunningham v. Dept. of Labor, DC-315H-17-0167-I-1 (NP 7/27/2022) (union activity: "However, even if we were to consider the appellant's argument that his termination was due to his affiliation with the union, it would not provide the Board with jurisdiction. See Mastriano v. Federal Aviation Administration, 714 F.2d 1152, 1156 (holding that allegations of discrimination based on union affiliation do not state a cause of action within the Board's jurisdiction under 5 C.F.R. § 315.806(b))."), aff'd, Cunningham v. MSPB, ___Fed. Appx.___ (Fed. Cir. 2023 NP); Moran v. Dept. of Treasury, 77 MSPR 532, 538 (1998) (without regulatory discrimination claims properly raised by a terminated probationer, the Board lacked jurisdiction to consider racial discrimination claim); Bedynek-Stumm v. USDA, 57 MSPR 176, 180 (1993) (unless there is jurisdiction over a probationer's removal, the Board lacked jurisdiction over allegations of age and sex discrimination); Anthony v. VA, 2 MSPR 345, 346, 2 MSPB 508 (1980) (Board lacks jurisdiction over an appeal from a probationary termination when the appeal is based solely on an allegation of religious discrimination). If the Board lacks jurisdiction of a probationer's EEO claim, the proper forum for adjudication may be EEOC. See Drumgold v. Dept. of Navy, 2 MSPR 568, 570 (1980). If a court concludes that the probationer was discriminated against in violation of Title VII but provides no retroactive reinstatement, the employee remains a probationer at the time of termination and is not constructively reinstated as a tenured employee. See Castle v. Dept. of Treasury, 68 MSPR 417, 422-23 (1995).
