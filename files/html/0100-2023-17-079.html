broida flra Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards, Subchapter 07: Procedures for Review of Arbitration Awards, Section A: Finality; Interlocutory Appeals, Subsection 05: Clarification, Correction of Awards, Part a: Effect of FLRA Decision Setting Award Aside on Ability of Arbitrator to Clarify Award
a. Effect of FLRA Decision Setting Award Aside on Ability of Arbitrator to Clarify Award

An arbitrator is precluded from reopening and clarifying an award that FLRA sets aside unless both parties request the arbitrator to do so. If both sides consent to the reopening of the award, it is immaterial to the jurisdiction of the arbitrator that one party later changes its position about the submission. See Navy Pub. Works Ctr., Norfolk and Tidewater Virginia FEMTC, 35 FLRA 93, 97 (1990); see SSA and AFGE, 32 FLRA 806, 808 (1988) (once FLRA sets aside award, arbitrator is not empowered to reopen matter to clarify award unless both parties request arbitrator to do so); cf. SSA and AFGE Local 2369, 34 FLRA 866, 870 (1990) (if parties agree to allow arbitrator to reopen award that has not been appealed to FLRA, doctrine of functus officio is inapplicable).

Dept. of Navy, Norfolk Naval Shipyard, Portsmouth and NAGE Local R4-19, 39 FLRA 692, 695 (1991), set aside an award because it did not conform to the Back Pay Act. The union then unilaterally submitted the case back to the same arbitrator. When that arbitrator ruled in favor of the union, the agency filed exceptions claiming the arbitrator was without jurisdiction to issue the second award. FLRA agreed:

Once the Authority has set aside an arbitrator's award, nothing precludes the parties from jointly requesting the arbitrator to reopen the matter. However, an arbitrator is precluded from reopening and modifying an award which the Authority has set aside unless both parties request the Arbitrator to do so....

...

[W]e reject the Union's argument that it has a right to unilaterally resubmit the same grievance to arbitration. Allowing a party to an arbitration proceeding to unilaterally resubmit the same grievance to arbitration could result in endless relitigation and lessen the finality that the arbitration process is designed to ensure. Finally, with regard to the Union's assertion that parties to an arbitration proceeding should be entitled to a decision if an arbitrator's decision is found defective by the Authority, the parties have that opportunity if they jointly request further consideration by an arbitrator.
