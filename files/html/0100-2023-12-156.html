2. Date and Place of Filing

The PFR is filed with the Clerk of the MSPB, 1615 M Street, NW, 5th Floor, Washington, DC 20419-0002 (Fax (202) 653-7130) or through the Board's e-filing system. A PFR filed on the 35th day after the date of the initial decision is timely. Keels v. Dept. of Navy, 9 MSPR 19, 20 (1981). A PFR is timely if it is mailed on the day that the initial decision is to become final. Bryant v. VA, 10 MSPR 391, 392 (1982); see Almazan v. Dept. of Air Force, 26 MSPR 318, 320 n.2 (1985) (initial decision stated it would become final unless a PFR was filed by September 28; a PFR mailed on September 28 was a timely filing). If the last date for filing falls on a weekend, the filing period is extended to Monday (unless it is a holiday). If the last day for filing a PFR is a holiday, the time is extended through the next workday, excluding Saturday and Sunday. Hughes v. Comm'n on Civil Rights, 26 MSPR 277, 278 (1985); 5 CFR 1201.23 (2022). The Board excused delays caused by foreign holidays for appellants in foreign lands. See, e.g., Sanpedro v. OPM, 45 MSPR 503, 504 (1990) (Bataan Day in the Philippines). But see Blevins v. Dept. of Army, 19 MSPR 487, 488 (1984) (declining to recognize a foreign holiday, but finding good cause for a one-day delay in filing an appeal). If the Board shuts down on a filing date due to external forces--in Washington, the threat of an inch or more of snow is often sufficient--and a party attempts to file a document that day by hand, the filing date becomes the next business day. See Slaughter v. USDA, 56 MSPR 349, 351 n.2 (1993) (water main break that made offices unusable).

Some appellants may have more than one appeal under adjudication with a regional office. The time for filing a PFR runs from the issuance of the initial decision in each appeal. That a PFR is timely filed as to one initial decision does not excuse a late PFR filed as to another initial decision issued from the same region. Bergman v. DOJ, 48 MSPR 84, 85 (1991).

There are many Board decisions on the timing and methodology of filing documents with the Board. Refer to Chapter 2, concerning the timing and methodology of filing appeals, for analysis that may be helpful concerning timeliness issues involving petitions for review.
