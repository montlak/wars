7. Examples of Awards Set Aside Based on Nonfact

An example of an award set aside for a gross error of fact is Hq., San Antonio Air Logistics Ctr., Kelly AFB and AFGE Local 1617, San Antonio, 6 FLRA 292, 294-96 (1981). Disputed was whether a local memorandum of understanding extending coverage to security police employees survived a later master agreement. FLRA concluded that the arbitrator misread what FLRA found to be a clear provision of the later agreement that excluded the special police employees from the unit. See DLA and AFGE Local 916, 62 FLRA 134 (2007) (setting aside award based on memorandum of agreement on motorcycle safety when arbitrator mistakenly concluded agency was party to agreement); DLA and AFGE Local 916, 53 FLRA 460, 462-64 (1997) (award set aside because arbitrator "failed to apprehend" that union official had authority to enter memoranda of understanding with agency). A gross mistake of fact would include a determination by an arbitrator based on a contract provision that was not in existence or at least not applicable to the operative events. Army Missile Command, Redstone Arsenal and Local 1858, AFGE, 18 FLRA 374, 375 (1985).

DOD, Defense Commissary Agency and AFGE Local 1035, 65 FLRA 310, 310-11 (2010), set aside an award based on a nonfact:

The grievance alleges that the Agency violated Article 27, Section 2 of the parties' agreement by exhibiting favoritism toward a particular Computer Assisted Ordering Worker (CAO-1) in several respects. The grievance was submitted to arbitration, where the Arbitrator framed the issue as follows: "Did the Agency violate any law, rule, regulation or [c]ontract in the way it treated CAO-1 in comparison to other members of the Randolph Commissary bargaining unit? If so, what, if any, is the remedy?"

The Arbitrator found that the Union "failed to prove that any employee was disadvantaged in a monetary or job performance manner." However, the Arbitrator found that the Agency "admitted...that favoritism has taken place." Accordingly, the Arbitrator sustained the grievance.

...

To establish that an award is based on a nonfact, the appealing party must show that a central fact underlying the award is clearly erroneous, but for which the arbitrator would have reached a different result. See NFFE, Local 1984, 56 FLRA 38, 41 (2000). The Authority will not find an award deficient on the basis of an arbitrator's determination of any factual matter that the parties disputed at arbitration. See id.

Here, the Arbitrator found that the Agency had admitted favoritism. However, there is nothing in the award, or in the portion of the Agency's post-hearing brief relied on by the Arbitrator, supporting a conclusion that the Agency admitted favoritism. Thus, the finding is clearly erroneous. Moreover, the finding is central to the award. In this regard, the Arbitrator found that the Union did not establish that the Agency acted improperly, "[b]ut it has been admitted by the Agency that favoritism has taken place." A review of the award as a whole leads to the conclusion that, but for the Arbitrator's erroneous finding, he would not have found favoritism. In addition, contrary to the Union's assertion, there is no basis to conclude that this particular factual finding--that the Agency conceded favoritism--was disputed below. Therefore, the central fact underlying the award is clearly erroneous, but for which the Arbitrator would have reached a different result. Accordingly, we find that the award is based on a nonfact, and set it aside. See, e.g., AFGE, Local 507, 58 FLRA 378, 381 (2003) (Chairman Cabaniss dissenting).

An award was vacated when the arbitrator set aside a suspension based on charged insubordination. The difficulty was that the charge sustained by the agency and supporting the suspension was a failure to follow instructions. "To analyze a disciplinary case using the elements of a different charge, and to conclude that the Agency failed to prove that charge, constitutes a clear error regarding a central fact." Dept. of Labor, OWCP and AFGE Council 73, 71 FLRA 726 (2020) (Member DuBester concurring). Set aside as based on a nonfact was an award that failed to recognize that a grievance was untimely. The date of the event leading to the grievance was earlier than the period allowed for filing the grievance. Dept. of Justice, FCC Coleman and AFGE Local 506, 71 FLRA 892 (2020) (Member DuBester concurring).
