4. Nonappropriated Fund

Lewis v. Dept. of Air Force, 31 MSPR 328, 330-31 (1986), determined that nonappropriated fund employees were not covered by restoration rights defined by 5 CFR 353.401 (1986) [now 5 CFR 353.103 (2022)] :

The presiding official found that appellant had a right to appeal the agency's denial of her request for restoration to duty after a work-related injury under the regulations set forth at 5 C.F.R. part 353, Restoration to Duty. He found that although appellant was not an employee as defined at 5 U.S.C. § 7511, appellant was an employee as defined in 5 C.F.R. § 353.103(c), "Persons covered," which states, in pertinent part, that:

The provisions of this part concerned with employee injury cover the following persons:

(1) A civil officer or employee in any branch of the Government of the United States, including an officer or employee of an instrumentality wholly owned by the United States, who was separated or furloughed from a position without time limitation as a result of compensable injury;...

The presiding official reasoned that NAF employees are individuals who work for organizations which are wholly owned instrumentalities of the United States and therefore that they are subject to the appeal rights provided at 5 C.F.R. § 353.401. This conclusion was erroneous since it overlooks other applicable, relevant statutory and regulatory provisions.

The Board possesses jurisdiction only if the appellant's case is appealable to it under some law, rule, or regulation. 5 U.S.C. § 7701(a). It is well-established that NAF employees are not employees for purposes of the adverse action provisions of the Civil Service Reform Act. Taylor v. Department of the Navy, 1 M.S.P.R. 591 (1980) (the definition of employee set forth at 5 U.S.C. 2105(c) precludes the Board from accepting jurisdiction of adverse action appeals from nonappropriated fund employees); see also Perez v. Army and Air Force Exchange Service, 680 F.2d 779 (D.C. Cir. 1982). It is equally clear that NAF employees are not employees for the purposes of 5 C.F.R. part 353.

The definition of employee set forth at 5 U.S.C. § 2105(c) provides that NAF employees are not subject to the provisions of 5 U.S.C. chapter 81, subchapter I (Compensation for Work Injuries).[1] However, the regulations establishing restoration to duty rights, including the right to appeal to the Board from a denial of a right to restoration, clearly are applicable only to those employees who sustain work-related injuries subject to the provisions of 5 U.S.C. chapter 82, subchapter I. See 5 C.F.R. § 353.101 ("This part sets forth rights and obligations of employees and agencies in connection with restoration following (a) military duty..., and (b) employee injuries subject to the provisions of 5 U.S.C. chapter 81, subchapter I."); 5 C.F.R. § 353.102(d) (Injury means "a compensable injury sustained under the provisions of 5 U.S.C. chapter 81, subchapter I...").

[1] Title 5 U.S.C. § 2105(c) states:

An employee paid from nonappropriated funds of the Army and Air Force Exchange Service, Army and Air Force Motion Picture Service, Navy Ship's Stores Ashore, Navy exchanges, Marine Corps exchanges, Coast Guard exchanges, and other instrumentalities of the United States under the jurisdiction of the armed forces conducted for the comfort, pleasure, contentment, and mental and physical improvement of personnel of the armed forces is deemed not an employee for the purposes of--

(1) laws (other than subchapter IV of chapter 53 and 7204 of this title) administered by the Office of Personnel Management; or

(2) subchapter I of chapter 81 and section 7902 of this title. (Emphasis added).

The statutory scheme of 5 U.S.C. chapter 81 provides that NAF employees are subject to subchapter II (Employees of Nonappropriated Fund Instrumentalities). Subchapter II provides that with respect to disability or death from injury, 33 U.S.C. chapter 18 (Longshoremen's and Harbor Workers' Compensation Act) applies to certain employees of nonappropriated fund instrumentalities. 5 U.S.C. § 8171(a).

Accordingly, we find that appellant, as a nonappropriated fund employee, is not an employee for the purposes of 5 U.S.C. chapter 81, subchapter I, and therefore is not an employee subject to the restoration rights provided at 5 C.F.R. part 353. Appellant, therefore, does not have a right of appeal to the Board under 5 C.F.R. § 353.401 from the agency's denial of her request for restoration.
