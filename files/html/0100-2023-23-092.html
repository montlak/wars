16. Applicant for Employment

With exceptions for VEOA, USERRA, and Individual Right of Action appeals, back pay is ordinarily reserved for employees, not for applicants for employment. See Hubbard v. EPA, 982 F.2d 531 (D.C. Cir. 1992) (awarding no back pay for an applicant whose constitutional cause of action was favorably resolved under the Administrative Procedure Act); Special Counsel and Hubbard v. EPA, 79 MSPR 542, 548 (1998) (confirming, in an OSC corrective action case, that an "applicant" is not entitled to compensation under the Back Pay Act), aff'd, Hubbard v. EPA, 205 F.3d 1315 (Fed. Cir. 2000); Special Counsel v. EPA, 70 MSPR 41, 57 (1996) (same). But cf. Morgan v. Dept. of Energy, 81 MSPR 48, 53-55 ¶¶ 11-16 (1999) (Back Pay Act provided a remedy for the denial of a promotion to an employee for whom the compensatory damages provisions of the WPA were inapplicable due to a lack of retroactivity). [An IRA case for an applicant might result in back pay as a component of consequential damages under 5 USC 1214, and refer to Chapter 13, "Statutory and Regulatory Requirements and Limitations," for IRA remedies; violations of VEOA and USERRA, discussed in Chapter 1, "Appeal Rights Based on Military Service and Veterans Preference," might also require monetary corrective action for applicants for positions.]

The Board lacked authority to award back pay to an applicant for employment whose Board appeal resulted in a reversal of a negative OPM suitability determination. Only employees qualify for back pay under the Back Pay Act, 5 USC 5596. In the appeal of an applicant, the Board can only direct the agency to restore the individual's name to an eligibility list for employment consideration. Reversal of the suitability determination entitles appellant to be returned to the eligibility list, but appointment to a position is at the discretion of the agency. Schaefer v. INS, 28 MSPR 566, 568-69 (1985) (5 USC 5596 does not provide the authority to award back pay and benefits to applicant for employment). See Lewis v. GSA, 54 MSPR 120, 123 (1992) (suitability determination; awarding no back pay for applicant); cf. Patton v. Dept. of Treasury, 88 MSPR 542, 543 ¶ 3 (2001) (noting an order in a suitability case returning the appellant to the status of tentative selectee). But see Martin v. DOJ, 90 MSPR 690, 697 ¶ 10 (2002) (when settlement of a suitability appeal placed the appellant in the position of an employee, he was entitled to back pay during the period of the agency's noncompliance with the settlement). [Refer to Chapter 1, "Remedies" in the discussion of suitability cases.]
