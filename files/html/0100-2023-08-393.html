1. Employees Serving in Probationary or Trial Periods

Gamble v. Dept. of Army, 111 MSPR 529, 531 ¶ 20, 2009 MSPB 118 (2009), explained that under the post-2008 OPM regulations, agencies may take suitability actions against trial (or probationary) period employees, or they may follow the processes to terminate employees on the basis of preappointment conditions. [Gamble is discussed later in this chapter, "Impact of 2008 OPM Suitability Regulations."] The analysis in Gamble was qualified by Scott v. OPM, 116 MSPR 356, 363 n.6, 2011 MSPB 50 (2011) (reconsid. den., Scott v. OPM, 117 MSPR 467, 2012 MSPB 25 (2012)) (suitability removal: "Conduct occurring after the appointment process is complete, such as the conduct at issue in this case, would not lie within the scope of such an [suitability] investigation or determination."). If an agency with authority delegated from OPM takes a suitability action against an individual who is a probationer, the ensuing appeal is a suitability appeal. See LaPre v. DOJ, 62 MSPR 329, 335 (1994) (discussed below concerning terminations of probationers). The suitability review for a termination probationer is guided and limited by OPM suitability criteria. Tenured employees receive more extensive review. See Weirich v. OPM, AT-0731-15-0629-I-1 (NP 3/18/2016) ("Here, however, the appellant did not allege that he was a tenured Federal employee. Thus, we find that the administrative judge acted properly in not conducting an independent penalty analysis because the Board's jurisdiction under 5 C.F.R. § 731.501 does not extend to reviewing or modifying the ultimate action taken as a result of a suitability determination. See Folio v. Department of Homeland Security, 402 F.3d 1350, 1355-56 (Fed. Cir. 2005); 5 C.F.R. § 731.501(b)(3).").
