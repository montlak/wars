3. Quantity and Sources of Evidence

An arbitrator is not bound by the Federal Rules of Evidence. DHHS, SSA, Office of Hearings and Appeals and AFGE Local 3615, 39 FLRA 407, 413 (1991). An arbitrator's exercise of discretion under the contract to admit or deny evidence first offered with a post-hearing brief is not a denial of a fair hearing. VA LA Healthcare System and AFGE Local 1061, 71 FLRA 953, 954 (2020) (Member DuBester concurring). An arbitrator may draw from any relevant source as an aid in interpreting a collective bargaining agreement. Delaware NG, Wilmington and ACT, Delaware Chap., 5 FLRA 50, 52 (1981). His award will not be set aside because he fails to specify or discuss particular items of evidence that were considered or that led to his award. DHHS, SSA, Southeastern Program Serv. Ctr. and AFGE Local 2206, 38 FLRA 1170, 1177 (1990); see AFGE Local 1867 and Dept. of Air Force, Air Force Academy, 59 FLRA 737, 738 (2004) (arbitrator need not specify or discuss particular items of evidence that were considered); NFFE Local 1984 and DOD, DCMD Philadelphia, 56 FLRA 38, 41 (2000) ("arbitrator is not required to specify or discuss specific items of evidence on which an award is based or which otherwise were considered by the arbitrator"). An arbitrator's failure to draw an adverse inference because evidence was missing was not a basis for setting the award aside on exceptions. NAGE Local R4-78 and VAMC Martinsburg, 56 FLRA 418, 420 (2000) (an EDP claim for asbestos exposure). A contention that an arbitration award is not based on substantial evidence may be characterized by FLRA as no more than disagreement with the arbitrator's findings of fact, reasoning, and conclusions on the evidence and testimony before him. AFGE Council 236 and GSA, Nat'l Capital Region, 12 FLRA 236, 236 (1983); see AFGE Local 1494 and DOJ, Bureau of Prisons, 57 FLRA 645, 647-48 (2001) (arbitration award concerning classification of work under OSHA regulation, 29 CFR 1926.1101, in proximity to friable asbestos; disagreement with arbitrator's findings provided no basis for finding award deficient). The arbitrator will not be faulted for considering events or evidence predating the grievance if the arbitrator's award squarely responds to the issue. Dept. of Educ. and AFGE Local 3896, 59 FLRA 820, 823 (2004) ("[T]he Arbitrator found such events were 'admissible as relevant and material for background purposes' in interpreting the parties' CBA and resolving the issue that was properly before him.").

FLRA defers to the arbitrator's findings of fact and does not supplement those findings through its own factfinding. AFGE Council of HUD Local 222 and DHUD, 54 FLRA 1267, 1275 (1998). Even if statements relied on by an arbitrator are in error, FLRA appears unwilling to overturn the award if there is other probative evidence in the record that could support the award. IBEW and Army Support Command, Hawaii, 14 FLRA 680, 681 (1984). At times FLRA is disinclined to permit arbitrators to draw inferences from settlement agreements. Compare AFGE Local 201 and DOD, DFAS, 57 FLRA 874, 877 (2002) (unsigned settlement containing no admission of wrongdoing; citing AFGE Local 1843, 51 FLRA 444, 449 n.5 (1995) (the arbitrator's reliance on settlement order was misplaced)), with Dept. of Navy, Naval Explosive Ordinance Disposal Technology Div. and AFGE Local 1923, 56 FLRA 280, 287 (2000) (arbitrator could draw evidentiary value from settlement).
