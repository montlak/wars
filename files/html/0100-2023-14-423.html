1. Mileage; Commuting Cost

A proposal was negotiable for payment of mileage expenses for meat inspectors rotating through different locations on assignment. The payment was discretionary with the agency and negotiable. AFGE Meat Graders Council and USDA, Food Safety and Quality Serv., Meat Grading Branch, 8 FLRA 118, 131-32 (1982). There was no impediment to the negotiability of a management-directed change in the manner that mileage was reckoned and entered on travel vouchers. The change did not involve an agency internal security practice. DHHS, SSA, Region VI and NFFE Local 1823, 10 FLRA 26, 40 (1982) (ALJ Decision).

A proposal was not negotiable that required management to guarantee that transferred employees not suffer financially from increased commuting costs. The subject matter of compensation for mileage was already covered by federal law and not a condition of employment subject to negotiation. AFGE Local 236 and GSA, 9 FLRA 825, 827 (1982) (Proposal 3). A proposal that allowed mileage expenses for commuting was contrary to the limitations of 5 USC 5704, governing payment of mileage expenses, and nonnegotiable. NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, IRS, 9 FLRA 726, 726-28 (1982); see NTEU Chap. 22 and Dept. of Treasury, IRS, 29 FLRA 348, 354-56 (1987) (Proposal 10: proposal would have been negotiable under 55 Comp. Gen. 1323, 1328 (1976), had it simply allowed travel expenses incurred by employees going from the post of duty to a detailed work site). [Refer later in this chapter to "Expenses Related to Relocation."]

Nonnegotiable in NFFE Local 2199 and Dept. of Interior, Nat'l Park Serv., Gulf Islands Nat'l Seashore, 66 FLRA 412, 413 (2011), was a proposal that an agency reimburse toll costs incurred by employees who were required to travel to a new work site:

The payment of employee travel expenses is governed by the Travel Expense Act (TEA), 5 U.S.C. §§ 5701-5752, and the FTR. See U.S. Dep't of the Treasury, Internal Revenue Serv., Plantation, Fla., 64 FLRA 777, 780 (2010); Soc. Sec. Admin., 63 FLRA 313, 315 (2009) (citing Naval Public Works Ctr., San Diego, 34 FLRA 750, 754 (1990)). Under the TEA, when an employee is "traveling on official business away from the employee's designated post of duty," he or she is entitled to reimbursement for "the actual and necessary expenses of official travel." 5 U.S.C. § 5702(a)(1). The FTR provides, in relevant part, that an agency "may pay only those expenses essential to the transaction of official business." 41 C.F.R. § 301-2.2. Thus, employees not engaged in official business must bear the commuting costs between their residences and official duty stations--even where the transfer from one duty station to another has increased those costs. See NAGE, Local R14-87, 21 FLRA 905, 906 (1986); see also AFGE, Local 3006, 47 FLRA 155, 159-61 (1993); U.S. Customs Serv., Chi.-O'Hare, 23 FLRA 366, 367 (1986).

The proposal requires the Agency to reimburse employees for commuting costs incurred due to a transfer from one duty station to another. The Union does not claim, and there is no indication in the record, that employees are engaged in official business during their commutes. Thus, the proposal requires the Agency to pay the travel expenses of employees not engaged in official business. As the TEA and FTR directly prohibit payment of such expenses, see, e.g., 5 U.S.C. § 5702(a)(1); 41 C.F.R. § 301-2.2, we find that the proposal is contrary to law. Accordingly, we dismiss the Union's petition.
