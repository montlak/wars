broida flra Chapter 08: Unfair Labor Practices Chapter 08: Unfair Labor Practices, Subchapter 02: Management Violations, Section D: Unilateral Changes in Employment Conditions and Past Practice, Subsection 08: Specific Changes Requiring Impact or Decision Bargaining, Part j: Pay and Fringe Benefits
j. Pay and Fringe Benefits

Failure to fulfill obligations to negotiate over the wage scales of prevailing rate employees whose wages were subject to collective bargaining was a ULP. Dept. of Energy, W. Area Power Admin., Golden and IBEW Local 640, 27 FLRA 268, 273 (1987) (unilateral change). Another decision determined to be improper a unilateral change in the prices charged to employees for meals served at an agency facility. Scott AFB and NAGE Local R7-23, 31 FLRA 1013, 1017 (1988); see Marine Corps Logistics Base, Barstow and AFGE Local 1482, 46 FLRA 782, 799 (1992) (ALJ Decision) (ULP by employer who changed the prices of soda in vending machines without notice or decision and impact bargaining), reconsideration denied, 47 FLRA 454 (1993). Defense Distrib. Region W. and Laborers' Int'l Union Local 1276, 43 FLRA 1539 (1992), concluded that the employer committed a ULP when it unilaterally restricted eating and drinking in some of its warehouses. See Naval Facilities Eng'g Serv. Ctr. and NAGE Local R12-28, 50 FLRA 377, 380 (1995) (ULP for employer to unilaterally terminate the "Employee of the Month" award program carrying benefit of "a parking space located close of the facility"; bargaining was required on substance of changes); VA, Veterans Canteen Serv., Newington and NAGE Local R1-109, 47 FLRA 631, 634 (1993) (failure to bargain increased food prices in Canteen Service was remedied by order directing agency to rescind price increases and to make employees whole for additional costs caused by changes in food prices); Dept. of Labor, Washington and AFGE Local 12, 44 FLRA 988, 994-95 (1992) (ULP in unilateral implementation of changes in parking garage operations); VA, VAMC, Veterans Canteen Serv., Lexington and NAGE Local R5-184, 44 FLRA 179, 190 (1992) (ULP was committed when employer and contractor agreed to downgrade food vending facilities available to employees). Impact bargaining was impliedly required by the Fourth Circuit as to an employer's implementation of a profit-sharing system. See DHHS, SSA v. FLRA, 983 F.2d 578, 582-83 (4th Cir. 1992), partially enforcing DHHS, SSA and AFGE Council 220, 41 FLRA 224 (1991). Impact bargaining was improperly avoided by an agency that offered a voluntary separation incentive of $25,000 to employees to induce their retirement to free up slots to convert other employees into permanent positions. See Dept. of Air Force, Air Force Materiel Command and AFGE Council 214, 54 FLRA 914, 919-21 (1998).
