b. Error in Formal Requirements

If a PFR arrives without a signature, or if it is evident that it was not served on the opposing party, the Board's Clerk may allow the petitioner additional time to cure the defects, a function that is within the Clerk's delegated functions under 5 USC 1204(g) [formerly 1205(f)]. As to length limitations, 5 CFR 1201.114 (2022) states:

(l) Rejection for failure to comply. The Clerk of the Board may reject material submitted for filing that does not substantially conform to the procedural requirements of this subpart by issuing a rejection letter advising the parties of the nature of the nonconformity and the requirements and deadline for resubmission. Any deadlines affected by the rejection will be addressed in the rejection letter.

When the errors are cured, the petitioner should not use that opportunity to expand upon the PFR that was originally submitted. Lipinski v. Dept. of Army, 35 MSPR 186, 188-89 (1987). But see Casuga v. OPM, 43 MSPR 287, 289 n.* (1990) (no impropriety when the appellant, who was told to correct some deficiencies in a PFR, submitted a new petition that met the Board's filing requirements). If the error is not cured, the PFR is subject to dismissal. See McKenzie v. EEOC, 38 MSPR 380, 383 (1988) ("additional views" of Chairman Levinson). The Board's Clerk may return a PFR that does not conform technically to the Board's regulations. See Lipinski v. Dept. of Army, 35 MSPR 186, 189 n.2 (1987).

The Board explained its procedures for treating PFRs with technical problems in Board Order 1060.10 (June 1, 1993), "Policy and Procedures for Handling Deficient Petitions for Review":

1. PURPOSE. This order provides the policy and procedures for handling deficient petitions for review.

2. POLICY. The Board will reject any petition for review that does not meet the basic requirements of 5 CFR 1201.114.

3. "DEFICIENT PETITION" DEFINED. For the purposes of this procedure, a petition lacking a certificate of service or a petition with an improper certificate is a "deficient petition" under 5 CFR 1201.114(a). An unsigned petition is an example of a deficient petition. A letter or envelope containing a signature that transmits a petition will be accepted as part of the petition and will satisfy the regulatory requirement. (For the purposes of this procedure, "signature" includes a mark or fingerprint, as well as a handwritten name.)

4. NOTICE OF REJECTION. A petition for review that does not meet the requirements of 5 CFR 1201.114 will be returned by the Clerk of the Board to the filing party. The Clerk's notice to the filing party will direct the party to refile a petition, with all deficiencies corrected, within 15 days from the date of the notice (25 days for overseas location) or 35 days from the date of the initial decision, whichever is later.... The notice will be sent by regular mail to the filing party or the party's representative. The notice will identify the deficiencies in the petition and state the action required to correct them. The Clerk's office will retain a copy of the petition....

...

If the refiled, perfected petition is received by the date specified in the Clerk's notice, it will be processed under regular PFR procedures....

5. UNTIMELY REFILING. If the refiled, perfected petition is not received by the date specified in the Clerk's notice and the filing party does not show cause for the untimely filing, the Clerk will send a show cause notice to the filing party and process the petition under the regular procedures applying to untimely filings.

6. FILE. A copy of the deficient petition for review and deficiency notice will be placed in the petition for review file.

Some technical deficiencies may be ignored (unless they are the subject of objection by the opposing party). In Washington v. USPS, 35 MSPR 195, 200-01 (1987), the agency argued that appellant's PFR should be denied because it was unsigned. The Board declined that invitation, noting both that the appellant's representative signed a certificate of service and that the agency showed no prejudice.
