broida flra Chapter 02: Unit Determinations and Elections Chapter 02: Unit Determinations and Elections, Subchapter 02: Representation Proceedings, Section A: Election Procedures, Subsection 08: Contract Bar, Part a: Request for Renegotiation
a. Request for Renegotiation

A request to negotiate modifications in an existing agreement prevents automatic renewal of the agreement. To conclude otherwise would unreasonably impede the ability to raise a question concerning representation for employees covered by the agreement. If, following the notice of intention to negotiate modifications and the termination date of the agreement, there is no agreement of fixed duration in existence, there is no contract bar to an election petition. Office of Sec'y, Hq., DHHS and NTEU, 11 FLRA 681, 685 (1983). NTEU was extended in DOD, Army Nat'l Guard Camp Keyes and AFGE Local 3012, 34 FLRA 59, 64-65 (1989):

[A] timely request to modify or renegotiate an existing agreement prevents automatic renewal of the agreement and precludes that agreement from barring a petition, where, as here, a negotiated agreement provides for automatic renewal unless a party requests renegotiation....

The fact that AFGE and the Activity have treated their agreement as having been renewed is not an extraordinary circumstance...which warrants reconsideration of the policy.... The Authority's policy regarding when an agreement bars a petition is consistent with the Statute because it both promotes stability during the term of an agreement of clearly fixed duration and does not unreasonably impede the ability of a competing union to raise a question concerning representation for the employees covered by an agreement....

The intent of parties to a contract with a clause carefully defining the duration of the contract may become confused by collateral events. In DHUD, Newark Office and AFGE, 37 FLRA 1122, 1127 (1990), the parties had a three-year agreement effective through November 13, 1984. The contract provided for its renewal, for periods of three years, on each third anniversary date, unless between ninety and sixty days prior to the anniversary date either party gave written notice to the other of its desire to amend or modify the agreement. On that occurrence, the agreement would remain in effect until the changes had been negotiated or approved. The contract was automatically renewed in 1984 and 1987, meaning that by its terms the next anniversary was November 1990. On November 17, 1989, the parties settled an unfair labor practice case by agreeing that they would reopen their negotiated agreement and, at a specified date, commence negotiations for a new contract. The settlement agreement stated that the present labor agreement would be extended until a new contract was concluded. On January 30, 1990, petitioner filed a petition for an election. The issue was whether the parties' contract barred the processing of that petition. FLRA found, on petition for reconsideration of the regional decision, that the election petition was barred:

Article 20 of the current collective bargaining agreement clearly sets forth the effective date and duration of the agreement. It also provides for automatic renewal in the absence of timely written notice of a party's desire to amend or modify the agreement.

The record further shows that a witness for the Activity testified that it was her understanding that, as part of the settlement agreement, the current collective bargaining agreement was to stay in effect until November 13, 1990.

Based on the foregoing, we reject the Regional Director's finding that "the effect of [the] settlement agreement was to rescind the [collective bargaining] agreement's definite three year term and replace it with an indefinite term, with no ascertainable date certain for expiration." Rather, by agreeing to negotiate a new collective bargaining agreement pursuant to Article 20 of their current collective bargaining agreement, NFFE and the Activity merely agreed to reopen the contract and commence negotiations early and reaffirmed the fixed term set forth in their current collective bargaining agreement.

We conclude that NFFE and the Activity's failure to stipulate in the settlement agreement, itself, that the current collective bargaining agreement would terminate on November 13, 1990, was an inadvertence that did not serve to convert their current collective bargaining agreement into one of indefinite duration. When both the settlement agreement and the current collective bargaining agreement are read together, as they must be, it is clear that NFFE and the Activity were reaffirming the fixed term of their current collective bargaining agreement and did not intend a new indefinite term for that agreement.
