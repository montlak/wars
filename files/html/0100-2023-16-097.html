broida flra Chapter 08: Unfair Labor Practices Chapter 08: Unfair Labor Practices, Subchapter 02: Management Violations, Section D: Unilateral Changes in Employment Conditions and Past Practice, Subsection 08: Specific Changes Requiring Impact or Decision Bargaining, Part o: Performance Standards and Critical Elements; Work Review Procedures
o. Performance Standards and Critical Elements; Work Review Procedures

Notice and impact and implementation bargaining is required for changes in performance standards and critical elements on an employee or employees. The union is entitled to notice of the changes and an opportunity to bargain. Air Force Sys. Command, Elec. Sys. Div. and NAGE Local R1-8, 14 FLRA 390, 403-04 (1984) (ALJ Decision); see POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 47 FLRA 10, 39-40 (1993) (relying on Nat'l Weather Serv. Employees Org., 37 FLRA 392, 395 (1990)). Implementation and impact bargaining is required as to changes in critical elements and performance standards. SSA and AFGE, 16 FLRA 1135, 1146 (1984) (ALJ Decision); see DHUD and AFGE Local 911, 56 FLRA 592, 592 (2000) (agency committed a ULP by unilaterally switching from a performance plan consisting of one critical element and three possible ratings to a plan consisting of five critical elements and five possible ratings; remedy required reappraisal of employees under former system); 56th Combat Support Group, MacDill AFB and NFFE Local 153, 43 FLRA 434, 451 (1991) (ALJ Decision) (including order to restore status quo and restore employees adversely affected by improper institution of performance plans). Management has a responsibility to bargain over the impact of a change in performance standards and critical elements as to adversely affected employees. Dept. of Transp., FAA and PASS, 19 FLRA 1004, 1007 (1985); SSA and AFGE Local 1923, 8 FLRA 517, 519 (1982) ("Respondent violated section 7116(a)(1) and (5) of the Statute by refusing to bargain concerning the impact and implementation of the changes in basic production requirements."). The failure to bargain over the impact of revised standards may warrant a "make whole" remedy at the conclusion of a ULP case. See DHHS, SSA and AFGE Gen. Comm., 50 FLRA 296, 299 (1995).

Impact does not make the standards negotiable. A proposal was not negotiable that management change performance standards as available space for employees was reduced. AFGE Local 12 and Dept. of Labor, 21 FLRA 1124, 1126-27 (1986). An impact proposal was nonnegotiable that required alteration of informal production standards after management changed work requirements. The proposal substantively limited the employer's discretion to determine quantity, quality, and timeliness of work production required of employees. DHHS, SSA, Baltimore and AFGE, 33 FLRA 454, 463, 465 (1988) (Proposal 5; no issue was raised as to whether the proposal constituted appropriate arrangement). The same case resolved as nonnegotiable Proposal 4, requiring a 90-day moratorium on citing employees for errors made relative to the employer's new work requirements. Impact bargaining as to performance levels does not extend to setting levels of performance for employees differing from the levels the employer plans to implement. The proposal interfered with managerial rights to assign and direct work. IRS Brookhaven Serv. Ctr. and NTEU Chap. 99, 14 FLRA 766, 767-68 (1984). The institution of a different means of reviewing work at an office constituted a change in working conditions within management's discretion, but required advance notice to the union for the purpose of impact and implementation bargaining. IRS and NTEU, 4 FLRA 488, 498-99 (1980) (ALJ Decision); see VA, Bd. of Veterans Appeals and AFGE Local 17, 68 FLRA 170 (2015) (Member Pizzella dissenting) (arbitration award review: under ULP theory, a new method of determining if employees were meeting performance expectations was subject to impact bargaining).
