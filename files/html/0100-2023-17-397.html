2. Allocation of Arbitration Costs

Issues regarding allocation of the fees and costs of arbitration should be raised with the arbitrator first; they will not first be considered by FLRA on exceptions. See NAIL Local 17 and Dept. of Navy, Fleet Readiness Ctr., 68 FLRA 279, 280-81 (2015) (FLRA declined to second-guess arbitrator's decision to split arbitration costs, fees under contract permitting equal allocation in split decision; concept of "prevailing party" applicable to counsel fee application had no application to contract cost allocation provision); Dept. of Energy, WAPA and AFGE Local 3824, 67 FLRA 376, 377 (2014) (recon. den., Dept. of Energy, WAPA and AFGE Local 3824, 67 FLRA 715 (2014)) ("Section 8.5 provides, in pertinent part, that '[t]he [A]rbitrator shall be empowered to determine the percentages o[f] his/her fee for which each party will be liable' and that, '[i]n making this determination, the [A]rbitrator shall be bound by the relative merits of each party's case.' However, there is no indication in the record that the Agency raised Section 8.5 at arbitration. Because the Agency could have presented its argument to the Arbitrator, but did not do so, we find that §§ 2425.4(c) and 2429.5 bar the Agency's essence exception."); Dept. of Treasury, IRS and NTEU Chap. 78, 63 FLRA 567, 573 n.6 (2009) ("There is no indication in the record that the Agency's argument concerning the apportionment of the Arbitrator's fees, pursuant to Article 43 of the parties' CBA, was raised before the Arbitrator. This issue could, and should, have been presented to the Arbitrator. Accordingly, we dismiss this exception because its consideration is barred by § 2429.5. See SSA, Office of Hearings and Appeals, Falls Church, Va., 59 FLRA 507, 509 (2003)."); cf. Dept. of Treasury, IRS and NTEU Chap. 97, 71 FLRA 192, 192 (2019) (Member DuBester dissenting) ("disagreement about how to interpret and apply a contractual cost-shifting provision is a 'grievance' within the meaning of § 7103(a)(9) of the Federal Service Labor-Management Statute").

Disagreements with an arbitrator's findings and conclusion about compliance with the procedural requirements of a negotiated agreement do not ordinarily provide a basis for finding the award deficient, Army Eng'r Dist., Los Angeles and NFFE Local No. 777, 12 FLRA 181, 181-82 (1983), and included within the category of procedural disputes are those involving allocations of the costs of arbitration. See NAIL Local 5 and DOD, DLA, 65 FLRA 502, 505 (2011) (cost allocation determination by arbitrator could not be characterized as irrational under contract); NFFE Local 2276 and USDA, Forest Serv., 61 FLRA 387, 390 (2005) (FLRA deferred to arbitrator's interpretation of contract clause permitting him to allocate arbitration costs); NAGE Local R4-27 and DOD, DCA, Ft. Lee, 60 FLRA 14, 16 (2004) (although contract stated loser was to pay arbitration fees, arbitrator could split fees when union prevailed on arbitrability issue and agency prevailed on merits); Dept. of Air Force, Dover AFB and AFGE Local 1709, 59 FLRA 827, 829 (2004) (arbitrator could properly split costs of arbitration when agreement, requiring loser to pay, was silent as to situations involving split decisions); Dept. of Air Force, Fairchild AFB and Fairchild Fed. Employees Union Local 987, 59 FLRA 434, 435 (2003) (sustaining award of arbitration costs between parties following interpretation of clause that permitted costs to be charged against losing party); NFFE Local 2030 and Dept. of Interior, BLM, Idaho Falls, 56 FLRA 667, 669-70 (2000) (denying exceptions to arbitrator's construction of contract clause establishing procedure for allocation costs of arbitration); NAGE Local R5-188 and Dept. of Air Force, Seymour Johnson AFB, 54 FLRA 76, 82 (1998) (arbitrator not bound by concept of "prevailing party" as used in counsel fee awards when determining whether award is split decision for purposes of allocating arbitration costs); NAGE Local R14-142 and DOD, DCA, 54 FLRA 14, 19 (1998) (declining to set aside award that equitably apportioned arbitration costs between parties despite contract clause calling on losing party to pay costs); NFFE Local 11 and Dept. of Air Force, Fairchild AFB, 50 FLRA 528, 529-30 (1995) (denying exceptions to award that declared there was no losing party, with result that costs were not assessed; contract had provision that loser pay costs unless arbitrator declares there is no losing party); DHHS, SSA, Office of Hearings and Appeals and AFGE Council 215, 48 FLRA 833, 837-38 (1993) (FLRA declined to sustain exceptions to award assessing against management portion of costs of hearing time attributable to improper agency actions; although contract required equal division of arbitration costs, arbitrator was free to formulate issues unless parties reached stipulation); Dept. of Air Force, McGuire AFB and AFGE Local 1778, 48 FLRA 740, 742 (1993) (denying exceptions by union concerning interpretation of contract clause specifying basis for allocating costs of arbitration between union and employer). But see Dept. of Air Force, Luke AFB and AFGE Local 1547, 62 FLRA 214, 216 (2007) (setting aside award varying contract provision that parties split arbitration fees: "Permitting arbitrators to add general exceptions to such agreements where the parties have not agreed to them would eliminate the predictability that such agreements provide and could chill a party from invoking arbitration in situations where its resources are limited."); Dept. of Air Force, Air Force Academy and AFGE Local 1967, 59 FLRA 540, 541 (2003) (setting aside portion of award allocating all arbitration costs against agency when contract clearly required equal divisions of costs and left arbitrator no discretion; contrary interpretation failed to draw essence from contract).

The right to determine the agency budget is not infringed by an award directing management to pay the full cost of a transcript. The award does not require the agency to include a particular program or operation in its budget. Only if the agency makes a substantial demonstration that an increase in costs is significant and unavoidable and is not offset by compensating benefits can an otherwise negotiable matter be found to violate the agency's right to determine its budget under § 7106(a) of the Statute. DHHS, SSA and AFGE, 26 FLRA 6, 8-9 (1987). When, however, a contract required the parties to split the cost of a transcript, a portion of an award requiring the agency to pay the full cost was set aside because the award failed to draw its essence from the agreement. SBA and AFGE Local 2951, 55 FLRA 179, 182 (1999). Note that the union may be able to secure a transcript from the agency through a statutory information request, even if the collective bargaining agreement requires a party who requests a transcript to pay its full cost. See VAMC, Ft. Lyon and AFGE Local 2430, 41 FLRA 1091, 1092-93 (1991). [Refer to Chapter 6, "Arbitration Procedures," "Grievance Procedures."]
