broida flra Chapter 04: Subjects of Bargaining: Procedures for Negotiability Determinations Chapter 04: Subjects of Bargaining: Procedures for Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 04: Framework for Negotiability Appeals, Section F: Disapproval of Agreement by Agency Head, Subsection 01: Master Agreement; Ratification; Time for Petition for Review

1. Master Agreement; Ratification; Time for Petition for Review

Submission of an agreement to an agency head for approval under § 7114(c) serves as the written request for an allegation of nonnegotiability under FLRA regulations. NFFE Local 1505 and Dept. of Interior, Nat'l Park Serv., Roosevelt-Vanderbilt Nat'l Historical Site, 7 FLRA 608, 608 (1982) (referencing prior regulation, 5 CFR 2424.3); cf. AFGE Local 3342 and DHHS, SSA, N.Y. Region, 36 FLRA 367, 370 (1990) (submission of agreement to agency head for approval or disapproval under § 7114(c) constitutes request for allegation of nonnegotiability; 15-day time limit to file petition for review starts on date disapproval is served on union). The agency head may delegate to subordinate officials the authority to approve or disapprove agreements under § 7114(c). POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 48 FLRA 546, 547 (1993) (no evidence that Secretary of Commerce had delegated to Commissioner of Patents and Trademarks authority to approve or disapprove a labor agreement under statute). If a contract has an automatic renewal or "roll over" provision, the agreement is subject to review as it renews itself. See Kan. Army Nat'l Guard and ACT, Kan. Army Chap., 47 FLRA 937, 942 (1993) (citing AFGE Local 1931 and Dept. of Navy, Naval Weapons Station, Concord, 32 FLRA 1023, 1071 (1988), rev'd as to other matters, No. 88-7408 (9th Cir. 1989)).

The agency head reviews an agreement that resulted from the parties' agreement on substantive matters. The agency head does not second-guess decisions already made on relative merits of proposals considered and accepted by the parties. The agency head review is on the legality of the provisions in the agreement of the parties. If the agreement is submitted to an agency head and portions are rejected as inconsistent with law, rule, or regulation, the agency is essentially alleging that the matters are not within the agency's duty to bargain. The union may seek review of the negotiability issues presented by the agency disapproval. ACT, Pa. State Council and Adjutant Gen., Dept. of Military Affairs, Pa., 7 FLRA 346, 347 (1981). The agency head's disapproval constitutes an allegation of nonnegotiability of the disapproved provisions. It is immaterial, once the provisions are disapproved by the agency head, that the matters covered by the disapproval had "been negotiated to closure." AFGE Local 1815 and Army Aviation Ctr., Ft. Rucker, 53 FLRA 606, 609 (1997). The agency's timely disapproval is critical to the processing of a negotiability appeal. A petition for review of negotiability issues filed by a union in response to an agency head disapproval that is not timely served on the union does not raise negotiability issues that may be addressed by FLRA under § 7117 of the Statute. AFGE Local 3172 and DHHS, SSA, San Jose Dist. Office, 46 FLRA 1515, 1515 (1993).

The 15-day limit for the union's negotiability appeal to FLRA starts on the date the disapproval is served on the union. See IAM Local 2333 and Dept. of Air Force, Hq. 2750th ABW, 46 FLRA 1208, 1209 (1993). The same procedure is followed as to agreements that are called something other than contracts, e.g., memoranda of understanding. See, e.g., AFGE Local 1760 and DHHS, SSA, 28 FLRA 1142, 1142-43 (1987). FLRA honors an agreement of the parties that the agency head's disapproval of various agreement provisions would lead to renegotiation of the disapproved provisions rather than the filing of a petition for review, saving remaining negotiability disputes for resolution through the appeals process. Tidewater Va. FEMTC and Dept. of Navy, Norfolk Naval Shipyard, Portsmouth, 42 FLRA 845, 848 (1991). If the parties determine that a proposal declared nonnegotiable by the agency will, if ruled negotiable by FLRA, be resubmitted for negotiation under a reopener clause of an existing contract, FLRA will dismiss the negotiability appeal as premature. A decision would be advisory. Dept. of Treasury, IRS and NTEU, 7 FLRA 610, 610-11 (1982). [Refer later in this chapter to "Mootness; Changed Circumstances," concerning mootness of negotiability appeals.]

For the purpose of determining when the period commences for filing a negotiability appeal, an agency head may not review and reject provisions of a contract before the contract is executed. AFGE, Nat'l INS Council and DOJ, INS, 8 FLRA 347, 380-81 (1982). If there is a dispute concerning the date of execution of the agreement, FLRA considers conflicts in evidence and makes the resolution it deems most reasonable. Bremerton Metal Trades Council and Naval Supply Ctr. Puget Sound, 32 FLRA 643, 644 (1988).

Agency head review of an agreement does not occur until after ratification makes the agreement effective. The union's right to ratification as a condition to agreement need not be expressed in ground rules; it need simply be reserved and that reservation must be communicated to the employer. If the agreement does not survive ratification, the employer commits a ULP by then refusing to reopen negotiations. SSA and AFGE Council 220, 46 FLRA 1404, 1405-06 (1993), stated at n.4:

The Respondent's argument that the Union's failure to ratify the MOU was of no effect because it occurred subsequent to the Respondent's review and approval of the MOU under section 7114(c) of the Statute is misplaced. As the Union properly had conditioned execution of the MOU on ratification, the section 7114 review, which is triggered by execution of an agreement, was not effective. See Patent Office Professional Association and U.S. Department of Commerce, Patent and Trademark Office, 41 FLRA 795, 803 (1991) ("the date of execution that triggers the time limits for...review under section 7114(c)(2) relates to the date on which no further action is necessary to finalize a complete agreement[.]").

See Ft. Bragg Ass'n of Teachers and Dept. of Army, Ft. Bragg Sch., 44 FLRA 852, 857 (1992) ("The date of execution that triggers the time limits for agency head review under section 7114(c)(2) relates to the date on which no further action is necessary to finalize a complete agreement. However, in this case, there is evidence that the parties intended to have further action after the local negotiators signed the agreement. Ratification issues aside, under AFGE Local 1770 and DOD Domestic Dependent Elementary and Secondary Sch., 64 FLRA 953, 953-54 (2010), ordinarily the execution of an agreement, triggering the time for agency head review, occurs with the last signature of the two parties:

A dispute arose as to whether the Agency head's disapproval of the provisions was timely; accordingly, the Authority issued a Notice and Order to Show Cause (Notice). In the Notice, the Authority explained that a union's petition for review of negotiability issues in response to an untimely Agency head disapproval does not raise negotiability issues that the Authority may address under § 7117 of the Statute. Instead, the Authority explained, absent a timely disapproval, "the agreement becomes effective on the 31st day after its execution, subject to the Statute and applicable law, rule, or regulation." Id. (citing AFGE, Local 3172, 46 FLRA 1515, 1515-16 (1993)).

The Union asserts that the thirty-day time period for disapproving the agreement began on either July 22, 2008, when the Union ratified the agreement, or on August 7, 2008, when the Union's negotiators signed the signature page. Therefore, the Union contends, the Agency head's disapproval of the agreement on September 18, 2008, was untimely. The Agency contends that the Agency head disapproval was timely because the thirty-day period did not begin until August 21, 2008, the date on which both parties re-signed the agreement.

As the Union explains, it submitted an undated version of the signature page to the Agency on August 7. The Union explains that this was done in response to a message from an Agency negotiator stating, in part, "Don't date it; I'll fill in a date after everyone signs." The Union contends that the Agency, by waiting two weeks before signing the agreement and submitting it to the Agency head, "gave itself an unreasonable extension between the date the contract should be considered to have been executed and the submission for [§] 7114 Agency Head Review."

Under § 7114(c) of the Statute, an agreement between an agency and an exclusive representative shall be subject to approval by the head of the agency. The agency head is required to act within thirty days from the date that the agreement is executed. If the agency head does not approve or disapprove the agreement within the thirty-day period, the agreement takes effect automatically on the thirty-first day. See, e.g., Patent Office Prof. Ass'n, 41 FLRA 795, 802 (1991) (POPA). Where an agency head timely disapproves an agreement under § 7114(c) of the Statute, the agreement does not take effect and is not binding on the parties.

The date of execution that triggers the time limit for agency head review under § 7114(c)(2) relates to the date on which no further action is necessary to finalize a complete agreement. POPA, 41 FLRA at 803. The Authority has recognized that the date of execution normally is the date the parties sign the agreement. Fort Bragg Ass'n of Teachers, 44 FLRA 852, 857 (1992). Here, the agreement was not finalized until both parties signed it, which occurred on August 21, 2008, when the Agency signed the agreement and placed a date on it. See Bremerton Metal Trades Council, 32 FLRA 643, 644 (1988) (date appearing on the signature page is the best evidence of the execution date). Contrary to the Union's contention, § 7114(c) does not require one party to sign an agreement within a certain number of days after the other party has signed it and does not impose any deadline on contract execution.

The decision by an agency head to disapprove a contract is of significance in future negotiations. "Where a petition for review concerns a dispute as to a proposal that is not substantively changed from a provision that was previously disapproved by the Agency head under § 7114(c) review, the effect of the petition is to seek review of the previous disapproval." By that reasoning, the petition for review is untimely if not filed within 15 days from the agency head's disapproval. NFFE Local 284 and DOD, Naval Air Eng'g Ctr., Lakehurst, 39 FLRA 973, 974 (1991) (local agreement; same result would occur with national agreement).
