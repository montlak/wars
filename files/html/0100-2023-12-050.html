4. Supplemental Submissions; Issues Belatedly Raised

FLRA will not consider submissions in negotiability cases other than those called for in the Statute and regulations, unless additional information is requested by FLRA, or unless FLRA, in its discretion, grants permission to file the submission. AFGE Local 695 and Dept. of Treasury, U.S. Mint, Denver, 3 FLRA 43, 44 (1980); see NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, IRS Office of Chief Counsel, 66 FLRA 809, 810 (2012) (recon. den., 66 FLRA 1030 (2012) ("In negotiability cases, the Authority's Regulations specifically provide for the filing of a union petition for review, an agency statement of position, a union response, and an agency reply. 5 C.F.R. §§ 2424.22, 2424.24, 2424.25, 2424.26. They do not provide for a union response to an agency reply. IFPTE, Local 3, 57 FLRA 699, 699 n.1 (2002)."). FLRA may request supplementation of initial submissions if they have not provided accurate or complete descriptions of the status of the parties' dispute. AFGE Local 3529 and DCAA, 3 FLRA 301, 301 (1980). If FLRA permits the agency to file a supplemental response to an argument first raised in the union's response, FLRA disregards argument that goes beyond the point newly raised by the union. See AFGE Council of Prison Locals and DOJ, Bureau of Prisons, 47 FLRA 836, 837-38 (1993).

FLRA accepted a supplemental statement from an agency addressing an appropriate arrangements argument that was first mentioned in the union petition for review but only fully developed in the union's response to the agency statement of position. AFGE Local 3295 and Dept. of Treasury, Office of Thrift Supervision, 44 FLRA 63, 63-64 (1992); see AFGE Local 1748 and Dept. of Labor, Kansas City, 73 FLRA 233, 234 (2022), supra; NAGE Local R1-187 and VA Boston Healthcare Sys., 64 FLRA 627, 627-28 (2010) (accepting supplemental agency submission unopposed by union); IFPTE Local 49 and Army Corps of Eng'rs, S. Pac. Div., 52 FLRA 830, 834-35 (1996) (FLRA accepts and relies on supplemental submission from union to explain the meaning of proposal); AFGE Local 2879 and DHHS, SSA, Office of Hearings and Appeals, San Diego, 49 FLRA 1074, 1077 (1994) ("Our Rules and Regulations do not authorize the filing of additional arguments in the context of a request to resume processing of a negotiability appeal.... We will consider the Union's supplemental brief...to the extent that it relates to attachments that were newly filed with the Agency's corrected statement of position."); AFGE Local 868 and Dept. of Interior, Chattanooga and Chickamauga Nat'l Military Park, 45 FLRA 224, 225 (1992) ("[Where] a union makes a bare claim in its petition for review that a proposal is an appropriate arrangement...and then makes specific arguments in support of that claim for the first time in its response to the agency's statement of position, we will grant the agency's request to file a supplemental statement to address those specific arguments and will provide the union an opportunity to respond."); AFGE Local 1923 and DHHS, HCFA, Baltimore, 44 FLRA 1405, 1413 (1992) ("As the Union raised arguments in support of its appropriate arrangements contention for the first time in its response to the Agency's statement of position, we will consider the Agency's supplemental submission addressing the Union's arguments. See 5 C.F.R. § 2424.8."); FEMTC of Charleston and Dept. of Navy, Charleston Naval Shipyard, 44 FLRA 683, 684 (1992) (rejecting agency's contention that union did not properly raise arguments in petition for review, adding "[w]e will, however, consider the arguments raised by the Agency in its supplemental submission in order to provide a more complete record on which to base our negotiability determinations.").

FLRA granted a motion to strike a portion of an agency's reply when the agency reply to the union's response raised a negotiability argument for the first time, NTEU and DHS, C&BP, 60 FLRA 367, 368-69 (2004):

In its Reply to the Union's Response, the Agency asserts, for the first time, that Proposal 10(a) concerns the technology of performing work. The Union moves to strike that portion of the Agency's Reply. The Union, citing 5 C.F.R. § 2424.26(a), argues that the Agency should not be permitted to make an argument concerning the technology of performing work because the Agency did not raise that argument in response to any of the arguments advanced by the Union in its Response.[5]

[5] 5 C.F.R. § 2424.26(a) provides, in pertinent part:

The purpose of the [A]gency's reply is to inform the Authority and the exclusive representative whether and why it disagrees with any facts or arguments made for the first time in the exclusive representative's response.

Under the Authority's Regulations, an agency's reply is "specifically limited to the matters raised for the first time in the exclusive representative's response." 5 C.F.R. § 2424.26(c). Moreover, the Authority's Regulations do not permit an agency to subsequently raise an argument that could have been raised in a statement of position but was not. 5 C.F.R. § 2424.32(c)(ii). Here, the Agency could have raised its § 7106(b)(1) argument in its SOP. Having failed to do so, it cannot raise the argument in its Reply.

The Agency, citing a number of cases, claims that the Authority has permitted parties to raise an issue for the first time in a later stage of the negotiability appeal process. But, none of the cases cited involved situations where, as here, an agency, in direct violation of the Authority's Regulations, attempted to raise an argument for the first time in its Reply. The Agency also claims that because the matter is electively negotiable under § 7106(b)(1), it can terminate bargaining at any point. However, because the Agency failed to assert that argument in its SOP, that issue is not properly before the Authority. Therefore, the Union's motion to strike that portion of the Agency's Reply is granted and the Agency's argument based upon the technology used to perform its work will not be considered.

See Antilles Consol. Educ. Assn. and DOD Domestic Dependent Elementary & Secondary Schools, 61 FLRA 327, 330-31 (2005) (declining to consider agency's claim of nonnegotiability not responsive to any of matters raised by union in its response).
