6. Nonappropriated Fund

A nonappropriated fund employee did not earn service credit for lack of a federal appointment, McDaniel v. OPM, 41 MSPR 634, 636 (1989):

We find that the present case is governed by our decision in Besozzi v. Office of Personnel Management, 40 M.S.P.R. 15 (1989). In Besozzi, we found that an appellant is not entitled to credit for civil service retirement purposes under 5 U.S.C. § 8332(b)(16) where the appellant was not an "employee" as described in 5 U.S.C. § 2105(a). 40 M.S.P.R. at 18. In order to meet this requirement, an appellant must have been appointed in the civil service by a Federal official acting in his official capacity, been engaged in the performance of a Federal function under authority of law or executive act, and been under the supervision of a named Federal official while engaged in the performance of the duties of the position. Id. The appellant in Besozzi failed to make this showing because the record did not contain an SF-50 or SF-52, evidence suggested that social security was being withheld from her pay, and she indicated on her application to make deposit or redeposit to the civil service retirement system that civil service retirement deductions were not withheld from her salary during her employment with the Central Base Welfare Fund (CBF). Id.

The appellant in the present case has failed to establish that she was an "employee" within the meaning of 5 U.S.C. § 2105(c) for the same reasons discussed in Besozzi. The record does not indicate that an SF-50 was ever issued regarding the appellant's appointment, the appellant's CBF contract suggests that social security was being withheld from her pay, and her application to make deposit or redeposit to the civil service retirement system indicates that civil service retirement deductions were not withheld from her salary during her employment with the CBF. We, therefore, conclude that the appellant has not shown that she was a Federal employee entitled to credit for civil service retirement purposes under 5 U.S.C. § 8332(b)(16), regardless of whether the duties she performed were consistent with those described in that section.
