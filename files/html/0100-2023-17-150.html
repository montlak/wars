broida flra Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards, Subchapter 08: Grounds for Review, Section D: Award is Based on "Nonfact", Subsection 07: Examples of Awards Set Aside Based on Nonfact, Part a: Remand for Clarification Required
a. Remand for Clarification Required

If an arbitrator does make an error of fact related to the outcome of the case, but it cannot be determined if the outcome would have been different had the error not occurred, FLRA may remand the case to the parties so that they may return the case to the arbitrator for clarification, with leave to file exceptions to the clarified award. Norfolk Naval Shipyard, Portsmouth and Tidewater Virginia FEMTC, 9 FLRA 538, 540 (1982). In GSA Region 9 and AFGE Council 236, 48 FLRA 1348, 1358 (1994) (following remand, 53 FLRA 925 (1997)), an arbitrator declined to consider a claim based on his erroneous finding that a specific citation for an asserted violation was not provided until the union filed its posthearing brief. FLRA was not persuaded that the same result would have been reached if the arbitrator recognized that the citation was earlier provided:

...If we are to find that the award is not based on a nonfact, we must find that the Arbitrator would have reached the same result despite his misapprehension. The Agency's argument based on the merits of the claims is irrelevant to such a finding.

A case was remanded when findings as to environmental differential pay entitlement appeared in error, but it could not be determined from the award whether the error was the basis for the resultant award. Dept. of Army, 6th Infantry Div., Ft. Richardson and AFGE Local 1834, Fort Wainwright, 35 FLRA 42, 46 (1990). FLRA directed the parties to resubmit an arbitration case to an arbitrator for clarification and interpretation when the findings of the arbitrator on the facts appeared to support the disciplinary action, but the conclusion drawn by the arbitrator, based on those facts, was to set the disciplinary action aside. VA and AFGE Local 3692, 19 FLRA 164, 166 (1985). Another case warranting remand for clarification from an arbitrator determined that an arbitrator erred in not counting a reprimand as a prior "offense" for purposes of application of a table of penalties. Dept. of Army, Natick Research Dev. and Eng'g Ctr. and NAGE Local R1-34, 44 FLRA 1251, 1254 (1992). But see DOD, Hale Koa Hotel and SEIU Local 556, 55 FLRA 651, 652-53 (1999) (interpretation of table of penalties incorporated into negotiated agreement could not be challenged based on nonfact when matter was disputed before arbitrator; Natick distinguished).

A case involving a claim for backpay for a temporary promotion was remanded when, in arbitration, the agency conceded that the grievant was qualified for a temporary promotion for a period of time, but the arbitrator found the grievant was not qualified to receive a temporary promotion. FLRA could not determine whether correction of the error would have resulted in a different result under the nonfact analysis. AFGE Local 779 and Dept. of Air Force, Sheppard AFB, 64 FLRA 672, 674 (2010). If it is unclear how the arbitrator resolved application of different statutes, each having a bearing on the possible outcome of a disciplinary case, on an exception claiming a "nonfact" determination FLRA may remand the award to the parties for resubmission to the arbitrator and proper, reasoned determination of the application of the statutes to the facts and contract. See Dept. of Army, Ft. Campbell and AFGE Local 2022, 46 FLRA 1304, 1308-09 (1993) (concerning application of federal statutes bearing on discipline imposed for alleged misuse of government-owned vehicle). FLRA has indicated, however, in other contexts, e.g., an award that is deficient under the Back Pay Act, that it will not remand the case to the arbitrator for reconsideration. VA Winston-Salem and AFGE Local 2880, 27 FLRA 44, 46 (1987). [Refer earlier in this chapter to "Procedure After Review."]
