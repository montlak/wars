4. Specific Instructions

Proposals outlining specific instructions for the performance of various tasks by employees are nonnegotiable. They infringe the right to assign work. AFGE Local 3511 and VA Hosp., San Antonio, 12 FLRA 76, 81 (1983). Proposals that assigned specific times for various duties of teachers were nonnegotiable because they interfered with work assignment. NFFE Local 1263 and Defense Language Inst., Foreign Language Ctr., 7 FLRA 723, 725 (1982). A proposal that employees need only conduct a set number of inspections per hour was a nonnegotiable limitation on management's right to assign and determine the amount of work required of an employee. AFGE Meat Graders Council and USDA, Food Safety and Quality Serv., Meat Grading Branch, 8 FLRA 118, 119 (1982).

A proposal was nonnegotiable that allowed nurses to decide to withhold medications they might consider unsafe for patients. Those decisions were to be made by physicians unencumbered by possibly conflicting rules of conduct in negotiated agreements. The proposal was an impermissible infringement on the right to assign work. VA Staff Nurses Council, Local 5032 and VAMC, Wood, 29 FLRA 849, 854-56 (1987). A proposal that a specified group of employees not administer medications infringed on the right to assign work, AFGE Local 1985 and VAMC Dublin, 55 FLRA 1145, 1147-48 (1999) (Proposal 1), as did proposals that permitted employees to decide whether and how to use an electronic paperwork process. The proposals were negotiable at the election of the agency because they concerned the methods or means of performing work under § 7106(b)(1). AFGE Local 3529 and DOD, DCAA Central Region, 57 FLRA 172, 175-76 (2001) (Proposals 1 and 10). Nonnegotiable was a proposal that relieved auditors from the responsibility of ensuring that certain materials were included in agency working papers. The proposal was not an appropriate arrangement because, although it alleviated the possibility of lowered appraisals, it excessively interfered with the right to assign work by precluding management from assigning the task and evaluating the result. AFGE Local 3529 and DOD, DCAA Central Region, supra, 57 FLRA at 179 (Proposal 9). [Refer in this chapter to "Protection of Employee Licensing; Adherence to Certification Requirements," "Critical Elements and Performance Standards."]

Confirming that it never hurts to ask, FLRA approved Proposal 4 as negotiable in AFGE Local 727 and Court Serv. & Offender Supervision Agency, 59 FLRA 674, 677 (2004), obliging management to consider permitting court supervision officers to phone a court to check in with the court until their case is called, instead of reporting in person to court. Proposals requiring management to "consider" certain matters in exercising a right under § 7106 do not affect the right.
