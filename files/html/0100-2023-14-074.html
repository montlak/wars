B. SPECIFIC LIMITS ON ASSIGNMENTS OF WORK

Limits on the ability of management to assign to unit employees certain security, repair, and maintenance activities were nonnegotiable. The proposals directly interfered with management's right to assign work. Int'l Ass'n of Firefighters, Local F-215 and Hq., 15th Infantry Div., Ft. Polk, 8 FLRA 417, 418 (1982). [Refer in this chapter to "Safety Restrictions; Risks to Safety and Health."] It was a violation of the right to assign work to negotiate a proposal that ordinarily precluded assignment of tasks to nurses that included mopping of floors, cleaning and moving beds, emptying garbage, secretarial duties. New York State Nurses Ass'n and VAMC, Bronx, 11 FLRA 578, 579 (1983); see NFFE Local 1655 and Dept. of Military Affairs, Illinois Air Nat'l Guard, 35 FLRA 815, 816 (1990) (also nonnegotiable: restrictions, contained in Proposals 1-4 and 6, on duties involving cleaning of work areas, mopping, dusting, and policing of trash, and precluding discipline if certain areas were not kept clean by particular employees); Illinois Nurses Ass'n and VAMC, N. Chicago, 27 FLRA 714, 744 (1987) (Proposal 11) (same type of proposal, same result as in VAMC Bronx, supra). A broader preclusion of assignment of registered nurses to replace licensed practical nurses or nursing assistants was also a nonnegotiable infringement on the right to assign work. VA Staff Nurses Council, Local 5032 and VAMC, Wood, 29 FLRA 849, 859-60 (1987) (Proposal 6). A proposal was not negotiable that prohibited assignment of certain duties to gate guards, i.e., duties relating to enforcement of laws, rules, and regulations other than those pertaining to entrance onto the post. The proposal precluded an agency from assigning specific duties. AFGE Local 2302 and Army Armor Ctr., 19 FLRA 778, 781 (1985) (Proposal 2).

The ability of management to assign work is Improperly limited through proposals requiring blocks of time to perform tasks. A proposal interfered with the right to assign work that required management to assign specific amounts of time for teachers involved in accreditation activities and committee activities. Panama Canal Fed'n of Teachers, Local 29 and DODDS, 19 FLRA 814, 819-21 (1985) (Proposals 7 and 12); see AFGE Local 1164 and SSA Dist. Office, New Bedford, 54 FLRA 1327, 1343 (1998) (Proposal 8 nonnegotiable, calling on agency to wait for thirty minutes before assigning additional interviews to a claims representative); AFGE, Nat'l Council of Social Security Payment Ctr. Locals and SSA, Office of Program Serv. Ctrs., 7 FLRA 818, 819 (1982) (proposals that allowed employees to decide to perform some work functions, e.g., locating folders pertaining to their work, in some areas but not others, are nonnegotiable intrusions on the right to assign work).

If the proposed limitation on a work assignment violates a management right, as it likely will, FLRA will consider the proposal under an arrangements test, but the union must show how a proposal will compensate for or address the claimed anticipated adverse effects of the agency's actions. See AFGE Local 3509 and DHHS, SSA, Greenwood, 46 FLRA 1590, 1614-15 (1993) (Proposal 13). A proposal to meet changing audit requirements of the agency, i.e., work evaluation methods, that precluded the agency from assigning certain types of work to employees being evaluated, was not an appropriate arrangement. AFGE Local 1760 and DHHS, SSA, 23 FLRA 168, 171-74 (1986) (Proposals 2-6).

A limitation or requirement concerning assignment of work may be nonnegotiable as a procedure or arrangement, but it may be permissibly negotiable under 5 USC 7106(b)(1), allowing the agency to bargain if it so chooses on the numbers, types, and grades of employees or positions assigned to any organizational subdivision, work project, or tour of duty, or on the technology, methods, and means of performing work. See AFGE Local 1164 and SSA Dist. Office, New Bedford, 54 FLRA 1327, 1350-52 (1998) (agency could elect to negotiate on various proposals for allocation of times and rotation schemes for interviewing tasks assigned to claims representatives).
