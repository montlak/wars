a. Decision Based on Affidavits, Documents

The Board more freely substitutes its judgment on credibility issues if the appeal is decided by the AJ on affidavits without a hearing; there is no need for special deference to the AJ if the demeanor of the witness whose credibility is disputed is not considered by the AJ through the observation of live testimony. See Marczewski v. OPM, 80 MSPR 343, 346-47 ¶ 5 (1998); Hillis v. OPM, 40 MSPR 592, 596 (1989) ("In reviewing the factual findings of one of its administrative judges, the Board is free to substitute its own determinations of fact...giving the...judge's findings only as much weight as may be warranted by the record and by the strength of the judge's reasoning....This is particularly true where, as in the case here, the...judge's findings of fact are based upon the written record and not the demeanor of the witnesses."); Moen v. Dept. of Transp., 28 MSPR 556, 560 (1985) ("while there was no hearing in this case, at the request of the appellant, the affidavits serve the same purpose as that of live testimony under oath"); cf. Zeidat v. VA, 59 MSPR 106, 110 (1993) ("[t]he AJ discounted...statements, citing to the appellant's own testimony for evidence that the two men worked at times in relative isolation from one another. In fact, these references flatly mischaracterize the appellant's testimony."); Cochran v. INS, 16 MSPR 343, 346 (1983) (threat case: the Board makes its own findings when demeanor observations of the AJ were not so much at issue as were the AJ's conclusions concerning record evidence). However, the Board may rely on the reasoning of the AJ and adopt his decision resolving an appeal presented on affidavits without separately detailing its own findings on those affidavits. Donato v. DOD, 34 MSPR 385, 389-90 (1987); see Howard v. Dept. of Interior, DE-1221-14-0009-W-1 (NP 11/7/2014) ("Although this case was decided on the written record, the Board has approved adapting to such cases the principles for resolving credibility issues. Goode v. Defense Logistics Agency, 45 M.S.P.R. 671, 674 n.2 (1990). In reaching his credibility findings, the administrative judge applied the appropriate factors set forth by the Board in Hillen v. Department of the Army, 35 M.S.P.R. 453, 458 (1987)."); Goode v. DLA, 45 MSPR 671, 674 n.2 (1990) ("[T]he administrative judge properly adapted the principles for resolving credibility issues to this case where no testimony occurred").
