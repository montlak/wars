c. EEO Complaint

An EEO complaint does not constitute an election of remedies precluding an IRA appeal, Collins v. Dept. of Transp., 89 MSPR 582, 589 ¶ 14 (2001):

The agency also appears to argue in its response that the Board lacks IRA jurisdiction over the appellant's removal because the appellant filed an equal employment opportunity (EEO) complaint regarding his removal, and the complaint is currently pending before the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC). The agency does not refer to, and we are unaware of, any law or regulation that deprives the Board of IRA jurisdiction over a personnel action on the basis that the personnel action is also the subject of an EEO complaint. Rather, IRA appellants are not subject to the appellate procedures of 5 U.S.C. § 7701 and the provisions of 5 U.S.C. § 7702; should they believe that the personnel action that forms the basis for their complaint is tainted by discrimination prohibited by 5 U.S.C. § 2302(b)(1), they "have at their disposal the...[EEOC] and its long-established procedures as an avenue to seek redress for alleged discrimination." Marren v. Department of Justice, 51 M.S.P.R. 632, 639-42 (1991), aff'd, 980 F.2d 745 (Fed. Cir. 1992) (Table).

See Amirmokri v. Dept. of Energy, 310 Fed. Appx. 410 (Fed. Cir. 2009 NP) (appellant could pursue a mixed-case through the EEO process and into district court while separately pursuing a related IRA case through the Board and into the Federal Circuit); Horton v. Dept. of Navy, 47 MSPR 475, 479 (1991) ("[B]y setting forth time limits for tiling an IRA appeal without regard to the agency resolution of the EEO complaint, we find that the statutory language indicates by its own terms that IRA appeal rights are independent of the EEO complaint process and vest upon the expiration of the time limits set forth at 5 U.S.C. § 1214(a)(3). We note that to find otherwise would create an inescapable conflict between the time limits established at 5 U.S.C. § 7702 and those established at 5 U.S.C. § 1214(a)(3)."); cf. Gray v. MSPB, 650 Fed. Appx. 775, 777 (Fed. Cir. 2016 NP) ("Of relevance here, under the WPEA an aggrieved employee now has the right, under certain circumstances, to seek corrective action from the board when he or she suffers reprisal as a result of filing an EEO complaint. See 5 U.S.C. § 2302(b)(9)(A)(i) (prohibiting an agency from retaliating against an employee for 'the exercise of any appeal, complaint, or grievance right' related to whistleblowing).").
