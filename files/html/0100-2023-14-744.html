5. National Guard

FLRA initially determined that a broad grievance procedure was negotiable for National Guard civilian technicians. Section 7121(c) of the Statute did not exclude technicians from its coverage; it was immaterial that adverse actions falling within the grievance system would be covered by a separate statutory procedure governing the technicians. 32 USC 709(e)(5), (6). It was the intent of Congress that employees subject to other personnel systems have the same option as employees covered by Title 5 to use a negotiated grievance procedure or an available appellate procedure when they wish to challenge those actions. NAGE Local R12-132 and California NG, 5 FLRA 201, 206 (1981), rev'd, California NG v. FLRA, 697 F.2d 874 (9th Cir. 1983); see NAGE Local R14-87 and Kansas Army Nat'l Guard, 3 FLRA 853, 856 (1980). The court in California NG decided that the Technicians Act plainly intended to leave to state adjutants general the final authority over adverse personnel actions against National Guard technicians (that decision also provided a summary of the history of the National Guard technicians' status as federal employees). Bowing to court decisions, FLRA determined that § 709(e) of the Technicians Act precludes use of grievance procedures as to separation, removal, discharge, suspension, furlough, RIF, and reduction in rank or compensation of technicians. ACT, Pennsylvania State Council and Pennsylvania Army and Air Nat'l Guard, 14 FLRA 38, 44 (1984) (Proposal 5) (citing Indiana Air Nat'l Guard v. FLRA, 712 F.2d 1187, 1191 (7th Cir. 1983)); see Montana Air Nat'l Guard v. FLRA, 730 F.2d 577, 578-79 (9th Cir. 1984); Nebraska, Military Dept., Office of Adjutant Gen. v. FLRA, 705 F.2d 945, 950-52 (8th Cir. 1983); New Jersey Air Nat'l Guard v. FLRA, 677 F.2d 276 (3d Cir. 1982); NFFE Local 1623 and S. Carolina NG, Columbia, 28 FLRA 633 (1987).

The National Guard may require that grievance provisions exclude topics within 32 USC 709(e)(5), § 709(e) of the Technicians Act of 1968. Nat'l Guard Bureau, Falls Church and AFGE, 16 FLRA 346, 348 (1984). A nonnegotiable proposal established a procedure for the processing, through the military chain of command, of military performance appraisals issued to National Guard Technicians. ACT, Pa. State Council and Adjutant Gen., Dept. of Military Affairs, Pa., 3 FLRA 50, 55 & n.12 (1980). FLRA concluded that the military status of the technician, who must, as a condition of employment, hold a military grade specified for his position, is not covered by the CSRA but is "totally mandated by law." The proposal concerned a subject that was not a "condition of employment." Id. at 55. The phrase excludes matters "specifically provided for by Federal statute." See 5 USC 7103(a)(14), (c). As to issues within the proper scope of the grievance procedure, it was permissible to negotiate for an expedited grievance procedure. ACT, Pa. State Council and Adjutant Gen. of Pa., 29 FLRA 1292, 1301 (1987) (Proposal 7).
