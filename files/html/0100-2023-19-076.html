b. Sexual Disorder

Sexual behavioral disorders are not protected under the Rehabilitation Act, Browder v. Dept. of Navy, 81 MSPR 71, 75-76 (1999):

[T]he disorder allegedly suffered by the appellant does not meet the definition of "disability" under the Rehabilitation Act. In its response to the petition for review, the agency correctly notes that the Rehabilitation Act was amended in 1992 to exclude persons with sexual behavior disorders such as paraphilia from the definition of "an individual with a disability." 29 USC § 706(8)(F) states as follows:

For the purposes of sections 791, 793, and 794 of this title, the term "individual with a disability" does not include an individual on the basis of--

(i) transvestitism, transsexualism, pedophilia, exhibitionism, voyeurism, gender identity disorders not resulting from physical impairments, or other sexual behavior disorders.[7]

[7] Section 791, as applicable here, refers to title I of the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 (42 USC § 21111 et seq.), which, in relevant part, contains the identical exclusionary language as that found in 29 USC § 706(8)(F)(i).

Because the appellant's alleged disorder is statutorily excluded from the definition of disability, the appellant has failed to state a cognizable claim of disability discrimination prohibited by 5 USC § 2302(b)(1). Therefore, as previously stated, the AJ's striking the appellant's claim on the basis that the appellant did not allege facts that, if proven, would establish a prima facie case of discrimination, was not harmful error because the appellant failed to allege prohibited discrimination.[8]

[8] See Den Hartog v. Wasatch Academy, 129 F.3d 1076, 1086, n.9 (10th Cir. 1997), where the court noted that the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) specifically states that psychological sexual disorders, such as the one involved in the instant case, are not disabilities and may not give rise to a claim under the ADA. Related cases include Equal Employment Opportunity Commission v. Board of Trustees of Wayne County Community College, 723 F.2d 509 (6th Cir. 1983) (EEOC lacked subject matter jurisdiction over age discrimination charge filed by individual excepted from definition of "employee" under the Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA)); accord Herold v. Hajoca Corp., 864 F.2d 317 (4th Cir. 1988), cert. denied, 490 U.S. 1107 (1989) (ADEA protects individuals who are at least 40 years of age and plaintiff may not make a claim unless he is in the protected class).
