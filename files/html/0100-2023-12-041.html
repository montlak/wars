6. Effect of EEOC Review on PFR

The Board has not defined the precise impact of an appeal to EEOC from an initial decision on a PFR or petition to reopen the same decision with the Board. In Brown v. NARA, 92 MSPR 95, 98 n.* (2002), the Board observed, as to an appeal reopened so that the Board could determine the effect of an OWCP decision on previously-sustained AWOL charges:

In the instant case, the appellant sought review of the Board's final decision from the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC), which issued a decision concurring in the Board's decision on March 22, 2002. Although we recognize that there are decisions suggesting that the Board loses jurisdiction over an appeal if the EEOC issues a final decision concurring in the Board's decision, see Austin v. Merit Systems Protection Board, 136 F.3d 782, 783 (Fed. Cir. 1998), and Williams v. U.S. Postal Service, 967 F.2d 577, 579 (Fed. Cir. 1992), we believe those decisions are distinguishable. In Austin, the court's statement that the Board lacks jurisdiction over an appeal once the EEOC issues a decision was arguably dictum because the court found that the EEOC's decision was a nullity and that the Board had jurisdiction to consider the appellant's petition for review. 136 F.3d at 786. In Williams, the appellant unsuccessfully sought EEOC and judicial review of a Board decision before returning to the Board, and the court held that the Board should have dismissed for lack of jurisdiction, not on timeliness grounds. 967 F.2d at 579. In the instant case, the Board's decision clearly put the parties on notice that further proceedings might be necessary based on new evidence (a final OWCP award). In contrast, the Board's decision in Williams did not contemplate reopening.
