15. MSPB Final Decision

The current practice of the Board is to issue decisions over the signature of the Board's Clerk. Dissenting or concurring opinions carry the name of the Board member author. Decisions are either precedential, carrying a Board citation by year and serial number of issuance, e.g., 2015 MSPB 4 (2015), or the decisions are what are known as nonprecedential final orders (NPFO), whose use began in about 2010, providing case analysis and result and carrying the date of issuance and the docket number assigned to the case at the region. Earlier, the Board used other means to issue decisions. Some particularly significant decisions were signed by one or more of the Board members. Before use of the NPFO, the Board often closed out appeals with what was described as a short form order, usually stating with not much reasoning (there might be the occasional footnote providing a little analysis) that the PFR was denied. Short form orders were not used to reverse or remand initial decisions, since the Board would be required to provide analysis of the reasons for its remand order.

For all cases, decided in all formats, before the decision of the Board members, the Board's Office of Appeals Counsel writes up a recommended decision and case analysis or justification through its staff attorneys. The OAC analysis is not given to the parties and it is not publicly available. Board members and their staff review the synopses of the cases and the proposed dispositions so that, according to the Board's Case Flow Management Study, the "Board Members will need to 'lay hands' on each case file for personal review only to the extent that such further review may be desired based on the staff briefing on an individual case." After the laying on of hands, the viewing of files on computer, or however the Board member arrives at his or her chosen result, the member then checks a box on a transmittal sheet indicating whether or not he or she concurs with the recommended decision and whether it may be issued by the Clerk. When it is ready, the Clerk issues a final decision based on the recommended decision and signs his name to that decision "for the Board." A copy of the transmittal sheet with the signatures or initials of the Board members (constituting the "voting record" of the Board), may be obtained for a particular case through a Freedom of Information Act request directed to the Board's Clerk in Washington, D.C.

The final decision will contain a statement of the administrative (EEOC) or judicial review rights that the Board thinks is appropriate to the character of the appeal. If the Board issues a decision with an incorrect or incomplete notice of review rights, the Board may reissue the decision with the proper notice, and the date of the final decision is the date of the corrected decision. See, e.g., Gay v. USPS, DA035386C9037-1 (12/17/1991) (not reported in MSPR). When the Board decision contains both an issuance date and a statement that it is the final Board order, the finality date is the date of issuance. See McLaughlin v. OPM, 62 MSPR 536, 548 (1994).
