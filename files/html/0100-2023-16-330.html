4. Format

Exceptions need not be particularly complex or innovative. FLRA determined to be sufficient in Dept. of Air Force, Scott AFB and NAGE Local R7-23, 34 FLRA 1129, 1134 (1990), exceptions that apparently did no more than incorporate a brief previously filed with the ALJ. Exceptions, however, ought to be specific. A party filing an exception must set forth "the specific findings, conclusions, determinations, rulings, or recommendations being challenged; the grounds relied upon; and the relief sought," under 5 CFR 2423.40(a)(1) (2022). DHHS, HCFA and AFGE Local 1923, 39 FLRA 120, 128 (1991); see Customs Serv., S. Cent. Reg. and NTEU Chap. 168, 53 FLRA 789, 794 (1997) (exception was sufficient that identified objectionable portion of judge's decision and informed FLRA of basis of exception); VAMC, Veterans Canteen Serv., Denver and AFGE Local 2241, 52 FLRA 16, 27 (1996) ("The Respondents' cross-exception seeking to dismiss the Medical Center as a respondent does not state any grounds or cite any authorities in support of the cross-exception. Therefore, the Respondents have not supported their cross-exception or provided a basis on which to reverse the Judge's decision on this point.").

FLRA will not likely strike exceptions for failure to conform to technical requirements relating to record citations, as long as the exceptions permit FLRA to identify the objections presented to the judge's decision. If new matter is submitted with exceptions, the opposing party may file a motion requesting FLRA to disregard the improper portion of the submission. FLRA will then consider the remaining arguments that are properly presented. See Dept. of Transp. and NATCA Y90 Local, 47 FLRA 110, 119-20 (1993); Eisenhower VAMC, Leavenworth and AFGE Local 85, 44 FLRA 1362, 1363 n.* (1992) (striking evidence that was not before ALJ, but denying motion to strike respondent's brief because it failed to provide record citations). Formal requirements may be enforced. See Dept. of Air Force, Willow Grove ARS and NAGE Local R3-32, 57 FLRA 852, 852 n.2 (2002) (declining to consider General Counsel's opposition to exceptions because it filed an original and three copies and failed to file another copy after being ordered to do so), reconsideration granted, result unchanged, 58 FLRA 277 (2003).
