6. Predetermination of Selectees or Eligibles

Proposals mandating selection of particular classes of employees for specific vacancies, e.g., a proposal requiring the agency to select a technician for a trainee position, are inconsistent with management's right to make selections for appointments from any appropriate source. ACT, N.Y. State Council and New York, Div. of Military and Naval Affairs, 11 FLRA 475, 475-76 (1983). Management could not be precluded from using placement procedures to fill positions if suitable in-house applicants or trainable in-house applicants could be used for the positions without compromising "mission accomplishment." AFGE Local 3296 and Nat'l Guard Bureau, Alaska NG, 33 FLRA 99, 101 (1988) (no basis for application of arrangements bargaining test). A proposal could not be negotiated that limited hiring for certain jobs to individuals who had served as either journeymen or apprentices. Int'l Plate Printers Local 2 and Dept. of Treasury, Bureau of Engraving and Printing, 25 FLRA 113, 145 (1987). The union could not require procedures implementing a job exchange program so that GS-12 employees in designated sections of the agency could compete under a posted announcement for a one-year detail. The proposal defined the personnel, by positions, who would be eligible for selection for the exchange program, limiting agency discretion to determine the qualifications and skills needed to perform the work in the positions in the job exchange program. AFSCME Local 2910 and Library of Congress, 18 FLRA 241, 242 (1985); see NFFE Local 1482 and DOD, DMA, Hydrographic/Topographic Ctr., Louisville, 45 FLRA 52, 66-75 (1992) (Proposal 4 nonnegotiable: GS-12 Senior Cartographer positions will not be limited to personnel from cartographer series; also nonnegotiable Proposal 5: senior cartographer positions be filled by employees at GS-11 for year prior to promotion with appraisals at or above fully successful level).

A nonnegotiable proposal precluded management from selecting individuals for vacancies other than from a list of eligibles for promotion. It prevented management from exercising its right under 7106(a)(2)(C)(ii) to select from other sources, e.g., reemployment priority lists, reinstatement, transfer, disabled, Veterans Readjustment eligibles, or others within reach of an OPM register. AFGE Local 32 and OPM, 8 FLRA 460, 461 (1982); aff'd AFGE Local 32 v. FLRA, 728 F.2d 1526, 1529-30 (D.C. Cir. 1984). A proposal that a selecting official choose from among highly qualified candidates directly interfered with the right of the agency to make selections from other sources of employees. The proposal was not an appropriate arrangement. It abrogated the right of management to make selections directly from other appropriate sources under any circumstances. AFGE Local 2317 and Marine Corps Logistics Base, Nonappropriated Fund Instrumentality, Albany, 29 FLRA 1587, 1600-02 (1987), distinguished in NTEU and PTO, 53 FLRA 539, 571 n.13 (1997) (proposal setting procedures for selection panel did not preclude selecting official from making selection from any appropriate source).

Nonnegotiable in AFGE Local 12 and Dept. of Labor, 60 FLRA 533, 537 (2004), was Proposal 4, that no unit position, except those specifically excepted from merit staffing competition, will be filled except as a result of advertising for the particular vacancy:

5 U.S.C. § 335.103(b)(4) provides that:

Selection procedures will provide for management's right to select or not select from among a group of best qualified candidates. They will also provide for management's right to select from other appropriate sources, such as re-employment priority lists, reinstatement, transfer, handicapped, or Veteran Readjustment Act eligibles or those within reach on an appropriate OPM certificate. In deciding which source or sources to use, agencies have an obligation to determine which is most likely to best meet the agency mission objectives, contribute fresh and new viewpoints, and meet the agency's affirmative action goals.

Proposal 4 requires the Agency to advertise all vacant bargaining unit positions not specifically excepted and to fill them from among candidates responding to the advertisement. Consequently, the proposal does not permit the Agency to fill positions from other appropriate sources, such as re-employment priority lists, reinstatement, or transfer. Cf. Soc. Sec. Admin., Chicago N. Dist. Office, 56 FLRA 274, 278 (2000) (arbitration award not deficient where award neither required agency to select from particular source nor prohibited agency from expanding pool of candidates). As such, Proposal 4 conflicts with 5 C.F.R. § 335.103(b)(4). Therefore, we conclude that Proposal 4 is outside the Agency's duty to bargain.
