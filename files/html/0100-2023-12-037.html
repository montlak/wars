3. Reply to PFR Response

The reply to the PFR response is limited to legal and factual issues raised in the response, Nye v. USPS, CH-0353-12-0570-I-1 (NP 6/4/2013):

...Applying the Board's amended regulation, which became effective November 13, 2012, we find that the Board need not consider the new arguments raised by the appellant in his reply. The Board's amended regulation does allow a reply to a response to a petition for review, but such a reply must be "limited to the factual and legal issues raised by another party in the response to the petition for review." 5 C.F.R. § 1201.114(a)(4). Here, while it is true that the agency argued in response to the appellant's petition for review that the appellant had not met the legal standard for being relieved of the consequences of his withdrawal, this did not open the door to the appellant raising brand new arguments in a reply that he did not raise in the petition for review itself. Accordingly, because the appellant failed to raise his challenges to the withdrawal of his appeal in his petition for review, the Board has not considered them.

Similarly, from Miller v. Dept. of Interior, DE-0752-15-0041-I-1 n.2 (NP 3/10/2016):

Following the agency's response, which noted the absence of any argument in the appellant's petition, the appellant submitted her reply, containing some arguments concerning the merits of her removal. However, a reply is limited to the issues raised by another party in the response to the petition for review. 5 C.F.R. § 1201.114(a)(4). It may not raise new allegations of error. Id. Accordingly, we will not consider the appellant's new arguments, first raised in her reply. See Boston v. Department of the Army, 122 M.S.P.R. 577, ¶ 5 n.3 (2015) (declining to consider new arguments that were first raised in a reply brief).

A request to allow submission of an additional pleading in the PFR process must be specific as to what will be provided to the Board, English v. SBA, DE-0752-16-0306-I-1 (NP 2/3/2023):

The appellant also has filed, after the record closed on review, a motion for leave to file an additional pleading. He asserts that he has new and relevant information that relates to his whistleblower allegations and shows that a management official made false statements.

Pleadings allowed on review include a petition for review, a cross petition for review, a response to a petition for review, a response to a cross petition for review, and a reply to a response to a petition for review. 5 C.F.R. § 1201.114(a). No other pleading will be accepted unless the party files a motion with and obtains leave from the Clerk of the Board. 5 C.F.R. § 1201.114(a)(5). Such a motion must describe the nature of and need for the pleading. Id. Here, the appellant has not sufficiently explained the nature of the information in question and how this alleged new evidence would change the outcome of his appeal. Therefore, we deny the appellant's motion for leave to file an additional pleading.
