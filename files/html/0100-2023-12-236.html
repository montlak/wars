3. Limited to Facts, Issues of Record

OPM is not entitled to reconsideration of a Board decision on the basis of an issue that was not raised by the parties and not decided by the Board in its decision. Nazelrod v. DOJ, 54 MSPR 461, 464-65 (1992); see Cofield v. GPO, 26 MSPR 261, 264 (1985) (compliance issue). If the agency did not raise an issue before the AJ, OPM may not raise the issue for the first time through a reconsideration petition; OPM must seek to protect the record earlier in the proceedings through intervention. See Horner v. Burns, 783 F.2d 196 (Fed. Cir. 1986). OPM cannot, through a reconsideration request, make up for evidentiary deficiencies in an agency case. Nothman v. Dept. of Army, 29 MSPR 190, 192 (1985). The Board rejected an OPM reconsideration petition premised on proof that it had approved a performance appraisal system; the proof was available to the agency during and before the hearing and should have been offered then. Hubbard v. Walter Reed Army Med. Ctr., 29 MSPR 187, 190 (1985).
