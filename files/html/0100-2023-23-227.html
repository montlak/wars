14. Settlement Involving Action of Third Party

Modrowski v. VA, 97 MSPR 224, 227-28 ¶¶ 8-10 (2004), involved a settlement that required expungement of a removal. The OPM retirement record referenced the removal and, upon being notified of the settlement, OPM lined-out the reference and added a reference to the appellant's retirement. The appellant argued that the settlement was breached because there remained a reference to the removal in government records, but the Board concluded that there was no breach when the VA did what it could and OPM acted in accordance with its established practices:

Simply put, the central issue in this case is whether the agency has complied with the settlement agreement by having OPM amend the appellant's Individual Retirement Record to reflect the cancellation of the removal, although the word "removal" still appears on the Individual Retirement Record. We find that the agency is in compliance.

While the appellant complains about the agency's reliance on OPM's policy regarding the correction of retirement records, the agency properly deferred to OPM. The Department of Veterans Affairs has no control over this government-wide policy set by OPM. Moreover, the appellant's Individual Retirement Record is in the physical custody of OPM. Thus, by requesting that OPM correct the appellant's Individual Retirement Record and determining that the correction has in fact been made consistent with OPM policy, the agency has done all it can do to effect the settlement agreement between the parties.

This case is distinguishable from the situation in King v. Department of the Navy, 130 F.3d 1031, 1033 (Fed. Cir. 1997), where the Navy agreed to remove all references to a removal action from Ms. King's Official Personnel File. Thereafter, however, Ms. King learned that personnel files maintained by OPM and the Defense Finance and Accounting Service (DFAS) contained references to the removal action. Id. The Navy argued that it had no control over the records maintained by OPM and DFAS, but the court observed that there was no evidence that either agency would have refused to comply with the settlement agreement if requested by the Navy. Id. at 1034. In the instant case, however, when requested to correct the appellant's retirement record, OPM informed the agency that its established policy limited the corrections it could make.
