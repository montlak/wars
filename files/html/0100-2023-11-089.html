1. FOIA Exemptions

FOIA exemptions are not, of themselves, justification for an agency to withhold information from a union, DOJ, Bureau of Prisons, FCI Ray Brook and AFGE Local 3882, 68 FLRA 492, 497 (2015):

The Agency argues that FOIA Exemption 7(e) prohibits the disclosure of the requested information and that the Judge's order to provide the information is contrary to law. This argument reflects a misunderstanding of FOIA and its exemptions. FOIA is "solely a disclosure statute"[62] and "does not prohibit release of data within the meaning of [§] 7114(b)(4) of the Statute."[63] The same reasons that led Congress to exempt certain categories of records from mandatory disclosure may give rise to anti-disclosure interests under § 7114(b)(4), but as discussed above, an agency must articulate its anti-disclosure interests "at or near the time" that it denies a union's request for information.[64] Here, the Agency failed to raise the anti-disclosure interests addressed by Exemption 7(e) "at or near the time"[65] that it denied the Union's request.
