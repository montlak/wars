broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 03: Training, Section A: Duration and Timing of Training

A. DURATION AND TIMING OF TRAINING

FLRA consistently determines that training assignments during duty hours are assignments of work covered by § 7106(a)(2)(B). Any proposal addressing the substantive aspects of training is a direct interference with management's right to assign work. NFFE Local 422 and Dept. of Interior, Bureau of Indian Affairs, 14 FLRA 48, 50 (1983). A proposal is nonnegotiable that calls on the employer to schedule required training during working hours. NFFE, Council of VA Locals and VA, 31 FLRA 360, 438 (1988) (Proposal 24). A proposal that is silent on when training would take place will be construed as "possibly requiring" training to be provided during duty hours. IAM and Dept. of Treasury, Bureau of Engraving & Printing, 33 FLRA 711, 738-39 (1988) (Proposal 14). A contract requirement that the agency supply training to employees At periodic intervals, on duty time, and for specific duration was nonnegotiable. Training during duty hours constituted work. The proposal directly interfered with the ability to assign work. NAATS and Dept. of Transp., FAA, 6 FLRA 588, 590-91 (1981).

A nonnegotiable proposal required training sessions to be less than an hour long, required the training to be completed by 3:00 P.M., precluded training on weekends and holidays, and precluded training outdoors during certain adverse weather conditions. Int'l Ass'n of Firefighters, Local F-61 and Philadelphia Naval Shipyard, 3 FLRA 438, 439 (1980). Another proposal was nonnegotiable that training drills not exceed two hours on any particular day. The drills were an assignment of work, their limitation infringed management's right to assign work, and the union did not demonstrate that the benefits to management of unrestricted training were sufficiently outweighed by reduced employee fatigue to make the proposal an appropriate arrangement. Int'l Ass'n of Firefighters Local F-159 and Dept. of Navy, Naval Station Treasure Island, 37 FLRA 836, 837-38 (1990) (Proposal 1).

A nonnegotiable proposal precluded firefighters' night drills more frequently than monthly, and then only at certain times. Considering the proposal under arrangements bargaining tests, FLRA balanced the equities and decided the agency's need for flexibility in its training program outweighed the employees' needs for sleep and predictable schedules. AFGE Local 1931 and Dept. of Navy, Naval Weapons Station, Concord, 32 FLRA 1023, 1038-40 (1988) (Provision 12). Not subject to bargaining was a proposal limiting off-base, on-the-job training to one day a week. AFGE Local 2031 and VAMC, Cincinnati, 39 FLRA 1155, 1157-59 (1991). A proposal requiring advance notice of training drills held after 4:00 P.M. and effectively precluding unannounced emergency drills after that time, was a nonnegotiable infringement on management's right to assign work. AFGE Local 1749 and Dept. of Air Force, 47th Flying Training Wing, Laughlin AFB, 12 FLRA 149, 150 (1983). The right of the agency to assign work encompassed training assigned to employees during duty hours; absolute limits on the assignment of training during certain hours or days were impermissible. Although health and safety were legitimate factors to consider as to outdoor training, the union proposal placed absolute limits on training extending beyond those considerations. A negotiable proposal bound management to endeavor to schedule indoor training for the winter and outdoor training during the summer. The proposal was distinguished from the analysis in Philadelphia Naval Shipyard by the words "will endeavor," a phrase that did not restrict management's prerogative to assign training or work. Int'l Ass'n of Firefighters, Local F-48 and Naval Support Activity, Mare Island Station, 3 FLRA 489, 490-91 (1980).
