broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 04: Job Content; Work Assignment, Section F: Control Over Work and Work Product, Subsection 02: Instructional Material, Equipment

2. Instructional Material, Equipment

The union could not require negotiation over standards for teaching materials, e.g., books. The instructional materials involved technology, methods, and means of performing work. The proposals were negotiable only at the election of the agency. Panama Canal Fed'n of Teachers, Local 29 and DODDS, 19 FLRA 814, 815-16 (1985). Proposals were not negotiable that sought to determine the type of materials that would be used in teaching courses by teachers in the unit. NFFE and Haskell Indian Junior College, Bureau of Indian Affairs, Dept. of Interior, Lawrence, 22 FLRA 539, 549-50 (1986) (Proposals 9 and 10). Provisions seeking to designate union representatives to committees responsible for studying and recommending adoption of textbooks, forms, programs, and materials used for teaching students were nonnegotiable infringements on the right of the agency to determine the means of performing work. The union was injected into the deliberative process leading to the exercise of the reserved management right. Negotiation over the composition of the committee equated to bargaining over the subject matter. The proposal also interfered with the right of the agency to assign work. It required assignment of specific duties to particular employees. Ft. Knox Teachers Ass'n and Ft. Knox Dependent Sch., 22 FLRA 815, 817-19 (1986) (Proposal 3). [Refer later in this chapter to "Joint Committees."]

A broad proposal demanding that management make reasonable efforts to obtain, for the use of unit employees, the equipment, facilities, and supplies necessary to the education process was rejected as nonnegotiable in Overseas Educ. Ass'n and DODDS, 29 FLRA 734, 753-55 (1987) (Proposal 13). The proposal infringed management's right to determine the methods and means of performing its work. In NFFE Local 2192 and Dept. of Interior, Nat'l Park Serv., 59 FLRA 868, 872 (2004), declared nonnegotiable "Option B," for management to allocate certain salary savings (of employees who were assigned temporary off-budget duties) to be used for equipment and supplies to assist other employees to perform their duties. The proposal interfered with the right of management to determine the means of performing its work and, as an arrangement, the proposal was insufficiently tailored to assist those employees whose workloads could increase as a result of the temporary assignments of other employees.

A proposal was negotiable that required that job-related reference materials be maintained at a location reasonably accessible to employees. No particular materials were designated. NFFE, Council of VA Locals and VA, 31 FLRA 360, 444 (1988) (Proposal 24); see POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 56 FLRA 69, 87-91, 97-98 (2000) (approving as arrangements to avoid lowered productivity and appraisals of patent examiners working with new computerized patent database proposals that: require arrangement of reference materials to facilitate research by patent examiners; examiners be able to maintain paper digests to augment research on a newly computerized patent database; computerized research terminals be located in areas to facilitate access by unit employees; provision of library support for patent research be continued; examiners be permitted to print materials from automated system when printing is reasonably necessary). A proposal was negotiable that allowed patent examiners to annotate digital files used in their patent searches. Past practice allowed annotation of paper files. The annotations on the computer files could be maintained separately from the database that contained patent information. The agency's ability to determine the means of performing work was not unduly burdened. POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 56 FLRA at 86-87.
