broida flra Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards, Subchapter 07: Procedures for Review of Arbitration Awards, Section B: Requirements of Exceptions; Regulations, subsection 13: Waiver of Exceptions

13. Waiver of Exceptions

May the parties waive the right to file exceptions to an arbitration award? In SSA and AFGE, 31 FLRA 1277, 1278-79 (1988), the union argued that the agency waived its right to file exceptions to an award concerning a performance appraisal. The agreement of the parties provided that "[t]he arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding on both parties." FLRA concluded that the clause was not a waiver, but its ruling did not entirely dismiss the possibility of a properly written and enforceable waiver clause:

Although parties may waive statutory rights, those waivers must be clear and unmistakable.... In determining whether a contract provision constitutes a clear and unmistakable waiver, we examine the wording of the provision and other relevant provisions of the agreement, bargaining history and past practice....

In this case, we conclude that Article 25, Section 7,...does not constitute a clear and unmistakable waiver of the right to file exceptions to expedited arbitration awards. The provision makes no mention of appeals or the filing of exceptions under section 7122(a) of the Statute being precluded. Furthermore, the Union fails to otherwise establish that such a result was intended by the parties....

See SSA Baltimore and AFGE Council 220, 60 FLRA 32, 33 (2004) ("The Agency's failure to file exceptions to the prior award does not constitute a clear and unmistakable waiver of its right under the Statute to file exceptions to the award now before the Authority."); EEOC, Baltimore Field Office and AFGE Nat'l Council of EEOC Locals, 59 FLRA 688, 689-90 (2004) (provision of contract permitting local arbitrator's award to be "moved" to National arbitration did not operate as clear, unmistakable waiver of agency's right to file exceptions); Dept. of Navy, Indian Head Div. and AFGE Local 1923, 56 FLRA 848, 850 (2000) (off-the-record discussion, not reflected in arbitration award, did not qualify as clear, unmistakable waiver of statutory right to file exceptions to award); cf. AFGE Local 2501 and DOD, DLA, New Cumberland, 56 FLRA 1052, 1054 (2001) "[A] union may contractually waive its rights under the Statute and an arbitrator may enforce the waiver."), reconsideration denied, AFGE Local 2501 and DOD, DLA, New Cumberland, 57 FLRA 278 (2001).
