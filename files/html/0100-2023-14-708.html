broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 21: Union Rights, Bargaining Rights and Procedures, Section I: Information from Management

I. INFORMATION FROM MANAGEMENT

Although the union has a statutory right to information from management, [discussed in Chapter 3, "Right to Information"] nothing precludes a contractual right to information from management, assuming compliance with Privacy Act or other statutory restrictions. The usual negotiability criteria apply. A proposal was not negotiable that would provide the union appraisals of supervisors. The proposal did not concern the conditions of unit employees. NTEU Chap. 91 and Dept. of Treasury, IRS, 17 FLRA 534, 535 (1985) (Proposal 1). A proposal for supervisors to account for the time spent on appraisals was outside the duty to bargain because it interfered with the right of management to assign work by requiring the assignment of specific duties to certain employees. NTEU Chap. 91 and Dept. of Treasury, IRS, 17 FLRA 534, 536 (1985) (Proposal 2).

Management was required to bargain over a proposal that the union be provided with an annual summary of disciplinary and adverse actions. The list was to provide the employee's position, department, proposed charge, and decision. The proposal did not violate the Privacy Act, for individual identifiers were not required. AFGE Council of Prison Locals, Local 1661 and DOJ, Fed. Bureau of Prisons, FCI Danbury, 29 FLRA 990, 1009-10 (1987) (Proposal 30). Management was also required to bargain over a proposal that the employer provide the union copies of all records maintained concerning unit employee qualification standards. Overseas Educ. Ass'n and DODDS, 29 FLRA 734, 814 (1987) (Proposal 62). Another proposal determined to be negotiable called on management to supply to the union copies of each SF-50 issued so that the union would be aware of new hires, position changes, and other actions involving employees. NFFE Local 2052 and Dept. of Interior, BLM, Boise Dist. Office, 30 FLRA 797, 801 (1987) (Proposal 3). FLRA will apply to proposals specific prohibitions against disclosure imposed by the Privacy Act, as that Act is implemented by records systems notices. FLRA will also overturn arbitration awards that enforce contract provisions that are contrary to the Privacy Act and implementing OPM regulations. See NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, BATF, 46 FLRA 234, 245 (1992).

A bargaining proposal for management to do no more than notify employees of a particular policy does not attempt to negotiate the policy itself. A proposal that management periodically issue a policy statement regarding "the inappropriateness of classroom interruptions" was negotiable. Proposals "which merely require an agency to notify employees of matters concerning their conditions of employment are negotiable as procedures." NFFE Local 1263 and Defense Language Inst., Presidio of Monterey, 29 FLRA 61, 69, 70 (1987) (Proposal 4).
