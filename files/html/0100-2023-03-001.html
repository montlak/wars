HOW TO USE THE MSPB GUIDE

Whatever your interest in using this Guide, we want to help you accomplish that objective. We are integrating law, regulations, thousands of decisions of the MSPB and of the courts, and we include portions of the MSPB Judges' Handbook as well as references to other source materials, including some found on the Internet. But with all of these resources, the law discussed in this Guide is but an introduction to the subject of American federal civil service law. Readers must follow the leads provided by the Guide and develop their own approaches to research and analysis.

Over the years, this Guide has adopted citation conventions of its own. For Board precedential decisions issued through the end of 2016, citations are generally to West's Merit Systems Protection Reporter (MSPR). For lack of a quorum, the Board issued no decisions from the beginning of 2017 through March of 2022, when its quorum was reestablished. West did not immediately recommence publication of the MSPR, and it has not done so through 2023. Dewey does not cite to the MSPR for Board decisions issued after 2017. Board precedential and nonprecedential decisions are posted on the Board's website, and since 2006, the Board reports precedential decision by year and decision number, e.g., 2023 MSPB ___ (2023), and those decisions are referenced in the Guide, often with decisional paragraph numbers. The Guide references page numbers. and often paragraph numbers, for MSPR cites for decisions before 2017.

Beginning in late 2010 the Board began issuing nonprecedential decisions.Thousands of decisions have been issued in nonprecedential form. Many restate the law in new ways or add to the existing body of caselaw. The decisions are too significant to ignore and, starting with the 2012 edition of the Guide, they are included in the text to the extent they merit citation. These decisions may be cited to the Board, but judges and the Board are not supposed to rely upon them. What weight nonprecedential decisions have with the Federal Circuit or other courts or adjudicators remains to be seen. MSPB nonprecedential decisions carry only a docket number. Precedential and nonprecedential Board decisions can be retrieved from the Board's website, www.mspb.gov.

Federal Circuit precedential decisions are printed in West's Federal Reporter. Federal Circuit nonprecedential decisions use one of two citation forms. Early decisions carry a docket number only. Recent decisions are in West's Federal Appendix. Recent Federal Circuit decisions can be retrieved through the court's website, http://cafc.uscourts.gov/home/case-information/opinions-orders/.

Block quotations from the MSPR and court decisions may omit footnotes, and if that occurs, we do not use the "[Footnote omitted]" signal. Block quotations will omit references to portions of the administrative record, e.g., "Agency Response to Appeal, Tab 4-0," or "Jt. App. 44." No ellipses are used when record references are dropped. Dissenting and concurring opinions generally are not excerpted in discussion of Board and court decisions.

Many references are to the Code of Federal Regulations and the United States Code. When references to the CFR and USC are made in the text (as opposed to quotations or parenthetical explanations following citations), effort is made to keep the citations current, or to show whether the CFR or USC reference was only valid for a past year. The regulations and statutes change, and not all references mentioned in the Guide are checked each year. Readers should check the current edition of the CFR and USC to ensure the validity of a citation.

Occasional references are made to decisions of the Office of Federal Operations of the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission. References to those decisions are to the EEOC docket numbers assigned to those decisions. Readers are cautioned to read full opinions, whatever their source, available through printed text, WESTLAW, LEXIS, CYBERFEDS, and through the MSPB or Federal Circuit websites.

For resources and research suggestions, check Chapter 18 of the Guide and the bibliography that follows that chapter.
