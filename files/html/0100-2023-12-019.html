broida flra Chapter 04: Subjects of Bargaining: Procedures for Negotiability Determinations Chapter 04: Subjects of Bargaining: Procedures for Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 04: Framework for Negotiability Appeals, Section E: Allegation of Nonnegotiability, Subsection 04: Failure of Agency to Make Allegation

4. Failure of Agency to Make Allegation

5 CFR 2424.21(b) (2022), provides:

If the agency has not served a written allegation on the exclusive representative within ten (10) days after the agency's principal bargaining representative has received a written request for such allegation, as provided in § 2424.11(a), then the petition may be filed at any time.

If the union makes a written request for an allegation of nonnegotiability and the agency fails to provide it within 10 days after receipt by any agency representative at negotiations, under 5 CFR 2424.21(b) (2022) the union may request FLRA to review the negotiability issues without a prior written allegation. AFGE Local 3342 and DHHS, SSA, N.Y. Region, 36 FLRA 367, 373 (1990) (union cannot rely on beliefs stated by management that it need not provide written statement of nonnegotiability as excuse for not submitting written request for negotiability determination); see AFGE Local 727 and Court Serv. & Offender Supervision Agency, 59 FLRA 674, 675 (2004) (failure to respond to request for allegation of nonnegotiability constitutes constructive allegation of nonnegotiability). If the agency fails to issue the written allegation, the 15-day time period for filing the petition for review does not begin to run; there does not appear to be a time limit on the union's filing if the agency does not provide its written allegation of nonnegotiability. NTEU and IRS, Buffalo Dist., 3 FLRA 337, 339-40 & n.2 (1980); see AFGE Local 1867 and Dept. of Air Force, Air Force Academy, 46 FLRA 199, 202-03 (1992) (FLRA will construe agency's failure to respond to request as constructive declaration of nonnegotiability that gives rise to a right of appeal to Authority); AFGE Local 3407 and DOD, DMA, Hydrographic-Topographic Ctr., Wash., 41 FLRA 265, 269 (1991) ("The Agency was not obligated to provide a written allegation within any time limits established under the Authority's Rules and Regulations. Instead, where a union requests an allegation and does not receive one in writing within 10 days after receipt of the request, the union may file a petition for review at any time after the expiration of the 10-day period."). It is immaterial that the agency provided the union an earlier unrequested allegation of nonnegotiability. AFGE Local 2052 and DOJ, Bureau of Prisons, FCI Petersburg, 31 FLRA 37, 38-39 (1988). The statute does not preclude the parties from negotiating a provision in their contract, enforceable in arbitration, that an agency make a written allegation of negotiability on request of the union. NOAA, NWS and NWSEA, 71 FLRA 1239, 1241 (2020) (Member Abbott concurring, Chairman Kiko dissenting) ("While the Regulations specify that a union 'may file a petition for review if it requests that the agency provide it with a written allegation concerning the duty to bargain and the agency does not respond to the request within ten (10) days,' we find nothing in the Regulations which prevent an agency from obligating itself through an agreement to making formal declarations of nonnegotiability upon request.").

The union's petition may be filed, once the agency fails to timely respond to a request for a nonnegotiability allegation, despite the agency's protestation to FLRA that bargaining continued, NTEU and DHS, C&BP, 70 FLRA 701, 701-02 (2018) (aff'd on other grounds, NTEU v. FLRA, 943 F.3d 486 (D.C. Cir. 2019)):

The Agency claims that the Union's petition should be dismissed because it was prematurely filed. The Agency asserts that it did not declare that the Union's proposals are nonnegotiable and that the parties are still "engaged in term negotiations." Therefore, the Agency requests that the Authority issue an order to show cause why the petition should not be dismissed as prematurely filed. In the alternative, the Agency asserts that "[s]hould the Authority reject the Agency's position on this matter, the Agency reserves the right to submit an additional [s]tatement of [p]osition in the future."

We find that the Union's petition is timely. Under § 2424.21(b) of the Authority's Regulations, "[i]f the agency has not served a written allegation on the exclusive representative within ten...days after the agency's principal bargaining representative has received a written request for such allegation,...then the petition may be filed at any time."

On April 10, 2016, the Union sent the Agency an email requesting an allegation of nonnegotiability concerning the proposals. The Agency did not respond to the Union's request. Therefore, under § 2424.21(b) of the Authority's Regulations, the Union had no specific time limit within which it was required to file its petition, and we find that its petition, which was filed on May 3, 2016, is timely.

Further, we deny the Agency's request, in effect, to file an additional statement of position. Section 2424.24(a) of the Authority's Regulations provides that an agency's statement of position must, "among other things,...supply all arguments and authorities in support of its position." As the Authority's Regulations require the Agency to set forth all of its arguments in its statement of position, we deny the Agency's request to file an additional statement of position.

Options available to the union as to the timing of a negotiability petition filed after an agency fails to provide a requested written allegation of nonnegotiability were reviewed in AFGE Local 1164 and SSA Roslindale, 65 FLRA 924, 924 (2011):

The Agency claims that the Union's petition should be dismissed because it was untimely filed. The record in this case indicates that the Agency provided the Union with an unsolicited written allegation of nonnegotiability (allegation). Subsequently, the Union requested an allegation from the Agency. The Agency did not respond to the Union's request, claiming that it had already provided an allegation and that it was not required to support its allegation with specificity until it filed its statement of position.

A union is not required to respond to an agency's unsolicited allegation. See 5 C.F.R. § 2424.11(c); AFGE, Local 3369, 49 FLRA 793, 794 (1994). Rather, the union may ignore the unsolicited allegation and instead elect to request a written allegation from the agency. See id. at 795. If an agency does not provide a written allegation in response to a union's written request, then a petition for review filed by the union is not subject to the time limits set forth in § 2424.21 of the Authority's Regulations, and the union may file its petition at any time. See 5 C.F.R. § 2424.21(b); AFGE, AFL-CIO, Local 2052, 30 FLRA 837, 839 (1987).

Here, the Union chose not to respond to the Agency's unsolicited allegation and instead requested an allegation. As the Agency did not provide an allegation in response to the Union's written request, the Union was not required to file its petition within any particular time frame. Consequently, the Union's petition was timely filed.

If the union contends that the agency was requested to make a declaration of nonnegotiability, the union must prove its request by producing a document that has the indicia of a request to the agency. See NFFE Local 341 and Dept. of Interior, Wapato Irrigation Project, 33 FLRA 830, 830-31 (1988) (unaddressed handwritten note insufficient to prove a request for a declaration was made to the agency). It is the union's responsibility to prove that the agency declared a proposal nonnegotiable or failed to provide a declaration in response to a request. If the union alleges that the agency declared a proposal out of the scope of bargaining, but the union fails to provide documentation demonstrating the agency's declaration, the union's subsequent appeal to FLRA may be dismissed. See AFGE Local 1760 and DHHS, SSA, 28 FLRA 160, 160-61 (1987).

If FLRA dismisses a union petition for review because a negotiability determination request was not made, the dismissal is without prejudice to either a proper agency determination or a procedurally proper union request for review. See NAGE Local R1-109 and VAMC Newington, 53 FLRA 526, 528 (1997) (if agency does not make written declaration of nonnegotiability, Authority may dismiss union's petition for review without prejudice to subsequent filing if agency fails to allege in response to union's petition that union's proposal is inconsistent with law, rule, or regulation); AFGE, Local 1978 and Dept. of Interior, Bureau of Reclamation, 1 FLRA 1043, 1044 (1979) ("[I]nasmuch as the union never sought and received an allegation from the agency, within the meaning of section 7117 of the Statute, as to whether the duty to bargain under the Statute extends to the matters proposed to be bargained by the union, the dismissal of your appeal is without prejudice. That is, if the matters proposed to be negotiated continue in dispute between the parties, an allegation as to their negotiability may be sought and a petition for review may be filed with the Authority....").
