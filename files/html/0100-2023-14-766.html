6. Final and Binding Arbitration

Nothing precludes negotiation of an arbitration clause that provides for an award that is final and binding on both parties. The requirement is consistent with § 7121(b)(3)(C)(iii), providing that a grievance not settled under a negotiated grievance procedure shall be subject to binding arbitration. FLRA observed that an award becomes legally binding only in either of two circumstances: when neither of the parties files exceptions within 30 days of the award; or when the award is sustained by the Authority after a timely filing of exceptions. Ft. Knox Teachers Ass'n and Bd. of Educ. of Ft. Knox Dependents Sch., 27 FLRA 203, 209 (1987) (Provision 3). A proposal is negotiable that requires the agency to implement awards in all cases if no exceptions are taken, even though the award may be contrary to law. The result was consistent with the principle expressed in Dept. of Navy, Customs Serv., New York and NTEU, Chap. 161, 21 FLRA 999, 1002 (1986), that unexcepted awards are final notwithstanding claims that the award is contrary to law. Illinois Nurses' Ass'n and VAMC, Hines, 28 FLRA 212, 246, 253 (1987) (Proposal 11). A proposal is nonnegotiable, however, that makes an award final and binding if the employer does not notify the union within 30 days of an award of its intent to file exceptions. The proposal shortens the period available to the employer for filing exceptions because it does not account for mailing time, and the provision is contrary to the statute, allowing a full 30-day period for filing exceptions--a period that cannot be extended or waived under FLRA regulation 5 CFR 2429.23(d) (2022). AFGE Local 1625 and Dept. of Navy, Naval Air Station, Oceana, 30 FLRA 1105, 1110 (1988).
