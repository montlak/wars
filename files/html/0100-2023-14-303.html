1. Time for Filling Vacancies; Effective Date of Personnel Action

AFGE Local 1738 and VAMC, Salisbury, 27 FLRA 52, 61-63 (1987) (Chairman Calhoun dissenting), declared negotiable Proposal 9, that if the employer decides to fill vacant positions, it will do so as soon as practicable if staff shortages create an undue hardship on unit employees. The proposal did not require management to fill any vacant position that it had not already decided to fill or establish a particular time for the vacancy to be filled. Another proposal approved called on management to fill nursing service vacancies in an expeditious manner. Nat'l Union of Hosp. and Health Care Employees, Dist. 1199 and VAMC, Dayton, 28 FLRA 435, 482-83 (1987) (citing AFGE Local 1738 and VAMC, Salisbury, 27 FLRA 52 (1987)); see AFGE Local 3354 and USDA, Farm Servs. Agency, 54 FLRA 807, 814 (1998) ("[O]nce management has decided to fill vacant positions, a proposal requiring the Agency to make a selection 'as soon as practicable,' when staff shortages create an undue hardship on unit employees, constitutes a negotiable procedure within the meaning of section 7106(b)(2) of the Statute.").

Negotiable in AFGE Local 1367 and Dept. of Air Force, Lackland AFB, 64 FLRA 869, 873 (2010), was a proposal that the agency fill vacancies to avoid overwork of housekeeping staff:

To the maximum extent possible, the parties agree that there will be times when position vacancies occur. The remaining housekeepers will be unable to meet the quality standards if they have too many rooms to clean. To the extent possible, the parties agree that the Employer will attempt to fill the vacancy in a reasonable amount of time in order to diminish the impact of additional rooms being assigned to the Housekeepers.

The proposal in AFGE Local 1367 was a permissible arrangement, 64 FLRA 873-74:

Agency

As set forth above, the Agency contends only, and without elaboration, that "all eight proposals" are "inconsistent" with the right to assign work, and that "none of the eight proposals are appropriate arrangements[.]" [10]

July 17, 2006), decision on remand, 64 FLRA 569 (2010)--which involved a proposal's excessive interference with management's rights under § 7106(b)(1) and on which the dissent relies--is inapposite.

Proposal 4 would benefit housekeepers by decreasing the stress, fatigue, and risk of work-related injuries that may result from reduced staffing. See NFFE, Local 2192, 59 FLRA 868, 871 (2004). Additionally, consistent with precedent, Proposal 4 is sufficiently tailored to benefit those housekeepers who are adversely affected by reduced staffing. See Prison Locals, 64 FLRA at 730. In this regard, as noted above, the Authority has found that a proposal that sought to mitigate employee fatigue and work-related injury that results from such fatigue constituted an arrangement. See AFGE, Local 3157, 44 FLRA at 1596-97. Based on the foregoing, we find that Proposal 4 is an arrangement.

With regard to whether the arrangement is appropriate, the Union asserts that Proposal 4 would benefit employees by decreasing the stress, fatigue and risk of work-related injuries that may result from reduced staffing. Additionally, the housekeepers' statements support the Union's claim that housekeepers suffer stress and exhaustion, in part as a result of their workload. By contrast, the Agency does not address the housekeepers' assertions or the Union's claims that the proposal would benefit housekeepers.

Further, the Agency does not demonstrate the extent to which Proposal 4 would burden the right to assign work. In this regard, the Agency does not demonstrate, and the record does not show, that Proposal 4 would burden the Agency's "right to determine the particular duties to be assigned, when work assignments will occur, and to whom or what positions the duties will be assigned." SSA, 64 FLRA at 203. Weighing the benefits to employees against the absence of demonstrated burdens on the right to assign work, we find that Proposal 4 is an appropriate arrangement under § 7106(b)(3) of the Statute.

A proposal fixing the effective date of promotions was negotiable. PATCO and Dept. of Transp., FAA, 4 FLRA 232, 234 (1980) (Proposal II). The proposal was that employees promoted to higher-grade positions at different facilities "will be promoted" when they enter on duty in the new position. The same decision required negotiation of Proposal III, setting the effective date for facility-wide reclassifications adjusting upward grades of employees whose positions were reclassified as the first full pay period after the requirements were met for reclassification of blocks of positions at given facilities. A proposal was acceptable that when the agency seeks to fill a vacancy from a certificate of eligibles, it do so within two weeks or cancel the vacancy. The proposal did not require the agency to select, nor did it preclude selections from other sources, or prevent the agency from reestablishing the vacancy and obtaining additional certificates. AFGE Local 32 and OPM, 8 FLRA 460, 467 (1982) (Proposal V). A requirement that management make a choice for a position within 30 days from the issuance of a selection certificate was a negotiable procedure. Nothing prevented management from reaching outside the certificate to select an appointee. Nothing prevented management from obtaining additional certificates if no selection was made within the time limit. NTEU Chap. 207 and FDIC, 14 FLRA 598, 598-601 (1984) (Proposal 1).
