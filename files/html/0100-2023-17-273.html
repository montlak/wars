broida flra Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards, Subchapter 08: Grounds for Review, Section G: Contrary to Law; Award is Inconsistent with Law, Rule, or Regulation, Subsection 14: Other Compensation and Leave Cases, Part j: Leave Without Pay
j. Leave Without Pay

An award will not be set aside as a violation of & 7106(b)(1), giving the agency the right to determine the numbers, types, and grades of employees assigned to any organization, project, or tour of duty, if the agency elected to bargain on the subject of when LWOP will be granted, and if the agency agreed to the provision interpreted by an arbitrator as a part of its negotiated agreement. AFGE Local 1917 and INS, E. Region, 13 FLRA 77, 78 (1983). Agency exceptions were denied as to an award that determined management wrongfully required documentation beyond what was spelled out in a contract article governing LWOP for disabled veterans seeking medical treatment. Exec. Order 5396, containing arguably more exacting requirements than did the contract for LWOP for veterans, did not limit or excuse failure to adhere to the negotiated provision. VA Nashville Regional Office and AFGE Local 2470, 72 FLRA 371 (2021) (Member Abbott concurring); see AFGE Local 906 and VA Member Services, Health Resource Center, 72 FLRA 761 (2022) (Member Abbott dissenting) (same analysis as VA Nashville); AFGE Local 1822 and VA, Member Services, 72 FLRA 595 (2021) (Chairman DuBester concurring) (setting aside an arbitrator's award that required documentation or justification beyond that involved in a VA settlement agreement concerning grants of leave for veterans seeking medical treatment).recon. den. 73 FLRA 22 (2022)) (same result as VA Nashville)).AFGE Local 1822 and VA, Member Services, 72 FLRA 595 (2021) (Chairman DuBester concurring) (setting aside an arbitrator's award that required documentation or justification beyond that involved in a VA settlement agreement concerning grants of leave for veterans seeking medical treatment).
