8. Chain of Custody

In AFGE, Dept. of Educ. Council of AFGE Locals and Dept. of Educ., 38 FLRA 1068, 1105 (1990), enforcement denied as to other proposals, Dept. of Interior, Minerals Mgmt. Serv. v. FLRA, 969 F.2d 1158, 1161-63 (D.C. Cir. 1992), Proposal 6, Section A, was declared negotiable, "All samples will be subject to a strict chain of custody":

Section A of Proposal 6 requires the Agency to maintain strict procedures concerning the transfer and transport of employees' urine samples to assure that each employee's sample is properly identified throughout the testing process. We find that section A is negotiable.

Section 2.2 of the final Guidelines contains the standards to which agencies must adhere in establishing procedures governing the handling and transportation of urine specimens from one authorized individual to another. That section implements section 503(a)(1)(A)(ii)(I) of Title V of Public Law 100-71...which mandates that the Secretary of Health and Human Services set standards which "require...strict procedures governing the chain of custody of specimens collected for drug testing[.]" Section A incorporates the requirement in law and regulation that the Agency develop rigorous "chain of custody" procedures.

[W]e interpret the phrase "chain of custody" in section A to be consistent with the definition of "chain of custody" in section 1.2 of the final Guidelines. The Agency has not offered any reason for its contention that section A is inconsistent with the Executive Order. We find that the Agency has failed to establish that section A is inconsistent with the Executive Order or the final Guidelines that implement the Executive Order.

Because it is not otherwise apparent from the record and because the Agency has not established that section A is inconsistent with any other law regulation or management's rights under the Statute, we find that there is no basis for finding section A nonnegotiable.

Proposal 6, demanding observance of chain of custody rules of the Guidelines, was negotiable in AFGE Local 3457 and Dept. of Interior, Minerals Mgmt. Serv., 42 FLRA 567, 584-86 (1991). Nonnegotiable in IFPTE Local 1 and Dept. of Navy, Norfolk Naval Shipyard, 49 FLRA 225, 246-49 (1994), was Proposal 6, that any person involved in the chain of custody would be removed from that chain should the person fail a drug test. The proposal interfered with the right to assign work. The proposal was not an appropriate arrangement because it absolutely precluded the agency from assigning any duties involving control over the specimens of unit employees to certain individuals. The interference with the right to assign work was determined to be excessive.
