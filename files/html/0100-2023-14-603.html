XIX. CRITICAL ELEMENTS AND PERFORMANCE STANDARDS

There is no bar to a proposal requiring elements and standards. A proposal was consistent with law and did not abridge management rights to assign work that required performance elements and standards for all General Schedule employees, GS-1 to GS-12, and for all Federal Wage System employees. IFPTE Local 25 and Dept. of Navy, Mare Island Naval Shipyard, 13 FLRA 433, 433-38 (1983). As will be seen in discussion that follows, the substance of performance standards is off limits for negotiation. Unions attempt to find ways to approach substantive negotiations through detailed procedural provisions designed to ensure accuracy or objectivity of standards. Unions also attempt to negotiate either substantive or detailed procedural aspects of standards under the appropriate arrangements bargaining test. Neither approach has worked particularly well for unions.

Most of the performance standard decisions discussed below are governed by 5 USC Chapter 43. NAF employees are not covered by Chapter 43. But standards for NAF employees have the same purpose as those set for employees covered by Chapter 43. FLRA approaches negotiability disputes over NAF performance-related disputes in the same fashion that it approaches disputes involving employees who are covered by Chapter 43. NAGE Local R14-52 and DOD, Defense Fin. and Accounting Serv., 45 FLRA 910, 913 (1992).
