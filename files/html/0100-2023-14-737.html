Q. UNION MEMBERSHIP REQUIREMENTS; SERVICE FEES

An agency shop proposal (compulsory contributions by all employees of the equivalent of union dues) was nonnegotiable in SEIU Local 556 and Dept. of Army, Army Support Command, Ft. Shafer, 1 FLRA 563, 564 (1979). The proposal was contrary to 5 USC 7102, guaranteeing to each employee the right to refrain from joining any labor union. The legislative history of the Reform Act demonstrated that an agency shop agreement was impermissible. The same result was reached in AFGE Local 1857 and Dept. of Air Force, McClellan AFB, 44 FLRA 98 (1992). In NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, Customs Serv., 46 FLRA 696, 702-04 (1992), FLRA disapproved Provision 1, seeking establishment of an arbitration fund allowing the union to require fees from nonmembers to finance arbitrations. The provision in NTEU discriminated against bargaining unit members on the basis of union membership. FLRA's ruling relied on NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, IRS, 38 FLRA 615 (1990). NAGE Local R7-51 and Dept. of Navy, Naval Pub. Works Ctr., 56 FLRA 941, 942 (2000), disapproved as nonnegotiable a proposal requiring the agency to deduct $2 from each biweekly paycheck of unit employees who have not joined the union and who did not have dues voluntarily deducted. The proposal, although it permitted employees to opt out of the arrangement, violated 5 CFR 550.312(a, b) (2000), requiring specific designations by employees of salary allotments.
