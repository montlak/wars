broida flra Chapter 08: Unfair Labor Practices Chapter 08: Unfair Labor Practices, Subchapter 02: Management Violations, Section B: Bargaining Failure, Subsection 21: Improper Disapproval of Agreement by Agency, Part c: Obligations Following Disapproval
c. Obligations Following Disapproval

If an agreement is to go into effect, it must be approved by the agency head in its entirety. The failure of an activity to implement any portion of the agreement following disapproval, unless there is prior agreement by the parties to do so, does not constitute a violation of the agency's duty to bargain in good faith. The agency is obligated to return to the bargaining table with a sincere resolve to reach agreement with the union, just as when the union membership fails to ratify an agreement. Dept. of Interior, Nat'l Park Serv. and NAGE Local R4-68, 20 FLRA 537, 541 (1985); see DODDS and Overseas Fed'n of Teachers, 27 FLRA 586, 595 (1987) (ALJ Decision) ("Where the disapproval [is] proper, the parties are required to return to the bargaining table to negotiate and attempt to reach agreement" and "'with a sincere resolve to reach agreement.'"). DOJ, INS and AFGE Nat'l Border Patrol Council, 37 FLRA 1346, 1367-68 (1990), added as to obligations concerning agency head review and disapproval of contract provisions:

It is well established that where an agency head timely disapproves an agreement under section 7114(c), the agreement does not take effect and it is not binding on the parties.... The parties are then free to renegotiate the disapproved provisions and the agency head may review the renegotiated provisions. Alternatively, the union may challenge the agency head's disapproval by filing a petition for review or an unfair labor practice charge. During the time that the parties are renegotiating, or the matter is pending before the Authority, there is no obligation to implement the portions of the agreement not disapproved, unless the parties agree to do so....

Here, after Respondent Justice properly disapproved the renegotiated Article 32, Section B, there was an outstanding dispute as to the negotiability of the provision. The Union challenged the disapproval by filing both a petition for review and an unfair labor practice charge. Respondent INS was not obligated to implement the agreement or the portions of the agreement that were not disapproved for two reasons. First, as we have indicated, an agreement that is timely disapproved does not take effect. Therefore, because Respondent Justice disapproved the renegotiated provision, there was no agreement in effect for Respondent INS to implement. Second, there is no evidence in the record that the parties agreed to implement any portions of the agreement that were not disapproved....
