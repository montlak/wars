4. Parties Encompassed by Remedial Order

If agency management at a higher level is not named as a respondent, agency management at a subordinate level will be held to have violated the Statute, even if subordinate level management was following orders. Dept. of Labor, Office of Ass't Sec'y for Admin. and Mgmt., San Francisco and AFGE Local 2391, 33 FLRA 429, 432 (1988) (refusal to supply names and addresses). [Refer earlier in this chapter to "Midterm Bargaining Obligation and the 'Covered By' Doctrine: SSA," concerning agency responsibility in refusal to bargain cases.] If, however, an entity is a respondent, an ALJ recommends that the complaint against that entity be dismissed, and no exceptions are filed to that recommendation, FLRA may still fashion a remedy as to that entity when it considers the case. That situation was reviewed in FLRA v. NASA, 120 F.3d 1208, 1216 (11th Cir. 1997), involving a complaint against an agency and its OIG. Exceptions were filed as to the liability of the OIG but not as to the agency itself. The reviewing court found no harm when FLRA imposed liability upon the agency:

NASA-HQ claims that the Authority's ruling lacked procedural fairness because, as the Authority recognized, no exceptions had been filed with respect to the ALJ's recommendation that the unfair labor practice complaint against NASA-HQ be dismissed. The Authority nevertheless determined that it was proper for it to address that issue because NASA-HQ was a party pursuant to 5 CFR 2421.11(b)(1)(i) ("party" means any agency "named as [a] charged party in a charge"), and because the Authority had previously addressed sua sponte matters that had not been excepted to by the parties.... NASA-HQ provides us with no authority indicating that the FLRA's conclusion that it had the power to modify the ALJ's rulings on grounds not excepted to by the parties is not entitled to deference.

A remedial order directing impact bargaining over adverse action procedures was directed to the National Office as well as the respondent's local office. The change was initiated at the direction of the National Office. Dept. of Treasury, IRS, Detroit and NTEU Chap. 24, 9 FLRA 437, 455 (1982) (ALJ Decision). However, a bargaining order may direct local bargaining if command-level negotiations would not remedy the violation of a locally implemented policy. Dept. of Air Force, Sacramento Air Logistics Ctr., McClellan AFB and AFGE Local 1857, 39 FLRA 1357, 1359 (1991). Advice, not direction, from a higher level authority to disregard an FLRA order did not constitute a ULP by the higher level organization. Kansas Army Nat'l Guard and NAGE Local R14-87, 10 FLRA 303, 306-07 (1982). [Refer later in this chapter to "Involvement of Higher-Level Agency Component," concerning interagency responsibility for ULPs.]
