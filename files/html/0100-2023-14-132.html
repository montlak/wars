VII. LEAVE ARRANGEMENTS

It is within management's right to assign work to grant or deny specific leave requests. Proposals requiring management to grant leave under specific circumstances deny management the right to assign work. NFFE, Council of VA Locals and VA, 31 FLRA 360, 373 (1988) (Proposal 4). Even a proposal that requires management to do no more than "ordinarily" grant leave is an infringement on the right to assign work. NFFE Local 405 and Dept. of Army, Army Info. Sys. Command, St. Louis, 42 FLRA 1112, 1124-27 (1991) (see Proposals 12-17; no appropriate arrangement claim). Proposals are generally negotiable that establish procedures, within the agency's discretion, for requesting and scheduling of leave, as long as the proposals do not interfere with management's right to assign work by compelling the allowance of leave under certain conditions. NFFE Local 1798 and VAMC, Martinsburg, W.Va., 27 FLRA 239, 244 (1987) (several detailed leave proposals were negotiable; proposals that required management to grant or make every effort to grant leave for personal emergencies or at other times was a nonnegotiable substantive condition on the right to assign work); see NFFE, Council of VA Locals and VA, 31 FLRA 360, 373 (1988) (procedures for informing employees of approval or disapproval of leave).

NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, Fin. Mgmt. Serv., 45 FLRA 696, 722 (1992), established the principle that substantive restrictions on management's right to deny annual leave constitute negotiable arrangements to be assessed based on the benefits and burdens to the parties. FLRA reasoned that the reasonable foreseeability that management will deny some leave requests creates an adverse effect upon employees. That decision denied the negotiability of Provision 5, providing that leave requests would be granted unless the employee's absence would create a "severe workload problem." The proposals impaired management's ability to accomplish its operations in an efficient and effective manner. See SSA and AFGE Council 220, 65 FLRA 339, 342 (2010) (arbitration award review: contract provisions addressing circumstances of management's denial of leave requests constitute arrangements ameliorating adverse effects flowing from management's right to deny leave).

Unless there is a regulation supported by a compelling need or other bar to negotiation, the agency has a duty to bargain over the substance and impact of an agency regulation concerning leave and excused absences before that regulation is implemented. DHHS, Office of Sec'y and AFGE Local 41, 20 FLRA 175, 178-79 (1985). Provisions concerning various types of sick, disability, and personal leave, were not blocked by the Antideficiency Act, Ft. Knox Teachers Ass'n and Bd. of Educ. of Ft. Knox Dependents Sch., 27 FLRA 203, 216 (1987):

In interpreting the Antideficiency Act the Comptroller General has held that salaries of Government employees, as well as related items that flow from those salaries such as retirement fund contributions, are obligations of the Government at the time they are earned, that is, when the services are provided. See, for example, 38 Comp. Gen. 316 (1958). In addition, the use of leave obligates appropriations which are current at the time the leave is taken. 50 Comp. Gen. 863, 865 (1971). Thus, contrary to the Agency's claim, the obligation of funds resulting from the taking of leave would not actually occur until the employee took the leave. Further, there would be no obligation of funds with respect to paying an employee when the employee returned from leave until the employee actually provided services....
