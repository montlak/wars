n. Sequential Allegations

If the appellant repeats allegations, and no action is taken, "the appellant's disclosures remained protected at each iteration." Schneider v. DHS, 98 MSPR 377, 385 ¶ 15 (2005). A general statement may be deemed to incorporate a more specific and protected disclosure. See Chambers v. Dept. of Interior, 116 MSPR 17, 30-31 ¶ 24, 2011 MSPB 7 (2011) ("[t]he appellant's statements that patrolling the George Washington (GW) Parkway with only two officers had already required the USPP to 'turn [their] backs on drunk drivers' in order to avoid requiring the officers to abandon their posts, identifies the specific cause of a substantial and specific danger to public safety that has already occurred. It is objectively reasonable to believe that a failure by Park Police officers to arrest suspected drunk drivers presents a substantial danger to other drivers and pedestrians on the GW Parkway. Her statement that 'people who travel to and from Washington, D.C., on our parkways' are 'in jeopardy' is also protected because it summarizes in conclusory form her identification of the specific and substantial threat of lax drunk driving enforcement."). [Refer to Chapter 4, "Whistleblowing Prohibited Personnel Practice Complaint or Appeal," for application of res judicata to successive whistleblower cases.]
