B. EMPLOYEE INVOLVEMENT IN APPRAISAL PROCESS

A demand was negotiable that a proposed performance appraisal be discussed with the employee prior to the reviewer and others in the appraisal chain. It delayed, but did not preclude, the issuance of an appraisal. A proposed appraisal clearly affected working conditions. NFFE and Dept. of Army, Ft. Monmouth, 13 FLRA 426, 426-28 (1983). It was a negotiable procedure to request that the rating supervisor prepare the appraisal, discuss it with the employee and give the employee an opportunity to comment in writing and sign the appraisal, and for the rating supervisor then to discuss the appraisal with the reviewer. Both the rater's appraisal and the final appraisal would be given to the employee and filed in his OPF. Army Eng'r Ctr. and AFGE Local 1052, 13 FLRA 707, 710, 713 (1984).

NTEU and USDA, Food and Nutrition Serv., 35 FLRA 254, 254-55, 257-60 (1990), ruled nonnegotiable, as an assignment of work, a proposal that supervisors discuss with each of their employees their appraisal of performance no more than three workdays prior to the initial preparation of the rating of record by the supervisor. But in POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 47 FLRA 10, 74 (1993), FLRA declared negotiable Provision 37 and rejected the notion that the proposal involved an assignment of work:

If the employee is dissatisfied with the rating recommended by the rating official, he may request reconsideration from the approving official with an oral or written explanation of why the employee believes he should have a higher rating. If the approving official assigns a rating that is lower than requested by the employee, the approving official must give written justification for the rating given. A copy of the final rating will be given to the employee.

POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 39 FLRA 783, 804, 807-08 (1991), resolved as negotiable a procedure the portion of Proposal 9 providing that "[t]he examiner will be informed whenever any question of clear error is raised," and a similar provision in Proposal 11:

...It requires the reviewer to discuss the allegation of error with the examiner at the time the alleged error is discovered. The purpose of the proposal is to give the examiner an opportunity explain or clarify his/her position, prior to a final determination that an error was committed. The proposal does not affect the substance of the review. That is, it does not affect the standards to be applied during the review, or the way in which the review is conducted. The proposal only requires communication between the reviewer and the examiner. The reviewer remains free to use his or her best judgment with respect to the case after discussion with the examiner.... [T]he requirement does not interfere with the Agency's deliberations concerning the review. After discussing the allegation with the examiner, the reviewers are free to discuss and deliberate among themselves without interferences from the Union....

POPA and Dept. of Commerce, PTO, 47 FLRA 954, 957-59 (1993), approved as negotiable Provision 2, calling on management officials who review and identify errors in employees' work to provide the employee with a list of errors before management officials communicate among themselves concerning the errors. Although the proposal required some assignment of work, management could designate the official who would perform the task.

Another proposal was approved that sought the right of union representation at meetings discussing performance ratings and related matters. The agency's duty to negotiate concerning that representation is not limited to the rights given to employees under § 7114(a)(2) of the Statute. AFGE, Nat'l Council of VA Locals and VA, 29 FLRA 515, 542, 565 (1987) (Proposal 8).
