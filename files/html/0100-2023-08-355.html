d. Termination of Proceedings to Pursue Case in Court

VEOA, but not USERRA, permits an appellant to terminate his Board case to pursue the claim in court. Eiler v. FTC, DC-4324-13-0317-I-1 (NP 4/25/2014), explained the route to judicial review of a VEOA claim first filed with the Board:

Under 5 U.S.C. § 3330b(a), in lieu of continuing the administrative redress procedure provided under 5 U.S.C. § 3330a(d), a preference eligible, or a veteran described by section 3330a(a)(1)(B) with respect to a violation described by such section, may elect to terminate those administrative proceedings and file an action with the appropriate United States district court not later than 60 days after the date of the election. Such an election may not be made before the 121st day after the date on which the appeal is filed with the Board, or after the Board has issued a judicially reviewable decision on the merits of the appeal. 5 U.S.C. § 3330b(b). The election shall be effective as of the date on which it is received, and the administrative proceeding to which it relates shall terminate immediately upon the receipt of such election. 5 U.S.C. § 3330b(c).

The Board's regulations similarly provide that, at any time beginning on the 121st day after an appellant files a VEOA appeal with the Board, if a judicially reviewable Board decision on appeal has not been issued, the appellant may elect to terminate the Board proceeding and file a civil action with an appropriate United States district court. 5 C.F.R. § 1208.24(a). The election is effective immediately on the date of receipt by the Board office where the appeal is being processed, id., and the Board will issue a termination order documenting the termination of the proceeding, 5 C.F.R. § 1208.24(b).

Accordingly, the appellant's VEOA appeal in this case is terminated. This determination as it relates to the VEOA appeal is neither an initial decision under 5 C.F.R. § 1201.111 nor a final Board decision. Therefore, it is subject neither to a petition for review nor a petition for enforcement by the Board. Nor is it subject to a petition for judicial review by the United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit. See 5 C.F.R. § 1208.24(b).

USERRA does not contain a comparable provision to VEOA, under which an appellant may elect to terminate a Board proceeding. See 38 U.S.C. §§ 4301-4333; 5 C.F.R. §§ 1208.11-1208.16; Practices and Procedures for Appeals under USERRA and VEOA, 65 Fed. Reg. 5410, 5411 (Interim Rule, Feb. 4, 2000) ("There is no provision in USERRA for a person who has filed a USERRA appeal with the Board to terminate the Board proceeding before it has concluded with the issuance of a decision."). The appellant has provided no basis for disturbing the initial decision's findings that the appellant did not establish Board jurisdiction over his USERRA appeal and, in any event, did not establish that his military service was a motivating factor in the agency's decision to not select him, even if the Board had jurisdiction over the case. See ID at 6-8. Accordingly, we DENY the petition for review as it relates to USERRA.
