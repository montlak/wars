E. DUES ALLOTMENTS UNDER 5 USC 7115(c)

5 USC 7115 provides:

(c)(1)Subject to paragraph (2) of this subsection, if a petition has been filed with the Authority by a labor organization alleging that 10 percent of the employees in an appropriate unit in an agency have membership in the labor organization, the Authority shall investigate the petition to determine its validity. Upon certification by the Authority of the validity of the petition, the agency shall have a duty to negotiate with the labor organization solely concerning the deduction of dues of the labor organization from the pay of the members of the labor organization who are employees in the unit and who make a voluntary allotment for such purpose.

(2)(A)  The provisions of paragraph (1) of this subsection shall not apply in the case of any appropriate unit for which there is an exclusive representative.

(B) Any agreement under paragraph (1) of this subsection between a labor organization and an agency with respect to an appropriate unit shall be null and void upon the certification of an exclusive representative of the unit.

Area Maintenance Support Activities, 86th Army Reserve Command and AFGE Local 4033, 32 FLRA 822, 824-25 (1988), is one of the few decisions to consider dues allotments under 5 USC 7115(c). Petitions for dues allotment are governed by 5 USC 7115(c) and §§ 2422.3(d), 2422.1(a) of FLRA's regulations. The three requirements for granting a "DA petition" are: (1) the petition must be for a unit having no exclusive representative; (2) the claimed unit must be appropriate; and (3) the petitioner must provide a showing of membership of not less than 10% in the unit claimed to be appropriate. The propriety of a unit may have been determined in a prior representation proceeding. The showing of interest in a prior, unsuccessful, representation case may demonstrate the validity of a DA petition.

Under FLRA regulations, when a petition for determination of eligibility for dues allotment is filed under 5 CFR 2422.1(a)(1)(ii), the petitioner must serve a copy of the petition on all known interested parties. 5 CFR 2422.4 (2022). After the petition is filed, and on request of the regional director, the activity must post copies of a notice to all employees affected by the petition. 5 CFR 2422.7 (2022). If the petition is amended to include other employees or so as to affect other parties, those parties must be notified of the petition and amendments so that their interests may be protected. The region is responsible for ensuring that the procedural protections are followed in the event of an amendment to the allotment petition. DOD, Defense Indus. Plant Equip. Ctr. and AFGE, 34 FLRA 114, 117-18 (1989).
