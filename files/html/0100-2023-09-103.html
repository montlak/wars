a. Grievances

Good cause for a late appeal is not established by a demonstration that outside grievance channels were being pursued. Briggs v. MSPB (Fed. Cir. 1986 NP No. 86-717); see Villaneuva v. DHUD, NY-0752-13-0077-I-1 (NP 1/17/2014) ("However, the appellant's decision to appeal his removal to the Board after he learned that the union had not filed a grievance on his behalf does not constitute good cause to waive the regulatory time limit for filing his appeal. See e.g., Eagleheart v. U.S. Postal Service, 102 M.S.P.R. 672, ¶ 5 (2006) and Shimmin v. Department of Justice, 63 M.S.P.R. 435, 439, aff'd, 43 F.3d 1486 (Fed. Cir. 1994) (Table) (an appellant's pursuit of appeal rights through other forums does not establish good cause for a delay in filing)."); Lackey v. USPS, SF-0752-10-0872-I-1 (NP 11/29/2011) ("Although the appellant's removal letter informed him of his right to file a Board appeal and a grievance on the same matter, it specifically stated that the filing of a grievance would not extend the time limit for filing an appeal with the Board. Accordingly, to the extent that the appellant is arguing that the delay was caused by the pursuit of his grievance and the agency's failure to comply with deadlines during the grievance process, this argument fails to constitute good cause for his untimely appeal." "Even if we were to consider this argument on the grounds that the appellant argued below that the arbitration process was unfair, the fact that the arbitration decision was unfavorable to the appellant does not establish good cause for the filing delay."); Anderson v. USPS, 109 MSPR 558, 560 ¶ 7, 2008 MSPB 193 (2008) ("Pursuit of a grievance does not extend the time for filing a Board appeal."); Masdea v. USPS, 90 MSPR 556, 559 ¶ 8 (2002) (implying that waiting for resolution of a grievance did not excuse a late Board appeal; finding it "well-settled" that awaiting the outcome in another forum will not provide good cause); Muse v. USPS, 82 MSPR 342, 346 ¶ 11 (1999) ("It is well settled...that pursuit of a grievance in another forum does not constitute good cause for the untimeliness of an appeal."); Allison v. USPS, 81 MSPR 335, 340 (1999) ("[T]he fact that the arbitration decision was unfavorable to the appellant does not establish good cause for the untimeliness."); Zuhlke v. USPS, 74 MSPR 401, 404 ¶ 13 (1997) (pursuit of a grievance does not constitute good cause for an untimely appeal); cf. Bacashihua v. MSPB, 811 F.2d 1498 (Fed. Cir. 1987) (affirming the Board's treatment of election requirements inherent in USPS grievance procedures).

Unless there were misrepresentations or other special circumstances, the period for filing a Board appeal will not be extended while the appellant seeks to obtain an arbitrability determination on a grievance that he would prefer to process under a labor contract rather than before the Board. Massingale v. MSPB, 736 F.2d 1521 (Fed. Cir. 1984); Pierce v. SBA, 20 MSPR 389, 390 (1984) (belated appeal was not excused by the appellant's attempts to obtain redress through an agency administrative grievance procedure); cf. Schaffer v. MSPB, 751 F.2d 1250 (Fed. Cir. 1985) (if there is Board jurisdiction over a reclassification appeal, it is the reclassification that triggers the appeal period, not the exhaustion of internal agency appeals); Clark v. Dept. of Navy, 3 MSPR 541, 543, 4 MSPB 60 (1980) (no good cause for lengthy extension of time to appeal as the appellant attempted to determine whether he could arbitrate a removal action). But see Lloyd v. SSA, 15 MSPR 217, 219 (1983), and decisions discussed in Chapter 1 concerning ineffective election of contract review procedures.
