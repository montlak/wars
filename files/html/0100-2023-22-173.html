3. Travel

Reasonable counsel fees, under Barton v. DHEW, 6 MSPR 720, 723, 6 MSPB 602 (1981), include expenses normally charged to a fee-paying client in the course of providing legal services. The Board awarded, in an appeal heard by the San Francisco office, plane fare for appellant's attorney of $502 for a trip to Washington to provide the appellant's response to the proposed WIGI denial. Car rental and parking expenses of counsel were approved in SSA v. Balaban, 39 MSPR 622, 625-26 (1989); see Garstkiewicz v. USPS, 50 MSPR 476, 479 (1991) ("We also find that the availability of a local attorney with the same specialized experience as the chosen representative is not a bar to the reimbursement of travel-related fees and expenses. An appellant may choose any representative as long as that person is willing and available to serve."); Goldbach v. OPM, 49 MSPR 9, 20 (1991) ("the Board orders agencies to reimburse travel costs to appellants who are awarded fees under 5 U.S.C. § 7701(g), where appropriate"). Hotel expenses were approved in Garcia v. USPS, 75 MSPR 198, 202 (1997).

To be compensable, travel costs must be itemized. Smit v. Dept. of Treasury, 61 MSPR 612, 622 (1994), remanded a case with the following observation:

...The administrative judge awarded the $1,000 even though no details were provided because she was aware of the representatives' travel and the cost seemed reasonable. The record, however, does not show that Ms. Erickson actually incurred expenses of $1,000 beyond what was directly incurred by the appellant. It does not appear that the administrative judge considered whether the travel expenses claimed by both the appellant and Ms. Erickson could be duplicative. Whether the travel costs are reimbursable as expenses incurred by Ms. Erickson or the appellant, the administrative judge should ensure that they are not duplicative. Therefore, the administrative judge should require Ms. Erickson to specifically identify the expenses with reference to trip dates and whether the amounts claimed are for hotel charges, airline tickets, meals, or other travel related expenses.
