C. METHOD AND PLACE OF FILING

Filing of an appeal at the regional office may be by personal delivery during business hours or by mail, fax or commercial delivery, or by electronic filing. 5 CFR 1201.22 (2022) provides:

(d) Method of filing an appeal. Filing of an appeal must be made with the appropriate Board office by commercial or personal delivery, by facsimile, by mail, or by electronic filing under § 1201.14.

(e) Filing a response. Filing of a response must be made with the appropriate Board office by commercial or personal delivery, by facsimile, by mail, or by electronic filing under § 1201.14.

5 CFR 1201.4(l) (2022) establishes the "date of filing" of an appeal:

Date of filing. A document that is filed with a Board office by personal delivery is considered filed on the date on which the Board office receives it. The date of filing by facsimile is the date of the facsimile. The date of filing by mail is determined by the postmark date; if no legible postmark date appears on the mailing, the submission is presumed to have been mailed five days (excluding days on which the Board is closed for business) before its receipt. The date of filing by commercial delivery is the date the document was delivered to the commercial delivery service. The date of filing by e-filing is the date of electronic submission.

The appeal should be filed at the appropriate Board office. 5 CFR 1201.22(a) (2022). The Board defines "appropriate regional or field office," 5 CFR 1201.4(d) (2022):

(d) Appropriate regional or field office. The regional or field office of the Board that has jurisdiction over the area where the appellant's duty station was located when the agency took the action. Appeals of Office of Personnel Management reconsideration decisions concerning retirement benefits, and appeals of adverse suitability determinations under 5 CFR part 731, must be filed with the regional or field office that has jurisdiction over the area where the appellant lives. Appendix II of these regulations lists the geographic areas over which each of the Board's regional and field offices has jurisdiction. Appeals, however, may be transferred from one regional or field office to another.

The Board's website, www.mspb.gov, has a linked section, "Contact" and an embedded link, "Office Locations," http://www.mspb.gov/contact/contact.htm, that gives the address and fax number of each of the Board's regional and field office, and with each office address is a geographical listing of the states, territories, or other regions within the jurisdiction of that office. Those locations are also provided by Appendix II, to 5 CFR Part 1201, "Appropriate Regional or Filed Office for Filing Appeals," https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-5/chapter-II/subchapter-A/part-1201#ap5.3.1201_1205.ii.

Chapter 2, section 1.b. of the Judges' Handbook describes the procedure that the Board's regional offices are to use in determining the proper place of filing:

Geographic Jurisdiction. The RO must ascertain whether it has geographic jurisdiction over the appeal. In appeals from OPM reconsideration decisions and from adverse suitability determinations, the appellant's residence at the time the appeal is filed controls. For other appeals, the location of the appellant's duty station when the action was taken general controls. See 5 C.F.R. § 1201.4(d). A possible exception to this principle is an appeal involving a directed reassignment, in which geographic jurisdiction may be based on the appellant's previous duty station. Appeals filed by applicants for appointment or promotion, under the WPA/WPEA, the VEOA, or the USERRA, may be directed to the office with jurisdiction over the area in which the appellant lives or to the office with the closest ties to the case, but because the circumstances of each case may vary widely there is no rule as to the appropriate office for these appeals set out in the Board's regulations.

Electronic filing is encouraged by the Board because the Board maintains all pleadings, orders, and filings electronically, readily and concurrently shared by Board members and staff working on appeals. The e-filing filing system is reasonably easy to use. It is economical. It saves the effort of mailing or faxing documents if all parties are e-filers. For those who use the e-filing system, a question in that script calls for designation of the Board office that will receive the appeal. However the appeal is filed, a regional office will likely determine in short order if it is the wrong office to adjudicate the appeal. If you learn that the appeal is lodged with the wrong office, let the AJ or regional director know. Phone calls work well.
