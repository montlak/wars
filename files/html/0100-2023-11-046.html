9. Internal Union Activities; Union Reporting Requirements

Not all union activities indirectly tied to representation or collective bargaining are beyond the limits of grants of official time. 5 USC 7131(b) precludes grants of official time for "internal union business," a phrase construed, according to legislative history, to include matters relating to the structure and institution of the labor organization. Reporting requirements imposed on unions serve the public by providing information concerning finances and structure of the organization. The external nature of the reports precluded their characterization as internal union business. A proposal was negotiable to grant official time to union officials to complete reports required by 5 USC 7120. AFGE Local 2823 and VARO, Cleveland, 2 FLRA 4, 5 (1979); see NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, BATF, 45 FLRA 339, 361 (1992) (Provision 4 negotiable: requirement that employer agree that designated union officials may use contractual allotments of official time to prepare and maintain records and reports required of union by federal agencies); NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, IRS, 38 FLRA 1366, 1368 (1991) (Proposal 1) (concerning official time for the preparation of IRS reports and preparation and maintenance of records and reports required from union by federal agencies); cf. AFGE Local 1399 and Dept. of Navy, March ARB, 66 FLRA 438, 439 (2012) ("As for the Union's reliance on NTEU, that decision held that a bargaining proposal providing for official time for the preparation of reports required by federal agencies did not conflict with § 7131, 38 FLRA at 1368; it did not hold that, as a matter of law, official time must be granted for such purposes. Accordingly, the Union's reliance on NTEU does not provide a basis for finding the award contrary to law."). Official time may not be granted for an employee's activity directed toward the internal management of a federal-sector professional organization; there was no showing of a direct relation between the organizational meetings and working conditions of employees in the unit. DHHS, SSA and AFGE, 27 FLRA 391, 396 (1987). FLRA declined to provide a statement of policy and guidance that was requested further to define when official time can be used for internal union matters. National Right to Work Legal Defense Foundation, Inc., 71 FLRA 531, 531-32 (2020) (Member DuBester concurring) (stating that issues pertaining to official time use could be better defined through facts developed in arbitration, ULP, or negotiability cases, and adding: "We note that Executive Order 13,837 has returned official time to the federal labor community's attention. Among other things, that Order: emphasizes that agencies should 'give special attention to ensuring' that official time is not used for 'internal union business in violation of [§] 7131(b)'; directs employees to obtain 'advance written authorization from their agency' before using official time; and states that the total amount of official time bargained under § 7131(d) of the Statute should ordinarily not exceed one hour per bargaining-unit employee each fiscal year. The effect and application of the Executive Order is most appropriately addressed through a case or controversy--not a general statement of policy or guidance."). Executive Orders 13,837, 13,839, and 13,836 were revoked by President Biden's Executive Order 14,003, of January 22, 2021. https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2021-01-27/pdf/2021-01924.pdf.
