C. NEGOTIABILITY ISSUE

FSIP does not decide novel negotiability disputes, Antilles Consol. Educ. Assn. v. FLRA, 977 F.3d 10, 15-16 (D.C. Cir. 2020):

The FLRA first held that the Impasses Panel lacked authority to decide whether the disputed workday provisions were negotiable, and thus lacked authority to order the parties to adopt them in the successor agreement. The Statute authorizes the Impasses Panel to resolve bargaining impasses. 5 U.S.C. § 7119(b)(1), (c)(1). This does not include authority to resolve antecedent legal questions about whether the disputed provisions are negotiable. Those questions turn on the scope of the duty to bargain in good faith, which the FLRA itself must determine. Id. § 7105(a)(2)(E). Thus, "issues of negotiability are to be resolved by the Authority, not the Panel." Commander Carswell Air Force Base, 31 F.L.R.A. 620, 624 (1988); see also Am. Fed'n Gov't Emps. v. FLRA, 778 F.2d 850, 854 (D.C. Cir. 1985). Nonetheless, if the Impasses Panel faces a proposal that is "substantively identical" to one that the FLRA already has found to be negotiable, the Panel may itself make the negotiability determination. Commander Carswell Air Force Base, 31 F.L.R.A. at 624-25.

The policy of the Panel ordinarily is to decline jurisdiction over the negotiability of a matter raised during the course of Panel proceedings. IBEW Local 121 and Dept. of Treasury, Bureau of Engraving and Printing, 10 FLRA 198, 198 n.1 (1982). FLRA may review a resultant negotiability dispute in a ULP proceeding if the agency fails to comply with the Panel order. See, e.g., DODDES and Antilles Consol. Ed. Assn., 71 FLRA 127 (2019) (Member DuBester dissenting) (recon. den., DODDES and Antilles Consol. Ed. Assn., 71 FLRA 359 (2019) (Member DuBester concurring), remanded for consideration of negotiability issue, Antilles Consol. Educ. Assn. v. FLRA, 977 F.3d 10 (D.C. Cir. 2020), FLRA decisions vacated as to remaining negotiability issue involving workday provision, leaving union to initiate negotiability appeal if proposal still relevant but rejected as nonnegotiable by agency, DODDES and Antilles Consol. Ed. Assn., 72 FLRA 414 (2021) (Chairman DuBester concurring; Member Abbott dissenting)).
