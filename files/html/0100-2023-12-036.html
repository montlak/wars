4. Untimely Petition; Waiver and Extension

Under 5 CFR 2429.23 (2022):

(a) Except as provided in paragraph (d) of this section, and notwithstanding § 2429.21(b) of this subchapter, the Authority or General Counsel, or their designated representatives, as appropriate, may extend any time limit provided in this subchapter for good cause shown, and shall notify the parties of any such extension. Requests for extensions of time shall be in writing and received by the appropriate official not later than five (5) days before the established time limit for filing, shall state the position of the other parties on the request for extension, and shall be served on the other parties.

(b) Except as provided in paragraph (d) of this section, the Authority or General Counsel, or their designated representatives, as appropriate, may waive any expired time limit in this subchapter in extraordinary circumstances. Request for a waiver of time limits shall state the position of the other parties and shall be served on the other parties.

(c) The time limits established in this subchapter may not be extended or waived in any manner other than that described in this subchapter.

(d) Time limits established in 5 U.S.C. 7105(f), 7117(c)(2) and 7122(b) may not be extended or waived under this section.

5 USC 7117(c)(2) sets the time for a negotiability appeal as 15 days after the agency makes the allegation of nonnegotiability.

FLRA dismisses untimely petitions. AFGE Local 2703 and Dept. of Commerce, NOAA, 2 FLRA 348, 348 (1979). Under 5 CFR 2424.21(a) (2022), the time limit for filing a petition for review of a negotiability determination is 15 days after the date that the exclusive representative is served with the agency's allegation that the duty to bargain in good faith does not extend to the matter proposed to be bargained or from an agency head's disapproval of a provision. Before rejecting a pleading as untimely, FLRA will generally issue an order to show cause if there is any possibility that a party can show that a submission is timely or that it can meet the standard for a waiver of a time limit provided in 5 CFR 2429.23 (2022). See AFGE Local 3434 and NASA Marshall Space Flight Ctr., 49 FLRA 382, 384 (1994). Filings attempted on the last possible day that go awry because of a technical problem for an e-filer find no forgiveness with FLRA. See AFGE Local 2228 and Dept. of Air Force, Ellsworth AFB, 71 FLRA 586, 588 (2020) (Member Abbott, concurring: "As well, the majority missed the opportunity to remind the LR community that we have previously held parties accountable for adverse results when they delayed until the last minute to file documents electronically through the FLRA's eFiling system. This petition is not the first filing, nor most likely will it be the last, to be dismissed under a cloud of vague assertions of technical problems. The Authority will not become the arbiter of whether sundry "the-dog-ate-it" excuses are or are not true. Either a filing is timely or it is not. In this case, it clearly was not. ").

Although the time limit for a union petition for review and for an agency (and union) position statement is statutory, under AFGE Local 3434 and NASA Marshall Space Flight Ctr., 49 FLRA 382, 384 (1994), pursuant to § 2429.23 of the FLRA regulations, FLRA may waive an expired time limit for an agency statement of position in a negotiability case in extraordinary circumstances. AFGE Local 3488 and FDIC, N.Y. Reg'l Office, 2 FLRA 912, 912 (1980), noted but did not exercise the authority to waive a time limit for a belated petition for review. It is doubtful that under current practice the 1980 FDIC decision furnishes a basis for waiver of the time limit for a negotiability appeal.

If an extension is provided by FLRA to cure a deficiency in a pleading, the date for filing the response is the date given in the extension. The Authority's rule allowing an additional five days to file other pleadings when those pleadings respond to documents received by mail, does not apply to an order requiring any correction to be filed by a certain date. AFGE Local 1931 and Naval Weapons Station, Concord, Cal., 34 FLRA 709, 710 (1990). If a further extension is requested to correct the deficiency, the request for the extension must be filed no less than five days before the established time limit for filing, and the request must state the position of the other party and be served on the other party. IBEW and Dept. of Interior, Bureau of Reclamation, Salt Lake City, Utah, 37 FLRA 468, 469 (1990); 5 CFR 2429.23(a) (2022). If a petition contains procedural deficiencies, an agency ordinarily has 30 days from the date it receives the perfected petition to file its statement of position. SEIU Local 200-B and VAMC, Syracuse, 44 FLRA 821, 823 (1992); see Int'l Org. of Masters, Mates and Pilots and Dept. of Navy, Chief of Naval Operations, 47 FLRA 218, 218-19 (1993) (where petition for review contains procedural deficiencies, an agency has 30 days to file its statement of position from date it receives a perfected petition).
