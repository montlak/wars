broida flra Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards Chapter 09: Review of Arbitration Awards, Subchapter 07: Procedures for Review of Arbitration Awards, Section B: Requirements of Exceptions; Regulations, Subsection 09: Effect of Failure to File Exception, Part a: Awards Under Authority of FSIP
a. Awards Under Authority of FSIP

Until 1988, an award resulting from a FSIP direction to proceed to interest arbitration was reviewable through the usual exceptions process. PTO and POPA, 15 FLRA 990, 990-91 (1984). Changes in the law now require that if the FSIP directs interest arbitration, subsequent review of negotiability issues is through the negotiability appeal process rather than through exceptions. See DODDS and Overseas Fed'n of Teachers, 33 FLRA 659, 661 (1988). Exceptions are used when the FSIP does no more than approve the parties' voluntary use of interest arbitration. Under preexisting law, the effect of the failure to file exceptions was the same whether the arbitration was conducted under the contract or whether the award was the result of an interest arbitration award culminating from FSIP-directed mediation and arbitration. DLA, Defense Gen. Supply Ctr., Richmond and Local 2047, AFGE, 28 FLRA 137, 140-41 (1987) (relying on VA and AFGE Nat'l Council of VA Locals, 23 FLRA 661 (1986)). An interest award issued under § 7119(b), through a procedure approved by FSIP as an appropriate procedure for resolving a negotiation impasse, was a final award if no exceptions were filed. To allow a party that had not filed exceptions to an award to defend its failure to implement that award in a subsequent ULP proceeding on grounds that should have been raised as exceptions under § 7122 would circumvent the procedures provided in § 7122(a) and frustrate the congressional intent respecting the finality of arbitration awards. Failure to implement an award resulting from FSIP-approved interest arbitration violated § 7116(a)(1), (6), and (8). Dept. of Air Force, Air Force Logistics Command, Wright-Patterson AFB and Council 214, AFGE, 15 FLRA 151, 152 (1984). An interest arbitration award unchallenged by exceptions was final and binding and could be enforced, without consideration of collateral attacks on the merits of the first award, in a subsequent arbitration. PTO and POPA, 26 FLRA 295, 297-98 (1987); cf. Pershing VAMC and AFGE Local 2338, 73 FLRA 67, 69 (2022) (Member Kiko concurring) ("A final action by the Panel is incorporated into a collective-bargaining agreement and the Authority considers arguments challenging an arbitrator's interpretation of a Panel decision as an argument that the award fails to draw its essence from that agreement."); Dept. of Air Force, Warner Robins Air Logistics Ctr. and AFGE Local 987, 56 FLRA 498, 500-01 (2000) (final action of the FSIP is incorporated in the parties' negotiated agreement, enforceable in arbitration).
