j. Opposition; Reply

The regulatory requirements governing oppositions to exceptions are, 5 CFR 2425.3 (2022) and 5 CFR 2425.5 (2022):

2425.3 Oppositions--who may file; time limits for filing; procedural and other requirements for filing.

(a) Who may file. A party to arbitration under the provisions of chapter 71 of title 5 of the United States Code may file an opposition to an exception that has been filed under § 2425.2 of this part.

(b) Timeliness requirements. Any opposition must be filed within thirty (30) days after the date the exception is served on the opposing party. For additional rules regarding computing the filing date, see 5 CFR 2425.8, 2429.21 and 2429.22.

2425.5 Content and format of opposition.

If you choose to file an opposition, then you may file your opposition on an optional form that is available on the FLRA's Web site at www.flra.gov, or in any other format that is consistent with this section. You meet this requirement if you file your opposition electronically through use of the FLRA's eFiling system on that Web site. Your failure to use, or properly fill out, an Authority-provided form will not, by itself, provide a basis for dismissing your opposition. If you choose to file an opposition, and you dispute any assertions that have been made in the exceptions, then you should address those assertions--including any assertions that any evidence, factual assertions, arguments (including affirmative defenses), requested remedies, or challenges to an awarded remedy were raised before the arbitrator. If the excepting party has requested an expedited, abbreviated decision under § 2425.7 of this part, then you should state whether you support or oppose such a decision and provide supporting arguments. You must provide copies of any documents upon which you rely (which you may upload as attachments if you file your opposition electronically through use of the FLRA's eFiling system), unless the Authority can easily access those documents (as discussed in § 2425.4(b) of this part) or the excepting party provided them with its exceptions.

Filing of the opposition is to be within 30 days of service of the exception. Under FLRA's rules governing service, 5 CFR 2429.27, service may be by email, but only when the receiving party has agreed to email service. If there is no agreement and the exceptions are served by both email and regular mail, the Authority's rule allowing for five days additional time from the date of mailing applies. AFGE Local 1858 and Dept. of Army, Redstone Arsenal, 66 FLRA 913, 914 (2012); see DHS, C&BP and NTEU, 68 FLRA 157, 159 (2015) (Member Pizzella dissenting) ("The Union has supported its contentions by submitting copies of correspondence with the Agency indicating that the Union did not agree to service by email. Consequently, the exceptions were not properly served by email, and the Union was entitled to an additional five days (after the thirty-day due date) to file its opposition. Because the Union filed its opposition within that additional five-day window, the opposition was timely filed.") (recon. den., DHS, C&BP and NTEU, 68 FLRA 722, 724 (2015) (Member Pizzella dissenting)). During the Pandemic starting in early 2020, FLRA is not accepting in-person filings. The FLRA website should be checked to determine when in-person filings will again be accepted.

The opposition filed to exceptions is not the place to raise an exception not already timely filed. See NRC and NTEU, 54 FLRA 1416, 1420 (1998) (a party may not raise additional exceptions in its opposition), reconsideration denied, 55 FLRA 666 (1999), on further consideration, 55 FLRA 1157 (1999); NAGE, Local R14-52 and Dept. of Army, Red River Army Depot, Texarkana, 46 FLRA 435, 438 n.2 (1992) ("In its opposition to the Union's exceptions, the Agency argued that the grievance is not arbitrable under the parties' collective bargaining agreement. The Agency's contention constitutes an exception to the Arbitrator's award. Because the Agency's exception was not filed within the time period applicable to the filing of exceptions, it will be dismissed. See American Federation of Government Employees, Local 2145 and U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs Medical Center, Richmond, Virginia, 39 FLRA 1045, 1047 (1991) (dismissing an untimely exception included in a party's opposition)."). An opposition to the exception may be filed by a party with 30 days after the date of service of the exception. 5 CFR 2425.3(b) (2022). An opposition to the exceptions must be timely filed under the regulations if it is to be considered. VA Northern Arizona VAHCS and AFGE Local 2401, 57 FLRA 922, 922 n.2 (2002) (lack of extraordinary circumstances sufficient to grant waiver of expired time limit for filing of union's opposition to exceptions); see DHS, C&BP and NTEU, 64 FLRA 916, 918-19 (2010) ("The Authority previously has declined to find extraordinary circumstances when a party conceded that its filing was "technically untimely" and failed to provide a sufficient basis to excuse the lack of timeliness. U.S. Dep't of the Treasury, Customs Serv., Wash., D.C., 38 FLRA 875, 877 (1990). Similarly, here, we do not consider the Union's opposition."); VAMC Detroit and AFGE Local 933, 64 FLRA 794, 795 (2010) ("a simple mistake in filing does not constitute a basis for waiving an expired time limit"); VAMC Lexington and NAGE, 54 FLRA 429, 433 n.5 (1998) (declining to consider an untimely opposition; respondent also failed to comply with order directing filing of evidence of service); NATCA, MEBA/NMU and Dept. of Transp., FAA, 46 FLRA 1367, 1368 (1993) (declining to consider opposition mistakenly filed with FLRA regional office, mailed by region to FLRA Headquarters within time for filing but arriving at Headquarters after filing period elapsed); cf. Bureau of Prisons, FCC Miami and AFGE Local 3690, 73 FLRA 154 (2022) (delivery by USPS to wrong address allowed reconsideration of procedural dismissal of arbitration exceptions); DHHS, NIH and AFGE Local 2419, 64 FLRA 266, 268 n.7 (2009) (excusing opposition that was belatedly received, but timely submitted and went astray because, although properly addressed to FLRA, it was delivered by Postal Service to wrong agency, returned to union, then remailed to FLRA; these constituted "extraordinary circumstances" warranting waiver of the time limit because union's untimely refiling was due to circumstances beyond its control).

Technical deficiencies in a statement of service of exceptions will not excuse a belatedly-filed opposition. See Bremerton Metal Trades Council and Dept. of Navy, Puget Sound Naval Shipyard, 59 FLRA 583, 583-84 (2004). The parties will not be allowed to rely on FLRA to issue an acknowledgment of receipt of exceptions to trigger the time for filing an opposition. See DOD, DMA, Hydrographic/Topographic Ctr. and AFGE Local 3407, 35 FLRA 1016, 1016 n.1 (1990). If an initial filing of exceptions by a party is deficient, but the other party files an opposition within the time allowed by regulations, the opposition will be considered timely and properly before FLRA. AFGE Local 169 and Tinker AFB, Air Logistics Ctr., Oklahoma City, 7 FLRA 27, 28 n.2 (1981). If the deficiency is not corrected on time, the opposition may be rejected. See AFGE Local 933 and VAMC Detroit, 64 FLRA 718, 718 n.* (2010) (dismissing agency opposition to exceptions when agency failed to timely file its corrected submission within time allowed by FLRA).

The time period for filing an opposition is as strictly construed as the time period for filing exceptions, as in AFGE Local 3230 and EEOC, 59 FLRA 610, 610 n.3 (2004), concluding that the agency improperly considered service of exceptions by commercial delivery to be the equivalent of mail delivery:

The Authority issued an Order to Show Cause why the Agency's opposition should not be dismissed as untimely. In its response to the Order, the Agency claims that its opposition was timely under 5 C.F.R. § 2429.22, which allows 5 days to be added to the filing deadline when exceptions are served by mail. In the alternative, the Agency argues that extraordinary circumstances prevented timely filing. Under the Authority's Regulations, the time limit for filing an opposition is 30 days after the date of service of the exception, 5 C.F.R. § 2425.1(c), which is the date the exception is either deposited in the U.S. mail, delivered in person, or received from commercial delivery. Id. at § 2429.27(d). Only if the exception is served by U.S. mail are 5 days added to the filing period. See id. at § 2429.22; see, e.g., AFGE, Local 3434, 49 FLRA 382, 384 (1994). The Union's exceptions were served by commercial delivery, not by U.S. mail and, therefore, the 5-day extension does not apply to the Agency's filing deadline. In addition, the Agency has not shown that extraordinary circumstances warrant waiving the expired deadline. Accordingly, we will not consider the Agency's opposition.

See AFGE Local 3105 and DHS, C&BP, 63 FLRA 128 (2009) (late receipt of an opposition filed by commercial delivery was rejected even though delivery time would likely have been the same had filing been by mail).

There is no provision in FLRA regulations for filing a reply to an opposition to exceptions, unless there are special circumstances that justify the reply submission. NAGE Local R5-169 and Dept. of Army, AAFES, Ft. Polk, 36 FLRA 348, 349 n.* (1990); see AFGE Local 2145 and VAMC Richmond, 63 FLRA 78 n.3 (2009) (FLRA's permission must be requested under § 2429.26 for filing supplemental submission); VA Hefner VAMC and AFGE Local 1738, 59 FLRA 422, 422 n.* (2003) (FLRA regulations do not provide for filing of response to opposition to party's exceptions; although § 2429.26(a) permits filing of additional documents, it is incumbent on moving party to demonstrate why FLRA should consider supplemental submission); AFGE Local 2128 and DOD DCMA, 58 FLRA 519, 519 n.1 (2003) (5 CFR 2429.26(a) grants FLRA leave to permit party to file additional documents, but moving party must demonstrate why FLRA should consider supplemental submission); Dept. of Treasury, Customs Serv. and NTEU Chap. 143, 52 FLRA 622, 625 (1996) (FLRA regulations do not provide for filing supplemental submissions in support of exceptions; "it is incumbent upon the moving party to demonstrate a reason why the Authority should consider such supplemental submission").
