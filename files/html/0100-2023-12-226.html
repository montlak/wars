broida mspb Chapter 05: Appeal and Discovery Chapter 05: Initial Decision and Petition for Review, Subchapter 02: Petition for Review; Reconsideration, Section J: Procedure on Remand, Subsection 02: Evidence, Findings From First Hearing, Part a: Effect of MSPB Order Vacating Initial Decision
a. Effect of MSPB Order Vacating Initial Decision

Should the Board vacate the initial decision, the AJ must reassess the entire case and readjudicate the merits. A supplemental initial decision is then insufficient; an entirely new initial decision must be prepared. See Southerland v. DOD, 119 MSPR 566, 572 ¶ 13, 2013 MSPB 46 (2013) (when the Board vacates an initial decision, the AJ is not bound, on remand, to his earlier findings; "That the administrative judge changed his analysis of the insubordination charges after he conducted the required credibility analysis on remand does not provide a reason to disturb his new findings."); Gregory v. FCC, 84 MSPR 22, 25 ¶ 6 (1999) ("By relying on vacated findings, the AJ failed to weigh all of the testimonial and documentary evidence from the original proceeding and the remand proceeding, and then provide a reasoned evaluation to support her findings of fact and conclusions of law."); Davis v. Dept. of Transp., FAA, 28 MSPR 130, 132 (1985) (requiring the AJ to reconsider and readjudicate a performance case that was initiated under Chapter 75 and remanded for consideration under Chapter 43 after the original AJ retired). If the Board vacates only certain findings, the AJ is to address only those findings. The Board may identify in its remand order the findings that should be made on remand. See Gillis v. Dept. of Army, 33 MSPR 270, 274 (1987). The Board may also direct, or determine through a motion to strike, the scope of the documentary evidence that the AJ will consider on remand. See Dodson v. Dept. of Navy, 33 MSPR 386, 390 n.2 (1987) (striking a deposition transcript that the agency submitted in response to the appellant's PFR); cf. Lopes v. Dept. of Navy, 119 MSPR 106, 111 ¶ 10, 2012 MSPB 135 (2012) (if an adverse action is set aside for lack of due process, on its second try and second appeal (assigned to the same AJ), the AJ may use findings from the first appeal with regard for due process and allowance for further development of evidence). [Refer earlier in this chapter to "Effect of Remand or Supplemental Initial Decision on Interim Relief"; refer later in this chapter to "Remand for More Evidence."]

A party sought to withdraw a PFR after the Board vacated an initial decision and directed rebriefing. The Board determined that the effect of the withdrawal of the PFR was to reinstate the previously vacated initial decision. See Augustine v. VA, SF-3443-00-0085-I-1 ¶ 2 (2003) (not reprinted in MSPR).
