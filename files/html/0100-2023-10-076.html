a. Avoidance of Unit Fragmentation

To avoid undue fragmentation of units, the unit sought should embrace employees with significant employment concerns or personnel issues that are different or unique from those of excluded employees. Dept. of Interior, Nat'l Park Serv. and FOP Lodge 1-f, 57 FLRA 582, 584 (2001). FLRA declined to find appropriate, as an addition to a unit of employees of an activity laboratory, personnel of an administrative organization. It was not determinative that the sought-after employees were in the same geographical location. The additional employees sought for the unit were from just one of the locations employing similar employees. FLRA determined that the unit was inappropriate because employees who would be included in, and those who would be excluded from, the unit sought performed the same functions, had the same job classifications, skills and qualifications, were engaged in common and integrated work projects, and had common supervision and working conditions. Among employees in the proposed unit, there was no clear and identifiable community of interest separate and distinct from those employees who would be excluded. Additionally, the unit sought was not coextensive with any administrative component or subdivision of the activity. The unit sought would fragment the activity and several of its components. Nat'l Marine Fisheries Serv., Southeast Fisheries Ctr. and AFGE Local 2875, 3 FLRA 499, 501 (1980). A unit of 42 of 421 activity employees involved in extensive interchange among jobs was inappropriate. It would lead to fragmentation of an integrated administrative component or subdivision of the agency on the basis of geographic location alone. IFPTE Local 3 and Naval Sea Support Ctr., Atl. Detachment, 7 FLRA 626, 627 (1982) (unit involving a Training Systems Detachment). A group of 35 of 688 employees did not share a distinct community of interest despite their inclusion, prior to a reorganization, in a larger unit represented by the union that petitioned for an election and certification of the proposed unit. USDA, OCIO and AFSME Council 26, 61 FLRA 879, 883 (2006) (citing DHS, C&BP and AFGE, 61 FLRA 485 (2006)).

A single division of NIH was not an appropriate unit. The area of consideration for RIFs and other personnel actions was facility-wide. There was a community of interest with the rest of the employee population of the main facility at Bethesda. The unit sought included just 118 of the approximately 1,400 professional employees at NIH. Personnel administration occurred through a central office. Nat'l Inst. of Child Health and Human Dev., NIH and NFFE Local 1967, 9 FLRA 526, 527-28 (1982); see Walter Reed Army Med. Ctr. and NAGE, 52 FLRA 852, 856-57 (1997) (disapproving unit of employees in the Radiology Department, one component among many units and many employees split into several other units of recognition).
