E. INTERNAL UNION BUSINESS ON DUTY TIME

Few FLRA decisions interpret § 7131(b) of the Statute, precluding the conduct of internal union business on duty time, as it relates to § 7116(b)(8), making the violation a ULP. AFGE Local 987 and Dept. of Air Force, Warner Robins Air Logistics Ctr., Robins AFB, 37 FLRA 119, 125-27 (1990), affirmed without comment the decision of an ALJ finding a violation. The case grew out of a reprimand issued to an employee who was also a union steward and member of the Local's election committee. The employee was reprimanded because during duty hours she engaged in some discussions relating to her duties as an election committee member. The ALJ observed:

AFGE Local 987 contends that these incidents...did not violate...the Statute because Purser was not on duty status. The FLRA, however, found that when a union official who was not an employee within the meaning of the Statute, solicited union membership of employees who were on duty status, the Union violated section 7131(b) of the Statute. Service Employees International Union Local 556, 17 FLRA 862 (1985).... In so holding the FLRA found, with respect to the Section 7131(b) requirement that internal union business be conducted during nonduty time, "This requirement applies to an employee's use of duty time and, as here, whenever a Union representative meets with employees on their duty time for such purposes."... I reject AFGE Local 987's contention that it had not violated Section 7131(b) of the Statute because Purser was in nonduty status while the employees she spoke to were in duty status.

...

Finally AFGE Local 987 contends that the violation involved here is de minimis and too insignificant to rise to the status of an unfair labor practice. This contention is rejected. I conclude that talking to eight different employees during their working hours about internal union business is not an insignificant or de minimis violation of the limitations set forth in Section 7131(b) of the Statute.

The ALJ noted in AFGE 987 that the ULP was not barred because the agency (charging party) reprimanded the union steward for her conduct. No election of remedies was required. The union was the respondent in the ULP charge, the employee was the "respondent" in the reprimand. The parties were not the same. Id. at 127.
