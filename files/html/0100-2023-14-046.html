F. TIMEKEEPING; LOGS

Changes in timekeeping procedures are negotiable. See DHHS, Region II and NTEU Chap. 218, 26 FLRA 814, 828 (1987) (ALJ Decision). A proposal was bargainable that mandated the establishment and use of a sign-in and sign-out register to record arrival and departure entries, including leave and overtime, on a daily basis. AFGE Local 1760 and DHEW, SSA, Northeastern Program Serv. Ctr., 8 FLRA 202, 202-03 (1982). The union could also compel negotiation of a procedure designed to avoid sign-in logs. Overseas Educ. Ass'n and DODDS, 29 FLRA 734, 758-60 (1987) (Proposal 15). Negotiations could be required as to a proposal that exempted unit employees from a requirement to use a time clock, and that permitted them to enter their time manually. The proposal affected working conditions. Recordation of time led to calculation of time and benefits. The agency's means and methods of performing work were not shown to be directly affected by the means chosen to record time. Planners, Estimators, and Progressmen Ass'n Local No. 8 and Dept. of Navy, Charleston Naval Shipyard, 13 FLRA 455, 455-58 (1983).

A negotiable proposal required employees report to their supervisors rather than be required to use a time clock. The proposal was consistent with the right of the agency to determine the methods and means of performing work. There was no requirement in the proposal that the agency assign particular work to the supervisors, that is, employees were only required to report to their supervisors, and no supervisory duty was defined by the proposal. AFGE Local 1603 and Dept. of Navy, Navy Commissary Store, Patuxent River, 16 FLRA 96, 96-97 (1984). Negotiations may be compelled over a proposal that obligates management to maintain records of the overtime assignments. NFFE, Council of VA Locals and VA, 31 FLRA 360, 425 (1988). It was determined, however, that it is management's right to require employees to keep time logs of their daily work and there is no right to bargain over the decision to require those logs. Dept. of Treasury, IRS and NTEU Chap. 10, 20 FLRA 46, 50 (1985).

Considered nonnegotiable--infringing the right to assign work--were proposals exempting bargaining unit employees from being required to enter time under an electronic timekeeping system, WebTA, designed to replace an existing automated timekeeping system, NLRBA and NLRB, 70 FLRA 289, 290 (2017):

We find that the proposals affect management's right to assign work under § 7106(a)(2)(B) of the Statute. In fact, it is difficult to imagine a proposal that would affect the right to assign work more directly or completely.

Accordingly, the proposals are outside the duty to bargain unless the Union can establish that they enforce an applicable law within the meaning of § 7106(a)(2),[14] or fall within an exception under § 7106(b).[15]

[14] See AFGE, Local 997, 66 FLRA 499, 500 (2012) (Local 997); NLRB Union, NLRB Prof'l Ass'n, 62 FLRA 397, 401-03 (2008), aff'd sub nom. NLRB Union v. FLRA, 313 Fed. Appx. 328 (D.C. Cir. 2009).

[15] See e.g., NTEU, 66 FLRA at 585-86; Local 997, 66 FLRA at 501.

The Union expressly concedes that it is not claiming that the proposals enforce "applicable laws" within the meaning of § 7106(a)(2), or that they fall within any exceptions under § 7106(b). As the Union concedes that the proposals do not fall within a limitation on management's rights, we find that the proposals are outside the duty to bargain.[18]

[18] See, e.g., NTEU, 66 FLRA at 585-86 (finding a proposal outside the duty to bargain where the proposal affected management's right to assign work and the union failed to assert that the proposal was either a procedure or an appropriate arrangement under § 7106(b)); Local 997, 66 FLRA at 501 (finding a proposal that affected management's right to discipline under § 7106(a)(2)(A) outside the duty to bargain where the union did not argue that the proposal was an exception to management's rights under § 7106(b), or that it enforced an applicable law under § 7106(a)(2)); Nat'l Weather Serv. Emps. Org., 63 FLRA 450, 453 (2009) (finding a provision contrary to management's rights where the union did not expressly state that the provision enforces an "applicable law" within the meaning of § 7106(a)(2), and did not assert that the provision was encompassed by any exception to management's rights under § 7106(b)).
