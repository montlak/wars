broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 11: Promotions, Vacancies, and Appointments, Section A: Advertising and Filling Vacancies

A. ADVERTISING AND FILLING VACANCIES

Although proposals requiring an agency to fill vacancies interfere with management rights under § 7106(a) of the Statute, AFGE Local 3354 and USDA, Farm Servs. Agency, 54 FLRA 807, 814-16 (1998), a proposal that the employer announce all vacancies within the eligibility of unit members, regardless of how the vacancies are to be filled, is a negotiable procedure under 7106(b)(2). AFGE, Int'l Council of Marshals Serv. Locals and DOJ, Marshals Serv., 2 FLRA 765, 765-66 (1980). A provision was negotiable that the agency post various positions under competitive procedures. The proposal did not require a selection, nor did it preclude management from filling the jobs through any appropriate sources. NTEU Chap. 55 and IRS, Columbia Dist., 15 FLRA 820, 822-23 (1984). A provision that announcements of vacant positions be posted on all bulletin boards for at least five days is a negotiable procedure under 7106(b)(2). DOD, Dept. of Navy, Navy Ordnance Station, Louisville and IAM Local Lodge 830, 4 FLRA 760, 776 (1980) (ALJ Decision); see Illinois Nurses' Ass'n and VAMC, Hines, 28 FLRA 212, 235-37 (1987) (Proposal 6 negotiable: procedures for notice of openings and mechanism assuring employees have reasonable opportunity to apply and be considered for positions); Illinois Nurses Ass'n and VAMC, N. Chicago, 27 FLRA 714, 738, 740-41 (1987) (Section 5 of Proposal 8 negotiable: procedure for informing employees of existing vacancies).

Detailed requirements concerning posting of announcements and the content of those announcements were approved as negotiable procedures in New York State Nurses Ass'n and VA Bronx Med. Ctr., 30 FLRA 706, 729-31, 763-64 (1987). A proposal that new positions other than lateral transfers be advertised within 30 days as promotional opportunities was negotiable, at least to the extent the proposal applied to positions within the bargaining unit. Nothing required the agency to fill newly created positions or restricted the right of management to fill those jobs from any appropriate source. FUSE, NAGE and Naval Underwater Sys. Ctr., 23 FLRA 360, 363 (1986); see AFGE Local 3354 and USDA, Farm Servs. Agency, 54 FLRA 807, 814 (1998) ("Once management has made a determination that it will fill vacant positions, a proposal requiring the Agency to post a notice concerning those vacancies constitutes a negotiable procedure within the meaning of section 7106(b)(2) of the Statute."); NFFE Local 1482 and DOD, DMA, Hydrographic/Topographic Ctr., Louisville, 45 FLRA 52, 55-56, 58 (1992) (Proposal 1 negotiable: requiring 30 days of advance posting of vacancies and establishing application procedures); Nat'l Union of Hosp. and Health Care Employees, Dist. 1199 and VAMC, Dayton, 28 FLRA 435, 453, 456 (1987) (approving negotiability of proposal that vacancies be posted for at least seven days). A proposal was within the scope of bargaining that required management to counsel all incumbent employees at a program office as to potential position availability prior to selecting any employee for reassignment. The required counseling included providing a list of all vacancies along with instructions on how to apply, coupled with a requirement that management give employees "every possible consideration" for the position. AFGE Local 1760 and DHHS, SSA, 28 FLRA 160, 162-64 (1987).

A provision for advertising vacancies coupled with a restriction on the area of consideration may be nonnegotiable. A proposal was nonnegotiable that required advertisement of all local vacancies under local merit promotion procedures and precluded management from expanding the area of consideration as long as there were local qualified employees. NAGE Local R7-23 and Dept. of Air Force, Scott AFB, 16 FLRA 367, 368 (1984).
