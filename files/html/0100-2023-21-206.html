c. AWOL Covered by Period of Compensation

Under New v. VA, 72 MSPR 574, 588-89 (1996), discussed in the prior section:

An employee who fully recovers from a compensable injury more than 1 year from the date compensation began and who seeks priority consideration for restoration under section 353.303 must demonstrate that her separation was the result of, or substantially related to, a compensable injury. See Miner v. United States Postal Service, 31 M.S.P.R. 369, 372 (1986), aff'd, 819 F.2d 280, 282 (Fed. Cir. 1987). An employee who has been removed for cause, other than a compensable injury, is not entitled to restoration rights and cannot appeal a denial of restoration to the Board. Wright, 62 M.S.P.R. at 128; Miner, 31 M.S.P.R. at 372-73. The Board has also held that a charge based on AWOL cannot be sustained where OWCP determines that the employee was entitled to compensation benefits as a result of a work-related injury for the entire period charged to AWOL. See Green v. Department of the Army, 38 M.S.P.R. 52, 54 (1988) (charges of failure to follow orders to report for work and AWOL could not be sustained where OWCP later found that the appellant was entitled to compensation for the period covering the conduct charged).

For some history of the New litigation, refer to the prior section and to New v. VA, 82 MSPR 609 (1999), following remand from Federal Circuit decision, New v. VA, 142 F.3d 1259 (Fed. Cir. 1998), determining that restoration was validly denied based on a 1988 removal for poor overall attendance, following remand from federal district court and other litigation, New v. VA, 99 MSPR 404 (2005), finding that appellant was entitled to priority consideration for restoration.). See Allen v. USPS, 88 MSPR 491, 495 (2001) (dates charged against the appellant as AWOL and leading to a last chance agreement that led to removal were, in turn, covered by a period of a compensation grant; as a result, the removal may have been related to the appellant's compensable injury); Green v. Dept. of Army, 49 MSPR 289, 293 (1991) (removal was set aside because a major charge, AWOL, was unfounded as a result of an OWCP finding of a compensable injury covering the AWOL charge, restoration was not barred based on a prior unrelated charge of alleged travel voucher charge that was not included in the removal proposal); Green v. Dept. of Army, 38 MSPR 52 (1988) (remanding for consideration of the effect of an OWCP approval of a period of time considered as AWOL by the agency and relied upon, among other charges, in his removal).
