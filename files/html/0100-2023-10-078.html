c. Organizational or Geographical Location; Telework

A compartmented unit will be rejected if other employees, sharing a community of interest at other locations, are not in the unit, with the likely result being proliferation of units following allowance of the smaller unit. Mid-Continent Mapping Ctr., Nat'l Mapping Div., Geological Survey and NFFE Local 1960, 4 FLRA 426, 430 (1980). Although a "residual" unit, consisting of all eligible unrepresented employees of the type covered by the petition, may be appropriate for a dispersed group of employees, FLRA will not approve a separate unit for a single employee: "[T]he principle of collective bargaining presupposes that there is more than one person on whose behalf bargaining takes place." GSA Las Vegas Fleet Mgmt. Ctr. and AFGE Council 236, 48 FLRA 1258, 1262 (1993).

A regional division level unit was approved when the division was geographically separated from other divisions headquartered in Washington, there were functional distinctions in the divisions, little interchange among employees, and distinct chains of supervision. Fish and Wildlife Serv., Fin. Ctr., Denver, Colo. and NFFE Local 2053, 14 FLRA 153, 155 (1984). If unions are competing over inclusion of employees into a local rather than a national unit, FLRA will consider whether the location sought to be organized is permanent or transitory. When the location was soon to be phased out, inclusion of the employees in a national unit would be more appropriate, a result that would reduce fragmentation and promote effective dealings and efficiency of agency operations. FAA, S. Region, Balboa and Prof'l Airways Sys. Specialists and Fed. Aviation Sci. and Technological Ass'n, 3 FLRA 709, 712 (1980). [Refer earlier in this chapter to "Anticipated Changes in Workforce; Continuity of Employment."] Absence of employees' "co-location" within an organization does not preclude a finding of community of interest. See DLA, Ft. Belvoir and AFGE, 60 FLRA 701, 705 (2005).

Employees working at remote locations through telework may create issues of unit inclusion. EPA and NTEU, 70 FLRA 533 (2018) (Member Abbott dissenting), affirmed a regional determination that an employee, who was hired and worked at the agency's central office in Washington, was no longer in a unit when the certification included professional employees employed by and located at the headquarters office, when the employee then relocated to the state of Washington, teleworked, and continued to report organizationally to the headquarters establishment but the agency changed her official duty station to Buckley, Washington. Member Abbott, dissenting, urged clarification of the law as to unit membership of the significant and increasing number of employees who telework.
