a. Agency May Rescind

The Board holds unto itself "no responsibility to protect the appellant from the consequences of her unjustified refusal to abide by the terms of the settlement agreement." Nease v. Dept. of Army, 103 MSPR 118, 128 ¶ 21, 2006 MSPB 243 (2006). The right of rescission is not limited to the appellant: an agency may obtain rescission of a settlement upon a material breach. Spragan v. DOJ, 70 MSPR 486, 495 (1996) (declining to impose sanctions on the appellant amounting to defaulting her on the underlying appeal: "[b]ecause of our action rescinding the settlement agreement, enforcement of the agreement is now moot and imposing sanctions will not serve the purpose of achieving compliance."). The option to rescind an agreement for material breach is bilateral: either party may raise an issue of material breach and assert a right to rescind an agreement and return to litigation over the underlying claim. See Crayton v. Dept. of Navy, 65 MSPR 509 (1994) (Table) (on remand, when agency elected to enforce rather than rescind agreement based on appellant's breach, AJ recommended that Board order appellant to submit to agency completed resignation SF-52 contemplated by agreement or authorize agency to consider settlement itself as appellant's resignation); Crayton v. Dept. of Navy, 62 MSPR 497, 501 (1994) (remanding to consider the agency's allegations that the settlement was breached by the appellant: "The administrative judge shall determine whether the appellant breached the settlement agreement, and, if so, he shall afford the agency the option of enforcing the agreement or rescinding the agreement and reinstating the appeal for adjudication of the merits of the removal action."); cf. Castellanos v. Dept. of Army, 51 MSPR 385, 389 (1991) ("As to the contention by each party that the other party violated the terms of the settlement agreement, these contentions are properly raised in a petition for enforcement before the regional office[.]").

Baig v. Dept. of Navy, 66 MSPR 269, 277 (1995), discussed options available to a nonbreaching party:

...We have held...that where there has been a material breach of the agreement, the nonbreaching party has the option of enforcing the terms of the agreement or rescinding the agreement and going forward with the original claim. See Betterly v. Department of Veterans Affairs, 47 MSPR 63, 66-67 (1991). In this case, the agency has not requested rescission as an alternative remedy. Therefore, if the agency wishes this remedy, additional proceedings must be conducted before a remedy is ordered. See Murphy v. US Postal Service, 54 MSPR 202, 206 (1992).[3]

[3] If the agency wishes to rescind the settlement agreement, it should file a new petition for enforcement requesting that relief with the New York Regional Office.
