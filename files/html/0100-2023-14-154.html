broida flra Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations Chapter 06: Subjects of Bargaining: Specific Applications of Negotiability Determinations, Subchapter 07: Leave Arrangements, Section H: Administrative Leave, Subsection 02: Health and Welfare, Part a: Blood Donors; Medical Problems
a. Blood Donors; Medical Problems

A negotiable proposal defined how excused absence would be calculated for employees donating blood. There was no compelling need for a contrary agency regulation. As to interference with the right to assign work, the agency could deny permission for employees to donate blood if the time needed interfered with the agency's work. NFFE Local 1429 and Dept. of Army, Letterkenny Army Depot, 23 FLRA 117, 119-20 (1986). But see AFGE Local 1815 and Army Aviation Ctr., Ft. Rucker, 53 FLRA 606, 613 (1997) (Provision 3: same result as NFFE Local 1655); NFFE Local 1655 and DOD, Nat'l Guard Bureau, Alexandria, 49 FLRA 874, 880 (1994) (no arrangements claim; negotiability of Provision 4, seeking mandatory leave for blood donors, was denied). Nonnegotiable was Provision 10 in NTEU and Dept. of Treasury, Customs Serv., 46 FLRA 696, 719-20 (1992), requiring the employer to grant administrative leave for blood donors. The proposal allowed management to refuse only when "work demands leave no other reasonable alternatives," meaning that the proposal infringed on the right to assign work. The proposal was not advanced as an arrangement. Another nonnegotiable proposal was unconditional, and the record did not support the determination in the Local 1429 decision that the agency could deny employees permission to donate blood when the time needed interfered with the agency mission. AFGE Local 1815 and Army Aviation Ctr., Ft. Rucker, 28 FLRA 1172, 1178 (1987) (Provision 7); see AFGE Local 1345 and Dept. of Army, Ft. Carson, 48 FLRA 168, 181 (1993) (nonnegotiable: Proposal 7, requiring mandatory administrative leave for blood donations).

A proposal was negotiable for administrative leave for time spent receiving medical care rendered by the agency. The (late) FPM permitted agencies to grant the leave for participation in officially sponsored and administered health programs authorized under 5 USC 7901. NAGE Local R1-109 and VAMC, Newington, 47 FLRA 651, 656 (1993).
