broida flra Chapter 08: Unfair Labor Practices Chapter 08: Unfair Labor Practices, Subchapter 02: Management Violations, Section D: Unilateral Changes in Employment Conditions and Past Practice, Subsection 08: Specific Changes Requiring Impact or Decision Bargaining, Part s: Upward Mobility, Promotion Programs
s. Upward Mobility, Promotion Programs

An expansion of the size of an upward mobility program, involving more employees and diminishing the opportunities for selection of those previously eligible, had more than a de minimis impact and required impact bargaining. The employees in the program were also subject to a greater chance of reassignment, and there was an effect on the nature and safety of their tasks. The date of implementation of changes in the program was negotiable, as long as it did not preclude management from acting at all. So, too, a proposal was negotiable that urged, but did not require, selection of qualified candidates who had not previously been selected to fill upward mobility positions. The proposal did not restrict management's right to select candidates. There was no difficulty with the negotiability of a proposal that management would, when possible, solicit volunteers to work with upward mobility trainees in units and situations requiring field team work before assigning the selectee to work with an equally qualified and capable nonvolunteer partner. There was no infringement on the right to assign work when management could choose between equally qualified workers. A proposal for specific training was outside the duty to bargain. It involved the assignment of work. Also requiring assignment of work and, accordingly, nonnegotiable, was a proposal requiring management to assign specific functions to regular employees who were working with upward mobility selectees, preventing the assignment of those functions to other personnel. A proposal to have the upward mobility selectee on the same shift as his training officer was negotiable only at the election of the agency. The proposal would directly affect and determine numbers and types of employees assigned to work projects or tours of duty. A proposal to limit the duration of upward mobility assignments was not negotiable. Proposals seeking to determine the duration of work assignments restrict the right to assign employees. Limitations on selection of employees for training details were not negotiable. Section 7106(a)(2)(C) reserves the right to management to make selections for appointments from any appropriate source. Limitations on the assignment of overtime work to trainees was an infringement on the right to assign work. Limits on the number of positions in a branch that could be filled through upward mobility were inconsistent with the authority of the agency to assign and hire employees. INS and Nat'l INS Council, AFGE, 18 FLRA 875, 876-84 (1985). A change in the promotional opportunities of unit employees has more than a de minimis impact upon employment conditions. Air Force Flight Test Ctr., Edwards AFB and AFGE Local 3854, 55 FLRA 116, 122 n.12, 124 (1999) (unilateral change through discontinuation of career ladder promotions).
