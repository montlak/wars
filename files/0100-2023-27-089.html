2. FOIA and Privacy Act Requests

The handling of FOIA and Privacy Act requests by agencies engenders numerous EEO complaints, often by the same individual. In general, the remedies for violation of FOIA and the Privacy Act are provided in those acts. An allegation that the agency failed to respond to a Freedom of Information Act request fails to state a claim. Chaklos v. Secretary of Army, 01964638 (1997).

However, in Nardella v. Secretary of Navy, 01960953 (1997), the Commission noted that although denial of a request under FOIA or the Privacy Act is not within the EEO purview, allegations of an agency's delay of a request under FOIA and the Privacy Act comes within the Commission's jurisdiction. The Commission held in Nardella that the agency erred by summarily rejecting the part of the appellant's complaint regarding delay in receiving requested documents under FOIA and Privacy Act.
