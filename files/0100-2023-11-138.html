3. Resources for Neutrals

Setting up an ADR program or having access to one means that agencies will need to find persons to serve as neutrals. Agencies may avail themselves to a variety of sources. Many larger agencies have mediation services that other agencies may use. The ADRA, at 5 USC 583, provides:

For the purposes of this subchapter, an agency may use (with or without reimbursement) the services and facilities of other Federal agencies, State, local, and tribal governments, public and private organizations and agencies, and individuals, with the consent of such agencies, organizations, and individuals. An agency may accept voluntary and uncompensated services for purposes of this subchapter without regard to the provisions of section 1342 of title 31.

Mediators may be requested from the Interagency Shared Neutrals Program, which is administered by the Department of Health and Human Services. The mediators in that program have completed 20 hours of mediation skills training, and have co-mediated cases with a trained mediator.

The Federal Mediation and Conciliation Service (FMCS) has trained mediators to assist federal agencies with EEO matters. The FMCS also can help agencies design systems for dispute resolution. The FMCS describes its system design services as follows:

Such work normally entails a needs assessment and diagnosis, and usually a site visit. Systems design also includes a "procedures audit" of the manner in which issues and problems are currently resolved, those options available for improvement, and a detailed plan for implementation, maintenance and evaluation of a new system.

The FMCS will also provide education and training to agencies on a variety of ADR issues. The FMCS' training programs include:

General ADR Overview

Basic and Advanced Mediation Training

Mediation Mentoring

Facilitation skills

System Design

Basic and Advanced Conflict Management.

The programs typically last from one to five days, and follow-up programs and resources are available. The mediation training program provides on-the-job mentoring by a FMCS mediator. The FMCS mediator will co-mediate some of the agency's cases with the trainee. The FMCS can be reached at (202) 606-5445.

Many Federal Executive Boards (FEB's) administer shared neutrals programs. FEB's exist in about 30 cities nationwide, and are outreach groups for the national headquarters of the Federal Executive Branch. FEB's work toward finding solutions to common management problems in federal agencies in the geographical areas that they serve.

The programs of the various FEB's have their own rules. There are, however, some commonalities among them. Most require their mediators, who are typically agency employees, to participate in a training course. In addition, most FEB's require agencies to agree to participate in the program, i.e., become "member agencies," before they can receive mediation services. The FEB provides mediators from other agencies. Many of the FEB's have websites with more specific information about their shared neutrals programs.
