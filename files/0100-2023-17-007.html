II. PROCEDURES FOR APPEAL--COMPLAINANTS

The procedures for both complainants and agencies to appeal a final agency action are set forth in one regulation at 29 CFR 1614.403, even though the procedures are different for each party. There is nothing wrong with the Commission's decision to set forth both procedures in one regulation. To the extent that there is an overlap between the two procedures, combining the procedures into one regulation prevents duplication. However, it also can lead to confusion. As a result, for the sake of clarity, the appellate procedures for each party are addressed separately.

Title 29 CFR 1614.403 provides:

(a) The complainant, agency, agent, grievant or individual class claimant (hereinafter appellant) must file an appeal with the Director, Office of Federal Operations, Equal Employment Opportunity Commission, at P.O. Box 19848, Washington, DC 20036, or by personal delivery or facsimile. The appellant should use EEOC Form 573, Notice of Appeal/Petition, and should indicate what is being appealed.

(b) The appellant shall furnish a copy of the appeal to the opposing party at the same time it is filed with the Commission. In or attached to the appeal to the Commission, the appellant must certify the date and method by which service was made on the opposing party.

(c) If an appellant does not file an appeal within the time limits of this subpart, the appeal shall be dismissed by the Commission as untimely.

(d) Any statement or brief on behalf of a complainant in support of the appeal must be submitted to the Office of Federal Operations within 30 days of filing the notice of appeal. Any statement or brief on behalf of the agency in support of its appeal must be submitted to the Office of Federal Operations within 20 days of filing the notice of appeal. The Office of Federal Operations will accept statements or briefs in support of an appeal by facsimile transmittal, provided they are no more than 10 pages long.

(e) The agency must submit the complaint file to the Office of Federal Operations within 30 days of initial notification that the complainant has filed an appeal or within 30 days of submission of an appeal by the agency.

(f) Any statement or brief in opposition to an appeal must be submitted to the Commission and served on the opposing party within 30 days of receipt of the statement or brief supporting the appeal, or, if no statement or brief supporting the appeal is filed, within 60 days of receipt of the appeal. The Office of Federal Operations will accept statements or briefs in opposition to an appeal by facsimile provided they are no more than 10 pages long.

Appeals should be sent to:

Equal Employment Opportunity Commission

Office of Federal Operations

P.O. Box 77960

Washington, DC 20013

It is not required, but appeals should be sent by certified mail so that the complainant has documentary proof of mailing in the event timeliness questions arise. Appeals may also be faxed to the Commission, or hand-delivered to the Office of Federal Operations.

The appeal procedure is a two-step process. First, the complainant must file a notice of appeal. In the notice, the complainant is not required to present arguments on his or her behalf, or even set forth in a general fashion the basis of the appeal. The Commission designed a form for filing notices of appeal and agencies are required to provide the form with any final agency action. 29 CFR 1614.110. In the second step of the process, the complainant has 30 days from filing the notice of appeal to submit a brief containing arguments in support of the appeal.

After the notice of appeal has been filed and prior to the time the complainant's brief is due, the complainant will receive an acknowledgment notice from the Commission. This notice informs the complainant of the EEOC docket number and also asks the complainant to respond to several routine questions so that the Commission may ensure that the decision being appealed is ripe for adjudication. For example, the complainant will be asked to state the date on which he or she received the final agency decision and the date upon which the notice of appeal was filed. If the notice of appeal was filed more than 30 days after receipt of the decision, the complainant will be asked to explain the reason for the delay. The docketing statement also asks if any grievance was filed over the matter, or whether any appeal was filed with the MSPB. The docketing statement should be promptly completed and returned to the Office of Federal Operations.
