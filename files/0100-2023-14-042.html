15. Transfers; Reassignments

Where a settlement agreement obligates the agency to transfer or reassign an employee, in the absence of a provision specifically setting forth the intended time period for the transfer or reassignment, the Commission will interpret it to be for a "reasonable amount of time." The settlement agreement in Joshua E. v. Secretary of Air Force, 0120161062 (2016), stated that the agency would "laterally transfer" the complainant. The agreement specified an effective date but no termination date for the transfer. The complainant alleged bad faith on the part of the agency when as part of a reduction in force his position was identified as excess and eliminated. The Commission, in Joshua B., disagreed, finding the agency in compliance with the terms of the settlement agreement.

The Commission has previously found that bad faith in negotiating and implementing a settlement agreement constitute a breach. Todd v. Soc. Security Admin., EEOC Request No. 05950169 (June 12, 1997); Dupuich v. Dep't of the Army, EEOC Appeal No. 0120073901 (Nov. 2, 2007). We have also found that "where an individual bargains for a position without any specific terms as to length of service, it would be improper to interpret the reasonable intentions of the parties to include employment in that exact position ad infinitum." See Holley v. Dep't of Veterans Affairs, EEOC Request No. 05950342 (Nov. 13, 1997); see also Buck v. Dep't of Veterans Affairs, EEOC Appeal No. 01A12339 (July 6, 2001) (finding no breach where Complainant was reassigned in accordance with a settlement agreement, and held the new position for more than a year before the position was reclassified and ultimately downgraded). Further, the Commission has held that in the absence of a specific time frame in a settlement agreement, it is interpreted to be for a reasonable amount of time. Parker v. Dep't of Defense, EEOC Request No. 05910576 (Aug. 29, 1991).

The record contains no evidence indicating that the agency was aware that this circumstance would occur when the parties entered into the Agreement; therefore, we do not find that it acted in bad faith. See Carter v. United States Postal Serv., EEOC Appeal No. 01A60569 (May 25, 2006) (finding, three years after placing Complainant in a modified clerk position as part of a Settlement Agreement, the Agency was not in breach when it offered Complainant a different modified assignment because the district offices decided to eliminate clerk positions).
