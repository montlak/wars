A. EMPLOYEES AND AGENCIES COVERED

The Civil Service Reform Act created a statutory right of collective bargaining in the federal sector. A full accounting of the changes in labor-management relations and collective bargaining brought about by the Reform Act is well beyond the scope of this book. For those interested in a detailed discussion of those rights, see Peter Broida, A Guide to Federal Labor Relations Authority Law and Practice (Dewey Publications, Inc. 2023). The statutory guidance is set out in chapter 71 of Title 5 of the U.S. Code. Under the Act, employees have the right to engage in collective bargaining activities. The Act applies to executive agencies, the Library of Congress, and the Government Printing Office. Excluded from the provisions of the Act are the General Accounting Office, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, the Central Intelligence Agency, the National Security Agency, the Tennessee Valley Authority, the Federal Labor Relations Authority, and the Federal Services Impasse Panel. 5 USC 7103. Agencies also include nonappropriated fund instrumentalities such as the Department of Veterans Affairs.

Employees covered are those within a collective bargaining unit subject to the Act. A collective bargaining unit is not defined by whether an employee is a member of the union. The union is obligated to represent all employees encompassed by the collective bargaining agreement, regardless of union membership. The collective bargaining agreement must include provisions for resolutions of grievances, but there is no requirement that the grievance procedure provide for processing allegations of discrimination. That is a matter of negotiation between the parties. However, the Statute is worded in such a way that allegations of discrimination are included within the scope of the negotiated grievance procedure unless they are specifically excluded by the collective bargaining agreement. See 5 USC 7121(a)(2).

Section 7121(d) on the election of remedies only applies to collective bargaining agreements negotiated after the effective date of the Civil Service Reform Act. Dudley v. Department of Defense, 01853158 (1986).
