C. ADOPTING FINDINGS OF INVESTIGATOR

Although the report of investigation should summarize the factual evidence, it should not contain conclusions. See Goodwine v. Secretary of Veterans Affairs, 01991479 (2000). However, it is not uncommon that investigation reports do contain the conclusions of the investigator.

Under the former Part 1614, it was appropriate for an agency to adopt the findings of an EEOC administrative judge as its final decision. 29 CFR 1614.109(i) (1992). "However, no such provision exists to allow an agency to adopt the findings and conclusions of an EEO investigator." Dorsey v. Secretary of Air Force, 01941767 (1994). Adopting the findings of the investigator did not satisfy the regulatory requirement that the agency must issue findings of fact and conclusions of law. See Reilly v. Secretary of Health and Human Services, SSA, 01950622 (1996). Presumably, under the 1999 amendments, an agency may not adopt the conclusions of the investigator as its final decision but must issue its own decision in the case. In the Dorsey case, the Commission further found it was error for the agency not to articulate a "complete and accurate statement of the law applicable to the case [and] that this failure of the agency to provide appellant with an adequate statement of the law, and an explanation as to how this complaint fails to meet the legal requirements, effectively deprives appellant of a meaningful right of appeal." See also Merrill v. Secretary of Defense, 01952903 (1997).

In Swanson v. Secretary of Army, 01952162 (1996), the Commission held that it would not, as a matter of course, remand a case for a new final agency decision where the agency improperly adopted the findings of the report of investigation. The Commission found the record in Swanson, along with the findings of fact and conclusions of law in the investigation report, were sufficient to provide the complainant with a "meaningful right of appeal." It found no remand was necessary, though it did warn the agency about the impropriety of issuing similar final decisions in the future.
