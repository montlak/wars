3. Hearing Notice

There is no explicit requirement that a complainant receive official written notice of the hearing date. Under its previous regulations at Part 1613, the Commission had held that the complainant is only required to receive actual notification of the time and place of the hearing. See Mar v. USPS, 01832110 (1984). However, in a later case decided under Part 1613, the Commission held that when a hearing is rescheduled, the administrative judge should notify both parties of the new hearing date, in writing, even if they were present at the time of rescheduling. See Sadlach v. USPS, 01861208 (1987). Written notification, the Commission observed, ensures that there is no misunderstanding with respect to the new hearing date.

In the Judges' Handbook, however, the right to written notice of the hearing at least 30 days in advance is implicit through the imposition of the requirement that the administrative judge issue a scheduling order. The Judges' Handbook at 7-2 provides:

At least thirty days prior to the hearing, the Administrative Judge shall issue a Scheduling Order which advises the parties regarding: the date, time, and location of the hearing; and any necessary instructions to the agency for securing the hearing facility, court reporter, and special accommodations.

In addition, the Scheduling Order may also include information regarding the requirements for a hearing room; the sequestering of witnesses; and the agency's responsibilities with respect to producing approved witnesses who are current federal employees, even if employed by another agency.

Receipt by complainant's attorney of a written notice from the administrative judge of a change in the hearing date constitutes constructive notice to the complainant. Relying upon Irwin v. Veterans Administration, 874 F.2d 1092 (5th Cir. 1989), aff'd 111 S. Ct. 453 (1990), the Commission found that the administrative judge was not required to also notify the complainant personally of the change in hearing date. Perry v. Postmaster General, 01920444 (1992).
