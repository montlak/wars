10. Final Agency Decisions and Actions

The agency is required to take "final action" on any complaint where a hearing has been requested, or to issue a "final decision" in any case where a hearing has not been requested. As noted above, the difference in the terminology used to describe the agency's final disposition of a complaint, depending on whether or not there has been a hearing, is by no means accidental.

For the first time, in its 2015 revisions to MD-110, the Commission has given formal definitions to a few terms that have previously been loosely defined--Final Agency Action, Final Agency Decision, Final Agency Order and Final Agency Determination. Final Agency Action refers to any action on the part of the agency that represents a final disposition of the complaint with the remaining terms defining the type of action taken by the agency. MD-110 at 1-3 provides:

"Final Agency Action" refers to an agency's last and final action on a complaint of employment discrimination. The final agency action may be in the form of a final agency decision, a final agency order implementing an Administrative Judge's decision, or a final determination on a breach of settlement agreement claim.

"Final Agency Decision" refers to a decision on a complaint of discrimination made by the agency, without an Administrative Judge, that is appealable to the Commission. It includes agency decisions to dismiss or agency decisions on the merits.

"Final Agency Order" refers to a decision by an agency to implement or not implement an Administrative Judge's decision, which is appealable to the Commission. Where the agency's final order does not fully implement the Administrative Judge's decision, the agency must simultaneously appeal to the Commission.

"Final Agency Determination" refers to an agency determination as to whether there was a breach of a settlement agreement that is appealable to the Commission.

If a hearing has taken place, within 40 days of receipt of the administrative judge's decision, the agency is required to issue a final agency order on the complaint. The final order either must fully implement the administrative judge's decision or, if any portion of the decision is not implemented, must be accompanied by an appeal to the EEOC. Title 29 CFR 1614.110(a) provides:

When an administrative judge has issued a decision under 1614.109(b), (g) or (i), the agency shall take final action on the complaint by issuing a final order within 40 days of receipt of the hearing file and the administrative judge's decision. The final order shall notify the complainant whether or not the agency will fully implement the decision of the administrative judge and shall contain notice of the complainant's right to appeal to the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission, the right to file a civil action in federal district court, the name of the proper defendant in any such lawsuit and the applicable time limits for appeals and lawsuits. If the final order does not fully implement the decision of the administrative judge, then the agency shall simultaneously file an appeal in accordance with 1614.403 and append a copy of the appeal to the final order. A copy of EEOC Form 573 shall be attached to the final order.

The 1992 version of Part 1614 required the agency to issue a final decision within 60 days of receipt of the administrative judge's recommended decision. If no hearing was requested, the agency had 60 days upon receipt of the complainant's request for a decision to issue a final agency decision. The final decision consisted of findings by the agency on each issue of discrimination raised by the complaint. See 29 CFR 1614.110 (1992). The burden was then on the complainant to appeal to the EEOC or to bring a civil action in the appropriate U.S. district court. See id.

The 1999 amendments to Part 1614 do not require the agency to provide any rationale in its final order declining to fully implement the decision of the administrative judge. That rationale, instead of being provided to the complainant in the first instance by way of a final agency order, is now provided directly to OFO by way of a brief in support of the agency's appeal.

If the complainant does not request a hearing or if the complainant requests a final agency decision on the record in lieu of a hearing, the agency is required to issue a final agency decision on the complaint. The regulations at 29 CFR 1614.110(b) describe what must be included in the agency's final decision:

The final decision shall consist of findings by the agency on the merits of each issue in the complaint, or, as appropriate, the rationale for dismissing any claims in the complaint and, when discrimination is found, appropriate remedies and relief in accordance with subpart E of this part. The agency shall issue the final decision within 60 days of receiving notification that a complainant has requested an immediate decision from the agency, or within 60 days of the end of the 30-day period for the complainant to request a hearing or an immediate final decision where the complainant has not requested either a hearing or a decision. The final action shall contain notice of the right to appeal the final action to the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission, the right to file a civil action in federal district court, the name of the proper defendant in any such lawsuit and the applicable time limits for appeals and lawsuits. A copy of EEOC Form 573 shall be attached to the final action.

The final decisions requirements of 110(b) also apply to agency decisions dismissing complaints in their entirety.
