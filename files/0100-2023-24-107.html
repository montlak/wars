N. JOB ANALYSIS

To give adequate consideration to how an employee with a disability may be accommodated, the agency may be required to perform a "job analysis." To assist agencies in accommodating employees with disabilities, OPM published the "Handbook of Job Analysis for Reasonable Accommodation," Personnel Management Series 720-B (July 1984). Although the Handbook is now more than 25 years old, it still accurately represents the process and the steps an agency may have to take to perform the kind of individualized assessment that is required for reasonable accommodation. The Handbook at p. 2 explains the role of job analysis in evaluating accommodations and how to perform a job analysis:

Accommodations are highly individualized; what may have been successful for one disabled person may not be appropriate or necessary for another. This holds true for individuals with the same or similar disabilities. In each case the need for an accommodation and the functional problem precipitating this need must be clearly identified and understood before accommodations can be made. This involves analysis of the known abilities and limitations of a disabled individual with respect to the functional requirements of a particular job and the nature of the work environment in which the job is performed. This is accomplished through job analysis.

"Job analysis" is the process by which information relative to a specific job, position, or occupation is collected analyzed, and interpreted. With respect to reasonable accommodation, it is the process through which accurate and complete information about the functional job requirements of a particular job and the nature of the work environment can be identified. A comparison of information collected through a job analysis process to information on the functional abilities and limitations of a disabled individual provides the basis from which potential problems can be identified and understood, and appropriate accommodations planned.

Job analysis is a systematic means of determining the agency's accommodation obligations. The agency must determine the full extent of the functional job requirements of a particular position, compare those functional requirements to the limitations of a particular employee and make a determination as to whether and how the agency can adjust those requirements to meet the abilities of the employee within his or her limitations.

In the Handbook at pp. 5-6, OPM designed a four-step procedure for planning an accommodation:

*Step One--Conduct the Job Analysis

A job analysis is conducted to gain a clear understanding of job requirements and the work environment. A form for documenting collected data...can simplify this step and facilitate further analysis.

*Step Two--Determine Functional Characteristics

The functional characteristics of the job applicant or employee must be identified to learn the type of handicap and to fully understand the nature of the handicap and the specific abilities and limitations of the individual.

*Step Three--Identify Problems Requiring Resolution

A careful comparison of job analysis data and the characteristics of the disabled employee or applicant is used to identify and understand incompatibilities which exist between the known physical and/or mental limitations of the applicant or employee and the job requirements and/or work environment which impede or are likely to impede satisfactory performance by the individual.

*Step Four--Identify and Evaluate Remedial Alternatives

Develop a list of potential remedies to determine the most reasonable methods to resolve identified problems. There are usually a number of possible solutions to a problem and the proper choice must be made on an individual basis. When considering possible alternatives, involvement of those who will be affected, especially the disabled individual, is recommended. Involvement of the disabled individual is essential to make a decision regarding the final selection of an accommodation. Accommodations prescribed without involvement of the disabled individual are rarely as effective or appropriate as those which are planned with such input.

This step-by-step process represents a common sense approach to reasonable accommodation. The Handbook also contains sample job analysis forms and three examples of how to apply job analysis to particular disabling conditions.
