E. DAMAGES UNDER EPA

The EPA has no provision for compensatory damages. However, the Act does provide for liquidated damages up to the amount of back pay recovered. See 29 USC 206(d)(1). The Act also provides that the period of relief may be extended from two to three years if the violation is willful. In Telford v. Secretary of Army, 05A00233 (2002), the Commission held:

In our prior decision, we found that the AJ was correct in finding that the agency's violation of the EPA was willful. As such, we agree with complainant's contention and find that as the EPA violation was willful and liquidated damages were appropriate, complainant is entitled to back pay from three (3) years prior to the date prior to the date on which the complaint in the instant matter was filed. See generally, Brinkley-Obu v. Hughes Training, Inc., 36 F.3d 336, 357-78 (4th Cir. 1994). In addition, as liquidated damages are automatic and do not depend on the agency's violation being willful, we amend our prior order to award complainant liquidated damages equal to the amount of back pay. Thus, the Commission will amend our prior Order to provide that the agency shall award back pay from September 23, 1991 and terminating on the date of complainant's retirement from the agency. In addition, the agency shall award complainant liquidated damages totaling an amount equal to back pay and benefits, with interest.

Relief for sex-based wage discrimination under the EPA can be pursued simultaneous with a complaint of sex discrimination under Title VII. Because of the overlap between the two statutes, if an employee is otherwise covered by the statutes, a complainant who establishes an EPA violation has also established a violation of Title VII. Miller v. Secretary of Navy, 01943457 (1995).

When a complainant does establish a violation of both the EPA and Title VII, he or she is entitled to recover under both statutes. However, duplicative recovery is not allowed. As a result, in appropriate circumstances, a successful complainant can recover back pay and liquidated damages under the EPA and compensatory damages under Title VII. See Smith v. Secretary of Veterans Affairs, 0420120003 (2013); Jones v. Secretary of Veterans Affairs, 01994717 (2000), aff'd Jones v. Secretary of Veterans Affairs, 05A10136 (2001). See also Hanley v. Federal Labor Relations Authority, 0720060033 (2008) (rejecting argument that award of liquidated damages amount to recovery of back pay under EPA and Title VII).
