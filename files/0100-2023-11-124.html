X. INCIDENTS OCCURRING AFTER PRECOMPLAINT COUNSELING

The 1999 amendments to Part 1614 set forth a new process for dealing with allegations of discrimination that occur after the precomplaint processing is completed. Under 29 CFR 1614.106(d), if the new allegations are like or related to claims raised in counseling, the pending complaint can be amended and no additional counseling is required. A new allegation is like or related to the initial complaint if it adds to or clarifies it, or could reasonably be expected to grow out of the initial complaint. See Scher v. Postmaster General, 05940702 (1995). If the new issues are not like or related to the matters that were raised in counseling, the complaints should be consolidated for processing under 29 CFR 1614.606. There is little practical difference between amending a complaint to include additional claims and consolidating complaints. The main difference is that consolidation of complaints under 1614.606 can occur only after precomplaint counseling and the filing of a formal complaint on the new issues. Amendment of complaints is discussed further in Chapter 3, subheading "Amendment and Consolidation of Complaints."

Prior to the 1999 amendments to Part 1614, an agency properly dismissed portions of a complaint that contained allegations of incidents that occurred subsequent to precomplaint counseling, but prior to the filing of a formal complaint. Austin v. Department of Health and Human Services, 01831786 (1984) (the agency properly advised the employee to seek precomplaint counseling on the issues). However, if the allegations of discrimination were "like or related to" the oriGINAl complaint, the proper procedure was for the agency to refer the complainant back for counseling on those issues.

Even prior to the enactment of the 1999 amendments, the Commission had begun to enunciate in its case law a position that favored amendment. In Sherman v. Postmaster General, 01965347 (1997), the Commission found that disciplinary actions that were foreseen by the appellant when she filed her complaint, but did not take place until later, were properly within the scope of her complaint.

Here, the agency has limited the scope of appellant's complaint to the events as they stood at the time the complaint was filed, without taking into account, at the time it dismissed her complaint, the consequences that arose from the incident at issue. The complaint was filed on May 14, 1996, in response to the events which occurred on April 17, 1996. On June 4, 1996, appellant received a letter detailing her seven-day suspension stemming from the April 17 incident. At the time of the filing of the complaint, appellant had not yet been disciplined for the April 17 incident. However, as indicated in her request for remedy, both in the formal complaint and in the EEO counselor's report, appellant was aware of the possibility of a suspension and an AWOL charge as a result of her actions. The resulting suspension and AWOL charge are a natural outgrowth of appellant's complaint, indicating that she did suffer direct and personal deprivation at the hands of the employer. Appellant is, therefore, an aggrieved employee. Because appellant has alleged that the adverse action was a result of reprisal for prior EEO activity, she has raised an allegation within the purview of the EEOC regulations.

Instead of remanding the complaint in Sherman for counseling, the Commission ordered the agency to include the subsequent events in its investigation of the oriGINAl complaint. This is the better approach, since it eliminates unnecessary counseling as well as a second complaint. See also Porter v. Secretary of Defense, 02965323 (1997) (an allegation of constructive discharge is like or related to an allegation of sexual harassment; the allegation was remanded for investigation); Lee-Buhl v. Secretary of Defense, 01966748 (1997) (alleged incidents of sexual harassment that occurred after precomplaint counseling are like or related to incident of harassment that was brought to the attention of a counselor; the allegations were remanded for investigation).
