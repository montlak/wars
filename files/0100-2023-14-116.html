3. Reinstating the Complaint and Tender Back Provisions

In Solano v. Secretary of Homeland Security, 01A53729 (2005), the Commission held that when a settlement agreement does not comply with the OWBPA, reinstatement of the entire complaint is appropriate and not just reinstatement of those claims based on age.

In Sandle v. Administrator of Veterans Affairs, 01995141 (2000), the Commission held that the complainant was entitled to reinstatement of her complaint because the agency had not complied with the OWBPA and she was not required to return any benefits obtained under the settlement. If the complainant prevailed, the Commission noted, she might be required to offset those benefits against her recovery. See also Rodriguez v. Postmaster General, 01A11256 (2001) (settlement agreement because it did not comply with OWBPA and complainant could pursue his age discrimination complaint without returning any benefits obtained under the settlement agreement, but those benefits may be offset against any recovery); Farley v. Secretary of Navy, 01A06004 (2001). The Commission reached a similar result in Hill v. Postmaster General, 01A60626 (2006).

Even when the settlement agreement itself contains appropriate OWBPA language an agency's failure to comply with the agreement can result in reinstatement of the age discrimination claim without a tender-back of settlement benefits. In Jennings v. Secretary of Veterans Affairs, 0120120607 (2012), the agreement contained the seven-day revocation language but the agency contended that it did not receive the complainant's revocation within the seven-day period. The Commission found that the revocation was timely and held in Jennings:

While the language of the settlement agreement in this case correctly contained the appropriate rights required by OWBPA, the Agency failed to implement one of those protections the right to revoke the agreement within seven days of its execution. Such conduct violates the spirit of the OWBPA protections. Consequently, we find that Complainant's retention of any consideration received under the settlement agreement is not an impediment to the reinstatement of her ADEA claim against the Agency. However, Complainant is advised that if she prevails on her EEO complaint, any monetary award may be subject to an off-set by the consideration that she received from the Agency under the settlement agreement. See Oubre v. Entergy Operations, Inc., 522 U.S. 422 (1998).

See also Francesca V. v. Secretary of Veterans Affairs, 0120170632 (2017), discussed supra under "Older Workers' Benefit Protection Act."

In Johnson v. Secretary of Transportation, 0120055936 (2007), the Commission expanded the tender-back doctrine to a settlement involving a discrimination complaint where failure to comply with the OWBPA was not the source of breach. In Johnson, the complainant settled an age discrimination complaint that provided for the agency to rescind a removal in place of a voluntary resignation and also required that the agency maintain the confidentiality of the terms of the agreement. The complainant alleged breach when the agency disclosed the rescinded removal to a prospective employer. The Commission found that the complainant was not required to tender back compensation received under the agreement in order to have her age discrimination claim reinstated. It did, however, note that any recovery may be offset by the benefits she had already received.
