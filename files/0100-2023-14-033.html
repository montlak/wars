6. Performance Appraisals

In a departure from its pronouncements on relief in performance appraisal cases, the Commission has held that a settlement agreement that requires an upgrade of a performance appraisal does not require a corresponding adjustment to the comments that accompany the appraisal unless specifically mandated by the agreement. In Bragdon v. Secretary of Health and Human Services, 01A04210 (2002), the complainant entered into a settlement agreement that required the agency to change her performance evaluation to outstanding. The Commission found the agency was not in breach of the agreement when it changed the overall rating but omitted all comments from the rating form. According to the Commission, if the complainant wanted comments consistent with the rating, it should have been negotiated as part of the agreement. In contrast, when the Commission orders a performance evaluation changed as the result of a finding of discrimination, it requires that the comments be changed to reflect the proper evaluation. See Chapter 21.

However, in Legg v. Postmaster General, 0120065122 (2008), the Commission found that the agency was in breach of a settlement agreement where the agreement called for the agency to remove two offending sentences for a performance appraisal narrative. The agency removed the two sentences, but replaced them with two different offending sentences suggesting that the complainant was most but not all office goals and had room for improvement. The Commission found that the agency violated "the spirit, if not the letter" of the agreement and "that it is highly unlikely that [the complainant] would have agreed to replace the negative sentences for other negative sentences." Id.
