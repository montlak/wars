8. Nonappropriated Fund Agencies

The Back Pay Act also does not apply to nonappropriated fund agencies such as the Army and Air Force Exchange Service, the Army and Air Force Motion Picture Service, the Navy Ship's Stores Ashore, Navy, Marine Corps and Coast Guard exchanges and certain other instrumentalities of the United States under the jurisdiction of the armed forces. See 5 USC 2105(c). As a general matter, employees of those agencies are not deemed employees for the purposes of most of the laws administered by OPM, including the Back Pay Act.

There is however, an exception to section 2105(c), that applies in cases of prohibited discrimination. In Carmon-Coleman v. Secretary of Defense, 04A30030 (2004), a case involving the Army and Air Force Exchange Service, the Commission found that in cases of unlawful discrimination the provisions of the Back Pay Act are applicable to such agencies and that Congress has waived sovereign immunity for purposes of providing interest on back pay awards. As the Commission explained in Carmon-Coleman:

Regarding claim A, the agency relies on the Supreme Court's 1982 decision in Army and Air Force Exchange Service v. Sheehan, 456 U.S. 728 (1982), which, in pertinent part, indicated that while the Back Pay Act permits federal employees to recover lost wages due to unwarranted personnel actions, Congress clearly denied this cause of action to the agency's employees, who are excluded because they are paid with non-appropriated funds, as set forth in 5 U.S.C. § 2105(c). Further, the agency claims that based on the foregoing, it may not be considered to have waived sovereign immunity, such that it is exempt from paying interest on a back pay award under the Back Pay Act. We disagree.

The Commission notes that 5 U.S.C. § 2105(c)(l)(A) provides, in pertinent part, that agency employees are not deemed employees for the purpose of the laws administered by the Office of Personnel Management (OPM) (such as the Back Pay Act), except for Section 7204 (emphasis omitted). The Commission notes that 5 U.S.C. § 7204(b) makes an exception for unlawful employment discrimination against an individual or a position held by an individual. In other words, agency employees who are victims of unlawful employment discrimination are deemed to be employees for the purpose of the laws administered by OPM, including the Back Pay Act. Unlike the Sheehan case, this matter concerns an unlawful employment discrimination claim. We therefore determine that petitioner may be deemed an employee of the agency under the 5 U.S.C. § 7204(b) exception, and that provisions of the Back Pay Act are applicable.

Furthermore, in 1987, after the Supreme Court's holding in Sheehan, Congress amended the Back Pay Act to allow for interest on back pay awards when federal employees are found to "have been affected by an unjustified or unwarranted personnel action which has resulted in the withdrawal or reduction" of compensation. 5 U.S.C. 5596(b)(l). Thus, the Back Pay Act amendment provides a limited waiver of sovereign immunity from payment of interest on back pay under Title VII. See Sullivan v. Department of Justice, EEOC Request No. 05901185 (March 2, 1992) (agency liable for interest on a back pay award in a constructive discharge case). Moreover, in the Civil Rights Act of 1991, Congress expressly waived the federal government's sovereign immunity regarding payment of interest for delay on all monetary awards to victims of employment discrimination in the federal government to the same extent that any non-federal entity would be subject to. Additionally, the Civil Rights Act of 1991 waived the government's sovereign immunity with respect to interest on back pay awards in Rehabilitation Act employment discrimination cases. See Mason v. Department of Defense, EEOC Appeal No. 01944837 (March 14, 1995).

Based on the foregoing, we find no authority for the agency's proposition that it does not fall within the purview of the Back Pay Act or the Civil Rights Act of 1991 because of the holding in Sheehan. We find that the agency must process petitioner's back pay award under the Back Pay Act, to include the payment of interest on that award.
