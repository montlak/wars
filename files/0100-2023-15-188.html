J. RECORD OF HEARING

The hearing is recorded and a verbatim transcript is prepared. In addition, all exhibits submitted as evidence must be made a part of the hearing record. 29 CFR 1614.209(h). Arrangement for transcription of hearings, including payment, is the responsibility of the involved agency. Agencies are instructed to have bills for transcriptions services submitted directly to the agency, but to have the transcripts sent directly to the administrative judge.

Under the former regulations, the administrative judge was responsible for transmitting the transcripts to the agency with a recommended decision, 29 CFR 1613.218(g), and the agency was responsible for transmitting a copy of the transcript to the complainant with its final decision, 29 CFR 1613.221(b)(2). The complainant did not know the contents, or results, until the issuance of a final agency decision.

Under the current regulations, the administrative judge is required to serve a copy of the transcript, the hearing record, and the decision on both parties. 29 CFR 1614.109(i).

Remand for a new hearing is proper when the transcripts to the original hearing are lost, since it is impossible for the Commission to thoroughly evaluate the case on less than the whole record. Williams v. Department of Health and Human Services, 01871807 (1987).

It is important that the presentation of evidence, arguments, objections, and all other discussion relative to procedure or substance take place on the record. This does not mean that representatives or complainant should not permit off-the-record discussions to take place. Often, preliminary discussions regarding such matters are done off the record. This permits the parties to express their thoughts to the judge and gives the judge an opportunity to explore resolutions of conflicts or determine how to rule. The ensuing on-record discussion is generally more organized and, as a result, clearer on the transcript.

However, it is important that the points raised in the off-the-record discussion are raised when the hearing resumes on-record. Unrepresented complainants do not always understand that off-the-record discussions do not appear on the transcript. In the absence of a record of such discussions, the Commission will not entertain allegations of error in off-the-record discussions. See Abraham v. Postmaster General, 01892543 (1989) (refusing to entertain allegation of ex parte communications with agency witness and counsel where record only showed that administrative judge went off-the-record several times without explanation and complainant offered no objection).

Once a decision is rendered the EEOC is no longer the custodian of the hearing record. The Judges' Handbook at 7-11 provides:

The Administrative Judge is responsible for returning the complete hearing record to the agency's designated point of contact at the conclusion of the hearing process. This enables the EEO office to issue a final order based on a review of the complete record. The complete hearing record consists of:

• all submissions by the parties to the Administrative Judge, including motions, pre-hearing statements, and briefs;

• all decisions, orders and correspondence issued by the Administrative Judge;

• the transcript of the hearing, if a hearing was conducted; and

• all hearing exhibits, including those not admitted into the record.

The hearing record does not include the report of investigation and complaint file provided by the agency. In situations where the Administrative Judge determines it appropriate, he/she may return his/her copy of the report of investigation to the agency.

The District or Field Office must retain a copy of all documents issued to the parties for a minimum of two years from the date the Office closes the case, but does not need to retain the hearing transcript or hearing exhibits. The report of investigation and complaint file may be returned to the agency if the agency provides postage or makes other arrangements to retrieve them. If the agency does not retrieve the Administrative Judge's copy of the investigative report and complaint file within sixty days, they may be destroyed.
