1. Failure to Issue Final Agency Decision

The Commission has held that where the agency fails to issue a timely final agency decision, the complainant has the right to file an appeal with EEOC upon expiration of the 60-day period for issuing the final agency decision. However, in a recent case, Glynda S. v. Attorney General, 0120133361 (2016), the Commission imposed default judgment as a sanction against an agency that did not issue a decision for more than a year after the expiration of the 60-day period. The Glynda S. case is discussed fully below in the section on "Default Judgment."

The Commission took a decidedly different approach in Cary J. v. Secretary of Health and Human Services, 2020002874 (2021), where it denied the complainant's appeal, but ordered the agency to issue its final decision within 30 days. Rather than electing a hearing, Cary J. requested an agency final decision. Six months later, the agency had not issued a final decision and the complainant appealed to the Commission. Denying the complainant's appeal, the Commission in Cary J. wrote:

The Agency states that OFO should dismiss Complainant's "appeal" on the grounds it is premature. The Agency sets forth that it has not yet issued a final decision in the instant matter. The Agency further asserts that EEOC should deny Complainant's request for a default judgment. The Agency asserts that Complainant has not been harmed by the delay. The Agency further asserts it has been delayed in issuing a final decision due to an extensive backlog.

The record does not contain a final agency decision and there is no evidence that the Agency has since issued a final decision while this matter has been pending before OFO.

The Commission considers final decisions and does not independently consider interlocutory appeals. See Cecile T. v. Environmental Protection Agency, EEOC Appeal No. 0120161511 (Dec. 20, 2016). Thus, there is no matter over which we currently have jurisdiction.2

Accordingly, the appeal is DISMISSED because the Agency has not issued a final decision. However, we will order the Agency to issue its final decision on this matter promptly pursuant to the following Order. Order

To the extent it has not already done so, the Agency is ORDERED to issue a final decision within thirty (30) calendar days from the date this decision is issued, pursuant to 29 C.F.R. § 1614.110(b). The Agency shall provide a copy of its decision to Complainant and to the Compliance Officer as referenced below.

Prior to Glynda S., in cases where the agency failed to issue timely final decisions, the Commission, in "interest of judicial economy," has construed the agency response to the appeal as the final agency decision. For example in Complainant v. Office of Personnel Management, 0120122901 (2014), the Commission found:

As an initial matter, we first shall consider the Agency's contention that the instant appeal should be dismissed as premature. We acknowledge that the instant record does not contain a document entitled "final agency decision." However, in the interest of judicial economy, we find that the Agency's lengthy "Response to Appellant's Appeal of Consolidated EEO Cases" can reasonably be construed as the equivalent of a final decision. See Duran v. Dep't of Air Force, EEOC Appeal No. 0120131046 (May 24, 2013) (Commission construed Agency's email correspondence to Complainant's counsel as final decision for the purpose of judicial economy); see also, Johnson v. Dep't of Interior (National Park Service), EEOC Appeal No. 0120132118 (September 9, 2013) (where Agency failed to issue a decision on Complainant's breach allegation, the Commission treated the Agency's submission on appeal as its final decision).
