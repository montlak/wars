A. RECRUITMENT

Under 5 USC 7201(c), OPM is responsible for prescribing regulations for federal agencies in the area of equal employment opportunity recruitment. The guidance of the Office is published at 5 CFR Part 720. Section 7201(c) requires:

(1) that each Executive agency conduct a continuing program for the recruitment of members of minorities for positions in the agency to carry out the [anti-discrimination] policy set forth in subsection (b) in a manner designed to eliminate underrepresentation of minorities in the various categories of civil service employment within the Federal service, with special efforts directed at recruiting in minority communities, in educational institutions, and from other sources from which minorities can be recruited; and

(2) that the Office [of Personnel Management] conduct a continuing program of--

(A) assistance to agencies in carrying out programs under paragraph (1) of this subsection; and

(B) evaluation and oversight of such recruitment programs to determine their effectiveness in eliminating such minority underrepresentation.

The section places a mandatory obligation on federal agencies to eradicate the effects of past discrimination by efforts designed to increase the pool of qualified minority applicants for positions within the agency.

Under 5 USC 7201(d), the EEOC is required to:

(1) establish the guidelines proposed to be used in carrying out the program required under subsection (c) of this section; and

(2) make determinations of underrepresentation which are proposed to be used initially under such program; and

(3) transmit to Executive agencies involved, to the Office of Personnel Management, and to the Congress the determinations made under paragraph (2) of this subsection.

OPM is required to report to Congress each year, no later than January 31, a report on its activities and those involved of agencies under section (c). 5 USC 7201(e).

OPM defines recruitment of minority candidates, at 5 CFR 720.202(e), as

the total process by which the Federal Government and the Federal agencies locate, identify and assist in the employment of qualified applicants from underrepresented groups for job openings in categories of employment where underrepresentation has been determined. It includes both innovative internal and external recruitment actions. It is also intended to cover processes designed to prepare qualifiable applicants (those who have the potential but do not presently meet valid qualification requirements) for such job openings through programs of training, work experience or both.

Agencies are required to submit their recruitment plans to OPM, upon request, and OPM, in consultation with the EEOC, may require the agency to develop additional plans if deemed necessary. 5 CFR 720.205.
