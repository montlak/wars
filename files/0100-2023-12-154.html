25. Harassment Investigations

As discussed thoroughly in Chapter 12, when an agency receives a complaint that an employee is being harassed on an EEO basis, it is required to conduct a prompt and fair investigation of the allegation. During that investigation, the agency also is required to take interim actions designed to stop the harassment if it is occurring. Sometimes, this means that the alleged harasser and alleged victim need to be separated during the investigation.

In Complainant v. Postmaster General, 0120141725 (2014), the Commission, consistent with longstanding policy that the alleged victim should not suffer adverse consequences from reporting the alleged harassment, found that the complainant was not aggrieved when he was sent home for two days and placed on administrative leave while the agency investigated a complaint:

Here, Complainant alleged that on October 19, 2013, he was sent home by an Agency manager for two days after a craft employee accused Complainant of improperly sending her flowers. The record indicates that Complainant received paid administrative leave for the two days he was sent home and that he was not disciplined. We determine that the Agency properly found in these specific circumstances, that Complainant was not aggrieved by being sent home while the sexual harassment allegation against him was being investigated. Moreover, even if proven to be true and viewed in a light most favorable to Complainant, this matter would not indicate that he has been subjected to harassment that was sufficiently severe or pervasive to alter the conditions of his employment. See Cobb v. Department of the Treasury, EEOC Request No. 05970077 (March 13, 1997).
