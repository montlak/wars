LIFE INSURANCE

Through the Federal Employee's Group Life Insurance program, employees and retirees have an opportunity to participate in a group plan that offers low rates and the convenience of payment through payroll deductions. While you are an employee, the government pays one-third of the cost of your basic life insurance. The U.S. Postal Service assumes the full cost of Basic Life insurance for its covered employees.

Most employees are eligible to participate in Basic Life and one or more options offered by FEGLI. Generally, new employees are automatically enrolled in Basic coverage which is effective the first day an employee is in a pay and duty status (i.e., not on annual leave, sick leave, donated leave, excused absence, military furlough, or otherwise absent from duty). The optional coverage becomes effective the day the employee enters a working pay status on or after the day life insurance election is received in the employing office.

If you do not complete a Standard Form 2817 waiving coverage, you will automatically be enrolled for Basic Life, and withholdings for it will be made from your pay. No proof of insurability is required for the Basic insurance you get upon being hired, or any optional insurance you sign up for during the first 60 calendar days of eligibility.

Your decision to elect or waive life insurance coverage should be based on your evaluation of your personal and family needs, your existing insurance coverage, and your long- and short-term plans. If you elect FEGLI coverage, you may cancel it at any time. Any questions regarding your actual insurance coverage should be directed to your employing office.

To check on the status of a claim, call OFEGLI at (800) 633-4542. From overseas, call (212) 578-2975. Full FEGLI information is available from the OPM website at https://www.opm.gov/healthcare-insurance/life-insurance/.

You cannot get specific information on FEGLI coverage through OPM. If you are a current employee and have questions regarding FEGLI coverage, contact your human resources office or personnel office. Annuitants must contact the OPM Retirement Services at (888) 767-6738 or through other options available at https://www.opm.gov/support/retirement/contact. Your employing agency maintains all of your records (including designation of beneficiary forms.)

FEGLI builds up no cash or loan value or paid-up extended insurance. It is intended as immediate protection against financial hardship or loss in the event of death. Thus, it is not offered as a form of life insurance with cash values such as might be purchased through non-governmental insurance agents.

Employees may assign their FEGLI to another person or to a trust. To find out how this works, you need to contact your agency personnel office or retirement system.

If you are insured under FEGLI, the booklets FE 76-21 for federal employees, RI 76-12 for retirees, and FE 76-20 for postal employees, summarize the benefits to which you are entitled, to whom these benefits are payable, to whom the claims must be submitted, and all of the other provisions of OPM's group policy which concern you as an individual holder. The relevant booklet, along with your copy of SF 2817 (during all non-open season elections), is your certificate of insurance, and explains the coverage you have elected as shown on your election form. These booklets are available through https://www.opm.gov/healthcare-insurance/life-insurance/reference-materials/#url=Publications-and-Forms, as is the comprehensive FEGLI Handbook.

The FEGLI Handbook is not itself a contract of insurance. Your basic rights and benefits are governed by provisions of 5 USC Chapter 87, 5 CFR Part 870, and by the Group Policy purchased by OPM.

This Group Policy, which provides the insurance referred to in the FEGLI booklet, may be modified or terminated; this could be required by changes in the FEGLI law or for other reasons. Any such modification or termination, however, will not adversely affect any claim arising from death or injury occurring before the modification or termination.

Part-time employees not limited to a year or less, and who do not have intermittent appointments without prearranged, regularly scheduled tours of duty, are generally eligible to enroll in the FEGLI program; the amount of their insurance coverage is determined in the same way as that for full-time employees.

Electing coverage

FEGLI doesn't have an annual open season. Open seasons are held infrequently and announced well in advance by OPM. However, as an employee there are many FEGLI actions you can take outside an open season like:

  • • Enroll or increase coverage (except Option C) by providing satisfactory evidence of medical insurability, which basically means passing a physical exam.
  • • Cancel or reduce life insurance at any time.
  • • Change beneficiaries at any time.

Employees can also enroll in any or all coverage that FEGLI offers or increase coverage up to the maximum that FEGLI offers, including Option C, within 60 days after a FEGLI life event. Qualifying life events are marriage, divorce, death of a spouse, and acquisition of an eligible child. Generally, eligible children are your unmarried dependent children under age 22.

FEGLI regulations allow a belated open season election if, within six months after an open season ends, an agency determines that an employee was unable to make the election on a timely basis for reasons beyond their control. If an agency makes such a determination, the employee must make the election within 31 days after the agency's notification. A belated election is effective the same date as a timely election and is subject to the same pay and duty status requirements.

Special election opportunities. 5 USC 8702 extends special election opportunities to civilian employees deployed in support of contingency military operations. In addition, 5 USC 8706(d) allows 24 months continuation of coverage for federal employees called to active military duty, with 12 months of coverage paid in full by the government, and the following 12 months paid in full by the insured.

Basic life insurance

Basic insurance amount. Coverage is equal to the greater of a) the amount of your annual basic pay rounded to the next higher thousand plus $2,000; or b) $10,000. This means there is a minimum of $10,000 on the amount of Basic insurance. There is no maximum amount of Basic insurance.

Part-time employees. The annual basic pay for a part-time employee for life insurance purposes is the basic pay applicable to the tour of duty in a 52-week work year. For example, if the annual salary for the position is $21,906, and the employee works half-time, then the annual basic salary would be half of this amount, or $10,953.

You must be enrolled in Basic insurance in order to elect any of the available additional options.

Extra benefit. Basic insurance is an even better value for younger employees because they automatically get an extra benefit without paying any additional premium. This extra benefit doubles the amount of life insurance payable if you are age 35 or younger. Beginning on your 36th birthday, the extra benefit decreases 10 percent each year until, at age 45, this extra benefit ceases. For example, if you have Basic insurance and an annual salary of $47,500, your insurance amount is $50,000. If you die at age 35, your beneficiaries would receive $100,000. If you die at age 40, your beneficiaries would receive $75,000.

In order to determine the total amount of Basic, including the extra benefit, use OPM's online calculator by going to https://www.opm.gov/retirement-services/calculators/fegli-calculator/.

Accidental Death and Dismemberment benefits. These represent an added feature of Basic and Option A insurance at no additional cost. (See below for the conditions of payments of these benefits.) AD&D benefits are payable in addition to regular Basic and Option A benefits if:

  • • You sustain bodily injury solely through violent, external, and accidental means.
  • • As a direct result of that injury, independently of all other causes, and within one year afterwards, you lose your life, limb (hand/foot), or eyesight.

Accidental Death benefits are equal to the amount of your full Basic insurance amount, but without any increase through an Age Multiplication Factor. These benefits are payable to your beneficiary. Accidental Dismemberment benefits for a single dismemberment (the loss of a hand, foot, or eye) are equal to one-half of the Basic insurance amount; however, if you lose any two of these (double dismemberment), the full amount is payable. Accidental Dismemberment benefits are payable to you. These benefits are not available after retirement.

"Living benefit" for terminally ill employees. Enrollees who have been certified as terminally ill with a life expectancy of nine months or less may collect either a full Living benefit (all of your Basic insurance) or a partial Living benefit (expressed as a multiple of $1,000), while still living. This applies only to Basic insurance.

Withholdings and contributions

Basic insurance. The cost of Basic insurance is shared by the employee and the government and is based on the Basic insurance amount. The employee's share is two-thirds of the total cost and is withheld from pay. The biweekly withholding from the employee is $0.16 per $1,000 of insurance. An Extra benefit for employees under age 45 is provided without additional cost to the covered employee. However, employees must elect Basic to be eligible for any of the options.

The government's share is one-third of the total premium payment and is contributed from agency appropriations or other funds available for the payment of salaries. The U.S. Postal Service pays the entire cost of its employees' Basic insurance.

Option C -- Family. Coverage after retirement

Basic. Your Basic coverage (including all three options, but not Accidental Death and Dismemberment coverage) may be continued when you retire if:

  1. 1. You retire on an immediate annuity.
  2. 2. You had been insured for Basic, or "regular insurance," coverage for the entire period(s) during which the coverage was available for you, or for the last five years immediately before the starting date of your annuity.
  3. 3. You are enrolled in FEGLI on the date you retire.
  4. 4. And you have not converted to an individual policy.

The amount of your Basic coverage (including any extra coverage determined by the Age Multiplication Factor) is continued into retirement until the end of the month following the month in which you reach age 65 (or when you retire, if later). At this point your Basic coverage may begin to be reduced, depending on the level you elected to retain at the time of your retirement.

Cost of post-retirement Basic coverage. The cost of post-retirement Basic insurance coverage depends upon the level of protection you want to retain after you reach age 65 (or after you retire, if later). When you retire, you must choose one of three Reduction Levels: No Reduction, 50 percent Reduction, or 75 percent Reduction. The reduction begins the second month after you turn 65, or the second month after you retire, whichever is later. All of these levels provide full coverage, as when employed, up to age 65, and then:

  • • 75 percent Reduction -- reduces 2 percent per month after reaching age 65, to a minimum amount of 25 percent of the insurance amount at retirement. The monthly cost for this before age 65 is $0.3467 per $1,000 of insurance and, after age 65, there is no further cost.
  • • 50 percent Reduction -- reduces 1 percent per month after reaching age 65 to a minimum of 50 percent of the insurance amount at retirement. The monthly cost for this before age 65 is $1.0967 per $1,000 of insurance, and, after age 65, $0.75 per $1,000 of insurance.
  • • No Reduction -- 100 percent of the amount of Basic Life is retained after age 65. The monthly cost of this is $2.5967 per $1,000 of insurance before age 65 and, after age 65, $2.25 per $1,000 of insurance.

If you choose either the 50 percent Reduction or No Reduction, the full cost of the additional protection you are choosing will be deducted from your annuity. These withholdings begin at retirement. You must elect No Reduction if you previously elected a partial Living benefit. The election of either of these levels must be made at retirement, but may be cancelled later, unless you previously elected a partial Living benefit. If cancelled, the amount of your insurance will then be computed as if you had elected the 75 percent Reduction; withholdings will drop to the amount required for the 75 percent election, but you will not be entitled to any refund.

Option A. This option may also be continued after retirement under the same conditions as for Basic above, if you continue Basic. It has an automatic 75 percent reduction. You do not have a reduction choice with Option A. Withholdings will be made from your annuity for Option A until you reach age 65. After age 65 and retired, no further withholdings will be required, but your Option A coverage will be reduced by 2 percent each month until it reaches $2,500 (25 percent of the $10,000 coverage). The first reduction is effective at the end of the month following the month in which you either retire or reach age 65, whichever occurs later.

Phased retirement. For FEGLI purposes, an employee in phased retirement status is treated the same as when they were working full time. Phased retirees will continue to be enrolled in FEGLI and will continue to pay the same share of the premiums as if they were a full-time employee, not a part-time employee. FEGLI benefit coverage amounts will be based upon the full-time salary for the position occupied during the phased employment period.

Beneficiaries

For the FEGLI coverage on your life, you can name any beneficiary(ies) you choose, including a trust. You can also change beneficiaries at any time. Unless you specify otherwise, death benefits will be paid in the order of precedence, as follows:

  1. 1. The beneficiary(ies) you designate;
  2. 2. If there is no such beneficiary, to your widow or widower;
  3. 3. If none of the above, to your child or children in equal shares, with the share of any deceased child distributed among the descendants of that child;
  4. 4. If none of the above, to your parents in equal shares or the entire amount to a surviving parent;
  5. 5. If none of the above, to the court-appointed executor or administrator of your estate;
  6. 6. If none of the above, to your next of kin under the laws of the state where you lived at the time of your death.

A designation must be made if you wish to name as beneficiary any person, firm, organization, or other legal entity not stipulated in the order of precedence above, or if you wish a different order of precedence, or paid to a trust you have established for your minor children. Payments may be made to the estate of a deceased employee or annuitant.

There are exceptions to the order of precedence in the case of court orders and assignment of insurance.

Standard Form 2823, Designation of Beneficiary, may be used, or a written statement that has been signed by the insured and witnessed by two persons not named as beneficiaries may also be accepted if it has been received in the employing office prior to the death of the insured.

Claims for family members covered under Option C are paid to you as the insured employee in accordance with the FEGLI law. No other designations are permitted.

Discontinuance of FEGLI

Employees. Your entire insurance coverage stops on the earliest of the following dates:

  1. 1. The date you separated from federal service (unless you were eligible to continue during retirement or receipt of workers' compensation).
  2. 2. The date of expiration of a period of 12 months of continuous nonpay status (except if in receipt of benefits from the Office of Workers' Compensation and otherwise entitled), but employees may elect to continue Option B coverage by making direct premium payments.
  3. 3. The date of any other change in your employment which results in your ceasing to be an employee eligible for government insurance.
  4. 4. The last day of the pay period during which your employing office receives a waiver of insurance coverage from you.
  5. 5. The date of termination of the Federal Group Policy.

Note on furloughs: Point 2 above applies in instances of furlough, including furloughs caused by any discontinuance in appropriations for an agency or for the government as a whole. Insurance coverage continues for up to 12 consecutive months while the employee is in a nonpay status, without cost to the employee or the agency. The nonpay status may be continuous, or it may be broken by a return to duty for periods of less than four consecutive months.

Retirees. As a retired employee, you can cancel or reduce coverage at any time. This includes cancelling all life insurance, cancelling just some types or multiples of coverage, or switching to a greater reduction choice. Your FEGLI coverage stops on the earliest of the following dates:

  1. 1. The date of the termination of your annuity.
  2. 2. The effective date of your voluntary cancellation of your coverage.
  3. 3. The date of the termination of the Group Policy.

If you are reemployed with the government, ask your personnel office about your insurance rights as a reemployed annuitant.

Continuation of coverage during active military duty. 5 USC 8706(d) allows 24 months continuation of coverage for federal employees called to active military duty, with 12 months of coverage paid in full by the government, and the following 12 months paid in full by the insured.

Assignment of life insurance

Employees and former employees have the right to assign their FEGLI coverage (Basic, Option A, and Option B) to another person(s), trust(s), or corporation which will then pay them a cash amount for these benefits, thus providing a further means of obtaining financial resources for terminally ill employees. Assignment means that the insured gives up ownership of Basic, Option A, and Option B life insurance coverage forever but continues to pay for the insurance coverage from salary or annuity.

Assignment is a one-time act and is irrevocable which means that once you assign, you can't change your mind or cancel the assignment. But if you have elected a Living benefit, you may assign any remaining insurance. Such assignment of insurance is generally made to comply with the requirements of a court order in the case of divorce, for inheritance tax purposes, to obtain accelerated death benefits, or to satisfy a debt.

If you assign your life insurance, you also assign:

  1. 1. Your right to cancel or reduce your insurance coverage.
  2. 2. Your right to designate and change beneficiaries.
  3. 3. Your right to convert to a private insurance policy if your FEGLI coverage terminates for any reason other than cancellation by the assignee(s).
  4. 4. Your right to change the post-retirement reduction schedule on your Basic insurance coverage after the original election.

If you assign your insurance, you must assign all of it (although you do not have to assign all of it to the same person, firm or trust). Also, you must assign percentages (no dollar amounts) adding up to 100 percent of the insurance. You cannot assign dismemberment insurance, the Extra benefit, or Option C.

The Form RI 76-10, Assignment of Federal Employees' Group Life Insurance, may be obtained from your employing office or retirement system.
