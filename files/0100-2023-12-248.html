10. Reasonable Accommodation

In Spraglin v. Secretary of Defense, DLA, 01964954 (1997), the Commission held that providing a requested accommodation may not render a complaint moot where the failure to accommodate was intertwined with other allegations:

Appellant alleged that he was denied a reasonable accommodation when the agency failed to provide him with a larger electric cart to move to and from his work site. The agency stated that appellant was provided with a larger electric cart and that this interim action rendered the reasonable accommodation allegation moot. Since appellant alleged that the agency's denial of his requested reasonable accommodation ultimately caused his nonpromotion at issue, we find that the reasonable accommodation allegation is intertwined with the allegation regarding the nonpromotion. We find that the reasonable accommodation allegation should also be considered, together with the previously accepted non-promotion allegation. Thus, we find that the reasonable accommodation allegation was not rendered moot by the agency's interim action.

Moreover, the question of whether an appellant was provided a reasonable accommodation does not lend itself to a decision on procedural grounds. In Burks v. Secretary of Health and Human Services, SSA, 01964783 (1997), the Commission determined that the agency improperly dismissed a reasonable accommodation claim on the ground of mootness. The Commission in Burks stated:

The agency has essentially found that allegation 1 is moot because appellant has been provided with a reasonable accommodation. Because appellant has filed the instant appeal, we find that appellant is challenging the determination that he has been provided with a reasonable accommodation. The Commission finds that such a question of whether appellant has been provided with a reasonable accommodation is best addressed on the merits after an investigation and not in a procedural decision. Therefore, we find that allegation 1 should not have been dismissed as moot and that the agency should continue processing allegation 1.

A transfer to another facility does not render moot a failure to accommodate claim where the complainant alleges that it was the agency's accommodation failure that led to his transfer. Ortiz v. Postmaster General, 05960270 (1998).
