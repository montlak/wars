1. Time Limits

The time frames for the agency to investigate consolidated complaints are set forth in MD-110 at 5-15:

When a complaint has been consolidated with an earlier filed complaint, the agency must complete its investigation within the earlier of 180 days after the filing of the last complaint or 360 days after the filing of the oriGINAl complaint. A complainant has the right to request a hearing, even in the case of consolidated complaints, after 180 days have passed since the filing of the oriGINAl complaint, even if the agency's investigation has not been completed.

Many times a complainant will recognize that the new complaint is not like or related to the oriGINAl complaint, and simply seek EEO counseling anew. In such a case, the time limits for EEO counselor contact set forth at 1614.105 apply. See Chapter 2, subheading "The 45-Day Time Limit."

The more complicated situation arises when a complainant thinks that the new complaint might be properly amended to the underlying complaint, and therefore follows the procedures for amendment of complaints by writing to the EEO Director or Complaints Manager. Fortunately, unlike with amendment of complaints, the Commission has explicitly set forth a time limit for complainants to seek counseling after seeking an amendment that is denied. If the new claim is not appropriate for amendment, but should instead be consolidated, the complainant must be advised in writing that he or she must seek additional EEO counseling on the claim. The letter that the complainant wrote requesting the amendment, however, serves as the date of EEO counselor contact under 1614.105. MD-110 at 5-14. Therefore, to be timely, the letter requesting amendment must have been postmarked within 45 days of the date of the alleged discriminatory incident that formed the basis for the request.
