ABOUT THE AUTHOR

Natania M. Davis began working for Dewey Publications as the primary editor of its federal sector employment and labor law texts. Her work for Broida and Associate representing employees and federal agencies before the MSPB and EEOC and in alternative dispute resolution settings soon followed. After moving to Worcester, MA, Natania practiced general civil litigation, where she represented individuals and entities in state and federal court and ADR settings in a variety of matters. After starting a family, she returned to Dewey Publications as its primary legal editor and author or coauthor of several books. She now resides in Bentonville, Arkansas, with her husband and three children.

The Founding Author, the late Ernest C. Hadley, a graduate of the Antioch School of Law in Washington, D.C., practiced law from 1985-2014 in Washington and Massachusetts, representing employees and, occasionally, agencies in the federal sector, wrote many books about federal sector employment law, and taught the subject to lawyers, EEO and personnel specialists, and union representatives. Mr. Hadley helped to form and for years led the Federal Employment Law Training Group. After his retirement from law practice, teaching and writing about employment law, Mr. Hadley and his wife moved from Cape Cod to Lunenburg, Nova Scotia. They there established Nevermore Press, publishing historical fiction and creative nonfiction with a focus on life in the Canadian Maritimes. An alumnus of Bates College, Mr. Hadley earned an MFA in Writing from the Vermont College of Fine Arts and he studied in Massachusetts at the Fine Arts Work Center in Provincetown and at the Truro Center for the Arts.
