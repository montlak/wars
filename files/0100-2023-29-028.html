A. DEFINITION OF MIXED CASES AND APPEALS

There are two distinct terms used to describe cases that involve the overlapping jurisdiction of the EEOC and the MSPB--"mixed case complaints" and "mixed case appeals."

A "mixed case complaint" is defined at 29 CFR 1614.302(a)(1) as:

Mixed case complaint. A mixed case complaint is a complaint of employment discrimination filed with a federal agency based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or genetic information related to or stemming from an action that can be appealed to the Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB). The complaint may contain only an allegation of employment discrimination or it may contain additional allegations that the MSPB has jurisdiction to address.

A "mixed case appeal" is defined at 29 CFR 1614.302(a)(2):

Mixed case appeals. A mixed case appeal is an appeal filed with the MSPB that alleges that an appealable agency action was effected, in whole or in part, because of discrimination on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, disability, age, or genetic information.

If the definition of a mixed case sounds somewhat confusing, it is. The processing of mixed cases is an equally confusing and convoluted procedure. Reduced to English, a mixed case involves a personnel action within the jurisdiction of the Merit Systems Protection Board in which the employee raises an allegation of discrimination. It is a mixed case because it involves matters that are within the jurisdiction of both the MSPB and the EEOC. In a mixed case an employee may elect the forum in which to pursue those allegations. If the allegations are pursued in the agency EEO process, it is referred to as a mixed case complaint. If the allegations are pursued in the context of an MSPB appeal, it is referred to as a mixed case appeal.

Although reprisal is not included as a basic form of discrimination under the mixed case regulations, the regulation was intended to cover reprisal. Michaels v. Postmaster General, 03A20015 (2002).

As explained more fully below in the section of Effects of Election, whether a case is pursued as a mixed case complaint or a mixed case appeal, there is no right to a hearing before the EEOC. The right to a hearing is before the MSPB.
